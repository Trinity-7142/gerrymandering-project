## Partisan gerrymandering v. Racial gerrymandering
These are two different types of gerrymandering based on who the disfavored groups are.

*Partisan* gerrymandering is when an electoral district is drawn to advantage one political party over the other, dampening the power of the disadvantaged party. There's an ongoing legal debate on whether a map should stand if it is a result of partisan gerrymandering, since the Supreme Court has ruled in 2019 that the federal court cannot intervene when there's a partisan gerrymander.

*Racial* gerrymandering is when an electoral district is drawn based on race to dilute the voting power of minority groups. Unlike partisan gerrymandering, racial gerrymandering is recognized as unconstitutional under the 14th Amendment's Equal Protection Clause.
