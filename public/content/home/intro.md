In a nutshell, gerrymandering refers to the political manipulation of electoral district boundaries to advantage a certain political party, candidate, group, or socioeconomic class. It rigs the process of drawing the map of congressional districts to twist and dilute representation of voters. And it can happen in multiple ways.
