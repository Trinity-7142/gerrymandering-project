### **State-Level structure**

State legislative gerrymandering is especially dangerous because those legislatures control their own district drawing, congressional redistricting, and voting laws. This enables the Self-Reinforcing cycle of gerrymandering.

### **With Bad Policy, Everyone Is Harmed**

Gerrymandering harms everyone; Not just voters in gerrymandered districts or the members of the disadvantaged party. The consequences of gerrymandering are bad policies, as proven in many cases. And those policies harm all constituents, regardless of how their individual district was drawn. Then it contributes to low public trust in government, as it protects representatives with agendas that don’t align with the interests of the voters of their district. And even though the voters are unsatisfied with their representatives and want to bring a change, it’s only harder to do so every election cycle since their voting power is effectively weakened as a consequence of the deliberate policies that are passed by the beneficiaries of gerrymandering. This is a gigantic chain that ties down a healthy, functioning democracy.
