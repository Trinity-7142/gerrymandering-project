Tim Sheehy has served as Montana's junior U.S. Senator since 2025 and is the youngest Republican senator and the second-youngest U.S. senator overall. Born in Ramsey, Minnesota, he grew up in Shoreview and graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy with a degree in history in 2008. As a Navy SEAL officer and team leader, he completed several deployments and hundreds of missions, earning two decorations for valor in combat. In 2014, Sheehy founded Bridger Aerospace, an aerial firefighting and wildfire management company. A spinoff, Ascent Vision Technologies, was acquired by CACI International in 2020 for $350 million. During his campaign, Sheehy cited immigration, education, and border security as his top three priorities. He has also championed keeping public lands in public hands. He defeated three-term incumbent Jon Tester with 52.6% of the vote in what became the most expensive Senate race in U.S. history on a per-voter basis. He faced scrutiny for an undisclosed board membership at a free-market land-policy think tank and for remarks made at a closed-door fundraiser that were characterized as racially stereotyping Montana's Crow people.
