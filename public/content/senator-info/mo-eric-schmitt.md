Eric Schmitt is a Republican serving as the junior U.S. Senator from Missouri since 2023. He grew up in Bridgeton, in North St. Louis County, and is a sixth-generation Missourian from a family of farmers, teachers, and small business owners. Raised in a middle-class family, he developed a strong work ethic and an early interest in public service. A national conservative, his Senate priorities include protecting free speech, fighting the administrative state, and ensuring that Missourians are taken care of. In the Missouri Senate, he authored two of the largest tax cuts in state history and championed legislation to end taxation by citation. His son Stephen, born with a rare genetic condition and on the autism spectrum, motivated him to lead a bipartisan effort ensuring coverage for autism therapies. As Attorney General, he sued the federal government claiming it was censoring speech on social media, a case that reached the Supreme Court.
