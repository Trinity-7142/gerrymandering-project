Sen. Shelley Moore Capito (R-WV), born November 26, 1953, in Glen Dale, West Virginia, comes from a prominent political family — she is the daughter of Arch Moore, a three-time governor whose conviction for corruption ended his political career. She developed a reputation as a moderate, drawing criticism from the Tea Party wing for supporting federal programs, while liberals criticized her opposition to the EPA and promotion of the coal industry. She supports policies that promote economic growth, unleash energy potential, and lift up working families. She has been a key champion of substance use disorder and criminal justice reform, particularly relevant in a state hard-hit by the opioid epidemic. Her ADVANCE Act supporting nuclear energy development was signed into law in July 2024. She became the first woman elected to the U.S. Senate from West Virginia and currently chairs the Republican Policy Committee, the fourth-highest-ranking position among Senate Republicans.
