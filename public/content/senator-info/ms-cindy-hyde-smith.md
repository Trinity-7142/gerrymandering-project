Born in Brookhaven, Mississippi, Hyde-Smith is a graduate of Copiah–Lincoln Community College and the University of Southern Mississippi. She is a cattle farmer and a committed conservative Republican. In 2010, Hyde-Smith switched parties and became a Republican, citing her conservative beliefs. She is the first woman elected to represent Mississippi in Washington, D.C., winning a six-year term in November 2020 following her April 2018 appointment and a 2018 special election victory. Her platform centers on agriculture, leading the Senate Pro-Life Caucus and opposing efforts to expand mail-order abortions, and strongly advocating for Second Amendment rights. She established herself as a leader on rural healthcare and led the bipartisan effort to establish an Office of Rural Health within the CDC. Notably, a 2018 video surfaced in which she made a remark about attending a "public hanging," which was widely criticized as racially insensitive; she later apologized, saying it was intended as a joke.
