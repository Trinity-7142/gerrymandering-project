Born and raised in the Flatbush neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York, Sanders grew up in a working-class household where financial struggles shaped his later focus on income inequality. Though he has held elected office as an independent since 1981, he identifies as a democratic socialist and is serving his fourth Senate term, making him the longest-serving independent member of Congress in American history. His proposed policies emphasize reducing economic inequality and expanding social programs and workers' rights, including championing a Medicare for All single-payer healthcare plan and free tuition at public universities, tax increases on the wealthy, and stricter Wall Street regulations. As chairman of the Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee, he passed landmark legislation reforming the VA health care system in 2014. He sought the Democratic presidential nomination in 2016 and 2020, finishing second both times.
