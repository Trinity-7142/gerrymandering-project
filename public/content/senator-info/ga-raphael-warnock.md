Born in Savannah, Georgia, Warnock grew up in public housing as the eleventh of twelve children raised by two Pentecostal pastors. The first in his family to graduate from college, he was inspired by Dr. King's teachings to attend Morehouse College and earned a Master of Divinity from Union Theological Seminary and later completed a Ph.D. in theology in 2006. A progressive Democrat, Warnock has served as Senior Pastor of Atlanta's Ebenezer Baptist Church — Martin Luther King Jr.'s former pulpit — since 2005. He campaigned on a platform that included expanding healthcare coverage, reforming the federal criminal justice system, and forgiving student loans. He is the first African American to represent Georgia in the Senate and the first Black Democrat elected to the Senate from a Southern state. In office, he championed efforts to cap insulin costs for seniors at $35 a month and worked to address the maternal mortality crisis.
