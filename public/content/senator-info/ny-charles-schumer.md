Born and raised in the Sheepshead Bay neighborhood of Brooklyn, Schumer is the son of a small-business owner, an upbringing that shaped his lifelong focus on middle-class economic concerns. A graduate of Harvard College and Harvard Law School, he is a liberal Democrat who has represented New York in the Senate since 1999. He has championed expanding access to health care, strengthening gun background checks, making college more affordable, and reforming immigration. Elected to a fifth term in 2022, Schumer surpassed Daniel Patrick Moynihan and Jacob K. Javits as the longest-serving U.S. senator from New York. In 2021, he became the first New Yorker to serve as Senate Majority Leader, shepherding landmark legislation including the Inflation Reduction Act, the CHIPS and Science Act, and the bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act. He now serves as Senate Minority Leader in the 119th Congress.
