Born on July 5, 1951, in Pontotoc, Mississippi, to a lawyer and state senator father, Wicker earned his B.A. and J.D. from the University of Mississippi. He served on active duty in the U.S. Air Force before retiring from the Air Force Reserve in 2004 as a lieutenant colonel. A conservative Republican, Wicker has championed pro-growth policies to create jobs, limit federal overreach, protect life, and maintain a strong national defense. Governor Haley Barbour appointed him to the Senate in December 2007 to fill the seat vacated by Trent Lott; in 2024, he comfortably won reelection against Democratic challenger Ty Pinkins. He currently chairs the Senate Armed Services Committee, where he has focused on modernizing the military and strengthening national security. He authored the MD CARE Act of 2001, creating NIH centers of excellence for muscular dystrophy research. Wicker has been one of Ukraine's strongest Republican supporters, voting each time for aid to Ukraine.
