Born in Chicago to a Colombian mother and Mexican father, Gallego was raised by a single mother working whatever jobs he could find — at a pizza joint, construction sites, and a meatpacking plant — before becoming the first in his family to attend college, graduating from Harvard University. He enlisted in the Marine Corps, deploying to Iraq in 2005 with Lima Company, 3rd Battalion, 25th Marines — a unit that lost 22 Marines and a Navy Corpsman in eight months. He now serves as the first Latino to represent Arizona in the U.S. Senate. Though previously known as a progressive, he moved toward the political center in his 2024 Senate campaign, emphasizing his military service on national security and distancing himself from earlier progressive stances, focusing on economic issues and border security. His key issues include veterans' and military affairs, tribal concerns, reproductive health care access, housing affordability, and Arizona's water supply. Notably, he was one of 12 Senate Democrats who joined Republicans to vote for the Laken Riley Act, and he has faced unverified misconduct allegations from a GOP congresswoman, which he has denied.
