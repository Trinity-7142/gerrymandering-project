Angus King (born March 31, 1944, in Alexandria, Virginia) is an Independent politician representing Maine in the U.S. Senate. He moved to Maine after graduating from law school, where he worked as a lawyer, counsel for the U.S. Senate Subcommittee on Alcoholism and Narcotics, a television host, and an energy company owner before entering electoral politics. He served two terms as governor (1995–2003), enacting an agenda that defied categorization as liberal or conservative — championing environmental protection and education reforms while positioning himself as a pragmatic small-business owner. Sworn in as Maine's first Independent U.S. Senator in January 2013, he caucuses with the Democratic Party for committee purposes and is one of only two independents in the Senate. In the Senate, King has focused on national security, intelligence oversight, clean energy, healthcare access, veterans' support, opioid relief, and rural broadband. He led efforts to pass the Student Loan Certainty Act of 2013, saving students over $50 billion in interest. When launching his successful 2024 reelection campaign, he cited concern that "we're losing the middle in the Senate" as a central motivation. Upon turning 81 on March 31, 2025, he became the oldest U.S. senator in Maine history.
