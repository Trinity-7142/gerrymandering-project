John Cornyn (born February 2, 1952) is the senior U.S. Senator from Texas, born in Houston and raised in San Antonio by a father who flew B-17s in World War II before becoming an Air Force officer. A graduate of Trinity University, St. Mary's University School of Law, and the University of Virginia School of Law, Cornyn established himself as a conservative, though he is considered a member of the Republican establishment rather than the Tea Party. He served as a district court judge, an associate justice of the Texas Supreme Court, and Attorney General of Texas before entering the Senate in 2002. As a senator, he is known for his conservative stance on defense, veterans affairs, immigration, and open government. With more than 80 of his own bills signed into law, he has championed policies to bolster the Texas economy, create safer communities, and enhance national security. Key legislative achievements include authoring the Justice for Victims of Trafficking Act, signed into law in 2015, and the 2007 OPEN Government Act, the most sweeping reform of the Freedom of Information Act in a decade. In 2024, Cornyn ran to replace Sen. Mitch McConnell as Senate GOP leader but was defeated by Sen. John Thune. He currently faces a Republican primary runoff against Ken Paxton on May 26, 2026.
