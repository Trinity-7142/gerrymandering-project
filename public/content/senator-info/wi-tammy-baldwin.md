Tammy Baldwin (born February 11, 1962) is an American politician and lawyer serving as the junior United States Senator from Wisconsin. Born and raised in Madison, she was raised by her grandparents because her mother struggled with mental illness and drug addiction — an experience that shaped her policy priorities. A self-described progressive in the tradition of Wisconsin's Robert M. La Follette, her platform centers on affordable health care, manufacturing, education, and retirement security for working families. She led passage of the Respect for Marriage Act, protecting same-sex and interracial marriages. Baldwin was the first openly LGBTQ person and first woman elected to the U.S. Senate from Wisconsin (2012). She won re-election to a third term in 2024 by a narrow 0.85% margin, defeating Republican Eric Hovde even as Donald Trump carried the state. A financial disclosure controversy arose when reports revealed Baldwin co-owned a Washington penthouse not listed on her financial disclosures, raising conflict-of-interest questions given her seat on the Senate Appropriations Committee.
