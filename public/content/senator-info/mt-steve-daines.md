Born in Los Angeles and raised in Bozeman, Montana, Steve Daines worked his way through college earning a Bachelor of Science in chemical engineering from Montana State University. Before entering politics, he held positions at Procter & Gamble and the Montana-based software firm RightNow Technologies. A conservative Republican, he has focused on job creation, border security, cutting government spending, and protecting Second Amendment rights. He serves on the Senate Committees on Finance, Appropriations, Foreign Relations, Energy and Natural Resources, and Indian Affairs. Elected in 2014, he became the first Republican to win a U.S. Senate seat in Montana in the 21st century. He served as NRSC chairman for the 2024 election cycle, running unopposed. In March 2026, Daines unexpectedly announced he would not seek reelection.
