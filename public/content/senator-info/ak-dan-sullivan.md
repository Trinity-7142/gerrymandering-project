Dan Sullivan is a Republican politician, attorney, and Marine Corps veteran serving as the junior U.S. Senator from Alaska since 2015. He grew up in a suburb of Cleveland, Ohio, studied economics at Harvard University, and earned joint foreign service and Juris Doctor degrees from Georgetown University. He served 30 years in the United States Marine Corps, retiring as a Colonel in the Reserve in 2024. A conservative Republican, Sullivan focuses on natural resource development, national security, and limiting federal overreach. He serves on the Commerce, Science and Transportation; Armed Services; Environment and Public Works; and Veterans' Affairs committees. Notable legislative efforts include a fentanyl-awareness campaign and related legislation signed into law in December 2025, and a provision securing a $25 million annual FAA investment in Alaska aviation safety. Sullivan has publicly disputed the scientific consensus on human-caused climate change.
