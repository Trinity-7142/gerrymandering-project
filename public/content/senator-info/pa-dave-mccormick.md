Senator David H. McCormick was born in Washington, Pennsylvania, raised in Bloomsburg, and is the son of two educators. A Republican, he graduated from West Point in 1987 and received the Bronze Star while serving in the 82nd Airborne Division during the Gulf War. He earned a Ph.D. from Princeton in 1996 and later served in the George W. Bush administration as Under Secretary of the Treasury for International Affairs. He subsequently served as CEO of Bridgewater Associates, one of the world's largest hedge funds. His 2024 campaign focused on inflation, expanding Pennsylvania's natural gas supply, and immigration. After a narrow 2022 primary loss, McCormick defeated three-term incumbent Democratic Senator Bob Casey Jr. in what was widely considered an upset. During his campaign, his Pennsylvania residency — as he split time between Connecticut and Pennsylvania — was a major issue.
