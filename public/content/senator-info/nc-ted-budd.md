Ted Budd is a Republican businessman and politician serving as the junior U.S. Senator for North Carolina, having previously represented North Carolina's 13th congressional district in the House from 2017 to 2023. He grew up on a cattle and commercial chicken farm in rural Davie County. Budd holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from Appalachian State University, a Master of Theology from Dallas Theological Seminary, and an MBA from Wake Forest University. A staunch conservative, his platform has centered on support for law enforcement, border security, and building a wall on the southern border. As a member of the Senate Armed Services Committee, he secured nearly $400 million for construction at North Carolina's military installations in the FY2024 defense bill. In 2025, he co-sponsored the bipartisan Kids Off Social Media Act, which would set a minimum age of 13 for social media use and restrict algorithmically targeted content for users under 17. His 2022 Senate campaign received endorsements from Donald Trump.
