James Elroy Risch is an American lawyer and politician who has served as the junior United States Senator from Idaho since 2009. Born to a lineman for a telephone company and a school cafeteria worker, Risch worked through college cleaning carpets, serving food, and harvesting peas — a working-class background that informs his emphasis on economic self-reliance. He has established himself as among the most conservative members of Congress, prioritizing Second Amendment rights, opposition to wasteful federal spending, and rural Idahoans' way of life. He currently serves as chairman of the Foreign Relations Committee and advocates for Idaho's interests on the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee. As governor in 2006, he lowered property taxes by 20% and set aside $100 million for schools. In the Senate, notable recent legislation includes the Accelerating Reliable Capacity (ARC) Act, which would establish a risk reduction program for new commercial nuclear reactors, and the Countering Wrongful Detention Act of 2025. Risch is up for reelection in 2026.
