Christopher Scott Murphy (born August 3, 1973) is an American lawyer, author, and politician serving since 2013 as the junior United States senator from Connecticut. He grew up in Wethersfield, Connecticut, and earned his bachelor's degree in history and political science from Williams College and his law degree from the University of Connecticut. A liberal-to-moderate Democrat, Murphy has been a strong voice in the Senate fighting for job creation, affordable health care, education, sensible gun laws, and a forward-looking foreign policy. Following the Sandy Hook elementary school shooting in 2012, he became one of the leading proponents of commonsense reforms to reduce gun violence, championing bipartisan bills aimed at expanding background checks. He also led a bipartisan effort to reform the mental health system, working across the aisle to craft the first comprehensive mental health bill in the Senate in decades. As a member of the Foreign Relations Committee, he has been an outspoken proponent of diplomacy and international human rights. In 2024, Murphy was elected to a third term.
