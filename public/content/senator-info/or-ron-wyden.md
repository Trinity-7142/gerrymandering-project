Ron Wyden is the senior United States Senator from Oregon, a seat he has held since 1996. Born in Kansas to Jewish refugees from Nazi Germany, he grew up in Palo Alto, California. He attended UC Santa Barbara on a basketball scholarship before transferring to Stanford University, then earned a law degree from the University of Oregon in 1974. After graduating, Wyden worked as an advocate for the elderly, co-founding the Oregon Gray Panthers. Known for libertarian-leaning stances within the Democratic Party, Wyden has been a prominent advocate for privacy rights, internet freedom, and limiting government surveillance. He serves as ranking member of the Senate Finance Committee. Key accomplishments include an influential role in the passage of Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act and authoring laws extending permanent wilderness protections to over 400,000 acres, including Mt. Hood and the Columbia River Gorge.
