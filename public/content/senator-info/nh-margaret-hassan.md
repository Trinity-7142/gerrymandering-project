Margaret "Maggie" Hassan is a Democrat elected to the U.S. Senate from New Hampshire in 2016. Born in Boston, she graduated from Brown University and earned a J.D. from Northeastern University School of Law, after which she worked in private practice and later as associate general counsel at Brigham and Women's Hospital. A moderate Democrat known for bipartisanship, she was named one of the most bipartisan members of Congress in 2024, ranking third among senators by the Lugar Center. Her Senate priorities include lowering health care costs, combating the opioid crisis, expanding broadband access, and addressing climate change. She secured a ninefold increase in opioid-crisis funding for New Hampshire and partnered with Republican Senator Bill Cassidy to pass a law ending surprise medical billing. She is the second woman in American history to be elected both Governor and U.S. Senator.
