Born on September 17, 1933, in New Hartford, Iowa, Chuck Grassley grew up farming corn and soybeans and also worked on factory floors for two decades, holding membership in the International Association of Machinists. A conservative Republican, his pragmatic conservatism and dedication to his constituents have allowed him to maintain a respected presence in Congress over the decades. His key priorities include extending the Trump-era tax cuts and pushing to lower drug prices and hold the pharmaceutical industry accountable. Grassley currently serves as President pro tempore of the Senate and has been Iowa's senior senator since 1981. He is the longest-serving U.S. senator in Iowa history. A notable tradition: since first taking office, Grassley has held public meetings in all of Iowa's 99 counties each year, giving rise to the term "full Grassley." In 2025, he clashed with President Trump over the Senate's blue slip tradition for judicial nominees, defending it as "a necessary and time-honored check on the executive branch."
