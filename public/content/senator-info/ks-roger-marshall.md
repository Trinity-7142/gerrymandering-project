Roger Wayne Marshall is a Republican politician, physician, and former military officer serving as the junior U.S. Senator from Kansas since 2021. A fifth-generation farm kid from Butler County, Marshall became the first in his family to attend college. He earned a B.S. in biochemistry from Kansas State University and an M.D. from the University of Kansas. He also served seven years in the U.S. Army Reserve, reaching the rank of captain. In the Senate, he has been a congressional leader on healthcare and agriculture policy and a national voice on pro-life issues. He serves on the Agriculture, Nutrition & Forestry; Finance; Budget; and HELP Committees. His priorities include improving healthcare access, supporting agriculture, and strengthening border security, and he has introduced legislation to expand rural telehealth services. Notably, Marshall was reported to have violated the STOCK Act by disclosing up to 17 months late stock purchases made by one of his dependent children. He has voted in line with President Trump's positions 98% of the time.
