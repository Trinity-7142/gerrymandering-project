Born on February 13, 1946, in Brooklyn, New York, Blumenthal came from a well-to-do family; his father was a prominent commodities broker. With a father who fled Nazi Germany and a mother who left Nebraska's farmland to become a social worker, Blumenthal was raised with a deep dedication to public service. A liberal Democrat, he has held a generally liberal position on social issues, championing the Affordable Care Act and supporting abortion rights. He served an unprecedented five terms as Connecticut's Attorney General, whose aggressive enforcement on consumer protection, environmental stewardship, and labor rights helped reshape the role of state attorneys general nationwide. Originally sworn in on January 5, 2011, Blumenthal is currently serving his third Senate term. In the Senate, his key priorities include veterans' legislation — expanding employment opportunities, increasing housing access, and providing health care for those exposed to toxic chemicals during service — as well as holding tech platforms accountable for privacy failings and harmful content. Notably, during his 2010 campaign, reports emerged that Blumenthal had misspoken by saying he served "in Vietnam," when he had in fact served stateside in the Marine Corps Reserves.
