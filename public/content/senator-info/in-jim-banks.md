Jim Banks was born on July 16, 1979, in Columbia City, Indiana. The son of a factory worker and a nursing home cook, he was the first in his family to attend college, earning a bachelor's degree from Indiana University and an MBA from Grace College. A staunch conservative Republican, his Senate priorities include strengthening American industry, ending the crisis at the southern border, and dismantling what he calls "needless" bureaucracy. He is a vocal advocate for strengthening the military, securing better care for veterans, cutting taxes, protecting the right to life, and defending religious freedom. He was the first Indiana state legislator to take an extended leave of absence to fulfill active military service, deploying to Afghanistan in 2014 and returning in 2015. In November 2024, Banks won Indiana's open Senate seat with more than 60% of the vote, having been endorsed by Donald Trump.
