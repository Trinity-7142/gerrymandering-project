Kevin Cramer (R) has served as the junior U.S. Senator for North Dakota since 2019. A native of Kindred, North Dakota, he chaired the state Republican Party from 1991 to 1993 and served as state Tourism Director and state Economic Development Director before entering Congress. A conservative advocate for energy security and the free market, Cramer has a record of cutting budgets and limiting government regulation. In the Senate, he serves on the Armed Services, Environment and Public Works, Veterans' Affairs, and Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs committees. In 2018, he defeated Senator Heidi Heitkamp, becoming the first Republican elected to North Dakota's Class I Senate seat since 1958. Notable accomplishments include leading efforts to create the U.S. Space Force, reauthorize the Export-Import Bank, and negotiate the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law. In December 2023, his son Ian stole his wife's car while in a mental health crisis and fatally collided with a police vehicle, killing a Mercer County deputy.
