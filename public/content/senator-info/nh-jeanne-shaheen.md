Born in St. Charles, Missouri, to a working-class family — her father rose to a management role in a shoe factory and her mother was a church secretary — Shaheen taught high school before moving to New Hampshire in 1973. She is widely considered a moderate Democrat, pragmatic and willing to reevaluate her positions. Shaheen is the first woman elected both governor and a U.S. senator. She was sworn into the Senate on January 3, 2009. Her priorities have centered on combating the opioid epidemic — her top legislative priority — delivering a sevenfold increase in treatment resources through her Appropriations Committee work, and authoring the Sexual Assault Survivors' Rights Act, signed into law in 2016. Her strong stance against Vladimir Putin's aggression led to her being sanctioned by the Kremlin. In 2024, she was ranked among the top 10 most bipartisan senators. In March 2025, she announced she would not seek reelection in 2026.
