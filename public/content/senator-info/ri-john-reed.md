John Francis "Jack" Reed (born November 12, 1949) is a Democratic politician and former Army officer serving as the senior U.S. Senator from Rhode Island, first elected in 1996. Born and raised in Providence in a working-class family — his mother worked in a factory and his father was a custodian — Reed earned degrees from West Point, Harvard Kennedy School, and Harvard Law School, and served in the 82nd Airborne Division as a paratrooper and ranger. He has consistently held generally liberal positions on social and economic issues. His legislative focus has emphasized national defense, education, economic development, and consumer protection. He chaired the Senate Armed Services Committee during the 117th and 118th Congresses, and authored the HEARTH Act, signed into law to streamline homeless assistance programs. Reed has missed only 0.5% of roll call votes since 1991, well below the senatorial median of 2.8%.
