Born in Chicago, Illinois, in 1957, Rosen grew up in a working-class family, was the first in her family to graduate from college, and worked multiple jobs — including as a cocktail waitress — while taking out student loans. A former computer programmer with no prior political experience, she is a Democrat who represents Henderson, Nevada. She is the first computer programmer and first former synagogue president ever elected to the Senate. Rosen was elected to the Senate in 2018, defeating one-term Republican Dean Heller, and was reelected in 2024, defeating Republican Sam Brown 48% to 46%. Her platform centers on restoring Nevada's tourism and hospitality industry, bolstering clean energy, and supporting small businesses, while capping insulin at $35/month and enabling Medicare to negotiate prescription drug prices. She helped write and pass the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law. A notable controversy arose when she blocked President Biden's judicial nominee Adeel Mangi in 2024, a vacancy subsequently filled by a Trump appointee.
