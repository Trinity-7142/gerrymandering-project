Born in Philadelphia and raised in Wilmington, Delaware, Blunt Rochester's father was an educator and city council president, while her mother worked as a retail clerk. A Democrat with a centrist, bipartisan reputation, she served in the cabinets of two Delaware governors as Secretary of Labor, Deputy Secretary of Health and Social Services, and State Personnel Director. Her Senate platform centered on creating good jobs, protecting seniors, and securing fundamental freedoms — from reproductive rights to voting rights. She founded and co-chaired the bipartisan Congressional Future of Work Caucus. She is the first woman and first African American to represent Delaware in both chambers of Congress, and is one of only the third and fourth Black women ever elected to the U.S. Senate, alongside Maryland's Angela Alsobrooks.
