A native of Gallatin, Tennessee, Hagerty's mother was a career schoolteacher and his father, a veteran, worked road construction. He earned a B.A. in economics from Vanderbilt University and a J.D. from Vanderbilt Law School. A conservative Republican, Hagerty built a career in private equity, co-founding Hagerty Peterson & Company. He served as the 30th U.S. Ambassador to Japan under President Trump. His platform centers on economic growth, border security, Second Amendment rights, and a strong military. He has been the primary sponsor of eight enacted bills, including the GENIUS Act on digital assets. Notably, in July 2022, Hagerty was reported to have violated the STOCK Act by failing to disclose stock sales from his dependent children's trusts on time.
