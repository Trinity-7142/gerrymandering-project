Born in the Southern Oregon timber town of Myrtle Creek, where his father worked as a millwright, Merkley was the first in his family to attend college. He earned an undergraduate degree from Stanford and a graduate degree in Public Policy from Princeton's Woodrow Wilson School. A progressive Democrat, his Senate goal is to make Washington work for working Americans — creating good jobs, protecting consumers, and ensuring access to affordable education. His platform centers on combating dark money in politics, addressing the climate crisis, and tackling rising costs and stagnating wages. He defeated two-term Republican incumbent Gordon Smith in 2008 and was reelected in 2014 and 2020. During his tenure, Merkley has been an advocate of progressivism and was the only U.S. senator to endorse Bernie Sanders in the 2016 Democratic presidential primaries. In October 2025, he held the Senate floor for 22 hours and 36 minutes, delivering the fourth-longest speech in Senate history.
