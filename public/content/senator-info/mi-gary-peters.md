Gary Charles Peters was born on December 1, 1958, in Pontiac, Michigan. His father was a public school teacher and World War II veteran; his mother, a French nurse's aide who met his father during the war, helped unionize her workplace. A Democrat, Peters volunteered for the U.S. Navy Reserve, earning a Seabee Combat Warfare Specialist designation and rising to the rank of Lieutenant Commander. He holds multiple advanced degrees, including a law degree from Wayne State University and an MBA from the University of Detroit Mercy. He has focused on a stronger economy, good-paying jobs, affordable health care, and Great Lakes protection. In the 117th Congress, Peters authored 19 bills signed into law — the most by any senator in over 40 years. A nonpartisan center recognized him as the 2nd-most bipartisan Senator during the 117th Congress. In January 2025, he announced he will not seek reelection in 2026.
