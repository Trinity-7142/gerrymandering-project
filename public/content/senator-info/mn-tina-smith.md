Tina Smith is a Democrat serving as the junior U.S. Senator from Minnesota since 2018. Born in Albuquerque, New Mexico in 1958, she worked on the Trans-Alaska Pipeline at age 19 to earn money for college before earning a B.A. in political science from Stanford University and an M.B.A. from Dartmouth College. She moved to Minnesota in the 1980s to work for General Mills and later became a vice president at Planned Parenthood of Minnesota. Governor Mark Dayton appointed her to fill the U.S. Senate seat vacated by Al Franken's resignation in 2018, and she won the 2018 special election and was elected to a full term in 2020. A progressive, she has been a vocal advocate for racial justice, women's reproductive rights, and clean energy. Her collaborative approach has led to passage of over 70 bills and provisions benefiting working families, farmers, veterans, and Tribal communities. In February 2025, Smith announced she will not seek reelection in 2026.
