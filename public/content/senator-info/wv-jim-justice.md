James Conley Justice II is an American politician and businessman serving since 2025 as the junior U.S. Senator from West Virginia and a member of the Republican Party, having previously served as the state's 36th governor from 2017 to 2025. Born April 27, 1951, in Charleston, he grew up in Raleigh County and earned both his bachelor's and master's degrees from Marshall University. He inherited a coal mining business from his father encompassing 94 companies and owns the Greenbrier, a luxury resort and National Historic Landmark in White Sulphur Springs. His net worth was estimated at $664.2 million in 2025, making him the richest U.S. senator as of that year. Though a registered Republican, he ran for governor as a Democrat in 2016, then switched back to the Republican Party within seven months of taking office at a rally with President Trump. Since his switch, Justice has been described as a moderate or liberal Republican by some observers. He has campaigned and governed with strong support for the coal industry and opposes raising taxes, though he has backed increasing teachers' salaries. His 2024 Senate campaign was overshadowed by lawsuits related to unpaid debts from his private businesses, including defaulted bank loans and unpaid taxes, with trustees twice moving to foreclose on the Greenbrier Resort. Justice became the first Republican to win his Senate seat since 1956.
