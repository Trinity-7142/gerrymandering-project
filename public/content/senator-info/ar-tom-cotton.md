Born on May 13, 1977, in Dardanelle, Arkansas, Cotton grew up on his family's cattle farm. He attended Harvard University and Harvard Law School, graduating in 2002. He then joined the U.S. Army as an infantry officer, completing combat tours in both Iraq and Afghanistan. His military decorations include the Bronze Star Medal, Combat Infantryman Badge, and Ranger Tab. He has served as the junior U.S. Senator from Arkansas since 2015. A member of the Republican Party, Cotton has established himself as a leading conservative voice on national security, immigration, and foreign policy. He is particularly known for hawkish foreign policy views toward China, Iran, and Palestine. In November 2024, Senate Republicans selected Cotton as conference chair, making him the third-highest-ranked Republican in the Senate. He made headlines in 2015 for co-authoring an open letter to Iran's leaders warning that any nuclear deal with the Obama administration would require congressional approval. Cotton is running for re-election to the U.S. Senate in the general election on November 3, 2026.
