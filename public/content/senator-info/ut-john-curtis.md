Born in Ogden, Utah, Curtis served a two-year LDS mission in Taiwan and is a fluent Mandarin speaker. He earned a business management degree from Brigham Young University and later served as COO of Action Target, a Provo-based shooting range supplier. Curtis is generally considered a moderate Republican. He was elected to the U.S. Senate in 2024 to succeed Mitt Romney. His platform centers on American energy dominance, fiscal responsibility, and countering China's influence. He is the founder of the Conservative Climate Caucus and passed 27 bills into law during his House tenure, addressing public lands, human trafficking, and small business regulation. Before even being sworn in, his potential opposition vote helped sink Matt Gaetz's nomination as Attorney General.
