Born in Malden, Massachusetts, on July 11, 1946, Markey was raised in a working-class Irish Catholic family. He was the first member of his family to attend college, earning both his bachelor's degree and J.D. from Boston College. A progressive Democrat, he has focused on climate change and energy policy, chairing the House Select Committee on Energy Independence and Global Warming from 2007 to 2011. He is the Senate author of the Green New Deal. His key issues include climate change, clean energy, net neutrality, nuclear non-proliferation, and consumer privacy. He co-authored the landmark Waxman-Markey bill, the only comprehensive climate legislation ever to pass a chamber of Congress. He is the longest-tenured House member ever elected to the Senate, and fended off a primary challenge from Joseph Kennedy III to win reelection in 2020. His progressive policies have earned him an "icon to Gen Z activists" reputation, and he is running for reelection in 2026.
