Sen. Padilla serves as the senior U.S. Senator from California
, 
raised in the working-class community of Pacoima in the San Fernando Valley, the son of parents who immigrated to Los Angeles from Mexico.
 
He earned a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering from MIT in 1994
 and is a **Democrat** whose political entry was shaped by opposition to anti-immigrant legislation; 
he has established himself as a champion of **voting rights**, **climate action**, and ensuring everyone has a fair shot at the American dream.
 
He has campaigned on securing COVID-19 relief, pushing for bold **climate action**, introducing legislation to increase **affordable housing**, and leading the fight for **voting rights** and **immigration reform**.
 
His first Senate bill, the **Citizenship for Essential Workers Act**, seeks to create a pathway to citizenship for undocumented immigrants who served as essential workers during the COVID-19 pandemic.
 
He is the **first Latino** to represent California in the U.S. Senate
, and 
was re-elected as California Secretary of State in 2018, receiving the most votes of any Latino elected official in the United States.
 
He drew national attention when federal agents forced him to the ground and handcuffed him as he sought to question Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem during her June 2025 press conference on immigration enforcement in Los Angeles.
