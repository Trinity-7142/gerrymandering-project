Alex Padilla (born March 22, 1973, Los Angeles, California) is a Democrat and the first Latino to represent California in the U.S. Senate. The son of Mexican immigrants — his father a line cook and his mother a house cleaner — he earned a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering from MIT in 1994. In the Senate, Padilla has established himself as a champion of voting rights, climate action, and immigration reform. He chairs the Senate Judiciary Subcommittee on Immigration, and his first bill sought a pathway to citizenship for undocumented essential workers. As California's Secretary of State (2015–21), he established automatic and same-day voter registration and secured $500 million to upgrade the state's voting systems. A notable recent controversy: Padilla was forcibly removed from a DHS press conference in June 2025 and briefly handcuffed before being released.
