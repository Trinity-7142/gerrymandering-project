Born in Lincoln, Nebraska, Fischer attended the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, earning a degree in education, and later built a ranching business with her husband in the Nebraska Sandhills. A Republican, she has served as Nebraska's senior U.S. senator since 2013, having previously served in the Nebraska Legislature from 2005 to 2013. Her positions are broadly conservative — opposing abortion and gun control while supporting a balanced-budget amendment and opposing tax increases. Her platform emphasizes national defense, agriculture, and infrastructure. She chairs the Subcommittee on Strategic Forces, focusing on America's nuclear triad, space as a warfighting domain, and missile defense. A landmark state-level achievement was the Build Nebraska Act, directing an estimated $1.2 billion in sales tax revenue to roads over 20 years. She is the first woman to be reelected to the U.S. Senate from Nebraska. Her 2024 reelection was notably competitive, with independent Dan Osborn closing what had been a 19-point margin of victory in 2018 to just 6.7 points.
