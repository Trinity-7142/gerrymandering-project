Born February 20, 1942, in Tuscumbia, Alabama, McConnell has said his family "almost went broke" after he was paralyzed by a polio attack at age two and received treatment at the Roosevelt Warm Springs Institute for Rehabilitation. His family moved to Louisville, Kentucky, when he was 13. A conservative Republican, McConnell has championed an internationalist foreign policy grounded in strong national defense, robust trade, and strategic alliances, along with tax cuts and limited government. In his seventh Senate term, he is Kentucky's longest-serving senator and served as the longest-serving Senate party leader in U.S. history, holding the majority leader post from 2015 to 2021. His decision to block a Supreme Court vacancy in 2016 gave Republicans the opportunity to confirm three justices, and he oversaw a total of 234 lifetime federal judicial appointments. In 2025, McConnell announced he would not seek reelection.
