Born and raised in Cheyenne and on a cattle ranch in Laramie County, Wyoming, Lummis is a Republican attorney serving as the junior U.S. Senator from Wyoming since 2021, having previously served as the state's at-large U.S. Representative from 2009 to 2017. She was a founding member of the House Freedom Caucus and is a staunch fiscal conservative. As chair of the Senate Banking Committee's Subcommittee on Digital Assets, Lummis became Congress's most consistent pro-crypto voice, repeatedly introducing bills to ease regulatory acceptance of cryptocurrency, including market structure legislation, crypto tax proposals, and the BITCOIN Act to establish a government bitcoin stockpile. She was the first woman to represent Wyoming in the Senate. Ethics watchdogs raised concerns about the timing of her crypto-industry fundraising relative to her introduction of crypto legislation. In December 2025, Lummis announced she would not seek reelection in 2026.
