Born in Shreveport, Louisiana, where his father was stationed with the U.S. Air Force, Boozman was raised in Fort Smith, Arkansas. He attended the University of Arkansas, playing football for the Razorbacks, later earning an optometry degree, and co-founded a private optometry clinic in 1977 while volunteering for low-income families. A conservative Republican, he opposes gun control and the Affordable Care Act, and has been particularly focused on agriculture and foreign trade given Arkansas's rural economy. He was elected to the Senate in 2010, defeating Democratic incumbent Blanche Lincoln by a 21-point margin. In January 2025, he was named chairman of the Senate Agriculture Committee, where his stated priority is passing a farm bill providing certainty and predictability for farmers and ranchers. A senior member of the Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee, he has authored key provisions improving treatments for service members with eye trauma and reinstating veteran GI Bill work-study benefits. Boozman underwent emergency heart surgery in 2014 to repair a flawed aorta, and won his third Senate term in 2022 with 65.8% of the vote.
