A fifth-generation Floridian born and raised in Plant City, Florida, Ashley Moody is a Republican politician and attorney serving as the junior United States Senator from Florida. She earned bachelor's, master's, and law degrees from the University of Florida, and a Master of Laws in International Law from Stetson University. In 2006, at age thirty-one, she became the youngest judge in Florida when elected as Circuit Court Judge of the Thirteenth Judicial Circuit in Hillsborough County. She then served as Florida's 38th Attorney General from 2019 to 2025, chairing Florida's Statewide Council on Human Trafficking and securing billions for the state through opioid litigation. In January 2025, Governor Ron DeSantis appointed Moody to the Senate seat vacated by Marco Rubio, who became U.S. Secretary of State. She is only the second woman ever to represent Florida in the Senate. As Senator, Moody is focused on border security, the rule of law, and supporting President Trump's agenda. She is running for the Republican nomination in the 2026 special Senate election in Florida.
