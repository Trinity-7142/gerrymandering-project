Todd Young is the senior Republican U.S. Senator from Indiana, serving since 2017. A self-described fifth-generation Hoosier, Young grew up in a working-class household, with early jobs including delivering newspapers and providing janitorial services at the family business. After high school, he enlisted in the Navy, earned an appointment to the U.S. Naval Academy, and graduated cum laude in 1995 with a degree in political science before commissioning into the Marine Corps. A conservative who has developed a reputation as a conservative problem solver, Young focuses on outcompeting China, border security, economic growth, affordable housing, veterans' issues, and artificial intelligence. His landmark legislative achievement is co-sponsoring the CHIPS and Science Act, a $280 billion bill to promote technology research and bolster the American semiconductor industry. Young is also noted for joining bipartisan bills among the most frequent of all senators.
