John Thune grew up in Murdo, South Dakota, the son of two educators. A graduate of Biola University and the University of South Dakota, Thune earned a reputation in Congress as a conservative Republican who generally votes with party leadership. He has been a leading advocate for 5G deployment and rural broadband expansion, and prioritizes agriculture, energy, and tax policy for South Dakota. He made history in 2004 by defeating a sitting Senate party leader for the first time in 52 years. Republicans elected Thune as Senate Majority Leader in November 2024, succeeding Sen. Mitch McConnell. Thune drew notable attention for pushing back on Donald Trump's false claims of winning the 2020 presidential election, though the two later reconciled. Politico has described Thune as "unambiguously conservative but temperamentally moderate."
