Thom Tillis (born August 30, 1960) is a Republican politician serving as a U.S. Senator from North Carolina. Born into a working-class family, his family frequently relocated for work during his childhood, and after high school he could not afford college, starting out as a warehouse records clerk earning minimum wage. Through hard work, he eventually earned his degree at 36 and became a top-level executive at PricewaterhouseCoopers and IBM. He served in the North Carolina House of Representatives from 2007 to 2015, including as State House Speaker from 2011 to 2015. Generally regarded as a moderate Republican, Tillis has voted for measures such as the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and the Respect for Marriage Act. His 2014 Senate campaign platform included repealing the Affordable Care Act and requiring a balanced federal budget. In the Senate, he played a major role in writing and passing the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act. In 2023, the North Carolina Republican Party voted to censure him over his stances on immigration and gay marriage, and in June 2025 he opposed the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act," after which he announced he would not seek a third term.
