Born in Narberth, Pennsylvania, Hickenlooper attended Wesleyan University before building a career as a petroleum geologist, then co-founding the Wynkoop Brewing Company in 1988 — one of the first brewpubs in the U.S. A Democrat, he served as the 42nd Governor of Colorado from 2011 to 2019 and as mayor of Denver from 2003 to 2011. In the Senate, he has focused on lowering healthcare and prescription drug costs, gun safety, protecting public lands, and combating climate change. He serves on the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee, where he has played a key role in advancing clean energy initiatives. He was the first person in Colorado history elected to the Senate after serving as both mayor of Denver and governor. While governor, he was fined $2,750 by the Colorado Independent Ethics Commission for twice violating the state's gift ban. In Congress, he has earned distinction for attracting bipartisan cosponsors on legislation.
