Martin Heinrich (born October 17, 1971) is the senior U.S. Senator from New Mexico, serving since 2013. Though born in Nevada, he grew up in Cole Camp, Missouri, the son of a telephone lineman and a factory worker. The first in his family to attend college, he earned a mechanical engineering degree from the University of Missouri in 1995. A Democrat based in Albuquerque, Heinrich focuses on clean energy jobs, New Mexico's national laboratories and military installations, and is a strong advocate for working families and public lands. He helped designate the Río Grande del Norte and Organ Mountains-Desert Peaks National Monuments. A co-founder of the bipartisan Senate AI Caucus, he has co-authored several AI-related bills addressing research funding, journalist protections, and AI's environmental impact. He comfortably won reelection in 2024 over Republican challenger Nella Domenici.
