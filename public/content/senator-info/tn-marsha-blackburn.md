Marsha Blackburn is the senior United States Senator from Tennessee, born in Laurel, Mississippi, to a father who worked in sales and management. She attended Mississippi State University on a 4-H scholarship, earning a B.S. in home economics. She later owned Marketing Strategies, a promotional event management firm. A staunch conservative, GovTrack estimated Blackburn to be the most ideologically conservative member of the Senate in the 2019 legislative year. Throughout her Senate career, she has advocated for conservative positions on fiscal, regulatory, and social issues, including border security, opposition to abortion rights, and support for veterans' programs and national defense. In 2018, she became the first woman elected to the U.S. Senate from Tennessee. Notable accomplishments include authoring the REPORT Act, enacted in 2024, which expanded reporting obligations for online providers to help protect children from sexual abuse. In August 2025, Blackburn announced her candidacy for governor of Tennessee in 2026.
