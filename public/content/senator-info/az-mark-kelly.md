Mark Edward Kelly (born February 21, 1964) is the senior United States Senator from Arizona, a seat he has held since 2020. Born in Orange, New Jersey, and raised in West Orange, he is the son of two retired police officers. He flew combat missions during the Gulf War as a naval aviator and commanded the final mission of Space Shuttle Endeavour in 2011. A Democrat who ran as a moderate in 2020 and voiced support for bipartisanship, Kelly's platform centers on creating good-paying jobs, cutting taxes for middle-class families, and lowering costs for Arizonans. Key accomplishments include helping pass the bipartisan infrastructure law and serving as a chief negotiator of the CHIPS and Science Act. Kelly and his wife Gabby Giffords co-founded Americans for Responsible Solutions, which campaigns for gun control. He was on Kamala Harris's short list for running mate in 2024.
