Born in Greenwich, Connecticut, Coons grew up in Hockessin, Delaware, where his parents struggled financially and divorced in the mid-1970s. He earned both a master's degree in religion from Yale Divinity School and a law degree from Yale Law School. A Democrat, Coons is recognized for his moderate policy positions and commitment to consensus-building. First elected in 2010, he has focused on bipartisan cooperation, economic development, foreign policy, and judicial integrity. His platform emphasizes student loan reform, environmental protections, and access to healthcare. In 2023, Coons helped secure a $750 million federal investment for the Mid-Atlantic Clean Hydrogen Hub, which will develop hydrogen energy production in and around Delaware and create as many as 20,000 jobs. Coons drew criticism for declining to fill a Third Circuit judicial vacancy during the Biden administration, which allowed President Trump to flip the court to a conservative majority in 2025. He became Delaware's senior senator and dean of its congressional delegation when Tom Carper retired in January 2025.
