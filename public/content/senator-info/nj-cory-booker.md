Cory Anthony Booker is a Democratic U.S. Senator from New Jersey and the state's first African American senator, serving since 2013. Raised in Harrington Park, New Jersey, his parents were among the first Black IBM executives. He earned degrees from Stanford University and Oxford on a Rhodes Scholarship before graduating from Yale Law School. A self-described liberal and progressive Democrat, he has a consistently liberal voting record. His policy priorities include criminal justice reform, notably co-crafting the First Step Act (2018), expanding economic opportunity, raising wages, and protecting the Affordable Care Act. He has championed "baby bonds" — Treasury-managed savings accounts for newborns — as a tool to address the racial wealth gap. Notably, in March–April 2025, he delivered the longest speech in U.S. Senate history, lasting 25 hours and five minutes, in protest of the second presidency of Donald Trump. He drew attention by being the only Democrat to vote to confirm Charles Kushner as U.S. Ambassador to France, and progressives previously criticized him in 2017 for voting against a proposal to lower prescription drug prices.
