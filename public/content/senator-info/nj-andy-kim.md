Andy Kim was born in Boston to immigrant parents from South Korea and grew up in Moorestown, New Jersey, a suburb of Philadelphia. His father became a scientist while his mother worked as a nurse. A Rhodes Scholar, Kim earned a Ph.D. in International Relations from Oxford University and went on to serve at USAID, the State Department, the Pentagon, and the National Security Council. A progressive Democrat, he served from 2019 to 2024 as the U.S. representative from New Jersey's 3rd congressional district before running for Senate. His campaign centered on restoring political integrity, creating affordable housing, expanding access to banking in underserved communities, and fighting for affordable healthcare, high-quality education, and protections for workers and seniors. Kim won the 2024 general election, becoming the first Korean-American in the Senate and the first Asian-American U.S. senator from New Jersey. He is also notable for winning the Democratic primary after successfully petitioning to abolish "county line" primary ballots in New Jersey.
