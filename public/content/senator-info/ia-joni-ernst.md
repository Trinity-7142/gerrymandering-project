Joni Kay Ernst is a Republican politician and retired military officer serving as the junior United States Senator from Iowa since 2015. Born and raised on a farm near Red Oak in southwestern Iowa, she was valedictorian of her high school class and earned a B.A. in psychology from Iowa State University in 1992 and a Master of Public Administration from Columbus State University in 1995. During the Iraq War, she served as commanding officer of the 1168th Transportation Company in Kuwait. She became the first woman to serve in federal elected office from Iowa and the first female combat veteran elected to the U.S. Senate. Her platform included a balanced budget amendment, abolishing the Department of Education, and repealing the Affordable Care Act. She served as chair of the Senate Republican Policy Committee from 2023 to 2025, making her the fourth-ranking Republican in the Senate. She drew intra-party scrutiny for initial hesitancy over Pete Hegseth's confirmation and for comments defending potential Medicaid cuts. In September 2025, Ernst announced she would not seek reelection in 2026.
