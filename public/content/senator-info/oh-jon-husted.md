Jon Husted is an American politician serving since 2025 as the junior United States Senator from Ohio. Born in the Detroit area in 1967 and placed for adoption shortly after birth, he was raised in Montpelier, Ohio, by adoptive parents; his father worked as a machine operator. A conservative Republican, Husted served as Speaker of the Ohio House of Representatives from 2005 to 2009 and as Ohio Secretary of State from 2011 to 2019, and as Lieutenant Governor from 2019 to 2025. Governor DeWine appointed him to the U.S. Senate to fill the vacancy created by the resignation of JD Vance, who became Vice President. In the Senate, his priorities include border security, law enforcement funding, and combating fentanyl. In the landmark 2018 case *Husted v. Philip Randolph Institute*, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled in his favor, setting a national standard for maintaining the integrity of voter rolls. Notably, in March 2026, Husted testified as a defense witness in the criminal trial of former FirstEnergy executives related to Ohio's House Bill 6 bribery scandal.
