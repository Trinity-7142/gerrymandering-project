John Karl Fetterman is the senior U.S. Senator from Pennsylvania, born in Reading, PA, and raised in an upper-middle-class suburban household. He studied finance at Albright College, earned an MBA from the University of Connecticut, and later an MPP from Harvard University. A Democrat, he previously served as mayor of Braddock and as Pennsylvania's lieutenant governor. As lieutenant governor, he advocated for marijuana legalization, economic justice, LGBTQ+ equality, and criminal justice reform. He defeated Republican Mehmet Oz in the 2022 Senate general election with 51% of the vote. In the Senate, he has been a vocal advocate for protecting and expanding SNAP and pushing for universal free school lunch. Since Trump's 2024 victory, Fetterman has become a critic of his own party and has indicated receptiveness to some Trump administration policies, drawing both attention and controversy. He also notably survived a stroke during the 2022 campaign and by March 2025 had missed more roll-call votes than any other U.S. senator that year.
