Born on February 2, 1982, in Enterprise, Alabama, Britt is the proud daughter of two small business owners and holds a conservative, Republican ideology rooted in faith, family, and free enterprise. An alumna of the University of Alabama, she earned a bachelor's degree in Political Science and a Juris Doctor from its School of Law. She served as president and CEO of the Business Council of Alabama and as chief of staff for Senator Richard Shelby. Britt is the first woman elected to the U.S. Senate from Alabama and the youngest Republican woman ever elected to the Senate. Her platform centers on border security as chair of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee, and she has introduced legislation on mental health care, educational freedom, and social media regulation for minors. Notably, President Trump signed her sponsored Laken Riley Act — requiring detention of undocumented migrants who commit theft-related crimes — as the first legislation of his second term. Britt delivered the Republican Party's response to President Biden's 2024 State of the Union address.
