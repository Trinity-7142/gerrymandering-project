Born and raised in a largely Black middle-class neighborhood of Prince George's County, Maryland, Alsobrooks graduated from Duke University and the University of Maryland School of Law, beginning her legal career before becoming a domestic violence prosecutor and appointed official in county government. The *Baltimore Banner* described her as center-left. Her Senate campaign focused on creating jobs, bringing down the cost of living, improving healthcare, and strengthening education. She supported capping insulin at $35, protecting Social Security, and expanding the ACA and Medicaid. On reproductive rights, she pledged to co-sponsor the Women's Health Protection Act. Notably, she was elected Maryland's first Black U.S. senator in November 2024, defeating popular former two-term Republican Governor Larry Hogan. During the campaign, she faced scrutiny after being found to have improperly claimed D.C. property tax credits intended for lower-income and senior residents.
