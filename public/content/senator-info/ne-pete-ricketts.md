Pete Ricketts is the junior United States Senator from Nebraska, serving since 2023. Born in Nebraska City and raised in Omaha, he is the eldest son of Joe Ricketts, founder of TD Ameritrade, and a part owner of the Chicago Cubs. He holds a B.A. in biology and an MBA in marketing and finance from the University of Chicago. A conservative Republican, his Senate priorities include securing the border, unleashing American energy, and extending the Trump tax cuts. His first co-sponsored Senate bills targeted federal abortion funding and access. As governor, he worked with the legislature to deliver over $12.7 billion in tax relief. He was appointed to the Senate by his gubernatorial successor, Jim Pillen, to fill the vacancy created by Ben Sasse's resignation, then won the 2024 special election with 62.6% of the vote.
