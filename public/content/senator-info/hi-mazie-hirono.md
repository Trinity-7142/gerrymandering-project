Born in Fukushima Prefecture, Japan, in 1947, Hirono was raised by a single mother who worked two jobs to support the family after immigrating to Honolulu. She earned a bachelor's degree from the University of Hawaii in 1970 and a law degree from Georgetown University in 1978. A progressive Democrat, she has displayed particular interest in education, transportation, and issues of importance to Hawaii. As a member of the Senate Armed Services and Veterans Affairs Committees, she works on legislation supporting Hawaii's role in Asia-Pacific security and honoring commitments to servicemembers and veterans. She has also championed the closure of the Red Hill Bulk Fuel Storage Facility following fuel leaks in 2021, helping secure $1 billion for its defueling. Hirono is the first elected female senator from Hawaii, the first Asian-American woman elected to the Senate, and the first U.S. senator born in Japan. She was reelected to a third term in 2024.
