Born in Camden, Arkansas, in 1954, Tuberville earned a bachelor's degree in physical education from Southern Arkansas University in 1976. Before entering politics, he was the head football coach at Auburn University from 1999 to 2008, where he led the Tigers to eight consecutive bowl appearances and an undefeated 13–0 season in 2004. In his first political campaign, Tuberville ran for Senate in 2020, winning the Republican primary and defeating Democratic incumbent Doug Jones. A conservative Republican, he opposes abortion rights, favors repealing the Affordable Care Act, supports border wall construction, and backs reducing the national debt through cuts to social programs while opposing cuts to Social Security, Medicare, or Medicaid. In the Senate, Tuberville serves on the Armed Services, Agriculture, and Veterans' Affairs Committees. In 2023, in protest of a Defense Department policy reimbursing travel for service members seeking abortions, Tuberville blocked all promotions of senior military officers for 10 months, delaying over 450 promotions — a move that drew sharp bipartisan criticism. He announced he would forgo a Senate reelection bid and instead run for governor of Alabama in 2026.
