Raised in a poor, single-parent household in North Charleston, South Carolina, Scott attended Presbyterian College on a football scholarship before graduating from Charleston Southern University in 1988, then worked in real estate and owned his own insurance agency. A strongly conservative Republican, he has championed a constitutional amendment to balance the federal budget and advocated a ban on earmarks. He has also led on the implementation of the Opportunity Zones initiative and played a role in workforce development and education issues. Scott is the first African-American senator in the Southern U.S. to be directly elected, the longest-serving African-American senator in U.S. history, and the first African-American senator to chair a full committee. He became chairman of the Senate Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs Committee in 2025 and was elected chair of the National Republican Senatorial Committee after the 2024 elections. He drafted the Republican police reform plan amid the 2020 George Floyd protests, though critics argued it did not go far enough, and the bill failed to pass the Senate.
