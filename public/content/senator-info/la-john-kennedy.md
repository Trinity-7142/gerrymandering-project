John Neely Kennedy is a Republican attorney who has served as the junior U.S. Senator from Louisiana since 2017. Born in Centreville, Mississippi, he was raised in Zachary, Louisiana, a small town north of Baton Rouge. He graduated magna cum laude from Vanderbilt University, earned his law degree from the University of Virginia School of Law, and received a Bachelor of Civil Law with first-class honors from Oxford University. A staunch conservative, Kennedy's platform has emphasized fiscal conservatism and his positioning as a Washington outsider. He is a strong critic of government debt, wasteful spending, and has opposed legislation he views as excessive. As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, he has secured millions in federal funding for Louisiana, including FEMA grants for hurricane recovery. In 2024, he drew controversy during a congressional hate-crimes hearing, prompting condemnation from civil rights groups. He was also one of six Republican senators who voted to sustain an objection to Arizona's electoral votes in January 2021.
