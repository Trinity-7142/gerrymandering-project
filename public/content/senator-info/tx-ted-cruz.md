Born in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, to parents working in the oil fields, Cruz grew up in the Houston area after his family returned to Texas. His father, Rafael Bienvenido Cruz, was born in Cuba and fled to the United States in 1957. After earning degrees from Princeton University and Harvard Law School, Cruz pursued a career in politics, serving as a policy advisor in the George W. Bush administration. Elected to the U.S. Senate in 2012, he became the first Hispanic American to serve as a U.S. senator from Texas. Described as a movement, social, and constitutional conservative, Cruz supports free trade, a flat tax, and opposes the Affordable Care Act, same-sex marriage, and expansive immigration reform. Since 2025, Cruz has chaired the Senate Commerce Committee. Notable controversies include a 21-hour Senate floor speech opposing the ACA in 2013, which contributed to a government shutdown, and voting on January 6, 2021, to omit Arizona and/or Pennsylvania from the counting of presidential electors.
