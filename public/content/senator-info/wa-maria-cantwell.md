Maria Cantwell is a Democratic U.S. Senator from Washington, serving since 2001. Born and raised in Indianapolis, Indiana, she grew up in a predominantly Irish American, working-class neighborhood; her father held several political offices. With the help of Pell Grants, she was the first member of her family to graduate college. She later worked as vice president of marketing for RealNetworks before entering the Senate. Her tenure reflects a mix of economic pragmatism and social liberalism. She has focused on reducing U.S. dependence on fossil fuels and promoting clean energy, internet privacy legislation, and protecting Washington's aerospace industry. She received the highest rating from the League of Conservation Voters in 2004 and holds a lifetime environmental score of 93%. She won re-election in November 2024.
