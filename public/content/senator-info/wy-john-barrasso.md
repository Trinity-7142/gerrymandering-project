Born in Reading, Pennsylvania, the son of a cement finisher, Barrasso graduated from Georgetown University and completed his medical residency at Yale before relocating to Wyoming and building a private orthopedics practice in Casper. During his 24 years as an orthopedic surgeon, he served as President of the Wyoming Medical Society and was named Wyoming Physician of the Year. A conservative Republican who generally votes with party leadership, he advocates an "all-of-the-above" energy strategy balancing traditional fossil fuels with emerging technologies, supports limiting EPA regulatory powers, opposes abortion rights and same-sex marriage, and favors tax cuts and reduced government spending. In 2024, Barrasso co-introduced the Energy Permitting Reform Act to accelerate permitting for energy infrastructure and mineral development. He was reelected in November 2024 with more than 75% of the vote and, in 2025, became Senate Majority Whip, the second-ranking Senate Republican.
