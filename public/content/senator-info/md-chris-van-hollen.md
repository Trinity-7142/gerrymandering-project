Born in Karachi, Pakistan, to American parents — his father a career Foreign Service officer — Van Hollen spent his youth in Sri Lanka, Turkey, and India. He holds degrees from Swarthmore College, Harvard's Kennedy School of Public Policy, and Georgetown University Law Center. A progressive Democrat, he has championed environmental initiatives to protect the Chesapeake Bay and promoted gun-control legislation. His priorities include building a more inclusive economy, raising wages for working families, and cutting costs on prescription drugs and childcare. First elected to the Senate in 2016 and reelected in 2022, he previously served seven terms in the House. Notably, in April 2025, Van Hollen traveled to El Salvador to advocate for the release of Maryland resident Kilmar Abrego Garcia, though he was denied entry to the CECOT prison.
