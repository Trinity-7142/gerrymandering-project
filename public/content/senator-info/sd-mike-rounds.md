Marion Michael "Mike" Rounds is an American businessman and politician serving as the junior United States Senator from South Dakota since 2015. Born in Huron and raised in Pierre, he is the eldest of 11 children. Rounds built a career as a partner in an insurance and real estate firm before entering politics. A conservative Republican, his platform centers on energy independence, cutting red tape, and keeping taxes low. He serves on five committees: Appropriations, Armed Services, Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs, Indian Affairs, and the Select Committee on Intelligence. Rounds co-chairs the Senate AI Caucus and served on the Bipartisan Senate AI Working Group. As governor, he signed legislation banning nearly all abortions in South Dakota, though the law was later repealed by voter referendum. His 2014 Senate campaign was beset by allegations of misconduct tied to an immigrant investment program investigated for embezzlement and bribery.
