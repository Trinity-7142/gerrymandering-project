Kirsten Gillibrand is an American lawyer and politician serving as the junior United States Senator from New York since 2009. Born in Albany, New York in 1966 and raised in upstate New York, she earned a degree in Asian studies from Dartmouth College and a law degree from UCLA. In the House, she was known as a conservative Democrat and member of the Blue Dog Coalition; in the Senate, she is regarded as one of the more liberal members. Her platform centers on establishing a national paid leave program, fighting the climate crisis, and helping pass major clean energy legislation. Key accomplishments include leading the repeal of "Don't Ask, Don't Tell," passing the 9/11 health bill, securing benefits for veterans exposed to toxic burn pits, and enacting the first federal law making domestic gun trafficking a crime. She also authored the STOCK Act, prohibiting members of Congress from trading stocks on insider information. Declaring "zero tolerance" on sexual misconduct, she was the first in her caucus to call on Senator Al Franken to resign.
