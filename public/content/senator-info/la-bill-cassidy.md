William Morgan Cassidy is the senior U.S. Senator from Louisiana, serving since 2015. He grew up in Baton Rouge, attending LSU where he earned a biochemistry degree and then a medical degree. He co-founded the Greater Baton Rouge Community Clinic, which offered free dental and health care to the working uninsured. Cassidy is regarded as a moderate Republican. His policy priorities center on healthcare, infrastructure, and Social Security reform; he passed the HALT Fentanyl Act and the SUPPORT Act to boost treatment and recovery services. He chairs the Senate HELP Committee and serves on the Finance, Energy and Natural Resources, and Veterans Affairs committees. Notably, he was one of seven Republican senators to vote to convict Trump in his second impeachment trial, after which the Louisiana Republican Party censured him. He faces a 2026 Republican primary challenge from multiple opponents.
