Alan Armstrong is an American businessman and politician serving since 2026 as the junior United States Senator from Oklahoma. A third-generation Oklahoman described as a staunch conservative, he resides in Tulsa. A Republican, he previously served as CEO of the oil and gas company Williams from 2011 to 2025. Armstrong was appointed to serve the remainder of Sen. Markwayne Mullin's term until January 2027, after Mullin resigned to lead the Department of Homeland Security. His deep experience with energy infrastructure positions him as a key figure in debates over affordable and reliable energy. He is the former Chair of the National Petroleum Council and a founding member of Natural Allies for a Clean Energy Future. Per Oklahoma law, Armstrong is ineligible to run for reelection.
