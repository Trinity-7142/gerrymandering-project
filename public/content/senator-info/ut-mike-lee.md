Michael Shumway Lee (born June 4, 1971) is an American lawyer and politician serving as the senior United States Senator from Utah, a seat he has held since 2011. Born into a Mormon family, Lee grew up in Utah, where his father, Rex Lee, became the first dean of the BYU law school and later served as Solicitor General under President Reagan. Lee graduated from BYU with a degree in Political Science, earned his law degree in 1997, and clerked for future Supreme Court Justice Samuel Alito. He first ran for Senate in 2010, aligning himself with the Tea Party movement, campaigning on reducing the size of the federal government and amending the Constitution to establish a flat tax and congressional term limits. After taking office, Lee followed a hard-right line, opposing entitlement programs and breaking with Republican leadership on civil liberties issues. He currently chairs the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee and the Judiciary Subcommittee on Antitrust, Competition Policy, and Consumer Rights. He has also pursued bipartisan efforts on surveillance reform, co-authoring legislation with Sen. Dick Durbin to require warrants for searches of Americans' digital data. Notably, in January 2025, Lee called for U.S. withdrawal from NATO.
