Born in 1964 in New Delhi, India, where his father was an aide to the U.S. ambassador, Bennet grew up in Washington, D.C. He is the son of Douglas J. Bennet, a former State Department official and president of Wesleyan University. He received a B.A. in history from Wesleyan University and a J.D. from Yale Law School, where he was editor in chief of the *Yale Law Journal*. A center-left Democrat, the Lugar Center ranked Bennet in the top third of senators for bipartisanship in 2023. He garnered notice as superintendent of Denver Public Schools for helping turn around the failing system before Governor Bill Ritter appointed him to fill the U.S. Senate seat vacated by Ken Salazar in January 2009. In the Senate, Bennet has championed bipartisan immigration reform as a member of the "Gang of 8," Colorado's public lands, and worked over a decade to protect 400,000 acres through the CORE Act. He was instrumental in passing the Inflation Reduction Act and played a critical role in writing its clean energy provisions. In April 2025, Bennet announced his candidacy in the 2026 Colorado gubernatorial election.
