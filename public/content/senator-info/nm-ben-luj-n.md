Born and raised on a small family farm in Nambé, New Mexico, by a union ironworker and a public school employee, Luján is a self-described progressive Democrat. He previously served as U.S. Representative for New Mexico's Third Congressional District and House Assistant Speaker in the 116th Congress before winning his Senate seat in 2020. When New Mexicans elected him to the Senate, Luján became the first Head Start graduate ever elected to the chamber and the first Hispanic to represent New Mexico in the U.S. Senate in more than 40 years. His platform centers on expanding access to quality health care, preserving natural resources, building a clean energy economy, and creating good-paying jobs. He has led the effort to strengthen the Radiation Exposure Compensation Act (RECA) program and championed legislation to support victims of the Hermit's Peak/Calf Canyon Fire. Notably, in January 2022, Luján was hospitalized after suffering a stroke affecting his cerebellum, requiring a decompressive craniectomy, but returned to the Senate within weeks. He is running for re-election, appearing on the Democratic primary ballot on June 2, 2026.
