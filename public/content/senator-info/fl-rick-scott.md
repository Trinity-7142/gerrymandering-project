Rick Scott is a Republican attorney, businessman, and Navy veteran serving as the senior U.S. Senator from Florida. He grew up in public housing in the Midwest, raised by an adoptive father who was a WWII veteran and truck driver and a mother who worked as a store clerk. In 1987, he co-founded Columbia Hospital Corporation, which merged to form Columbia/HCA, the nation's largest for-profit health care company. He was pressured to resign as CEO in 1997 amid findings that the company defrauded Medicare, Medicaid, and other federal programs. As governor, he championed over $10 billion in tax cuts and regulations cuts linked to 1.7 million new jobs, dropping unemployment from 11% to 3.3%. In the Senate, his priorities include reducing government spending, securing the border, countering China, and protecting Social Security and Medicare. His controversial 2022 "Plan to Rescue America" proposed sunsetting all federal legislation, including entitlement programs — a plan Senate Republican leader Mitch McConnell publicly disavowed. He was reelected in 2024, defeating Democrat Debbie Mucarsel-Powell by over 12 points.
