Lisa Murkowski is an American attorney and politician serving as the senior United States Senator from Alaska, having held the seat since 2002. Born in Ketchikan and raised across the state, she grew up in a politically prominent family — her father worked as a banker before entering politics. Murkowski is often described as one of the Senate's most moderate Republicans and a swing vote, focusing on energy development, Alaska Native affairs, and infrastructure. A leader on energy and public lands issues, she supports the safe and efficient production of all forms of domestic energy. She chaired the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee from 2015 to 2021 and has chaired the Indian Affairs Committee since January 2025. Notable controversies include her initial appointment to the Senate by her own father, which many Alaska voters viewed as an act of nepotism, and her 2021 vote to convict Donald Trump during his second impeachment trial, for which the Alaska Republican Party censured her. After losing the 2010 Republican primary, she ran as a write-in candidate and won the general election — a historic achievement.
