Ronald Harold Johnson (born April 8, 1955) is the senior United States Senator from Wisconsin, born and raised in Mankato, Minnesota. Before entering politics, he was chief executive officer of a plastics manufacturer in Oshkosh, Wisconsin, where he resides with his wife Jane. A conservative Republican, Johnson entered politics to oppose the Affordable Care Act, crediting the Tea Party movement with his political ascent. As a senator, he gained a reputation for opposing government spending and seeking a balanced budget, stalling or voting against budget proposals from both parties for not offsetting spending. He chairs the Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee and also serves on the Budget, Foreign Relations, and Commerce, Science and Transportation committees. He was reelected in 2016 and again in 2022, narrowly defeating Lieutenant Governor Mandela Barnes. Johnson has drawn controversy for leading investigations into Hunter Biden and alleged 2020 election irregularities, and for espousing skeptical views on COVID-19 vaccines and climate science.
