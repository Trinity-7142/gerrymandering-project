Timothy Michael Kaine is the junior Democratic U.S. Senator from Virginia, serving since 2013. He grew up in Kansas City, Missouri, where his father had an ironworking shop, earned a B.A. in economics from the University of Missouri, and a J.D. from Harvard Law School, taking a year off to lead a Jesuit technical school in Honduras. A center-left Democrat, he began his career as a civil rights lawyer in Richmond. He serves on the Armed Services, Budget, Foreign Relations, and HELP Committees. His priorities include healthcare as a right, protecting the ACA, Medicare, and Medicaid, clean energy, and gun violence reduction. He was Hillary Clinton's 2016 vice-presidential running mate. Notably, he broke with most Senate Democrats to advance a bipartisan deal ending the 2025 federal government shutdown, drawing criticism from progressives. He won reelection in 2018 and again in 2024.
