Brian Schatz has served as the senior U.S. Senator from Hawaii since 2012. He moved to Hawaii at age two and grew up with his three brothers, raised by his father Irwin — a longtime University of Hawaii medical school professor — and his mother Barbara. A progressive Democrat, Schatz previously served in the Hawaii House of Representatives and as Lieutenant Governor. He also served as CEO of Helping Hands Hawaii, an Oahu nonprofit social service agency. In the Senate, he has led legislation on climate action, health care, criminal justice reform, and technology. As chair of the Senate Committee on Indian Affairs, he secured hundreds of millions of dollars for the Native Hawaiian community. In 2025, he co-introduced the bipartisan Kids Off Social Media Act, which would set a minimum age of 13 for social media use and restrict algorithmically targeted content for users under 17. In April 2023, he was reported as the most popular senator in the country, with a 65% approval rating.
