Tammy Duckworth is the junior United States Senator from Illinois, serving since 2017. Born in Bangkok, Thailand, to an American father and a Thai mother of Chinese descent, her childhood spanned Thailand, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, and Hawaii due to her father's UN refugee work. The family briefly lived on public assistance before Duckworth earned degrees from the University of Hawaii and George Washington University. In 2004, her Black Hawk helicopter was hit by a rocket-propelled grenade in Iraq, causing her to lose both legs and some mobility in her right arm. She is the first person born in Thailand elected to Congress, the first woman with a disability elected to Congress, and the first senator to give birth while in office. A progressive Democrat, she advocates for rebuilding infrastructure, protecting Illinoisans from lead poisoning, growing manufacturing jobs, supporting minority-owned small businesses, and making college more affordable. Her landmark Drinking Water and Wastewater Infrastructure Act became law as part of the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, providing over $35 billion for water resource and lead removal projects nationwide. The Center for Effective Lawmaking ranked her among the top five most effective Democratic Senators overall.
