Rand Paul, born January 7, 1963, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, is a Republican U.S. Senator representing Kentucky. A graduate of Duke University School of Medicine, he practiced as an ophthalmologist in Bowling Green, Kentucky, from 1993 until his Senate election in 2010. Paul identifies as a "libertarian conservative," favoring limited government, low taxes, reduced federal spending, and a balanced budget. He has also sought the abolishment of all foreign aid and consistently champions a non-interventionist foreign policy. He has never voted for an unbalanced budget and has returned $8.3 million to the Treasury from his Senate office budget. He currently chairs the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs. In 2015, Paul staged an over ten-hour Senate speech opposing NSA surveillance programs authorized under the Patriot Act. He was re-elected in 2016 and won a third term in 2022.
