Josh Hawley is the senior United States Senator from Missouri, serving since 2019. Born in Springdale, Arkansas, to a banker and a teacher, Hawley graduated from Stanford University in 2002 and Yale Law School in 2006. A native of small-town Lexington, Missouri, his political beliefs have been described as populist and socially conservative. He positions himself as a deeply religious Christian and an advocate for working-class and rural Midwesterners. His platform focuses on criticism of Big Tech and social media companies, often breaking with fellow Republicans in support of their regulation, support for tariffs — especially toward China — and stricter border enforcement. Hawley previously served as the 42nd Attorney General of Missouri, before defeating two-term Democratic incumbent Claire McCaskill in 2018, and was reelected in 2024. In December 2020, he became the first senator to announce plans to object to the certification of Joe Biden's 2020 presidential election victory, leading efforts in the Senate to do so — a defining and widely noted controversy of his tenure.
