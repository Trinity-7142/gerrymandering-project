James Lankford is an American Southern Baptist minister and politician serving as the senior U.S. Senator from Oklahoma. Born March 4, 1968, in Dallas, Texas, he earned a master's degree in divinity and moved his family to Oklahoma, where he worked for the Baptist General Convention. He served over 20 years in ministry, including 15 years as Director of Student Ministry and director of Falls Creek Youth Camp, the largest youth camp in the United States. A staunch conservative Republican, he became known for his opposition to gun-control legislation, taxes, environmental regulation, and federally funded health insurance. His platform centers on fiscal discipline, accountability, life, and traditional family values. He has been recognized as the Senate's top-ranked "Taxpayers Friend" by the National Taxpayers Union. Lankford was the lead Senate Republican negotiator on a bipartisan border security bill, which collapsed after Donald Trump opposed it, with Senate Republicans blocking the bill in a floor vote. The Oklahoma Republican Party formally censured Lankford for his role in those negotiations.
