A third-generation Michigander raised on a family farm in Holly, Michigan, Slotkin's political identity is defined by a pragmatic, mission-driven approach often described as "Midwestern moderate." A former CIA analyst who served three tours in Iraq alongside the U.S. military, she went on to hold national security roles at the Pentagon and White House under Presidents Bush and Obama. She won the 2024 Democratic primary with 76% of the vote and defeated Republican nominee Mike Rogers in the general election, outperforming the top of the ticket. Her Senate platform centers on lowering prescription drug costs, reshoring critical supply chains, creating jobs, and getting money out of politics. After Trump's 2025 State of the Union address, she delivered the official Democratic response. She is the first Member of Congress to have two school shootings occur in her district.
