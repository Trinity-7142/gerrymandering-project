Born on February 16, 1987, and raised in the Atlanta metro area, Jon Ossoff is a Democrat who has served as Georgia's senior U.S. Senator since 2021 and is the youngest incumbent U.S. senator. He is the state's first Jewish senator. Mentored by civil rights legend Congressman John Lewis, he previously served as managing director of an investigative production company that worked with reporters to document corruption in foreign countries. In his 2020 Senate campaign, Ossoff pledged to expand access to affordable health care and called for raising the federal minimum wage to $15 an hour. In office, he passed more standalone bills into law than any other freshman senator in his first two years. Notable achievements include helping cap the cost of insulin for seniors at $35/month and passing bipartisan legislation to protect children from online exploitation. He has also introduced legislation to ban corporate PACs and is leading the charge to ban stock trading by members of Congress.
