Amy Klobuchar is the senior United States Senator from Minnesota, serving since 2007. Born in Plymouth, Minnesota, she graduated from Yale University and the University of Chicago Law School. Her grandfather worked in the iron ore mines of Northern Minnesota, and her father was a newspaperman while her mother was an elementary school teacher — a working-class Midwestern background she often highlights. A mainstream liberal Democrat, she is pro-choice on abortion, supports LGBT rights and the Affordable Care Act. Her legislative priorities include antitrust reform, passing bipartisan Farm Bills, and expanding broadband access, with her provisions included in the bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act. According to GovTrack, Klobuchar passed more legislation than any other senator by the end of the 114th Congress. She was the first woman elected to the U.S. Senate from Minnesota. In January 2026, she filed to run for governor of Minnesota.
