A native South Carolinian, Graham grew up in a blue-collar family in the small town of Central, where his parents ran a restaurant and pool hall. The first member of his family to attend college, he earned both his undergraduate and law degrees from the University of South Carolina. A neoconservative, Graham is a war hawk and a strong advocate for interventionist foreign policy. In the Senate, he has been known for conservative positions, including opposition to abortion and support for the death penalty, while sometimes breaking with party lines to pursue bipartisan legislation. He chaired the Senate Judiciary Committee during the 116th Congress and now chairs the Senate Budget Committee in the 119th Congress. His career gained national attention during his 2016 presidential campaign and his evolution from a vocal Trump critic to a staunch ally. In 2014, he received a Bronze Star Medal for meritorious service as a senior legal adviser to the Air Force in Iraq and Afghanistan.
