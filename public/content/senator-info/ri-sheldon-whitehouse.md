Sheldon Whitehouse was born on October 20, 1955, in New York. He grew up in a Foreign Service family, living across Vietnam, Cambodia, South Africa, Congo, Guinea, Laos, and Thailand. A graduate of Yale University and the University of Virginia School of Law, he served as U.S. Attorney and Attorney General of Rhode Island before being elected to the Senate in 2006. A political progressive and climate hawk, Whitehouse plays a key role in crafting policies addressing climate change, environmental protection, and a price on carbon. Since 2012, he has spoken on the Senate floor about climate change every week the Senate has been in session. He authored the bipartisan Comprehensive Addiction and Recovery Act, the first significant federal law to address the opioid crisis. He has also championed efforts to root out dark money from elections and make Congress and the courts more accountable. Notably, an ethics watchdog group filed a 2025 complaint accusing Whitehouse of a conflict of interest for supporting legislation that benefited his wife's nonprofit. He was reelected to a fourth term in November 2024, defeating Republican Patricia Morgan by 20 points.
