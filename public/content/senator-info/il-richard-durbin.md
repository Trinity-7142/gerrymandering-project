Born in East St. Louis, Illinois, to an Irish-American father and a Lithuanian-born mother, Durbin worked at a meatpacking plant during his high school years. He graduated from Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service and its Law Center. A liberal Democrat, his career has focused on gun control and safety, consumer protection, federal funding for medical research, limits on cigarette smoking, and expanding health insurance coverage. He first introduced the Dream Act over 20 years ago, legislation to give undocumented immigrants who grew up in the U.S. a path to citizenship. He is also a lead author of the bipartisan First Step Act, enacted in 2018 and considered the most significant criminal justice reform legislation in decades. As of 2024, Durbin is the longest-serving Senate party whip in U.S. history. He announced in April 2025 that he would not seek reelection for a sixth term, citing his age.
