Born in Las Vegas, Nevada, to a politically connected family — her father was a longtime head of the Las Vegas Convention and Visitors Authority and Clark County Commissioner — Cortez Masto grew up in a union household and learned the value of organized labor from a young age. She is a Democratic lawyer and politician serving as Nevada's senior U.S. Senator since 2017, having previously served as the state's Attorney General. She made history as the first woman elected to represent Nevada in the Senate and the first Latina elected to serve in the Senate. She campaigned on raising the minimum wage, protecting Medicare and Social Security, and passing comprehensive immigration reform. As Attorney General, she gained national attention for her role in a mortgage-fraud settlement that brought Nevada more than $1.9 billion. More recently, she announced she would helm ModSquad, a PAC supporting moderate Senate Democrats.
