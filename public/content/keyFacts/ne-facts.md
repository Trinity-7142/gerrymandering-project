Nebraska comprises three congressional districts. Congressional and state legislative lines are both drawn by the state legislature as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. A special redistricting committee tasked with drafting plans comprises three legislators from each congressional district, with no more than five affiliated with the same political party. On September 30, 2021, the Nebraska State Legislature approved a new congressional map 35–11, which Gov. Pete Ricketts signed into law shortly afterward; the map took effect for Nebraska's 2022 congressional elections. The Redistricting Committee adopted guidelines to preserve the cores of prior districts, prohibit the use of partisan data, and forbid the intentional favoring or disfavoring of any party, group, or person. A notable controversy centered on the 2nd Congressional District (Omaha): the committee ultimately moved forward with a congressional map that splits Douglas County, home of Nebraska's "blue dot" that awarded one Electoral College vote to Democrats in 2008 and 2020.
