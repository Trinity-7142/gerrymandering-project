In Georgia, both congressional and state legislative district lines are drawn by the state legislature, with a simple majority in each chamber required to approve redistricting plans, which are subject to veto by the governor. Georgia maintained 14 congressional seats following the 2020 census. The post-2020 congressional map was pushed through a 2021 special session by Republican lawmakers, and Governor Brian Kemp signed it on December 30, 2021. On October 26, 2023, a federal court ruled that the state's congressional and legislative district boundaries violated the Voting Rights Act, directing the General Assembly to develop new maps by December 8, 2023. GOP lawmakers had redrawn the congressional map from an 8–6 Republican majority to a 9–5 Republican majority in 2021. The legislature passed remedial maps, which the judge upheld on December 28, 2023; plaintiffs appealed, and the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals heard arguments in January 2025.
