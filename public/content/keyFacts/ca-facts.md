California is the most populous U.S. state and holds 52 congressional seats. Congressional district boundaries are drawn by the California Citizens Redistricting Commission (CCRC), an independent body — though the districts drawn in 2021 were overridden in 2025 by California voters with the passage of Proposition 50, replacing them with maps drawn by the state legislature. The CCRC voted 14–0 in favor of the post-2020 census congressional map on December 20, 2021, delivering it to the secretary of state on December 27, 2021. The commission is composed of 14 regular Californians with partisan balance — five Democrats, five Republicans, and four decline-to-state members. During the 2021 process, the commission faced scrutiny and was taken to court over alleged secret meetings. In November 2025, citing Texas's proposed mid-decade redistricting, California voters ratified a constitutional amendment temporarily allowing the legislature to redraw congressional maps.
