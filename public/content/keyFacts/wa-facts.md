Washington's congressional districts are drawn and adopted by the Washington State Redistricting Commission, a five-member, politically appointed body. The Commission releases proposed plans — one per party-appointed commissioner — and the final plan must be approved by three of the four voting commissioners, with a fifth nonpartisan member serving as facilitator with no voting power. Washington's congressional delegation held at 10 seats following the 2020 reapportionment. Current maps stem from the 2021 cycle: the Commission failed to transmit a plan by the midnight November 15 deadline but agreed to a set of maps on November 16, 2021; the Washington Supreme Court accepted the drafts, ruling the Commission had "substantially complied" with the deadline, and the map took effect for the 2022 congressional elections. Notably, the Commission was forced to acknowledge that its November meeting violated the state's Open Public Meetings Act, settling a lawsuit and agreeing to pay about $130,000 in legal fees, with individual commissioners fined $500 each.
