Wyoming has a single at-large congressional district, making congressional redistricting unnecessary. The district was first created when Wyoming achieved statehood on July 10, 1890, and has existed uninterrupted ever since. As of the 2020 Census, Wyoming's one representative serves 577,719 residents. For state legislative maps, legislative plans are enacted by the Wyoming State Legislature, subject to the Governor's veto. During the 2022 budget session, lawmakers enacted House Bill 100, adding one Senate seat and two House seats. Gov. Mark Gordon (R) allowed the maps to become law without signing the redistricting bill. Notably, in 2011, concerns were raised that legislators drew lines incorporating rural land into urban districts to protect incumbents, and critics also objected to prison gerrymandering — counting prisoners as residents of the districts where their prisons are located rather than their home addresses.
