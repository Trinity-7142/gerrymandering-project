South Dakota has a single at-large congressional district, meaning congressional redistricting is not required. The second seat was eliminated after the 1980 census redistricting cycle, leaving the single at-large district the state has had since. State legislative lines are drawn by the state legislature as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. The legislature held a special session in November 2021 to enact new state House and Senate districts based on the 2020 census data. Both chambers approved the final compromise, known as the Sparrow map, with the House voting 37–31 and the Senate 30–2; Gov. Kristi Noem signed it into law that same night. The two competing proposals mainly differed in their approach to Native American reservations and the rural areas around Rapid City. Notably, South Dakota has a history of conflicts over Native voting rights; in 2001, Native American groups successfully sued the state over districts that weakened tribal voters' voices. During the 2021 process, the redistricting committees voted 10–3 to restrict use of the legislature's redistricting software to committee members and staff, citing concerns about managing too many public submissions.
