North Carolina is currently divided into 14 congressional districts. Districts are drawn by the state legislature through ordinary statute and, unlike most states, the resulting plans are not subject to the governor's veto. The legislature redrew the state's congressional districts in 2023, allowing Republicans to pick up three seats in the 2024 elections. Most recently, in October 2025, the General Assembly approved a new map for North Carolina's 14 congressional districts for use in the 2026 elections. This is abnormal, as redistricting typically happens once every ten years, and it marks the first time in the modern era that North Carolina has redrawn maps mid-decade without being court-ordered to do so. North Carolina is considered one of the most extremely gerrymandered states in the nation and has been home to a decade's worth of redistricting litigation.
