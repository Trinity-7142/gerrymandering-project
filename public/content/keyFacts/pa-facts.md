Pennsylvania comprises 17 congressional districts. Congressional district boundaries are redrawn after every federal decennial census by legislative action — a bill that proceeds through both chambers of the General Assembly and is signed by the Governor. Following the 2020 census, the legislature passed a congressional plan in January 2022, but it was vetoed by Governor Wolf; when no new plan emerged, the state Supreme Court adopted a congressional map on February 23, 2022, effective for that year's elections. The court ultimately selected the "Carter map" in a 4–3 ruling, which had been submitted by a group of Pennsylvania citizen-petitioners. This intervention was not unprecedented: in the 2010 redistricting cycle, the state Supreme Court similarly found the legislature's plan to be an impermissible partisan gerrymander and, when the legislature failed to pass a replacement, drew its own congressional map in February 2018. Notably, Pennsylvania law does not specify how congressional districts should be redrawn or what criteria must be used to ensure fairness — a recurring source of controversy that has repeatedly drawn the courts into the map-drawing process.
