Minnesota has eight congressional districts. The Minnesota Legislature holds primary authority to draw and enact congressional and legislative plans, subject to the Governor's veto. However, since the 1980 census, courts have drawn the congressional districts in the absence of enacted redistricting plans, because the legislature could not agree on new maps. Most recently, in the 2020 cycle, the legislature again failed to adopt redistricting plans by the statutory deadline, and the state Supreme Court appointed a special redistricting panel, which adopted new congressional and state legislative plans on February 15, 2022. The legislature modestly adjusted the plans through legislation passed on May 22, 2022, which the governor signed on June 2, 2022. Notably, there have been proposals over the past several years to move Minnesota toward using an independent redistricting commission, but none have become law or been put to voters via a constitutional amendment.
