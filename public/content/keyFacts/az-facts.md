Arizona is divided into nine congressional districts, whose lines are drawn by the Independent Redistricting Commission (IRC), created in 2000 when Arizona voters passed Proposition 106, a citizen initiative that removed redistricting authority from the state legislature. The commission is composed of five members, four of whom are selected by the majority and minority leaders of each legislative chamber from a pool of 25 nominees comprising 10 Democrats, 10 Republicans, and 5 unaffiliated citizens. On January 18, 2022, the commission adopted the current congressional map by a vote of 3-2. The nonpartisan chairwoman joined the two Republican members in favor, while the two Democratic members were opposed. Notably, the U.S. Supreme Court upheld the commission's constitutionality in *Arizona State Legislature v. Arizona Independent Redistricting Commission* (2015), rejecting the Legislature's claim that a voter-created commission unconstitutionally stripped it of redistricting power. The 2021 cycle was also Arizona's first redistricting without Department of Justice preclearance protections, after a key provision of the Voting Rights Act was struck down in *Shelby County v. Holder* (2013).
