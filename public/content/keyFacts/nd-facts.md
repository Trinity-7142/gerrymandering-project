North Dakota has only one at-large congressional district, meaning the 2020 Census apportionment confirmed North Dakota would keep one seat, eliminating the need for any line-drawing or enactment process and producing no litigation over congressional districts. For state legislative districts, the North Dakota Legislative Assembly draws and enacts legislative plans, subject to the Governor's veto. The North Dakota Legislature held a special legislative session in November 2021 and approved the redistricting plan, which Gov. Doug Burgum (R) signed on November 11, 2021. A notable controversy followed: the Turtle Mountain Band of Chippewa Indians filed a federal lawsuit to block North Dakota's state legislative redistricting plans on vote dilution grounds. On May 14, 2025, a federal appeals court overturned a lower court ruling that had struck down the state's 2021 maps; the U.S. District Court had previously ordered the state to adopt a remedial map proposed by the Turtle Mountain Band and the Spirit Lake Tribe.
