Florida is divided into 28 congressional districts, each represented by a member of the U.S. House of Representatives. Both congressional and state legislative district lines are drawn by the state legislature; congressional lines are adopted as regular legislation and are subject to gubernatorial veto. The current map was enacted after Gov. Ron DeSantis vetoed earlier boundaries drawn by the GOP-controlled legislature, and DeSantis signed the new congressional map into law on April 22, 2022. In 2010, over 63% of Florida voters approved the "Fair Districts Amendments" to the state constitution, intended to prohibit lawmakers from intentionally drawing districts that favor incumbents or political parties. Notably, in January 2026, Gov. DeSantis called a special legislative session on congressional redistricting for April 2026, a mid-decade redraw that Florida Democrats argue violates the state constitution.
