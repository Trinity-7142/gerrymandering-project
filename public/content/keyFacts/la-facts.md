In Louisiana, congressional districts are drawn by the state legislature as ordinary legislation, subject to gubernatorial veto. Following the 2020 census, the legislature enacted a congressional plan (HB 1) on February 18, 2022; the governor vetoed it, but the legislature overrode that veto on March 30, 2022, and the plan was subsequently struck by a federal court as a violation of the Voting Rights Act. Although roughly a third of the state's population is Black, the 2022 map contained only one majority-Black district. On January 19, 2024, the legislature passed a remedial plan (SB 8), which the governor signed on January 22, 2024, and that plan remains subject to litigation. The Supreme Court case *Louisiana v. Callais* centers on whether lawmakers properly balanced constitutional and Voting Rights Act protections when enacting SB 8's two majority-Black districts. After oral arguments in March 2025, the Court opted to schedule the case for reargument rather than issue a decision.
