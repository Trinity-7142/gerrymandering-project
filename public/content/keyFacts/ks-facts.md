Kansas's legislature is responsible for drawing the boundaries of the state's four congressional districts, with plans subject to the governor's veto. The legislature can override a veto with a two-thirds vote in each chamber, and Republicans currently hold veto-proof majorities in both chambers. In the 2020 cycle, the legislature passed a congressional redistricting plan (SB 355) on January 26, 2022; the governor vetoed the bill on February 4, 2022, but the legislature overrode her veto on February 9, 2022. The map proved highly contentious: the House and Senate passed the plan in just eight days, and it split the Kansas City metro area into two separate congressional districts for the first time in decades. A district court judge found the new districts to be racially and politically gerrymandered in ways that violated the state constitution, but the Kansas Supreme Court ruled the map legal — a landmark decision, as the court had never previously considered the legality of gerrymandering.
