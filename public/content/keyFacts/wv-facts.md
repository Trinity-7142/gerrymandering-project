West Virginia's congressional and state legislative lines are both drawn by the state legislature, as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. West Virginia's state constitution requires that districts be compact, contiguous, and preserve county lines. West Virginia's post-2020 congressional redistricting followed the state's loss of a U.S. House seat in the 2020 apportionment, reducing its delegation from three to two districts. The Senate passed the congressional map in a 30–2 vote, the House approved it 84–12, and Gov. Jim Justice (R) signed it into law on October 22, 2021, taking effect for the 2022 elections. A notable consequence of the new map was that the legislature drew two safe Republican seats, placing Reps. Alex Mooney and David McKinley in the same district, forcing the country's first member-vs.-member primary of that election cycle. Critics also raised concerns that the approved maps were drawn behind closed doors and ignored residents who asked that their communities be kept whole.
