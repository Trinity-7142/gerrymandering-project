Redistricting is the responsibility of the Utah Legislature, which redraws congressional, legislative, and state school board districts based on Utah's resident population. Following the 2020 census, Utah retained its four congressional seats. The legislature passed its congressional plan (HB 2004) on November 10, 2021, and Gov. Spencer Cox signed it on November 12, 2021. The new map was drawn by the state Legislature and largely ignored plans proposed by the state's advisory Independent Redistricting Commission. A major controversy surrounds voters' 2018 passage of Proposition 4, which created an independent redistricting commission and banned partisan gerrymandering; the legislature rendered it fully advisory via SB 200 in 2020. The 2021 map carved up Salt Lake County among all four congressional districts, ensuring no competitive districts in Utah's congressional delegation. A judge ruled the legislature's subsequent remedial map also violated Proposition 4, adopting a court-drawn map as the judicial remedy; that map is in place as of early 2026.
