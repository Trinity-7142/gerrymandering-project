Arkansas has four U.S. congressional districts. Congressional lines are drawn by the state legislature as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. Notably, Arkansas has no formal redistricting criteria for congressional maps. Following the 2020 census, lawmakers approved two identical bills — HB 1982 and SB 743 — with the House voting 59–30 and the Senate 21–12 in favor. Governor Asa Hutchinson declined to sign the plans, allowing them to go into effect without his signature, on January 14, 2022. A notable reform effort failed when advocates submitted signatures for an independent citizens' redistricting commission initiative, but the Secretary of State invalidated the signatures, and the state Supreme Court removed the measure from the ballot. Subsequently, the Christian Ministerial Alliance and a group of voters filed a lawsuit in May 2023 alleging that Arkansas's 2nd Congressional District constituted a racial gerrymander in violation of the 14th and 15th Amendments.
