Missouri's eight congressional districts are drawn by the state legislature and are subject to veto by the governor. The General Assembly can override a veto with a two-thirds vote in each chamber, and Republicans currently hold veto-proof majorities in both chambers. Following the 2020 census, the legislature passed a congressional plan (HB 2909) on May 11, 2022, which the governor signed on May 18, 2022. Notably, in August 2025, Gov. Mike Kehoe (R) announced a special session for congressional redistricting and released a proposed map that would net Republicans one additional U.S. House seat. The map aimed to net one additional Republican U.S. House district by drawing parts of Kansas City into surrounding rural districts. A group of Missouri voters filed a legal challenge arguing the mid-decade map violated the Missouri Constitution; the challengers resided in Kansas City's Congressional District 5, which would be split into three separate districts under the new map. In a 4–3 ruling, the Missouri Supreme Court rejected arguments that the constitution allows redistricting only once per decade, with the majority holding that while the constitution requires action after each census, it does not expressly bar a later map.
