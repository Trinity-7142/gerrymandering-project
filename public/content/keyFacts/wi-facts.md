Wisconsin's congressional and state legislative lines are both drawn by the state legislature, as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. Following the 2020 census, Wisconsin retained eight congressional seats. The congressional maps were last set in April 2022, when the Wisconsin Supreme Court selected a map in the *Johnson v. Wisconsin Elections Commission* lawsuit, with only minor changes from the map passed by the legislature 10 years earlier. The maps were court-drawn because due to political gridlock, it has become common for Wisconsin redistricting to be conducted by courts. Notably, after a liberal justice won the 2025 state Supreme Court election, liberal groups promptly filed redistricting lawsuits challenging the congressional map, with two petitions and one complaint filed making different arguments for why courts should toss the current map. Wisconsin's split Senate delegation reflects its swing-state status — it is one of only three states represented by both a Republican and a Democrat in the Senate — yet Democrats hold only 2 of 8 House seats.
