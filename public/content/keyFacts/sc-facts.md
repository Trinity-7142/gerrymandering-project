In South Carolina, congressional and state legislative district boundaries are drawn by the state legislature and are subject to veto by the governor. South Carolina comprises seven congressional districts. Following the 2020 census, the General Assembly passed S. 865, and Governor Henry McMaster signed it on January 26, 2022, establishing seven congressional districts for use beginning with the 2022 election. The maps were approved along strict party lines — the Senate in a 26–15 vote and the House in a 72–33 vote — with Republicans supporting and Democrats opposing the proposals. Civil-rights groups immediately challenged the plan in *Alexander v. South Carolina State Conference of the NAACP*, alleging that District 1 was an unconstitutional racial gerrymander; a three-judge federal panel agreed in January 2023, ordering lawmakers to redraw the district. On May 23, 2024, the U.S. Supreme Court reversed that decision, and the original map was used for South Carolina's 2024 congressional elections.
