Oklahoma has five congressional districts. Its congressional lines are drawn by the state legislature as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. The legislature passed the current congressional plan (HB 1002) on November 19, 2021, which the governor signed on November 22, 2021, following a special session called by Governor Stitt beginning November 15, 2021. To gather public input, Oklahoma held 30 public town halls — the most in state history. Despite this outreach, the maps drew partisan criticism; one Democratic lawmaker cautioned that it would be "naive to assume that there weren't political influences outside of this building dictating what happens inside of this building." Notably, several ballot initiatives in the 2020 cycle aimed at shifting redistricting authority away from the legislature all failed or were withdrawn. Oklahoma's substantial Native American population also presents special challenges in ensuring adequate representation.
