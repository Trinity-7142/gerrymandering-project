Colorado is currently divided into eight congressional districts, having added its eighth seat following the 2020 Census due to population growth. The current maps were drawn by two independent 12-member commissions established by ballot initiative in 2018: the Colorado Independent Congressional Redistricting Commission and the Colorado Independent Legislative Redistricting Commission. The Congressional Commission selected its final map on September 28, 2021, with an 11-1 vote, and the Colorado Supreme Court approved the plan on November 1, 2021. Adopting a final plan requires at least eight of the 12 commissioners to vote in favor, including at least two unaffiliated members. The commission replaced an older lawmaker-run process that had repeatedly ended in legal challenges and court-drawn maps. Notably, the Colorado constitution does not explicitly bar mid-cycle redistricting, and as of late 2025 a debate has emerged over whether emergency redistricting should be permitted — a proposed 2026 ballot amendment would allow the governor to suspend the independent commission and appoint a temporary body to redraw districts in response to national redistricting imbalances.
