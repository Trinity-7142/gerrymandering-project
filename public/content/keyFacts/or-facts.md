In Oregon, the state legislature is primarily responsible for drawing both congressional and state legislative district lines. Congressional lines are drawn as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. State law prohibits maps from being drawn to favor or disfavor any political party, incumbent, or candidate, and bars diluting the voting strength of any language or ethnic minority group. Oregon's congressional delegation grew from 5 to 6 seats following the 2020 reapportionment. Gov. Kate Brown (D) signed the new congressional map into law on September 27, 2021, approved by the House 33–16 and the Senate 18–6. Before the vote, nearly all House Republicans boycotted the special session to protest the process, returning only in sufficient numbers to allow a quorum. Republicans alleged partisan gerrymandering, arguing that "drawing congressional lines to ensure five out of six seats for your party long-term is gerrymandering," though challenges to the congressional redistricting plan were ultimately rejected. In the 2020 cycle, several ballot initiatives proposed creating an independent redistricting commission, but they failed to collect enough signatures to make the ballot.
