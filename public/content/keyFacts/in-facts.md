Indiana's congressional districts are drawn by the state legislature as a regular statute, subject to the governor's veto. If the General Assembly fails to enact a plan by its deadline, a five-member backup redistricting commission is formed to adopt a plan. In the 2020 cycle, the legislature passed congressional and state legislative plans in one bill (HB 1581) on October 4, 2021, which the governor signed the same day, with the new maps made effective November 8, 2022. The state Senate approved the maps 36–12 and the House 64–25, with all affirmative votes cast by Republicans and bipartisan opposition. Efforts by Democratic lawmakers to establish an independent redistricting commission have been repeatedly blocked in the legislature. Notably, in December 2025, the Republican-dominated Indiana Senate voted 31–19 to reject a mid-decade redrawing of the state's congressional maps, with 21 Republicans joining all 10 Democratic senators in blocking the plan—making Indiana the first Republican-led state legislature to vote down President Trump's push to create more GOP-friendly congressional seats ahead of the 2026 midterms.
