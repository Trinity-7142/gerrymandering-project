Maryland's congressional district lines are drawn solely by the state legislature and may be vetoed by the governor. The state has 8 congressional districts. The General Assembly enacted the current congressional districts under SB 1012 (2022), based on population changes reported in the 2020 U.S. Census. That process was contentious: the House of Delegates initially passed a redistricting plan 97–42 and the Senate approved it 32–15, both on party-line votes in December 2021, but a circuit court struck the plan down as an unconstitutional partisan gerrymander. Gov. Larry Hogan signed a revised map into law on April 4, 2022. Most recently, Gov. Wes Moore's effort to redistrict Maryland mid-decade failed when the state Senate refused to advance it, with Senate President Bill Ferguson staunchly opposed even after the House passed a new map. Ferguson argued that further redistricting could expose Democrats to legal turmoil and result in a court-ordered map even less favorable to the party.
