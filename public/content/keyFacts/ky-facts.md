Kentucky sends six representatives to the U.S. House, a number held steady since the 1990s and confirmed again by the 2020 Census. Congressional and state legislative lines are drawn by the state legislature as a regular statute, subject to gubernatorial veto. The current maps were adopted on January 20, 2022, after the Republican-controlled General Assembly overrode Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear's veto. Notably, Kentucky's veto threshold is low: the legislature can override a redistricting veto with just a simple majority of elected members in each chamber, rather than the two-thirds supermajority required in many states. In his veto message, Beshear stated he was vetoing the map because it "was drafted without public input and reflects unconstitutional political gerrymandering." The Kentucky Supreme Court ultimately upheld the maps in December 2023, finding they did not reach "the level of a clear, flagrant, and unwarranted violation of constitutional rights."
