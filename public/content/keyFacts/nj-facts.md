New Jersey comprises 12 congressional districts. Congressional lines are drawn by a 13-member politician commission; the four legislative leaders and the chairs of the two major political parties each choose two commissioners, and those twelve then choose a thirteenth independent member to serve as chair. There are no state law requirements for drawing congressional districts. The current map was enacted on December 22, 2021, and took effect for the 2022 congressional elections. The commission voted 7–6 to approve the Democratic map proposal, with all six Democratic members and tiebreaker — former New Jersey Supreme Court Judge John Wallace — voting to approve, while all six Republican members voted against. Notably, Chair Wallace stated he voted for the Democratic map because the prior cycle's map had been drawn by Republicans, concluding that "fairness dictates that the Democrats have the opportunity to have their map used for the next redistricting cycle." Both Republican and progressive groups criticized the process for what they described as a lack of transparency and limited public input, especially in the final stages.
