In Texas, congressional and state legislative district boundaries are drawn by the state legislature. Following the 2020 census, Texas was apportioned 38 congressional districts, two more than after the 2010 census. The current post-census maps were enacted on October 25, 2021, after both chambers approved a finalized version on October 18, 2021, and Gov. Greg Abbott signed them into law. Critics have characterized the maps as racial and partisan gerrymanders, noting that both of Texas's new congressional districts were majority-white despite voters of color accounting for 95% of the state's population growth over the prior decade. In a notable mid-decade development, the governor called a special session in 2025, and the legislature passed new congressional lines (HB 4) on August 23, 2025, signed August 29. A federal district court enjoined that map in November 2025, but the U.S. Supreme Court stayed the injunction, leaving the new map in effect for 2026 elections pending appeal.
