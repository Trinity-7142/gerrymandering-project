This joint resolution sought to overturn the Department of Energy’s furnace-efficiency rule for consumer furnaces. Supporters argued the rule was an energy-efficiency mandate that would raise costs and limit consumer choice; opponents said the rule would save energy and money over time, and that killing it would remove a federal standard meant to improve efficiency. It passed the Senate but did not become law; if enacted, it would have nullified the furnace rule, and under the CRA DOE generally could not issue a substantially similar rule without later statutory authorization.
