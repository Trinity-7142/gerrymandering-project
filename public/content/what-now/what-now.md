Now that you know what gerrymandering is and how it works, here’s some next steps that you can take: 

# Know your district.
Knowing which Congressional District you are a part of, and which representative you have is the first step.
## Resources

- [United States House of Representatives | Find your Representative](https://www.house.gov/representatives/find-your-representative)

- [Ballotpedia | Enter your address to see elections on your ballot](https://ballotpedia.org/Main_Page)

# Contact your elected officials
If you find any issues, directly contacting your elected officials can be an effective way to voice your concerns.
## Resources

- [Common Cause | Find your Representatives](https://www.commoncause.org/find-your-representative/)

- [USAGov | Find and Contact Elected Officials](https://www.usa.gov/elected-officials)

- [Congress.gov](http://congress.gov/) | Find your members

# Stay informed on the 2030 redistricting cycle
The 2030 census will directly determine how congressional districts are redrawn for the entire next decade. Be a watcher for fair and transparent district mapping by understanding what’s happening now.
## Resources
- [United States Census Bureau | 2030 Census](https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/decennial-census/decade/2030/2030-census-main.html)
