Grace Meng is an American lawyer and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for New York's 6th Congressional District since 2013. Born in Elmhurst, Queens, and raised in the Elmhurst, Bayside, and Flushing sections of the borough, she attended local schools, graduated from Stuyvesant High School, attended the University of Michigan, and earned her law degree from Yeshiva University's Benjamin Cardozo School of Law. A Democrat, she is the first Asian American elected to the United States Congress from New York. Her platform centers on affordable healthcare, good jobs, and schools safe from gun violence for Queens' working and middle-class families. In Congress, she serves on the House Appropriations Committee as Ranking Member of its Subcommittee on Commerce, Justice, Science and Related Agencies. She passed the COVID-19 Hate Crimes Act into law and chairs the Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus and co-chairs the House Bipartisan Taskforce for Combatting Antisemitism. Her pro-Israel stance drew constituent backlash, and AIPAC was her largest campaign contributor in the 2023–2024 cycle. In 2026, Chuck Park is challenging her in the Democratic primary.
