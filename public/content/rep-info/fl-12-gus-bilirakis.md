Born in Gainesville, Florida, and now based in Palm Harbor, Bilirakis operated a law practice specializing in probate and estate planning that he took over from his father, Michael Bilirakis, who served in Congress from 1983 to 2007. A Republican, he first entered Congress in 2007, succeeding his father. His FL-12 district includes all of Citrus and Hernando counties and most of Pasco County. A conservative, he is a signer of the Taxpayer Protection Pledge and a pledge by Americans for Prosperity to vote against climate legislation that raises taxes. He strongly opposes illegal immigration but, as a grandchild of Greek immigrants, recognizes the benefit of legal immigration. As a senior member of the Energy and Commerce Committee, he chairs the Innovation, Data, and Commerce Subcommittee, and his legislative work has resulted in 75 enacted bills since 2015, earning him recognition as Florida's Most Effective Republican Lawmaker by Vanderbilt's Center for Effective Lawmaking. A 2018 controversy arose when he released a campaign ad touting work on opioid legislation he did not have a hand in crafting.
