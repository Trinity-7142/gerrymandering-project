Born into a Jewish family in Brooklyn on June 13, 1947, Nadler is a resident of Manhattan's Upper West Side and a Democrat who has served in Congress since 1992. Known as the "liberal lion" of New York's congressional delegation, he has championed progressive causes throughout his career. His platform centers on women's rights, abortion rights, gun safety, civil liberties, LGBTQ+ equality, and addressing climate change. As Chairman of the House Judiciary Committee, Nadler led both successful impeachments of former President Donald Trump. He also passed the 9/11 Health and Compensation Act, securing permanent funding for first responders. In September 2025, Nadler announced he would not seek reelection in 2026.
