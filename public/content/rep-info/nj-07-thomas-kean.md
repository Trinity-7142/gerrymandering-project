Thomas Kean Jr. was born in Livingston, New Jersey, the son of former New Jersey Governor Thomas Kean and grandson of former Congressman Robert Kean — making him part of one of the state's most prominent political dynasties. He holds a degree from Dartmouth College and a Master of Arts in Law and Diplomacy from the Fletcher School at Tufts University. A Republican, he has served as U.S. Representative for NJ-07 since 2023, having previously represented New Jersey's 21st legislative district in the State Senate from 2003 to 2022, including as minority leader. In Congress, he serves on the Energy and Commerce and House Foreign Affairs Committees, focusing on lowering costs, improving healthcare access, strengthening infrastructure, and expanding education opportunity. During his 2022 campaign, Kean, who self-describes as pro-choice, promised voters he would support abortion rights. He is a member of the Problem Solvers Caucus, joining to signal his commitment to "working across the aisle." Notably, in 2024 Kean — one of 17 House Republicans representing a Biden-won district — endorsed Donald Trump.
