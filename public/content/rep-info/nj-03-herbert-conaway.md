Dr. Herb Conaway, a lifelong resident of Burlington County, is the son of a teacher and a nurse. He earned an undergraduate degree from Princeton University, a medical degree from Jefferson Medical College, and a law degree from Rutgers Law School. A Democrat, he began his career serving in the U.S. Air Force, rising to the rank of Captain in the Medical Corps at McGuire Air Force Base. He then served 25 years in the New Jersey General Assembly, chairing the Health Committee and serving on the Budget and Military and Veterans Affairs Committees. Upon his swearing-in in January 2025, he became the first Black physician with voting privileges to serve in Congress. His platform centers on equitable and affordable healthcare, safe communities, quality public schools, good jobs, and environmental protection. In Congress, he sits on the Committees on Armed Services and Veterans' Affairs.
