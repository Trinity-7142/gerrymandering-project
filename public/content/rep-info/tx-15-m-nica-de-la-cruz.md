Born in Brownsville, Texas, De La Cruz worked as an insurance agent and business owner before entering politics. She comes from humble beginnings — her grandmother immigrated from Mexico, and her mother raised her as a single parent, instilling values of perseverance and self-reliance. She is the first Latina and first Republican to represent Texas' 15th Congressional District. A conservative Republican, she advocates for border security, preserving the American Dream, and protecting seniors' benefits. In her first term, she secured passage of five pieces of legislation, including the first bill in nearly 20 years from her community to be signed into law, targeting fentanyl trafficking. She also created the South Texas Water Working Group to address the region's water needs. Notably, in June 2024, Politico reported De La Cruz earmarked $2.4 million in taxpayer funds to an organization whose founder was subsequently arrested for allegedly stealing $500,000 from Ponzi scheme victims.
