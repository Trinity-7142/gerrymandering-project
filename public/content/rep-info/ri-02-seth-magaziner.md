Born in Bristol, Rhode Island, Seth Magaziner is a Democratic U.S. Representative for Rhode Island's 2nd Congressional District, having previously served as the state's General Treasurer from 2015 to 2023. He earned a bachelor's degree in history from Brown University and an MBA from Yale, and worked as both an elementary school teacher and a vice president at Trillium Asset Management. His platform centers on protecting Social Security and Medicare, lowering prescription drug costs, expanding the Affordable Care Act, enacting gun safety legislation, and defending abortion rights. He has co-sponsored the bipartisan TRUST in Congress Act to ban lawmakers from trading stocks. As state treasurer, he spearheaded a school construction initiative and created clean energy programs at the Rhode Island Infrastructure Bank. A residency controversy arose during his 2022 campaign when opponents noted he lived outside the district, though he subsequently relocated to Cranston.
