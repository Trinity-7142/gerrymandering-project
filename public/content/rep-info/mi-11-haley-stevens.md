Haley Stevens (D) has served as the U.S. Representative for Michigan's 11th congressional district since 2019. Born in Rochester Hills and raised in an entrepreneurial household where her parents ran a landscaping business, she developed an early emphasis on hard work and fiscal responsibility. Before Congress, she served as Chief of Staff to the U.S. Auto Rescue Task Force, the federal initiative that helped save General Motors, Chrysler, and over 200,000 Michigan jobs during the Great Recession. A centrist Democrat, her platform centers on manufacturing, research and development, workforce education, and working families. Her first bill signed into law, the Building Blocks of STEM Act, expanded opportunities for girls and students of color in STEM, and she was a driving force behind the CHIPS and Science Act. A notable 2022 primary controversy involved approximately $3 million in spending by pro-Israel lobbying groups on her behalf. She is currently a candidate in the 2026 U.S. Senate election in Michigan.
