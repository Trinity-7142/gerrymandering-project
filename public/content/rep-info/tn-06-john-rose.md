John Williams Rose, born February 23, 1965, in Cookeville, Tennessee, grew up working on his family's multigenerational farm in the Temperance Hall and Lancaster communities of DeKalb and Smith Counties. He earned a bachelor's in agribusiness economics from Tennessee Tech, a master's from Purdue, and a J.D. from Vanderbilt. A farmer, attorney, and small business owner, Rose describes himself as a political outsider and is considered one of the most conservative members of Congress. He focuses on reducing the national debt and reining in government spending. He is a staunch opponent of abortion and a strong advocate for Second Amendment rights. He serves on the House Agriculture Committee and House Financial Services Committee. Notably, Rose signed an amicus brief contesting the 2020 presidential election results and voted to object to the certification of those results in January 2021. He is currently running for Governor of Tennessee in the 2026 gubernatorial election.
