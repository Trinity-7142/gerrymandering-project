Born in Panama City, Florida, of Greek descent, Patronis's family has run Captain Anderson's, a landmark seafood restaurant in Panama City, since 1967, and he remains a partner in the business. A Republican, he has served as U.S. Representative for FL-01 since April 2025, having previously served as Florida's Chief Financial Officer from 2017 to 2025. With President Trump's endorsement, Patronis won the FL-01 seat in a special election with a double-digit victory, triggered by Matt Gaetz's resignation after being nominated for attorney general. His congressional priorities include supporting the America First Agenda through cutting taxes, reining in spending, investing in the military, and reforming veterans' services. He serves on the Transportation & Infrastructure and Small Business committees. As CFO, Patronis spearheaded divesting $2 billion from BlackRock over ESG investing concerns. Controversially, Florida's top financial regulator Ronald Rubin filed a lawsuit accusing Patronis of orchestrating a "pay-to-play" scheme, allegations Patronis's office disputed as misleading and false.
