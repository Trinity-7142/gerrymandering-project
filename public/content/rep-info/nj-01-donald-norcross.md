Donald W. Norcross is a Democratic U.S. Representative for New Jersey's 1st congressional district in South Jersey. Born in Camden and raised in Pennsauken Township, he spent years as an electrician before rising through the ranks to become a business agent for IBEW Local 351 and later president of the Southern New Jersey AFL-CIO, representing thousands of workers for nearly 20 years. He has noted working minimum-wage jobs and relying on unemployment and disability insurance as a single parent — experiences that shape his center-left, labor-focused agenda. His platform centers on improving healthcare access, raising wages, and expanding educational opportunities. In Congress, he co-founded the Labor Caucus and bipartisan Building Trades Caucus, and introduced the No Tax Breaks for Union Busting Act. As ranking member of the Armed Services Subcommittee on Tactical Air and Land Forces, he successfully secured a new fleet of KC-46 air refueling tankers for Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst. He also passed a "Buy America" law signed by President Biden to bolster domestic manufacturing and national defense.
