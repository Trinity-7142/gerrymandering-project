Bennie Gordon Thompson (born January 28, 1948) is a Democrat serving as U.S. Representative for Mississippi's 2nd congressional district since 1993. Born in Bolton, Mississippi, to a mechanic father and a teacher mother, his commitment to public service was shaped by early involvement in the civil rights movement — at Tougaloo College he joined the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee and helped lead voter registration drives. His legislative platform focuses on homeland security, civil rights, agriculture and rural issues, equal education, and health care reform. He chaired the House Homeland Security Committee from 2007–2011 and 2019–2023, becoming the first Democrat and first African American to lead it. He helped deliver over $237 million in pandemic relief to Mississippi's HBCUs. He was nominated for the 2025 Nobel Peace Prize for his role on the January 6th Committee.
