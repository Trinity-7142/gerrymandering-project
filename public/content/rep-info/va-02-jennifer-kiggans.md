Jennifer Ann Kiggans is a former United States Navy helicopter pilot and geriatric nurse practitioner serving as the U.S. Representative for Virginia's 2nd congressional district since 2023. Born in Tampa, Florida, she is an alumna of Boston University. She served ten years in the U.S. Navy, completing two deployments to the Persian Gulf as an H-46 and H-3 helicopter pilot. After her service, she used her GI Bill benefits to become a board-certified Adult-Geriatric Primary Care Nurse Practitioner. A Republican, her platform centers on restoring strength in the economy, securing the borders, and reasserting America's strength on the world stage. In Congress, she serves on the House Armed Services Committee, the Committee on Natural Resources, and the Committee on Veterans' Affairs, where she chairs the Subcommittee on Oversight & Investigations. She was notably the only Republican from Virginia to vote to expel Representative George Santos, and voted for the One Big Beautiful Bill Act in May 2025. Her support for DOGE-related federal workforce cuts drew controversy, particularly in a district with a high concentration of federal employees.
