Michelle Fischbach is an American politician and attorney serving since 2021 as the U.S. Representative from Minnesota's 7th congressional district. She lives on a hobby farm in Paynesville and is a conservative Republican. She was the first woman in Minnesota history to serve as President of the Minnesota Senate, initially from 2011–12 and again from 2017–18. In her 2020 victory, she was the only Republican to flip a seat that wasn't open or held by a freshman Democrat, defeating a 30-year incumbent by 14 points. Her platform centers on fiscal responsibility, honoring veterans, supporting law enforcement, fixing a "broken" health care system, and defending religious liberty. She serves as co-chair of the House Pro-Life Caucus and is a staunch proponent of Second Amendment rights. On January 7, 2021, Fischbach was one of 139 representatives to object to the certification of electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania in the 2020 presidential election.
