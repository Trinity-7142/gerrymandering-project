Rep. John Joyce (born February 8, 1957) is a Republican dermatologist serving Pennsylvania's 13th congressional district since 2019. Born and raised in Altoona, Pennsylvania, he earned his undergraduate degree from Penn State University and his M.D. from Temple University School of Medicine, completing his residency in internal medicine and dermatology at Johns Hopkins Hospital. He and his wife established Altoona Dermatology Associates in 1991, serving patients across Central Pennsylvania for over 25 years. Joyce has campaigned on delivering affordable, quality healthcare to rural communities, lowering energy prices, and expanding high-speed internet access across south-central Pennsylvania. In Congress, he has served as Chair of the Energy and Commerce Oversight and Investigations Subcommittee. Notably, Joyce voted against certification of the 2020 presidential election and signed an amicus brief supporting Texas v. Pennsylvania, which sought to contest the election results. His district carries a Cook Partisan Voting Index of R+22, making it Pennsylvania's most Republican district.
