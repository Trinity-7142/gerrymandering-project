Steven Horsford (born April 29, 1973) is a Democratic U.S. Representative for Nevada's 4th congressional district, serving since 2019. Born and raised in Las Vegas, his mother immigrated from Trinidad as a teenager and gave birth to him at age 17. While in high school, he worked at Pizza Hut and cleaned kennels at a veterinarian's office. He made history as the first African American Nevada State Senate Majority Leader and the first African American to represent Nevada in Congress. He also chaired the Congressional Black Caucus from 2023 to 2025. He currently serves on the House Committee on Ways and Means. His platform centers on tackling the climate crisis, including investments in clean energy and zero-emission vehicles, gun control measures including universal background checks and an assault weapons ban, and comprehensive immigration reform creating paths to legal status. In 2020, Horsford acknowledged an extramarital affair with a former Senate intern that began in 2009, when she was a college student and he was Nevada's Senate Majority Leader.
