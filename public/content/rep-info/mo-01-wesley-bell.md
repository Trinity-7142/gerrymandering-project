Wesley Bell is an American attorney and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Missouri's 1st congressional district since 2025. Born and raised in North St. Louis County, Bell grew up in a union household that instilled in him a deep appreciation for public service and the dignity of hard work. He worked multiple jobs to put himself through college at Lindenwood University before earning his law degree from the University of Missouri-Columbia. A Democrat, he previously served as St. Louis County prosecuting attorney from 2019 to 2025, becoming the first Black county prosecuting attorney in St. Louis County history after defeating long-time prosecutor Bob McCulloch in a major upset. In Congress, Bell fights for policies that strengthen working families, advance environmental justice, address climate change, and expand access to affordable healthcare and public safety. He defeated incumbent Cori Bush in the 2024 Democratic primary, though his campaign received over $8.5 million from pro-Israel lobbying groups such as AIPAC and the United Democracy Project. He serves on the House Committee on Armed Services and the Committee on Oversight and Government Reform.
