Born in Lansing, Michigan and raised in what he describes as a "single-parent, poverty-stricken home," Mike Kennedy is an attorney and physician serving as the U.S. Representative from Utah's 3rd congressional district since 2025. He earned his MD from Michigan State University and his JD from Brigham Young University's J. Reuben Clark Law School while taking law classes at night and running his medical practice during the day. A conservative Republican, he is focused on restoring fiscal responsibility, securing America's borders, and pushing back against federal overreach. At the federal level, Kennedy has prioritized health care reform to reduce Medicare and Medicaid's burden on the national debt, and has pledged to secure federal funding for infrastructure projects ahead of the 2034 Olympics. As a state lawmaker, he sponsored a successful bill to ban sex reassignment surgeries for minors. Notably, Kennedy co-introduced the bipartisan Algorithm Accountability Act, which modernizes Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act and establishes a duty of care for platforms deploying algorithms that amplify harmful content.
