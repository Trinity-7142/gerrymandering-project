Rep. Mary Miller (R) is a farmer and politician serving Illinois's 15th congressional district since 2021. Born in Oak Park, Illinois, her career background spans farming, business management, and teaching. She is a member of the House Freedom Caucus and serves on the House Committee on Agriculture, the Committee on Education & Labor, and the Committee on House Administration. Her platform centers on conservative priorities including border security, election integrity, and traditional family values. On the House Administration Committee, she has advanced voter ID requirements in federal elections and led legislation to ban insider trading by Members of Congress. After redistricting, she defeated fellow incumbent Rodney Davis in the 2022 Republican primary, aided by an endorsement from Donald Trump. She drew controversy shortly after taking office for quoting Adolf Hitler outside the U.S. Capitol. She also objected to the Electoral College votes of Arizona and Pennsylvania on January 6, 2021.
