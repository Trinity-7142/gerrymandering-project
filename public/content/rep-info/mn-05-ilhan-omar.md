Born in Mogadishu, Somalia, Omar and her family fled the country's civil war, spending four years in a Kenyan refugee camp before settling in Minneapolis in 1997. She earned a bachelor's degree in political science and international studies from North Dakota State University in 2011. Sworn into office in January 2019, she became the first African refugee to serve in Congress, the first woman of color to represent Minnesota, and one of the first two Muslim-American women elected to Congress. A progressive Democrat, she has advocated for a $15 minimum wage, universal healthcare, student loan debt forgiveness, DACA protections, and abolishing ICE. She has secured over $40 million for the district in community projects. Omar has drawn controversy for sharp criticism of Israel and statements many have condemned as antisemitic, and a financial disclosure showing holdings between $6 million and $30 million was later amended to show assets between $18,000 and $95,000, which she attributed to an accounting error.
