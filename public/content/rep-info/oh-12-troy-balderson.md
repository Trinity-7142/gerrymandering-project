Born on January 16, 1962, in Zanesville, Ohio, Balderson grew up in a rural setting, developing a background rooted in hands-on labor and family enterprise. His professional experience includes co-owning Balderson Motor Sales. A conservative Republican, he served as an Ohio state senator representing the 20th district from 2011 until his election to Congress, where he chaired the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee. He currently serves on the House Energy and Commerce Committee. His platform emphasizes energy production, reducing inflation, and opposing regulatory expansion. Balderson voted against the Respect for Marriage Act in 2022. He has missed only 0.6% of roll call votes since 2018, better than the median among currently serving representatives.
