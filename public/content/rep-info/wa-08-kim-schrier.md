Rep. Kim Schrier is a Democrat who has served as the U.S. representative for Washington's 8th congressional district since 2019. Born and raised in Los Angeles, California, she grew up the daughter of a public school teacher and an engineer, earning an astrophysics degree from UC Berkeley and an M.D. from UC Davis, completing her residency in pediatrics at Stanford. She is the first pediatrician elected to Congress. Her district includes suburbs east of Seattle and extends across the Cascades to rural central Washington. She made expanding Medicare and the Affordable Care Act the centerpiece of her original campaign. In office, she has worked to lower prescription drug costs and oppose corporate price gouging. In 2025, she was one of 46 House Democrats who joined Republicans to vote for the Laken Riley Act, drawing notice for crossing party lines on immigration.
