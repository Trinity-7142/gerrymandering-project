Mike Lawler was raised in South Salem and Suffern, New York. He earned a Bachelor of Science in accounting and finance from Manhattan College in 2009 and was named valedictorian of his graduating class. A moderate Republican, Lawler was rated the fourth most bipartisan member of the House during the 118th Congress by the Bipartisan Index of The Lugar Center and the McCourt School of Public Policy. He serves on the House Financial Services Committee and chairs the Foreign Affairs Subcommittee on the Middle East and North Africa. A key platform priority has been tax relief: as a leading member of the SALT Caucus, he secured an expansion of the SALT deduction cap from $10,000 to $40,000 for qualifying families, a provision signed into law in 2025. He first won his seat in 2022, becoming the first Republican to defeat a sitting DCCC chairman in over 40 years. In October 2024, resurfaced photos showed Lawler in blackface at a 2006 Halloween party, drawing public scrutiny ahead of his re-election.
