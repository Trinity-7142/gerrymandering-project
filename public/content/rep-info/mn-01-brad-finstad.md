Born in New Ulm, Minnesota, Finstad grew up on his family's multigenerational farm in Brown County. A graduate of the University of Minnesota with a degree in Agriculture Education, he and his wife Jackie operate an agriculture business in New Ulm while running their generational family farm. A conservative Republican, he champions agricultural policy, rural economic development, and support for veterans. In Congress, he is committed to advancing farm policy written by farmers for rural communities and serves on the House Committee on Small Business, working to reduce red tape and promote fiscal discipline. Finstad was first elected in a 2022 special election to finish the term of the late Rep. Jim Hagedorn and won a third term in November 2024 with 58.5% of the vote. Nearly half of his sponsored bills fall in the agriculture and food policy area.
