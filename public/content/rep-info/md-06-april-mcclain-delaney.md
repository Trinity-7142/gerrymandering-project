Born in Buhl, Idaho, to a potato farmer father, Democrat April McClain Delaney brings a rural, working-class background to a moderate-to-progressive ideology aligned with the New Democrat Coalition. She earned her B.A. from Northwestern University and a J.D. from Georgetown, later serving as Deputy Assistant Secretary of Communications at the Department of Commerce's NTIA and as Director of Common Sense Media. Her campaign platform centered on gun safety legislation, codifying abortion rights, and expanding healthcare access, including Medicare drug price negotiation. She also championed broadband expansion and digital equity. She narrowly won her 2024 general election and became one of the first women to represent Maryland in the House since 2016. Notably, she voted against a bill to reopen the federal government during a 43-day shutdown, then drew scrutiny for touting the bill's funding for her district.
