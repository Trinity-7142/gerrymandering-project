Born in 1960 in Dayton, Ohio, Turner grew up in East Dayton in a working-class household — his mother was a public school teacher and his father a union manufacturing worker. He holds a bachelor's degree from Ohio Northern University, an MBA from the University of Dayton, a law degree from Case Western Reserve University, and a doctorate from Georgetown University focused on urban economic development. A former attorney, he also served as mayor of Dayton. Sometimes described as an "urban Republican," Turner is a conservative who prioritizes national security, defense jobs, and fiscal responsibility. He served as Chairman of the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence from 2023 to 2025. He was the first Dayton official to hold a leadership role on a national security committee in the U.S. House. During his tenure, Wright-Patterson Air Force Base — the largest single-site employer in Ohio — roughly doubled in on-base employment.
