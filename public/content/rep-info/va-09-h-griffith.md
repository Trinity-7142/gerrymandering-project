Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Griffith relocated to Salem, Virginia in infancy, where his career as an attorney specialized in criminal traffic law, constitutional law, and civil litigation. He is a member of the Republican Party and the Freedom Caucus. Griffith served in the Virginia House of Delegates from 1994 through 2010, serving as majority leader from 2000 through 2010. His platform champions free enterprise, fiscal restraint, and Second Amendment rights, while highlighting his work on energy and coal industry issues, which remain central to the district's economy. He has led legislation to permanently classify fentanyl analogs as controlled substances. Notably, on January 6, 2021, Griffith was one of 147 Republican members of Congress who objected to certifying the 2020 presidential election. He secured his eighth term in Congress with 73.5% of the vote in 2024.
