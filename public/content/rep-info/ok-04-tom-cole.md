Tom Cole (born April 28, 1949) is a Republican U.S. Representative for Oklahoma's 4th Congressional District, serving since 2003. A fifth-generation Oklahoman, he resides in Moore. An enrolled member of the Chickasaw Nation, Cole is the longest-serving Native American in the history of Congress. His academic background includes an M.A. from Yale and a Ph.D. from the University of Oklahoma, both in British history. Cole has established a conservative voting record, consistently voting anti-abortion and for gun rights, while holding pro-business views and supporting free trade, the military, and veterans. His platform emphasizes smaller government, lower taxes, and border security. Cole serves as the 43rd Chairman of the House Appropriations Committee — the first Native American and first Oklahoman to hold the gavel. He is considered the foremost expert in the House on Native American issues.
