Born and raised in Houston to a military family, Hunt attended the United States Military Academy, earning a B.S. in leadership and management with mechanical engineering in 2004. He later earned three master's degrees at Cornell University. After West Point, he served eight years in the Army as an Apache helicopter pilot, including one combat deployment to Iraq and two to Saudi Arabia as a diplomatic liaison officer. A conservative Republican, Hunt vowed to restore American energy independence, secure the southern border, and mitigate Houston's flooding threat. He sits on the House Judiciary, Natural Resources, and Small Business Committees. Notably, he sponsored the Royalty Resiliency Act, which became Public Law 118-81 in September 2024 — the only oil and gas legislation signed into law by President Biden. Upon being sworn in, Hunt became the first Black graduate of a U.S. military academy elected to Congress.
