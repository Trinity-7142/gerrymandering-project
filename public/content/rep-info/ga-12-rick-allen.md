Rick W. Allen (born November 7, 1951) is a Republican U.S. Representative for Georgia's 12th congressional district, serving since 2015. A native of Augusta, Georgia, he graduated from Auburn University in 1973 and worked as a project manager before founding R.W. Allen and Associates in 1976, an Augusta-based construction company. Throughout his tenure, Allen has maintained strong conservative positions on social and economic issues. His platform priorities include reducing inflation, securing the border, supporting Georgia farmers, and expanding American energy production. The son of a farmer, he serves on the House Agriculture Committee, advocating for Georgia's farming industry. In 2024, an analysis found Allen appeared to have violated the STOCK Act by being up to six-and-a-half years late in reporting 136 financial transactions worth up to $8.5 million. He was also among the 139 House Republicans who objected to certifying the 2020 presidential election results. Allen was reelected in November 2024 with 60.3% of the vote.
