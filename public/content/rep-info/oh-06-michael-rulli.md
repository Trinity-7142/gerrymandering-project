Michael Anthony Rulli is a Republican politician and businessman serving as the U.S. Representative for Ohio's 6th congressional district since 2024. Born and raised in the Mahoning Valley, he is the director of operations at Rulli Bros. Markets, a family-owned chain of grocery stores. Rulli was a Democrat until the late 1990s, later joining the Republican Party. His platform centers on an "America First" approach, opposing job offshoring and prioritizing border security. He is a member of the DOGE Caucus, committed to eliminating wasteful federal spending. In the Ohio Senate, he was one of 12 senators to vote against HB 6, the legislation at the center of one of the nation's largest corruption scandals. A notable controversy arose in November 2022 when Rulli fired a handgun at two teenagers he believed were trespassing; his wife later also fired shots at one of the teenagers and family members who returned to retrieve hunting equipment, with disputed accounts of the incident.
