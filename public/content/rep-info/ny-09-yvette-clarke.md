Born in Flatbush, Brooklyn, to Jamaican immigrant parents — her mother, Una S. T. Clarke, held a City Council seat for over a decade — Yvette Clarke is a Democratic U.S. Representative who has served NY's 9th congressional district since 2013, first entering Congress in 2007 representing the 11th district. A progressive, she is a senior member of the House Energy and Commerce Committee. Her platform centers on Medicare for All, affordable housing, and humane immigration policy. She has led on tech policy, authoring the DEEPFAKES Accountability Act to combat malicious AI-generated content. She was elected chair of the Congressional Black Caucus in December 2024. A notable controversy involves reports that her campaign literature overstated her Oberlin College graduation, which she disputed.
