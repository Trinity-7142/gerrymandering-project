Representative Marlin Stutzman is a fourth-generation farmer from Howe, Indiana. His business journey began at 16 with his own beef cattle operation; by 18, he had joined his father in growing the family business into a multi-million-dollar enterprise. A conservative Republican, Stutzman is a member of the Freedom Caucus. He returned to Congress in 2025 for his fourth term after previously serving from 2010 to 2016. His platform emphasizes fiscal responsibility, pro-life policy, and free-market healthcare, including health savings accounts, portable coverage, and tax credits for businesses. In his prior tenure, he authored the Conservative Congressional Budget and was the original sponsor of the Right to Try legislation, later signed into law. He currently serves on the Financial Services Committee and the Budget Committee.
