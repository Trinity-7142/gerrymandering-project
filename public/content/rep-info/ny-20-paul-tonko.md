A lifelong resident of Amsterdam, New York, of primarily Polish descent, Tonko received a B.S. in mechanical and industrial engineering from Clarkson University in 1971. A member of the Democratic Party, Tonko has been called a staunch progressive. He served in the New York State Assembly from 1983–2007, chairing its Energy Committee for 15 years, and was appointed president and CEO of the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority from 2007 until 2008. He is now an eighth-term member of the U.S. House, representing the Capital Region, including Albany, Schenectady, Troy, and Saratoga Springs. His platform centers on clean energy, climate, addiction and mental health, and consumer protection. He serves as Ranking Member of the Energy and Commerce Subcommittee on Environment, Manufacturing, & Critical Materials. Notably, he sponsored Timothy's Law, a 2006 New York measure requiring health insurers to cover mental health treatment. He voted with President Biden's stated position 100% of the time in the 117th Congress, according to FiveThirtyEight.
