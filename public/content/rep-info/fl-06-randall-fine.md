Randall "Randy" Fine is a Republican U.S. Representative for Florida's 6th congressional district, serving since April 2025. Born into a Jewish family in Tucson, Arizona, he was raised in Kentucky and earned a bachelor's degree in government and an MBA from Harvard University. After working for McKinsey & Company and as a teaching fellow at Harvard, he became a corporate executive in the casino gambling industry. A staunch conservative, his campaign priorities included border security, election integrity, defending life, and protecting the Second Amendment. Fine won a special election for the seat vacated when Rep. Mike Waltz was appointed National Security Advisor. He has drawn scrutiny for residing in Melbourne Beach, which falls outside the 6th congressional district. His outspoken remarks on Islam and the Israeli-Palestinian conflict have generated significant public controversy.
