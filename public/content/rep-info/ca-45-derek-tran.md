Democrat Derek Tran has served as the U.S. Representative for California's 45th Congressional District since 2025. He grew up in the San Gabriel Valley as the son of Vietnamese refugees; his father fled Vietnam in 1975 after the fall of Saigon. Tran is the third Vietnamese American elected to Congress and the first to represent California. He served eight years in the U.S. Army Reserve, including activation for Operations Noble Eagle and Enduring Freedom, before receiving an honorable discharge. He subsequently worked as an attorney fighting on behalf of consumers and workers. His campaign platform centered on reproductive rights, which he called a "number one issue," including support for nationwide access to IVF, protecting Medicare and Social Security, and expanding affordable housing through the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit. He defeated incumbent Republican Michelle Steel by just 653 votes out of nearly 316,000 cast. In Congress, he serves on the House Armed Services Committee and House Small Business Committee. His casework office has recovered more than $5 million in federal benefits for constituents.
