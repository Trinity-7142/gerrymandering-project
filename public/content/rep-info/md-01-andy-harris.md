Born in working-class Brooklyn in 1957, Harris found his calling studying medicine at Johns Hopkins University, where he worked nights and weekends as a blood bank technician to help pay tuition. He earned his B.S. in biology and M.D. from Johns Hopkins, and later a master's in health policy and management. Harris served in the Navy Medical Corps during Operation Desert Storm. He was first elected to Congress in 2010 and is the only Republican member of Maryland's congressional delegation. A staunch conservative, as Chairman of the House Freedom Caucus and Chairman of the Agriculture Appropriations Subcommittee, he focuses on fiscal responsibility and taxpayer protection. In 2023, he called for increased natural gas and coal production to lower the national deficit. Harris has been a leading congressional critic of marijuana decriminalization efforts.
