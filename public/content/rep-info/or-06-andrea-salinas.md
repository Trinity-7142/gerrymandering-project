Rep. Andrea Salinas is a first-generation American and the daughter of a Mexican immigrant — her father immigrated to the United States as a child and picked cotton and tomatoes before eventually serving as a police officer. Salinas earned a B.A. in psychology from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1994. A Democrat, she campaigns on affordable quality health care, an economy that works for everyone, and clean air, land, and water. In the Oregon House, she served as House Majority Whip and chaired the House Health Care Committee before winning OR-06 in 2022. In Congress, she secured passage of a bill restoring the right of the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde to pursue land claims and compensation. She also serves as Co-Chair of the bipartisan Mental Health Caucus. Following her 2022 victory, her Republican opponent filed a lawsuit over a television ad that initially sought to block her from taking office. She is currently serving her second term.
