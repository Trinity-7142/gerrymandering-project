Born in Los Angeles and raised in South Los Angeles, Rep. Judy Chu later worked as a psychology professor at Los Angeles City College and East Los Angeles College for twenty years before entering public life. She represents the 28th Congressional District, which includes Pasadena and the west San Gabriel Valley of southern California. A progressive Democrat, she has championed issues such as health care access, immigration reform, and environmental protection. She serves on the House Ways and Means Committee, which has jurisdiction over legislation pertaining to taxes, revenues, Social Security, and Medicare. In 2009, she became the first Chinese American woman elected to Congress. She served as Chair of the Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus from 2011 until 2025, helping lead the Tri-Caucus alongside the Congressional Black Caucus and Congressional Hispanic Caucus. She was arrested at a Capitol protest in June 2022 and paid a $50 fine per the House Ethics Committee.
