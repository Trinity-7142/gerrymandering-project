Torres immigrated to the United States from Guatemala and is a lifelong resident of Pomona. She worked as a 911 dispatcher with the Los Angeles Police Department for 17 years and holds a B.A. in Labor Studies from the National Labor College. A Democrat, her platform centers on healthcare access, affordable housing, public safety, and workers' rights. As the only Central American-born Member of Congress, she has directed the Appropriations Committee to fund programs addressing the root causes of migration and promoting human rights. Drawing on her dispatcher background, she authored a law modernizing the 9-1-1 system to route cell phone callers to their local police department. After her 2022 reelection, Torres accused El Salvador's President Nayib Bukele of interfering in her race, as he had urged voters to oppose her.
