Rep. Bill Foster is a scientist and businessman representing the 11th Congressional District of Illinois, a position he has held since 2013. Born in 1955 in Madison, Wisconsin, he lives in Naperville and is a Democrat and member of the New Democrat Coalition. He is the only PhD physicist in Congress, having received his doctorate in physics from Harvard University. Before Congress, he worked as a high-energy physicist at Fermilab and was part of the team that discovered the top quark. His platform focuses on economic recovery, raising workers' wages, and supporting policies to make essentials more affordable for low- and middle-income families. In the 118th Congress, he was appointed to the House Bipartisan Task Force on Artificial Intelligence. His Better Energy Storage Technology (BEST) Act passed with bipartisan support and was signed into law.
