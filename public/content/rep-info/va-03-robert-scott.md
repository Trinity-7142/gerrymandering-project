Born in Washington, D.C. and raised in Newport News, Virginia, the son of a surgeon and a teacher, Bobby Scott is a graduate of Harvard College and Boston College Law School. A Democrat serving VA-03 since 1993, he is the dean of Virginia's congressional delegation and the first Filipino American voting member of Congress. His 1992 election also made him the first African American elected to Congress from Virginia since Reconstruction. A progressive legislator, Scott serves as the top Democrat on the House Education and Workforce Committee and has championed raising the federal minimum wage. He has authored landmark bills including the Every Student Succeeds Act, the Raise the Wage Act, and the Juvenile Justice Reform Act. In 2017, a former congressional fellow alleged sexual harassment; Scott denied the claim, and the case was mutually dismissed in 2021.
