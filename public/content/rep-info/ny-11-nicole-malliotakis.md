Born in Manhattan and raised in Staten Island's Great Kills neighborhood, Malliotakis is the daughter of a Greek father and a Cuban mother who fled Cuba following Fidel Castro's rise to power. A Republican, she was first elected to the New York State Assembly in 2010 and later became the GOP's 2017 New York City mayoral nominee, losing to incumbent Bill de Blasio. She was elected to Congress in November 2020, defeating an incumbent Democrat by more than 18,000 votes. As of 2025, she is the only Republican representing New York City in Congress. Her platform focuses on strengthening the economy, securing the southern border, tackling inflation, unleashing America's energy potential, and making New York safe and affordable. She serves on the House Ways and Means Committee and is the first Republican from New York City to do so in 30 years. Notably, on January 6, 2021, Malliotakis voted to object to counting Arizona's and Pennsylvania's electoral votes, a decision that drew controversy. She was instrumental in shaping key tax provisions, including quadrupling the SALT deduction to $40,000 for individuals and families.
