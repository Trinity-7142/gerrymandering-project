Summer Lee (born November 26, 1987) is a Democratic U.S. Representative for Pennsylvania's 12th congressional district, serving since 2023. Raised in North Braddock, PA, she earned a B.A. in journalism from Penn State and a J.D. from Howard University School of Law. A progressive, she became the first Black woman to represent Pennsylvania in Congress. She is a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, Congressional Black Caucus, and Justice Democrats. Her platform centers on workers' rights, environmental justice, Medicare for All, gun control, and campaign finance reform, including the Abolish Super PACs Act and the OLIGARCH Act, which would impose a tax on extreme billionaire wealth. Since taking office, she has delivered over $2.4 billion in federal investment to Western PA. Controversially, Lee emerged as one of the first voices calling for a ceasefire in Gaza, fueling attacks from pro-Israel groups, and was one of only 37 House Democrats to vote against providing military aid to Israel in 2024.
