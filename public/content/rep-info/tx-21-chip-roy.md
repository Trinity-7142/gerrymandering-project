Charles Eugene "Chip" Roy (born August 7, 1972) is an American attorney and politician serving as the U.S. representative for Texas's 21st congressional district. Born in Bethesda, Maryland and raised in Lovettsville, Virginia, his parents were conservatives whose support of Ronald Reagan helped shape his political views. His career includes working as a federal prosecutor and federal banking analyst, serving as first assistant attorney general of Texas, chief of staff to Sen. Ted Cruz, and senior advisor to Gov. Rick Perry. A member and policy chair of the House Freedom Caucus, Roy is considered part of the most conservative faction of the House Republican Conference. His platform centers on international affairs, immigration, government reform, and fiscal conservatism. Late in 2024, Roy opposed a Trump-backed proposal to raise the debt ceiling, prompting Trump to call for a Republican primary challenger. In August 2025, Roy announced his candidacy for Texas Attorney General in the 2026 elections.
