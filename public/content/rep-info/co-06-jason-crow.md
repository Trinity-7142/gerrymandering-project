A working-class upbringing shaped Jason Crow — he held minimum-wage jobs in high school and worked construction while enlisting in the National Guard to help fund his college education. He earned a B.A. from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 2002 and a J.D. from the University of Denver Sturm College of Law in 2009. A former Army Ranger, Crow completed three combat tours in Iraq and Afghanistan with the 82nd Airborne Division and 75th Ranger Regiment, earning the Bronze Star Medal. He is the first Democrat to represent CO-06, a district covering Aurora, Littleton, Centennial, and surrounding Denver metro areas. A centrist Democrat, his priorities include climate change, job creation, and gun violence prevention. He serves on the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence and the House Armed Services Committee. He served as one of seven impeachment managers during President Trump's first Senate trial in 2020.
