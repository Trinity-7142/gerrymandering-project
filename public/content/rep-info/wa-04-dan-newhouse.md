A third-generation Yakima Valley farmer, Dan Newhouse brings real-world experience to Congress as a businessman and former state legislator. He earned a bachelor's degree in agricultural economics from Washington State University and previously served as director of the Washington State Department of Agriculture and as a member of the Washington House of Representatives. A conservative Republican, his platform supports efforts to "defund all sanctuary cities, fully secure our border, build a wall on the southern border, and fix our dangerous immigration system." He introduced the first agriculture labor reform bill to pass the House in over 30 years — the Farm Workforce Modernization Act — to ensure a stable, legal agricultural workforce. Newhouse was among the ten House Republicans who voted to impeach Donald Trump during his second impeachment, and is one of two such Republicans to win renomination and reelection in both 2022 and 2024. In December 2025, Newhouse announced he will not seek a seventh term.
