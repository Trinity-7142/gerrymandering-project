Scott Perry is a Republican congressman and retired Army National Guard brigadier general representing Pennsylvania's 10th congressional district. Raised by a single mother who relocated to south-central Pennsylvania, Perry's family relied on public assistance for several years, and he grew up in a home that initially lacked electricity and running water. He worked his way through college and earned a B.S. in business administration from Penn State University. In November 2021, Perry was elected chair of the House Freedom Caucus, the most conservative House Republican group. His platform centers on border security, Second Amendment rights, and balancing energy needs with environmental protection. Perry made national headlines for his alleged connections to attempts to overturn the 2020 election, and was cited more than 50 times in a Senate Judiciary report outlining Donald Trump's effort to reverse the election results. In August 2022, Perry disclosed that his cellphone was briefly seized by the FBI on a search warrant.
