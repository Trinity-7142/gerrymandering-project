An immigrant from Taiwan who came to the U.S. at age 3, Lieu's parents sold gifts and jewelry at flea markets to make ends meet before building a small business. He earned two degrees from Stanford University and a J.D. from Georgetown University. A progressive Democrat, his issue priorities include environmental protection and climate mitigation, cybersecurity, and consumer safety. As one of four Members of Congress with a computer science degree, he is frequently sought out for his insights on technology, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity. His legislative accomplishments include authoring a first-in-the-nation ban on gay conversion therapy for children. He serves as Vice Chair of the Democratic Caucus, making him the highest-ranking Asian American ever to serve in House leadership. Lieu served on active duty with the U.S. Air Force JAG Corps and later in the Air Force Reserve, attaining the rank of colonel.
