Born in Kingston, Pennsylvania, Rob Bresnahan is a fifth-generation Northeastern Pennsylvanian who took the reins of his grandfather's union electrical contracting company at age 19, expanding the business across the commonwealth. After earning a business degree from the University of Scranton, he became CEO of Kuharchik Construction, growing it to over 150 employees. A conservative Republican, his campaign platform centered on border security, reducing federal spending, and cutting taxes. He defeated incumbent Democrat Matt Cartwright in November 2024. In Congress, Bresnahan is a member of the Problem Solvers Caucus and Republican Governance Group. A notable controversy arose when reports revealed he had made hundreds of stock trades — including during the 2025 market volatility following Liberation Day tariffs — despite having campaigned on prohibiting congressional stock trading.
