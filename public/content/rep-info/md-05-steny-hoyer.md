Steny Hamilton Hoyer is a Democrat who has served as the U.S. Representative for Maryland's 5th congressional district since 1981. Born in New York City in 1939, he grew up in Maryland, graduated from Suitland High School, earned a B.A. magna cum laude from the University of Maryland, and a J.D. from Georgetown University Law Center in 1966. In the House, Hoyer built a reputation as a moderate liberal. He served twice as House Majority Leader, from 2007 to 2011 and again from 2019 to 2023. He is the longest-serving member of the House from Maryland. His platform has centered on a "Make It In America" agenda focused on job creation through reinvigorating American manufacturing. Hoyer is a strong supporter of Israel and has long been allied with AIPAC, which drew criticism from progressive colleagues amid the Gaza conflict, reflecting deepening Democratic divisions over the issue. In January 2026, Hoyer announced he would not seek re-election.
