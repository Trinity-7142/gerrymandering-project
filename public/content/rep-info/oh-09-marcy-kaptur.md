A native Toledoan of Polish-American, working-class heritage, Kaptur grew up in the same modest house she still lives in today; her family ran a small grocery store, and her mother served on a union organizing committee at a local factory. She earned a history degree from the University of Wisconsin–Madison and a master's in urban planning from the University of Michigan, then worked as an urban planner in Toledo before serving as a domestic policy advisor in the Carter administration. A Democrat representing OH-09 since 1983, she is the longest-serving woman in congressional history. Her platform centers on opposing trade deals she views as harmful to the middle class, holding Wall Street accountable, expanding healthcare access, reducing prescription drug costs, and protecting the Great Lakes. She serves on the House Appropriations and Budget Committees and championed the legislation that established the National World War II Memorial. In 2024, she narrowly defeated Trump-endorsed Republican Derek Merrin by less than one percentage point — the first time in her career she failed to win a majority.
