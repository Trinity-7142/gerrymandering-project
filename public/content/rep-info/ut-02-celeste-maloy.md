Born in Cedar City, Utah, and raised in Hiko, Nevada in a working-class household with five siblings, Republican Rep. Celeste Maloy brings a rural, conservative background to Congress. She began her public service career as a soil conservationist before earning her law degree from Brigham Young University and becoming a deputy Washington County attorney. Prior to serving in Congress, she shaped conservative policy as Chief Legal Counsel to Rep. Chris Stewart, whom she was elected to succeed in a 2023 special election. Her platform centers on helping families keep more of their earnings, securing the border, and defending Utah values. She serves on the House Appropriations and Natural Resources Committees, focusing heavily on public lands issues. She has introduced legislation to transfer federal lands to Utah, improve programs for women-owned businesses, and streamline federal permitting. Her 2024 primary victory over challenger Colby Jenkins was confirmed by recount — by just 176 votes. She is a niece by marriage of Cliven Bundy and cousin of Ammon Bundy, known for their involvement in the 2014 Bundy standoff.
