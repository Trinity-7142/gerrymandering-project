James Comer is a Republican politician from Kentucky representing the state's 1st congressional district. He grew up in Monroe County, attended Western Kentucky University to study agriculture, and subsequently took out a loan to purchase his own farm. He served six terms in the Kentucky House of Representatives beginning in 2000, then was elected Kentucky's Commissioner of Agriculture in 2011. Comer was elected to Congress in 2016 to represent the 1st Congressional District, covering 35 counties from the Mississippi River to Central Kentucky. A staunch conservative, he focuses on agricultural policy, government oversight, and rural economic development. He served on the 2018 Farm Bill Conference Committee and remains the only formerly elected Commissioner of Agriculture in Congress. He currently serves as Chairman of the House Committee on Oversight and Accountability. As chair, Comer launched a high-profile investigation into President Biden and his family, though it had yet to unearth evidence of direct presidential involvement as of August 2024. He was reelected in 2024 with 74.7% of the vote.
