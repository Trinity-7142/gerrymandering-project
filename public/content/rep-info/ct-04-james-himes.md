Born in Lima, Peru in 1966 to American parents, Himes spent his early childhood abroad while his father worked for the Ford Foundation and UNICEF. He grew up as the child of a single working mom in a small town. He attended Harvard College, captaining the lightweight crew team, and earned a Rhodes Scholarship to study Latin American studies at Oxford. His career began at Goldman Sachs, where he rose to Vice President working in Latin America. A member of the centrist New Democrat Coalition, Himes champions business innovation, green energy, and affordable healthcare. He serves as Ranking Member of the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence and sits on the House Financial Services Committee. He helped craft and pass the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform Act. Notably, in November 2025, he was the sole Connecticut representative to vote for a Republican resolution condemning socialism, arguing the Trump administration was itself "the most socialist administration in two and a half centuries."
