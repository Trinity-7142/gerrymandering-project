A Wilmington native and longtime public servant, Representative McBride is a progressive Democrat and advocate for LGBTQ equality, racial justice, an expanded social safety net, workers' rights, and green technologies. She previously served as national spokesperson for the Human Rights Campaign and worked for former Governor Jack Markell and the late Attorney General Beau Biden. Her congressional campaign emphasized paid family and medical leave, raising the minimum wage, protecting reproductive rights, expanding healthcare access, and addressing economic insecurity. In her first state senate term, she passed the landmark Healthy Delaware Families Act, marking the largest expansion of Delaware's social safety net in decades. She won the 2024 general election with 58% of the vote, becoming the first openly transgender member of the U.S. Congress. Her election prompted Republican Rep. Nancy Mace to introduce a bill banning transgender women from Capitol Hill bathrooms, which Speaker Mike Johnson subsequently enacted.
