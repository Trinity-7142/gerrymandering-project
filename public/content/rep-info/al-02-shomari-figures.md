Born in Mobile, Alabama, Shomari Coleman Figures is an attorney and politician serving as the U.S. Representative from Alabama's 2nd congressional district since 2025. He is the son of Michael Figures, a civil rights attorney and Alabama state senator, and Vivian Davis Figures, who succeeded her husband in the Alabama Senate after his death. He earned both a bachelor's degree in criminal justice and history and a J.D. from the University of Alabama, later clerking for a federal judge, working on President Obama's re-election campaign, and serving in the White House and Department of Justice. He focuses on expanding voting rights, improving public safety, and advancing economic opportunities for underserved communities. Figures is a member of the New Democrat Coalition and the Congressional Black Caucus. A notable controversy arose when cryptocurrency political action committees spent millions to elect him during the primary, and Figures subsequently asked the Democratic National Committee to adopt more pro-crypto policies. In office, he has pursued bipartisan work, including the Made in America Jobs Act, which passed the House and would grant eligibility to projects that help relocate foreign jobs to the United States.
