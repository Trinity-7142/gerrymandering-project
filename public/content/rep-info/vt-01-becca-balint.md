Born in Heidelberg, West Germany, and raised in Peekskill, New York, Balint moved to Vermont in 1994, where she worked as a teacher and became active in local politics. As the daughter of a working-class mother and immigrant father, she is deeply committed to defending American democracy and protecting vulnerable communities. Balint has been described as a progressive. She has focused on increasing access to affordable housing, mental health care, and protecting democracy. While majority leader of the Vermont State Senate, she led the passage of the first gun safety laws in the state's history. She is the first woman and openly LGBTQ+ person to represent Vermont in Congress — prior to her election, Vermont was the only state that had never elected a woman to Congress. Notably, she was the first Jewish member of Congress to call for a ceasefire in the Gaza war. Her record has drawn scrutiny: while not acting unlawfully, her leadership PAC's acceptance of corporate donations has been seen as "in tension" with her campaign messaging pledging to reject corporate money.
