Born in Altamonte Springs, Florida, Boebert's family later settled in Rifle, Colorado; she dropped out of high school after becoming pregnant, later earning her GED. She credits working at McDonald's with instilling conservative values and a belief in self-reliance. From 2013 to 2022, she owned Shooters Grill in Rifle, where staff were encouraged to carry firearms openly. In 2020, she defeated five-term incumbent Scott Tipton in an upset primary victory and has served in Congress since 2021. She switched to Colorado's 4th congressional district for 2024, winning the general election on November 5, 2024. A hard-right Republican, she has aligned with the Freedom Caucus and the Second Amendment Caucus. Her platform emphasizes support for the energy industry and market-driven energy policy, opposition to green-energy mandates, abortion, and COVID-19 vaccine requirements. Notable controversies include being removed from a Denver theater in 2023 for vaping and disruptive behavior, confirmed by surveillance video; she later apologized.
