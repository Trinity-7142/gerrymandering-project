Born in San Mateo, California, the daughter of a beer truck driver and a school cafeteria employee, Lofgren attended Stanford University on a California State Scholarship and worked the night shift at the Eastman Kodak plant to cover expenses not included in her scholarship. A Democrat, Lofgren is in her 15th term in Congress, having been first elected in 1994. Her platform centers on immigration reform and defense of DACA, drawing on her background as a longtime immigration attorney and law professor. As Ranking Member on the House Science, Space and Technology Committee, she focuses on spurring innovation and cultivating the nation's STEM pipeline. She served as a House Manager in Donald Trump's 2020 impeachment trial — the first woman to ever present a presidential case to the Senate — and is the only Member of Congress to have participated in all four modern impeachment proceedings. Critics have questioned whether she has taken a sufficiently strong stance on Big Tech accountability, despite bipartisan support for antitrust legislation.
