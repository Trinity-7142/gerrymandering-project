Rep. Diana DeGette is a fourth-generation Coloradoan and progressive leader representing Colorado's 1st District, based in Denver. She graduated from South High School in Denver before earning her bachelor's degree from Colorado College and her law degree from New York University. A Democrat, she has served since January 7, 1997. Her platform centers on lowering the cost of insulin, enforcing environmental laws, and addressing the climate crisis. The landmark legislation she is best known for championing is the 21st Century Cures Act, widely hailed as one of the most important pieces of legislation Congress has passed in recent years. In January 2021, she was named an impeachment manager for the second impeachment trial of President Trump. Notably, Colorado's longest-serving House member is currently facing the toughest primary of her career, with a 28-year-old progressive and a University of Colorado regent both muscling their way onto the June 2026 ballot.
