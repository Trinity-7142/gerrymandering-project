Rep. Jim Costa (D) is a U.S. Representative for California's 21st congressional district. Born in Fresno, California, he is a descendant of Portuguese immigrants whose grandparents came from the Azores and established a family dairy in Fresno's Kearney Park. Costa grew up working on the family dairy and now farms almonds in the San Joaquin Valley. A centrist Democrat, he chaired the Blue Dog Coalition during the 116th Congress. As a senior member of the House Agriculture Committee, he has helped craft multiple Farm Bills and secured funding for water infrastructure projects including the B.F. Sisk Dam and Sites Reservoir. As a member of the House Democrats' Border Security Task Force, Costa supports border security measures including hiring more border patrol agents and combating fentanyl trafficking. In 2025, Costa was one of ten Democrats who joined Republicans in voting to censure Rep. Al Green for interrupting President Trump's State of the Union address, reflecting his reputation for occasional bipartisan independence.
