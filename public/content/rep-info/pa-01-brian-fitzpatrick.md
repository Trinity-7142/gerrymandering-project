Born in Philadelphia and raised in Levittown, Pennsylvania, Brian Fitzpatrick is a Republican attorney and former FBI agent who has represented Pennsylvania's First District since 2017. His district covers all of Bucks County, a mostly suburban area north of Philadelphia, as well as a portion of Montgomery County. Before entering public office, Fitzpatrick served 15 years as an FBI Special Agent and Federal Prosecutor, graduating first in his class at the FBI Academy and winning the FBI Director's Award. A moderate Republican, his platform centers on border security, combating the opioid epidemic, and government reform. In the 119th Congress, he sits on the House Ways and Means Committee and the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. Fitzpatrick was ranked the most bipartisan legislator for the fifth consecutive year in 2024 and co-chairs the bipartisan Problem Solvers Caucus. Notably, he was one of two Republicans to vote against President Trump's signature reconciliation bill and was the first Republican to sign a discharge petition to extend Affordable Care Act subsidies.
