Rep. Doris Matsui (D) was born in the Poston War Relocation Center internment camp in Arizona and grew up in Dinuba, in California's Central Valley. After growing up on a farm, she graduated from UC Berkeley and later served as Deputy Assistant to the President in the Clinton White House. She has represented Sacramento and its surrounding areas since 2005, succeeding her late husband Robert Matsui via a special election. A progressive Democrat, her priorities include health care access, infrastructure investment, climate resilience, and emerging technology policy. She authored the CHIPS for America Act, investing $52 billion in the U.S. semiconductor industry, and secured over $4 billion in federal flood-prevention investments for the Sacramento region. She serves as Ranking Member of the Communications and Technology Subcommittee on the House Energy and Commerce Committee.
