Laurel Lee is a Republican attorney and former judge representing Florida's 15th congressional district. Born at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio, she is the daughter of a U.S. Air Force officer who attained the rank of major general and a public-school teacher. Her district encompasses eastern Hillsborough County, including Thonotosassa and Plant City, as well as parts of Pasco and Polk counties. A former federal prosecutor and judge, Lee brings legal experience to public safety issues, having prosecuted complex cases involving RICO, human trafficking, and child exploitation. She previously served as Florida's 36th Secretary of State under the DeSantis Administration, where she worked to strengthen the state's elections infrastructure and cybersecurity defenses. In Congress, she focuses on job growth, limiting government overreach, border security, and national security. She was appointed as an impeachment manager in the case against DHS Secretary Alejandro Mayorkas and served on the Bipartisan Task Force on the Assassination Attempt on President Donald J. Trump. Notable legislative achievements include two bills signed into law — the REPORT Act, strengthening child sexual abuse reporting requirements, and the RISA Act, reforming foreign intelligence surveillance.
