Daniel Sachs Goldman (born February 26, 1976) is a Democratic politician and lawyer representing New York's 10th Congressional District since 2023. Born in Washington, D.C., Goldman comes from a prominent, wealthy family; his great-grandfather was Walter A. Haas, president of Levi Strauss & Co. He is among the wealthiest members of Congress, with an estimated personal net worth of up to $253 million. Goldman resides in lower Manhattan with his wife and five children. A moderate-leaning Democrat, he focuses in Congress on strengthening democratic institutions, advancing gun safety measures, and protecting reproductive rights. Before running for office, he served as lead counsel in the first impeachment of former President Donald Trump and as an Assistant United States Attorney in the Southern District of New York. During his 10-year tenure as an AUSA, Goldman focused on organized crime and white-collar prosecutions. He now serves on the House Committee on Oversight and Accountability. Goldman and NYC Comptroller Brad Lander are set as opponents in the 2026 Democratic primary for NY-10.
