Born and raised in Riverside, California, Takano comes from a Japanese American family whose grandparents and parents were sent to internment camps during World War II. He taught British literature in public schools for 23 years before entering Congress. He holds a bachelor's degree in government from Harvard (1983) and an MFA in creative writing from UC Riverside (2010). A progressive Democrat, Takano became the first openly gay person of color elected to Congress in 2012. He serves as Ranking Member of the House Committee on Veterans' Affairs and as a member of the Education and the Workforce Committee. A key legislative accomplishment is the Honoring our PACT Act, comprehensive legislation addressing toxic exposures for veterans. Takano co-chairs the Congressional LGBT Equality Caucus and is a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus and the Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus.
