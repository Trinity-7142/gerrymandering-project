Mike Ezell is a Republican U.S. Representative for Mississippi's 4th Congressional District, serving since 2023. A lifelong Pascagoula native, his father served on the police force and his mother worked as a church secretary. While serving full-time as a police officer, Ezell attended night school and earned a criminal justice degree from the University of Southern Mississippi. He previously served as sheriff of Jackson County from 2014 to 2022, focusing on eliminating corruption and modernizing law enforcement. Ezell defeated incumbent Steven Palazzo in the 2022 Republican primary runoff amid ethics concerns surrounding Palazzo's campaign finances. In Congress, he has focused on law enforcement funding, border security, and military support. He chairs the House Transportation and Infrastructure Subcommittee on Coast Guard and Maritime Transportation. Ezell has been endorsed by former President Donald Trump and signed the Americans for Tax Reform pledge.
