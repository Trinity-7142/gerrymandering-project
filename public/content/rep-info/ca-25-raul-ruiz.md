Rep. Raul Ruiz, M.D. (D), grew up in Coachella, California, where both of his parents were farmworkers. He went on to Harvard University, where he earned his Medical Degree, a Master of Public Policy, and a Master of Public Health — becoming the first Latino to earn three graduate degrees from Harvard. A center-left Democrat, he has prioritized public health, veterans' affairs, and environmental protections. He has fought to protect Medicare and Social Security and advocate for veterans. Ruiz authored the Burn Pit Registry Act to help veterans exposed to toxic substances. He also secured federal funding for environmental restoration projects at the Salton Sea to address public health and ecological concerns. He serves on the House Energy and Commerce Committee.
