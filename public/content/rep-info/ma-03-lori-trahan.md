Rep. Lori Trahan was born and raised in a working-class family in Lowell, Massachusetts. Her father was a union ironworker and her mother was a domestic worker who juggled various part-time jobs while raising four girls. The first in her family to graduate from college, Trahan earned a scholarship to play Division I volleyball at Georgetown University. A Democrat in office since 2019, she sits on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce and advocates for expanding access to affordable healthcare, addressing the addiction crisis, rebuilding infrastructure, tackling climate change, and protecting children online. She secured passage of the Leonel Rondon Pipeline Safety Act in response to the Merrimack Valley gas explosions. The House Ethics Committee launched a campaign finance investigation in 2019, but voted unanimously to dismiss it in 2020, finding no violation of House rules or laws. In 2024, Trahan was reelected unopposed with 97.5% of the vote.
