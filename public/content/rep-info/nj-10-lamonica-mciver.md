Born and raised in Newark's Central Ward, McIver grew up in modest circumstances — the oldest of four children whose mother struggled with substance abuse. A first-generation college graduate, she earned a degree in English Literature from Bloomfield College and a master's in educational leadership and policy from Seton Hall University. A member of both the Congressional Progressive Caucus and the Congressional Black Caucus, she ran on a platform addressing skyrocketing costs of food, housing, healthcare, and education, as well as climate-related flooding threatening the district. The first Black woman to represent NJ-10 and the youngest ever elected to the House from New Jersey, she won a special election following the death of Rep. Donald Payne Jr. and was sworn in on September 23, 2024. She was indicted on charges of "forcibly impeding and interfering with federal officers" during a congressional oversight visit to a Newark ICE facility, charges she has disputed as political in nature.
