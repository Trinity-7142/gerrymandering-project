Born in Bluffton, Ohio, Latta earned his B.A. at Bowling Green State University and his J.D. at the University of Toledo College of Law. He is the U.S. Representative for Ohio's 5th congressional district, currently in his 10th term having served since 2007, and is a member of the Republican Party. His father, Del Latta, represented the same district from 1959 to 1989. A conservative Republican, he serves on the House Energy and Commerce Committee and chairs its Energy Subcommittee. His legislative priorities include energy security, technology, and drug enforcement — notably sponsoring the SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 on autonomous vehicles and the SECURE Grid Act. In December 2020, Latta was one of 126 Republican House members to sign an amicus brief supporting Texas v. Pennsylvania, contesting the 2020 presidential election results. He has missed only 0.3% of roll call votes since 2007, better than the median among current representatives.
