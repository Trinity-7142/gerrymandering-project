Benjamin Lee Cline, born February 29, 1972, in Stillwater, Oklahoma, grew up in Rockbridge County, Virginia, and worked as an attorney, first serving as an Assistant Commonwealth's Attorney in Harrisonburg and Rockingham County, before opening a private practice. A conservative Republican, Cline established himself over 16 years in the Virginia House of Delegates "as a conservative who opposes abortion rights and seeks to protect gun rights," pledging to take his fiscal conservatism to Washington. He is a member of the Problem Solvers Caucus, Freedom Caucus, and Republican Study Committee. He serves on the Committees on Appropriations, Budget, Judiciary, and the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. Notably, in December 2020, Cline signed an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania* contesting the 2020 presidential election results, and on January 6, 2021, voted against certifying President-elect Biden's election.
