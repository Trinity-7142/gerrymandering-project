William Richard Keating (born September 6, 1952) is an American lawyer and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Massachusetts's 9th congressional district. Raised in Sharon, Massachusetts, Keating attended Boston College and Suffolk University Law School. A Democrat, he is a member of the New Democrat Coalition. He raised his profile advocating for criminal justice issues in both houses of the Massachusetts General Court from 1977 to 1999 before becoming District Attorney of Norfolk County, where he served three terms before being elected to Congress. In the 119th Congress, he sits on the House Armed Services Committee and Foreign Affairs Committee, with much of his work focused on domestic issues central to his district, such as the fishing industry and nuclear safety. He has emerged as a national expert on homeland security and led the Congressional investigation into the Boston Marathon attacks. He also worked to secure a new cybersecurity mission at Joint Base Cape Cod, creating local jobs and strengthening national security.
