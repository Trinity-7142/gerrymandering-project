Born in Des Moines, Iowa, and raised in Sturgis, South Dakota, by a barber father and registered-nurse mother, DesJarlais earned degrees in chemistry and psychology before obtaining his M.D. from the University of South Dakota, then moved to East Tennessee in 1993 to practice medicine as a generalist. A Tea Party-aligned conservative representing a predominantly rural district, he champions fiscal restraint, Second Amendment rights, and limiting federal overreach. His platform centers on fiscal discipline, border enforcement, tax cuts, budget balancing, repealing the Affordable Care Act, and reforming federal spending programs. A notable legislative accomplishment was authoring a 2016 law permitting military personnel to carry firearms at recruiting stations, passed in response to the Chattanooga terrorist attack. In the 119th Congress, he was named chairman of the House Armed Services Strategic Forces Subcommittee. He has faced significant controversy: transcripts surfaced of a recorded call in which he pressured a patient with whom he was having an affair to obtain an abortion, and the Tennessee Board of Medical Examiners issued a formal reprimand and fine for engaging in sexual relationships with patients. Despite these controversies, DesJarlais, first elected in 2010, is currently serving his eighth term.
