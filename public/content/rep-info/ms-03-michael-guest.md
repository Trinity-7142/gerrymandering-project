A Rankin County resident and graduate of Brandon High School, Michael Patrick Guest was born on February 4, 1970, and earned a bachelor's degree in accounting from Mississippi State University and a J.D. from the University of Mississippi School of Law. Prior to being elected to Congress in 2018, Guest served for 11 years as District Attorney for Madison and Rankin Counties. A lifelong conservative Republican, he champions limited government, free market economic principles, lower taxes, fewer regulations, Second Amendment rights, and opposition to abortion. He currently chairs the House Ethics Committee and serves on both the House Appropriations Committee and the Homeland Security Committee. He became the first Republican freshman House member to pass legislation in the 116th Congress — the Terrorist and Foreign Fighter Travel Exercise Act of 2019, signed into law by President Trump. Notable controversies include a reported violation of the STOCK Act in 2021 for failing to properly disclose stock trades and voting against certifying the 2020 electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania.
