A fifth-generation Montanan, Zinke was born in Bozeman and lives on the same Whitefish property his family has called home since the 1950s. His father was a plumber. He served as a U.S. Navy SEAL from 1986 until 2008, retiring as a commander, and was awarded the Bronze Star for his service. A conservative Republican, his legislative agenda focuses on restoring accountability to federal spending, American energy dominance, and bolstering national security. Zinke served as President Trump's Secretary of the Interior from 2017 to 2018, becoming the first SEAL and first Montanan since statehood to occupy a Cabinet position. An Inspector General's report concluded that Zinke had repeatedly violated ethical rules and then lied to investigators, leading to his resignation. In 2024, he defeated Democratic nominee Monica Tranel with 52% of the vote. On March 2, 2026, Zinke announced he would not seek re-election.
