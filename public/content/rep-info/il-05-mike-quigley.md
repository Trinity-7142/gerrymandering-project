Mike Quigley (born October 17, 1958) is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 5th congressional district since a 2009 special election. He was raised in Carol Stream, Illinois, and worked his way through college — first Roosevelt University, then the University of Chicago, and Loyola University Chicago. On the Cook County Board of Commissioners, he gained a reputation as a reformer, opposing tax hikes and government inefficiency. In Congress, he has focused on the environment, small businesses, the LGBTQ community, healthcare access, and gun violence prevention. He serves on the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence and co-chairs the Congressional Ukraine Caucus. As the highest-ranking Democrat on the Appropriations Subcommittee on Transportation, Housing, and Urban Development, he has prioritized public transit and infrastructure. Notably, in September 2023, Quigley was the sole Democrat to vote against a government funding bill, citing its lack of aid to Ukraine. In July 2024, he became one of the first prominent Democrats to publicly call on President Biden to withdraw from the presidential race.
