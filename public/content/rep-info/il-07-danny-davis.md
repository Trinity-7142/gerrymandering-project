Danny K. Davis is a Democrat who has served as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 7th Congressional District since 1997. Born in Parkdale, Arkansas, he moved to Chicago's West Side in 1961 after earning a B.A. from Arkansas A.M. & N. College, later earning a Master's from Chicago State University and a Ph.D. from the Union Institute. Before Congress, he had careers as an educator, community organizer, health administrator, and civil rights advocate. A progressive Democrat, Davis has focused on job creation, poverty, health care, education, youth, and criminal justice reform. He serves on the House Ways and Means Committee as Ranking Member of the Worker and Family Support Subcommittee. Notable accomplishments include securing over $374 million in federal aid for Predominantly Black Institutions and championing the Maternal Infant and Early Childhood Home Visiting program, which dedicated $5.2 billion to improve mother and child health. A notable controversy arose in 2004 when Davis participated in a ceremony crowning Reverend Sun Myung Moon, who declared himself the Messiah, at the Dirksen Senate Office Building. Davis announced in July 2025 that he will not seek reelection in 2026.
