Lucy McBath is a Democratic U.S. Representative serving in the House from a district in the suburbs of Atlanta, Georgia. Born in Joliet, Illinois, her father was a dentist, *Black Voice* newspaper owner, and NAACP Illinois chapter president — a civil rights upbringing that shaped her public service. In the 1990s, she became a Delta Air Lines flight attendant and relocated to Atlanta. In 2012, her 17-year-old son Jordan Davis was shot and killed at a Florida gas station, prompting national debate over stand-your-ground laws. Before running for Congress, she served as national spokesperson and Faith and Outreach Leader for Everytown for Gun Safety and Moms Demand Action. Her platform centers on gun safety measures — including universal background checks, raising the minimum purchase age to 21, and keeping firearms from domestic abusers — alongside healthcare access and education. She became the first Democrat to represent GA-06 since the district moved to Atlanta's northern suburbs in 1993. The district was later redrawn by court order as a majority-Black district spanning parts of Cobb, Douglas, Fayette, and Fulton counties. McBath has been the primary sponsor of 8 enacted bills.
