Raised in South Orange County by a Mexican-American mother and a Jewish-American father, Democrat Mike Levin attended Duke University School of Law and worked as an environmental and energy regulatory attorney before entering politics. His top priorities include combating climate change, protecting natural resources, and leading legislation to transition the U.S. to zero-emission vehicles and ban new offshore drilling along Southern California's coast. A strong veterans' advocate, the Veterans of Foreign Wars awarded Levin its Congressional Award for Legislator of the Year in 2024. Since taking office in 2019, he has secured over $1 billion in federal funding for the district, and the nonpartisan Center for Effective Lawmaking rates him among the top five percent of House members. Notably, in July 2024, Levin was among approximately 20 congressional Democrats to publicly call for President Biden to withdraw from the presidential race.
