Born in Rahway, New Jersey, and raised in Iselin, Rep. Chris Smith earned a B.A. in business administration from Trenton State College before joining his family's sporting goods company. He is a member of the Republican Party, having switched from the Democratic Party in 1978. As of 2025, Smith is tied for the longest-currently-serving member of the House of Representatives. A staunch social conservative, Smith has made his mark as a co-chairman of the Congressional Pro-Life Caucus, and his platform has emphasized border security, fighting inflation, crime, parental rights, and American energy independence. He has focused much of his career on promoting human rights abroad, including authoring the Victims of Trafficking and Violence Protection Act of 2000. His Autism CARES Act of 2024 will provide more than $1.95 billion over five years. As chairman of the Veterans' Affairs Committee, Smith broke with his party over veterans' benefits funding, leading Republicans to strip him of the chairmanship in 2005.
