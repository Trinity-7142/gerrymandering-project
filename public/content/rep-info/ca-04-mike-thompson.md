Born and raised in St. Helena, California, in a working-class family and the grandson of Italian immigrants, Democrat Mike Thompson has represented Northern California in Congress since 1999. He dropped out of high school to enlist in the U.S. Army, serving in combat during the Vietnam War as a staff sergeant and platoon leader with the 173rd Airborne Brigade, where he was wounded in action and awarded the Purple Heart. He later returned to school using the GI Bill and became the first in his family to earn a college degree, graduating from California State University, Chico. Thompson is a member of the conservative Blue Dog Coalition. His platform centers on lowering costs for families, ensuring corporations pay their fair share in taxes, gun violence prevention, clean energy, and health care. He serves on the House Committee on Ways and Means as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Tax and as a senior member of the Health Subcommittee. Notably, following the Newtown school shooting, Thompson was appointed Chair of the House Gun Violence Prevention Task Force; he is himself a hunter and gun owner who supports the Second Amendment while advocating for universal background checks. His GREEN Act was signed into law as part of the Inflation Reduction Act, using the tax code to expand clean energy incentives.
