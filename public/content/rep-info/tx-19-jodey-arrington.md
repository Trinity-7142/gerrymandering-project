Rep. Jodey Arrington was raised in Plainview, Texas, the son of a tractor salesman and a schoolteacher. A conservative Republican, he is a graduate of Texas Tech University whose career spanned the Office of the Texas Governor, the White House, the FDIC, and later the presidency of a healthcare innovation company and the Vice Chancellorship of the Texas Tech University System. In January 2017, he became only the fifth person to represent Texas' 19th Congressional District since its formation in 1935. His platform has centered on fiscal restraint, agriculture, energy, and tax reduction — he has served on the House Ways and Means Committee since 2019, prioritizing rural America's critical issues of agriculture, energy, and healthcare. At the beginning of the 118th Congress, he was elected Chairman of the House Budget Committee. As a member of Congress, Arrington sponsored the One Big Beautiful Bill Act during the 119th Congress, which he introduced May 20, 2025, and was signed into law July 4, 2025. On November 11, 2025, Arrington announced he would not run for re-election in 2026.
