Bradley Scott Schneider is a Democratic businessman and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 10th congressional district. Born in Denver, Colorado, he earned a B.S. in industrial engineering from Northwestern University and an MBA from Kellogg, then worked at a kibbutz in Israel before returning to Chicago. He worked as a management consultant and industrial engineer in Deerfield, Illinois prior to entering politics. Schneider has campaigned as a moderate Democrat and described himself as "pragmatic and a moderate." He serves on the House Ways and Means and Foreign Affairs Committees, earning distinction for leadership on international tax, healthcare innovation, and the SALT deduction cap. He is co-founder and co-chair of the bipartisan Abraham Accords Caucus. He has been a noted gun safety advocate, helping pass the first major gun legislation in 30 years, including background checks and red flag laws. In 2014, Schneider changed his financial filing status in a manner that prevented disclosure of his wife's income.
