Jared Golden is a Democratic U.S. Representative for Maine's 2nd congressional district, serving since 2019. He grew up in Leeds, a small town in Androscoggin County. A Marine Corps veteran, he served two combat tours in Iraq and Afghanistan, rising to corporal and receiving the Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal. He later earned a history and politics degree from Bates College and worked on the staff of Republican Senator Susan Collins. Ideologically, Golden is a conservative Blue Dog Democrat who has advocated what he calls "progressive conservatism." His platform centers on defending the Affordable Care Act, lowering prescription drug costs, and supporting labor, believing stronger unions build a stronger middle class. He has been notably effective at securing district funding, bringing in more Community Project Funding than any other House Democrat in FY 2024. In November 2025, Golden announced he would not seek re-election in 2026.
