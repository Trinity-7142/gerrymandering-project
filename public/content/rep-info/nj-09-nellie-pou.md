Rep. Nellie Pou was born and raised in a blue-collar family in Paterson, New Jersey, where her father worked for the city and her mother was a homemaker. A Democrat, she was sworn into the U.S. House of Representatives on January 3, 2025, making history as the first Latina from New Jersey to serve in Congress. She was selected to replace Rep. Bill Pascrell on the ballot following his death in August 2024. Her platform centers on tackling an affordability crisis by fighting to bring down the costs of housing, groceries, child care, and more for working families, alongside expanding healthcare access and protecting the ACA. In Congress, she holds seats on the House Transportation and Infrastructure Committee and the House Homeland Security Committee. As Ranking Member of the Task Force overseeing World Cup security, her advocacy was instrumental in forcing DHS to release withheld federal security funding. She faces attacks from both progressives — over a lobbying-group-funded trip to Israel — and Republicans over her opposition to their immigration and spending policies.
