Born in Daly City, California, Mullin is the son of Gene Mullin, who served three terms in the California State Assembly. His career experience includes founding KM2 Communications, and his elected experience began on the South San Francisco City Council from 2007–2012, serving as Mayor in 2011. He then represented San Mateo County in the California State Assembly from 2012–2022, serving as the longest-tenured Speaker pro Tempore of the modern era. In 2022, Mullin was elected to represent California's 15th Congressional District in the U.S. House of Representatives. A Democrat, he has focused on addressing the high cost of living, climate change, democratic institutions, California's innovation economy, and U.S. leadership in medical research. After two years in Congress, House Democratic Leadership appointed him to the influential House Energy & Commerce Committee. Prior to Congress, he was the architect of a $3.7 billion climate resilience package and was instrumental in passing the largest climate investment in California's history, totaling $15 billion. Notably, Mullin left a California hospital bed with an IV attached in February 2025 to fly to Washington for an emergency budget vote.
