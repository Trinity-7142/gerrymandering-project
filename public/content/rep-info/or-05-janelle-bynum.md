Raised in Washington, D.C., Janelle Bynum is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Oregon's 5th congressional district since 2025. The daughter of teachers, she learned the importance of community service at a young age. She earned scholarships to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and an MBA. She later relocated to Clackamas County, Oregon, to help run a McDonald's franchise. Her platform centers on creating good-paying jobs, strengthening public education, and lowering costs for healthcare, prescription drugs, and housing. She was appointed to the House Financial Services Committee, becoming the first Oregon freshman named to the committee in 28 years. She is the first Black member of Congress elected in Oregon. She defeated incumbent Republican Lori Chavez-DeRemer in a close 2024 race.
