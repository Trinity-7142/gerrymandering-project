Born in Columbus, Georgia, and raised in Smiths Station, Alabama, Williams was raised by her grandparents and went on to become a progressive Democratic legislator now serving Atlanta's GA-05. Her career included serving as vice president of Public Policy at Planned Parenthood Southeast and deputy director of civic engagement at the National Domestic Workers Alliance. After the death of John Lewis in 2020, Williams was selected by the Democratic Party of Georgia Executive Committee to replace him on the November ballot. In Congress, she serves on the House Financial Services Committee, championing affordable housing, working to close the racial wealth gap, and fighting for a financial system that prioritizes working families. Her legislative priorities include voting rights, reproductive justice, social justice, economic justice, and healthcare. She has introduced measures such as the Tech to Save Moms Act, bipartisan legislation to address racial disparities in maternal health care, and the OHH SNAP Act to combat food insecurity among college students. Notably, Williams was arrested in 2018 during a protest at the Georgia State Capitol over the handling of the gubernatorial election; charges were later dropped. She also faced criticism from within her party, including from Senator Jon Ossoff, over the Democratic Party of Georgia's performances in the 2022 and 2024 state elections, ultimately resigning as state party chair on March 31, 2025.
