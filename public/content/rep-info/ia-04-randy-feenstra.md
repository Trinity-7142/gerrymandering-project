Randy Feenstra (born January 14, 1969) is a Republican politician and businessman born and raised in Hull, Iowa. Before Congress, he served as Hull City Administrator, Sioux County Treasurer, and Chairman of the Iowa Senate Ways and Means Committee, where he authored the largest income tax reduction in Iowa state history, signed into law in 2017. He serves on the House Ways and Means Committee and the House Agriculture Committee. He champions fiscal responsibility, border security, opposition to abortion, and the interests of Iowa's family farmers and biofuels industry. Feenstra won his seat by defeating nine-term incumbent Steve King in the 2020 Republican primary. His legislative focus has centered primarily on agriculture, taxation, and science and technology. A notable controversy arose when, as Feenstra sought support for his gubernatorial campaign, he spent nearly $33,000 from his Congressional office budget on radio advertising. Feenstra is retiring from Congress in 2026 to run for Governor of Iowa.
