Raised by a single mother in modest circumstances, McCormick worked as a paperboy and put himself through college at Oregon State University. He is an American physician and Republican member of the U.S. House of Representatives. McCormick served in the U.S. Marine Corps and Navy for over 20 years, with deployments across Africa, South Korea, Afghanistan, and the Persian Gulf. In Congress, he serves on the House Armed Services, House Oversight, and Science, Space, and Technology Committees. His platform centers on jobs, healthcare, and protecting the Second Amendment. He cosponsored legislation recognizing personhood as beginning at conception and authored a resolution censuring Rep. Rashida Tlaib for statements related to the October 7, 2023 Hamas attack on Israel. Notably, fellow Georgia Republican Marjorie Taylor Greene accused McCormick of a physical altercation; an investigation by the Speaker found her allegations could not be corroborated. McCormick's nearly 30-point victory margin in 2024 confirmed that the redrawn GA-07 is no longer a battleground.
