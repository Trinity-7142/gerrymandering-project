A native Tucsonan and granddaughter of a bracero who immigrated from Mexico, Grijalva is the daughter of former U.S. Representative Raúl Grijalva, who represented the district from 2003 until his death in 2025. A progressive Democrat, she previously served on the Pima County Board of Supervisors and on the Tucson Unified School District Governing Board from 2002 to 2022. She was sworn into the House in 2025, becoming the first Latina ever to represent the State of Arizona. Her platform centers on expanding public education, putting more money in working families' pockets, and defending workers' right to unionize. She also champions environmental justice and a green jobs revolution in renewable industries. Notably, her swearing-in was delayed by 50 days, setting a record for any member entering the House after a special election, after Speaker Mike Johnson refused to swear her in. In December 2025, she reported being pepper-sprayed by an ICE agent during a raid in Tucson while identifying herself as a Member of Congress.
