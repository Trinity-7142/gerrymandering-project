Born in Troy, Ohio and raised in Champaign County, Jim Jordan is a Republican congressman first elected in 2006. A two-time NCAA Division I wrestling champion at the University of Wisconsin, he won the 1985 and 1986 championships. Currently in his 10th term in the House, Jordan is a staunch fiscal and social conservative who champions limited government, reduced federal spending, and strict immigration enforcement. He co-founded the right-wing populist House Freedom Caucus and served as its first chair from 2015 to 2017. He currently chairs the House Judiciary Committee. Controversies include an Ohio State University investigation into sexual misconduct by a team physician during Jordan's time as an assistant wrestling coach, which concluded the physician had abused 177 student-patients — Jordan denied knowledge of the abuse — and defying a subpoena to testify before the January 6th Committee in 2022.
