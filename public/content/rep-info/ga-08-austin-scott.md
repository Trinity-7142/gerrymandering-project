Austin Scott (born December 10, 1969) is the U.S. Representative for Georgia's 8th Congressional District, serving since 2011. Born in Augusta, Georgia, he graduated from the University of Georgia with a B.B.A. in Risk Management and Insurance and owned and operated an insurance brokerage firm for nearly twenty years. A conservative Republican, he supports limited government, small business job growth, a strong national defense, and a healthy agricultural economy. Now in his eighth term, Scott serves as Vice Chairman of the House Agriculture Committee and chairs its Subcommittee on General Farm Commodities, Risk Management, and Credit. His district is home to two U.S. Air Force bases: Moody and Robins. Notably, in 2001 Scott was the first Republican in the Georgia House to work with Democrats to remove the Confederate battle emblem from the state's flag. In 2023, Scott entered the House Speaker election, facing Jim Jordan, and was considered a close ally of Majority Leader Steve Scalise, though he was ultimately defeated, garnering 81 votes to Jordan's 124.
