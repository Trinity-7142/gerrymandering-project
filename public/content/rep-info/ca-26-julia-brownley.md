Julia Brownley (born August 28, 1952) is a Democrat representing California's 26th Congressional District, with a hometown of Westlake Village. She holds a bachelor's degree in political science from Mount Vernon College and an MBA from American University. Before entering politics, she worked in marketing and sales. Her platform focuses on expanding opportunities for middle-class families, lowering healthcare costs, affordable higher education, environmental protection, and veterans' services. Her most notable legislative achievements include the Female Veterans Suicide Prevention Act (2016) and the Deborah Sampson Act (2021), the most comprehensive legislation for women veterans in over a decade. In January 2026, Brownley announced she would not seek reelection and will retire in 2027.
