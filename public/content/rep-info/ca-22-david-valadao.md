Born and raised in Hanford, California, to Portuguese immigrant parents, David Valadao is a Republican politician and dairy farmer representing California's 22nd congressional district, which comprises part of the San Joaquin Valley. Deeply involved in the dairy industry, he has held leadership roles with the California Milk Advisory Board, the Western States Dairy Trade Association, and as Regional Leadership Council Chairman for Land O' Lakes, Inc. He campaigns on providing clean water to the district, supporting farmers and ranchers, and protecting veteran services. As a member of the House Appropriations Committee, he secured $55 million in direct funding for Central Valley community improvement projects. A moderate Republican, Valadao was one of ten Republicans who voted to impeach Donald Trump during Trump's second impeachment, and one of just two of that group to be renominated and reelected. In 2025, he withheld support for a House budget resolution over Medicaid cuts, and was one of eight House Republicans to publicly push Speaker Mike Johnson to protect the program.
