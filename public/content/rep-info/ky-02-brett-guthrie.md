Born in Florence, Alabama, Guthrie resides in Bowling Green, Kentucky. A former manufacturing executive and U.S. Army veteran, he earned a B.S. in mathematical economics from West Point and a master's in Public and Private Management from Yale. Following his military service, he joined a Bowling Green-based manufacturing business started by his father. A conservative Republican, he has served in Congress since 2009. In December 2024, Guthrie was elected to chair the House Energy and Commerce Committee in the 119th Congress. He has expressed commitment to restoring U.S. energy dominance, ensuring online safety for children, and reducing healthcare costs. He played a key role in authoring the SUPPORT for Patients and Communities Act to combat the opioid crisis.
