Seth Moulton is a Democratic U.S. Representative for Massachusetts's 6th congressional district — covering many of Boston's northern suburbs, including his hometown of Salem — serving since 2015. A Harvard-educated physicist, he joined the Marine Corps after graduation, serving four tours in Iraq before earning joint graduate degrees in business and public administration. Regarded as a moderate Democrat, Moulton is a member of the New Democrat Coalition and has urged bipartisanship and compromise. He sits on the House Armed Services, Transportation and Infrastructure, and Select Committee on Strategic Competition with China. He is Congress's leading advocate on high-speed rail. Following the 2024 election, Moulton sparked significant controversy within his party by publicly opposing transgender participation in girls' sports, leading to his campaign manager's resignation and constituent protests. In October 2025, he announced a primary challenge against incumbent U.S. Senator Ed Markey.
