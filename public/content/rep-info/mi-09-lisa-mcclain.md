Born and raised in Stockbridge, Michigan, McClain earned her Bachelor of Business Administration from Northwood University after attending Lansing Community College. Prior to serving in Congress, she spent more than 30 years building a career in the private sector, experience she draws on to advocate for her constituents. A conservative Republican, her policy focus spans agriculture, economics, financial services, national defense, immigration, healthcare, and veterans affairs. After serving as Secretary of the House Republican Conference in the 118th Congress, she was elected by her colleagues as Chairwoman of the Conference for the 119th Congress. Notable legislative achievements include co-sponsoring the Patient Advocate Tracker Act, signed into law in 2022, and sponsoring a bill awarding the Congressional Gold Medal to 13 service members killed during the Afghanistan withdrawal, signed into law in 2021. Controversies include falsely claiming at a 2022 Trump rally that Trump had "caught Osama bin Laden," and twice being late to report between $360,000 and $900,000 in stock trades made by her husband, violating the STOCK Act.
