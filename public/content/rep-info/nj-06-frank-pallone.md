Born on October 30, 1951, in Long Branch, New Jersey, Frank Pallone began his public service as an attorney advocating for coastal communities. He holds degrees from Middlebury College, the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University, and Rutgers School of Law–Camden. A liberal Democrat and member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, Pallone is serving his 18th full term, representing a district covering most of Middlesex County and the Bayshore and oceanfront areas of Monmouth County. He is the top Democrat on the House Energy and Commerce Committee. His platform centers on environmental protection, affordable health care, and strengthening the middle class. Key accomplishments include playing a central role in authoring and passing the Affordable Care Act and leading passage of the Inflation Reduction Act during the 117th Congress. He has also led efforts to ban offshore drilling and ocean dumping to protect New Jersey's coastline. Notably, in 2024 Pallone was one of 16 Democratic representatives to vote to undo President Biden's pause of some weapons shipments to Israel, drawing attention as a divergence from many in his party.
