Russell Fry was born in Surfside Beach, South Carolina, and lives in Murrells Inlet. His parents taught him from an early age the value of hard work; growing up, his family lived in a cinderblock home with no heat or air-conditioning as his parents struggled from paycheck to paycheck. He earned a B.A. from the University of South Carolina in political science in 2007 and a J.D. from Charleston School of Law in 2011, later working as an attorney with Coastal Law, LLC. A Republican, Fry has served as U.S. representative for South Carolina's 7th congressional district since 2023. His conservative platform includes support for unrestricted gun ownership, stricter immigration policies, and expanded school choice. He has cited the economy and border security as his biggest priorities. Fry launched his congressional bid in 2021 challenging incumbent Tom Rice over Rice's vote to impeach President Trump; Trump endorsed Fry, and Fry defeated Rice by 26.6 percentage points in the Republican primary. He was elected president of the congressional freshman class during orientation week. He currently serves on the House Committees on Energy and Commerce and the Judiciary.
