Born in India and raised across India, Indonesia, and Singapore, Jayapal came to the United States alone at age 16 to attend college. She earned a bachelor's degree from Georgetown University and an MBA from Northwestern University, becoming a U.S. citizen in 2000. Before entering elected office, she spent twenty years in global public health and as a national advocate for immigrant and civil rights, including founding OneAmerica, the largest immigrant advocacy organization in Washington State. She became the first Indian American woman to serve in the U.S. House of Representatives and chaired the Congressional Progressive Caucus from 2021 to 2025. A prominent progressive Democrat, her platform centers on Medicare for All, the College for All Act, the Ultra-Millionaire Tax, and protections for domestic workers and detained immigrants. A notable controversy arose in July 2023 when she described Israel as a "racist state" before later apologizing and redirecting criticism toward the Netanyahu government.
