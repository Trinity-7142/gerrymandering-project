Greg Stanton grew up in west Phoenix and is the son of a teacher and a shoe salesman. He graduated from Marquette University on a Harry S. Truman Scholarship and earned a law degree from the University of Michigan. A Democrat, he previously served as Mayor of Phoenix from 2012 to 2018 and on the Phoenix City Council from 2000 to 2009. He was first elected to Congress in 2018 and re-elected in 2020, 2022, and 2024. Stanton is a member of the New Democrat Coalition. In Congress, he has focused on Arizona's water future, investing in transit, lowering costs for working families, and building out the state's 21st-century economy. In 2017, *Governing* magazine named him one of its Public Officials of the Year for expanding light rail and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. He has also championed bipartisan legislation to address drought and secure Colorado River water resources for Arizona.
