A lifelong Central New Yorker and grandchild of Irish immigrants, Mannion was raised on Syracuse's Tipperary Hill by a telephone company worker and a railroad employee. He spent nearly three decades teaching high school science and served eight years as the West Genesee Teachers' Association president, representing 400 teachers and nurses. A Democrat, Mannion became the first Democrat to win the Syracuse-based district since 2012 by defeating incumbent Republican Brandon Williams in the 2024 election. His campaign platform centered on reproductive rights, protecting Social Security and Medicare, improving schools, and lowering costs for working families. As a state senator, he championed the Green Chips legislation that helped attract Micron's $100 billion chip fabrication investment to Clay, NY. During the campaign, three former staffers accused Mannion of creating a hostile work environment, but after an investigation he was cleared of any wrongdoing.
