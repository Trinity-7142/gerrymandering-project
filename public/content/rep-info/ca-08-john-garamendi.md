John Garamendi was raised on his family's ranch in Mokelumne Hill, California. He earned a B.A. in business from UC Berkeley and an MBA from Harvard Business School, then served in the Peace Corps in Ethiopia from 1966 to 1968. A progressive Democrat, Garamendi is focused on growing the middle class and is a leading voice in Congress for "Make It In America" policies to bring manufacturing jobs back to America. He is resolutely opposed to efforts to privatize Social Security, dismantle Medicare, and defund Medicaid. He previously served as California's insurance commissioner, U.S. Deputy Secretary of the Interior, and the state's 46th lieutenant governor before being first elected to the House in a special election in 2009. He sits on the Armed Services and Transportation and Infrastructure Committees. In July 2024, Garamendi announced a cancer diagnosis and said he planned to continue working while receiving treatment.
