Young Oak Kim is a South Korean-born American politician and businesswoman serving as the U.S. Representative for California's 40th congressional district. Born in 1962 in Incheon, South Korea, she immigrated to the U.S. in 1975, living first in Guam and then Hawaii. She holds a bachelor's degree in business administration from USC, and went on to work as a financial analyst and clothing business owner. A Republican, Kim was among the first Korean-American women elected to Congress in 2020. She serves on the House Financial Services and Foreign Affairs Committees, chairing the Subcommittee on East Asia and the Pacific, and on the Select Committee on Strategic Competition with China. Her campaign platform centers on law and order, border security, national security, and protecting economic opportunity. She is consistently ranked among the most bipartisan and effective members of Congress, with nearly 40 bills passed through the House and over a dozen signed into law. She received the 2024 Newsweek-Stubblefield Institute Civility Award for her commitment to bipartisanship.
