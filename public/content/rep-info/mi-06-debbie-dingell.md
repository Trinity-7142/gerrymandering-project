Debbie Dingell (born 1953) is a Democratic U.S. Representative from Michigan, serving the state's 6th congressional district since 2023. Before Congress, she spent over three decades in the auto industry, serving as President of the GM Foundation and a senior public affairs executive. A center-left Democrat, she co-chairs the Medicare for All Caucus and champions domestic manufacturing, particularly the automotive sector in Southeast Michigan. As Co-Chair of the Great Lakes Task Force and the PFAS Task Force, she has secured funding for the Great Lakes Restoration Initiative and led efforts to combat PFAS chemical contamination. When first sworn in, she became the first non-widowed woman in Congress to succeed her husband — the late Rep. John Dingell, the longest-serving member of Congress in U.S. history. After she voted to impeach President Trump in 2019, Trump mocked her late husband at a rally; members of both parties came to her defense, and Dingell called for a return to civility.
