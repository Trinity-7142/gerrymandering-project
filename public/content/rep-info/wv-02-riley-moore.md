Riley McGowan Moore (born July 1, 1980) is a Republican serving as the U.S. Representative for West Virginia's 2nd congressional district since 2025. Born in Morgantown, he began his career as a welder in a mining operation before earning a bachelor's degree in government from George Mason University and a master's degree in strategic security studies from the National Defense University. A conservative, his platform centers on energy, immigration, and fiscal issues. As State Treasurer, he fought ESG policies in financial services and was the first elected official in America to divest West Virginia tax dollars from BlackRock over their support for ESG. He is the only freshman member in the House or Senate to serve on the Appropriations Committee. In 2025, he introduced a bill to ban student visas for Chinese citizens, which various groups labeled Sinophobic. His grandfather, Arch A. Moore Jr., was a former governor of West Virginia, and his aunt, Shelley Moore Capito, is also a politician.
