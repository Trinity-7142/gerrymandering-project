Raised among the Palouse wheat fields in Colton and Pullman, Washington, the son of a public-school teacher and a WSU forestry professor, Republican Michael Baumgartner is currently serving his first term in Congress as the Representative of the 5th district of Washington. He earned a bachelor's degree in economics from Washington State University and a Master's of Public Administration in international development from Harvard University. He served as a U.S. State Department officer at the U.S. Embassy in Baghdad, Iraq, and later worked as a counternarcotics advisor in Afghanistan. In Congress, his platform centers on securing the border, reducing the cost of living, and ensuring America's strength. He serves on the Foreign Affairs, Judiciary, and Education and Workforce Committees. Notably, he won the 2024 general election with over 60% of the vote, outperforming his predecessor Cathy McMorris Rodgers' 2022 result despite not being an incumbent.
