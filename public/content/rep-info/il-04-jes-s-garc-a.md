Born in Los Pinos, a small village in Durango, Mexico, García is the youngest of four children; his father worked under the WWII-era bracero program before settling in Chicago, and the family immigrated with permanent resident status in 1965. The family settled in Chicago's Little Village neighborhood, where García continues to reside. In 2019, he became the first Mexican American from the Midwest to serve in Congress. Throughout his career he has been described as a progressive. He is a coalition builder committed to expanding access to quality education, affordable housing, and economic opportunity. Key priorities include immigration reform and DACA protections — he led 86 House members in urging DHS to reduce delays in processing DACA renewal requests — and he has delivered billions in infrastructure projects for Illinois as a senior member of the Transportation Committee. As Cook County Commissioner, he raised the minimum wage, mandated paid sick leave, and passed an ordinance ending the county's cooperation with ICE — the first of its kind nationally, later followed by over 250 localities. He announced in November 2025 he would not seek reelection, timing the announcement after his chief of staff filed to run, effectively clearing the primary field — a move widely criticized as an attempt to engineer his own succession.
