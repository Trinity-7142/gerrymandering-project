Born and raised in the federal housing project of Mayberry Village in East Hartford, Larson describes himself as a product of public housing, public education, and public service. A lifelong Democrat, he graduated from East Hartford High School and Central Connecticut State University. First elected to Congress in 1998, he now serves in his fourteenth term on the House Ways and Means Committee, including as Ranking Member of the Social Security Subcommittee. His platform centers on protecting Social Security, climate action, and working-family economic relief. He co-introduced the Green New Deal and helped write the Inflation Reduction Act. In the Connecticut State Senate, he authored the nation's first Family and Medical Leave Act. In 2025, Larson rejected calls to step aside amid "generational change" concerns after suffering a complex partial seizure on the House floor.
