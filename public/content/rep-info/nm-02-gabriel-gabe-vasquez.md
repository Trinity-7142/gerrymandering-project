Rep. Gabe Vasquez is a first-generation Mexican-American from Las Cruces who grew up in the borderlands. Raised in the El Paso–Juárez–Las Cruces region and spending part of his childhood in Ciudad Juárez, Mexico, he was the first in his family to be born in the United States. A Democrat, he expanded his 2024 victory margin to over 4% and now serves his second term fighting for economic opportunity, reproductive healthcare access, and affordable healthcare. He is a member of the New Democrat Coalition and the Congressional Hispanic Caucus. He secured passage of the Secure Rural Schools Reauthorization Act of 2025, restoring $9 million in annual federal support for rural New Mexico counties. In December 2025, NBC News and The New York Times reported that Vasquez voted against a bill to reopen the federal government but then touted the money the bill delivered to his district.
