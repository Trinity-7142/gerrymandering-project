Born in Houston, Texas, to Tamil physician parents who emigrated from India, Subramanyam now lives in Ashburn, Virginia. A Democrat, he served as a White House technology policy advisor under President Obama, leading a task force on job creation, IT modernization, and emerging technology regulation. He became the first South Asian American elected to the Virginia General Assembly in 2019. He made history again as Virginia's first Indian-American congressman, narrowly defeating Republican Mike Clancy by 4.6% in 2024. His platform centers on healthcare affordability, education, transportation, and protecting federal workers. In the state legislature, he passed landmark legislation to lower the costs of tolls, utility bills, and prescription drugs. In Congress, he introduced the LEASH DOGE Act to increase transparency and oversight of the Department of Government Efficiency.
