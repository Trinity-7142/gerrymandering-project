Rep. Yassamin Ansari was sworn into the U.S. House of Representatives in January 2025, making history as the youngest woman in the 119th Congress and the first Iranian American Democrat. A proud daughter of immigrants and a Stanford University graduate, she was later selected for the John Gardner Fellowship and served as a senior policy advisor to two UN Secretaries-General, working on the Paris Agreement. A progressive Democrat, she has advocated for climate action, labor unions, and LGBTQ rights. Her platform centers on housing affordability, abortion protections, climate action, and building an economy that lifts the middle class. While serving on the Phoenix City Council, she created an Office of Heat Response and Mitigation. She was elected Democratic freshman class president in November 2024. Notably, she had been endorsed by a pro-Israel lobbying group and opposed a Senate resolution that would have blocked arms sales to Israel during the Gaza conflict — a position that drew attention given her otherwise progressive profile.
