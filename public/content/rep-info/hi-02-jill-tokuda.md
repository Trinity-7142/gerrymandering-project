Born and raised in Kāneʻohe, Hawaiʻi, Tokuda is a fourth-generation Japanese American with Okinawan heritage and was the first in her family to attend college. A Democrat aligned with the progressive wing of her party, she ran on a platform focused on improving healthcare access — especially for rural communities — affordable housing, and gun safety. From 2006 to 2018, she represented Kāneʻohe and Kailua in the Hawaiʻi State Senate, chairing the Ways and Means Committee and managing the state's $14 billion budget. In Congress, she serves on the House Committee on Armed Services, the Select Committee on the Chinese Communist Party, and as Ranking Member on the Subcommittee on Conservation, Research, and Biotechnology on the Committee on Agriculture. Following the 2023 Hawaiʻi wildfires that devastated Lahaina, she introduced the MAUI STRONG Act and the Natural Disaster Tax Relief Act to support affected residents and businesses. In her first year in Congress, she helped secure over $134 million in direct funding for community projects across the district.
