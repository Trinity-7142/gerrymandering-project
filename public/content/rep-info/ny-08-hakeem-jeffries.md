Born and raised in Brooklyn's Crown Heights neighborhood, Jeffries attended Binghamton University, Georgetown University for a Master of Public Policy, and NYU School of Law. He graduated with honors and worked as a corporate lawyer before entering public life. A mainstream Democrat, Jeffries has championed safer communities, affordable housing, and lower costs. He advocates for reducing child care, health care, education, and prescription drug costs. Unanimously elected House Democratic Leader in January 2023, he became the first person of color to lead a major party in Congress. He played a leading role in passing the Bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act and the Inflation Reduction Act. A past controversy: while at Binghamton University in 1992, he wrote an essay defending his uncle, Leonard Jeffries, who had made antisemitic comments; his spokesperson has stated he "does not share the controversial views espoused by his uncle."
