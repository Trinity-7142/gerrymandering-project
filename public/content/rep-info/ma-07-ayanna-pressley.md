Born in Cincinnati, Ohio, and raised in Chicago, Illinois, Ayanna Pressley is the first woman of color elected to Congress from the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. She left Boston University to work full-time to support her mother, later rising through political ranks as a staffer for Rep. Joseph P. Kennedy II and a political director for Sen. John Kerry. She was also the first Black woman elected to the Boston City Council. A member of the progressive "Squad," her positions align with the progressive wing of the Democratic Party, focusing on racial and economic justice, healthcare reform, and addressing systemic inequities. She has championed reproductive justice, immigrant justice, consumer protections, and justice for survivors of sexual violence. Notable recent efforts include leading a House-passed measure to extend Temporary Protected Status for Haiti. She drew controversy after calling eviction an act of violence, despite appearing to profit from rental income.
