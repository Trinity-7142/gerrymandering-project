Robert Brown Aderholt is an American politician and attorney from Alabama. Born in Haleyville, Alabama, his father was a part-time minister and circuit judge for more than 30 years. He graduated from Birmingham-Southern College with degrees in history and political science, then earned his J.D. from Cumberland School of Law at Samford University. A socially conservative Republican and member of the Tea Party Caucus, he has represented Alabama's 4th congressional district since 1997. He supports pro-growth initiatives that create jobs, strong immigration standards, and robust national security. In the 119th Congress, he chairs the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor, Health & Human Services, and Education — responsible for the largest non-defense segment of the federal budget. Aderholt has used his position on the House Appropriations Committee to secure millions in federal funds for infrastructure, industries, and institutions within his district. Notably, he was reported to have received money from FTX, the defunct cryptocurrency exchange; his office did not respond to inquiries about what was done with the funds.
