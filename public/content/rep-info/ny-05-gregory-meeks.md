Gregory Weldon Meeks (born September 25, 1953) is an American lawyer and politician who has been a U.S. representative from New York since 1998. His family settled in East Harlem after migrating from South Carolina, and he grew up in a public housing project. A Democrat aligned with the pro-growth New Democrat Coalition, his platform centers on economic development, affordable housing, and multilateral foreign policy. He was the first Black Member of Congress to chair the House Foreign Affairs Committee and currently serves as its Ranking Member. He secured $21 million in federal tax credits for affordable housing in Jamaica, Queens. Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington named him one of the most corrupt members of Congress in 2013, citing claims about a below-market home purchase and an undisclosed private loan.
