Donald Sternoff Beyer Jr. is a Democratic politician, businessman, and diplomat serving as the U.S. Representative for Virginia's 8th congressional district since 2015. Born in the Free Territory of Trieste, the son of a U.S. Army officer, he was raised in Washington, D.C. After college, Beyer built the family automobile dealership business, expanding it to nine franchises across Northern Virginia. A center-left Democrat, his key passions include climate change, fusion energy, and carbon pricing. He serves on the House Committee on Ways and Means and the Joint Economic Committee. He is founder of the bipartisan Fusion Energy Caucus and co-chair of the bipartisan Artificial Intelligence Caucus. He previously served as Lieutenant Governor of Virginia from 1990 to 1998 and as Ambassador to Switzerland and Liechtenstein under President Obama. Notably, in 2022 he enrolled at George Mason University to pursue a master's degree in computer science with a concentration in machine learning.
