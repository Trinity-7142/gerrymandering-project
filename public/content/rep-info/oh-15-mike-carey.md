Rep. Mike Carey (born March 13, 1971) is a Republican politician and former coal lobbyist serving as the U.S. Representative for Ohio's 15th congressional district. Raised in Sabina, Ohio, he earned degrees in economics from Marion Military Institute and history from Ohio State University. He served in the Army National Guard from 1989 to 1999. Prior to his election, Carey worked as vice president of government affairs for American Consolidated Natural Resources, a coal company, and served as chairman of the board of the Ohio Coal Association. A conservative Republican, Carey was first elected in a 2021 special election with endorsements from then-former President Donald Trump and former Vice President Mike Pence. He champions American energy independence, veterans' benefits, and law enforcement support, and currently serves on the House Ways and Means Committee and the Committee on House Administration. Following the 2024 election, Carey was considered a "top contender" to fill the U.S. Senate seat vacated by Vice President-elect J.D. Vance.
