Born and raised in Indianapolis, Jefferson Shreve is a Republican politician and businessman serving as U.S. Representative for Indiana's 6th congressional district since 2025. He considers himself a moderate Republican. Before Congress, Shreve founded Storage Express, a self-storage company he grew over three decades before merging it into a NYSE-traded national firm. His legislative priorities center on economic development, reining in federal spending, border security, and conservative Hoosier values. He sits on the Transportation & Infrastructure and Foreign Affairs Committees. In his first year, he secured the second-highest federal funding total among Indiana's House delegation. Notably, Shreve ran for Indianapolis mayor in 2023, losing to the incumbent, and during that campaign called for banning assault weapon sales and repealing permitless carry — positions he later revised for his congressional run.
