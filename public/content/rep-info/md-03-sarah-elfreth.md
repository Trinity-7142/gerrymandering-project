Born and raised in Barrington, New Jersey, where her stepfather worked as a locomotive engineer and her mother as a probation officer, Democrat Sarah Elfreth represents Maryland's 3rd congressional district covering Anne Arundel, Howard, and Carroll Counties. She moved to Maryland to study political science at Towson University and later earned an M.P.P. from Johns Hopkins University. She became the youngest woman ever elected to Congress from Maryland after winning a 22-way Democratic primary. Her platform centers on expanding reproductive healthcare access and restoring federal abortion protections, banning assault weapons and expanding background checks, and fighting for climate justice and protecting the Chesapeake Bay. She serves on the House Armed Services Committee and House Natural Resources Committee. A notable legislative achievement: she was regarded as one of the most productive members of the Maryland Senate, having passed 84 bills during her tenure — more than any other legislator during that time. During her 2024 congressional campaign, she drew attention as the only candidate who expressed openness to accepting corporate PAC contributions.
