Jonathan Luther Jackson has served as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 1st Congressional District since 2023. Raised in Chicago's South Shore neighborhood, he holds a B.S. in Business from North Carolina A&T University and an MBA from Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management. A Democrat and son of Rev. Jesse Jackson, he previously served as national spokesman for the Rainbow/PUSH Coalition, a financial analyst, and a business owner. Campaigning as a progressive, he was endorsed by Bernie Sanders and other figures in the progressive wing of the party. His platform centers on advancing equality and justice, improving access to quality education and healthcare, and protecting the environment. In Congress, he is a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus and the Congressional Black Caucus and serves on the House Committee on Agriculture and the House Committee on Foreign Affairs. Notable legislative efforts include leading a bipartisan effort to sanction Houthi rebels through the Sanction Sea Pirates Act and cosponsoring a resolution affirming a U.S. obligation to provide reparations for the enslavement of Africans.
