A proud Colorado native raised in Grand Junction, Hurd comes from a middle-class background — his father was a psychologist who counseled low-income families. After earning law degrees from the University of Denver and Columbia Law School, he clerked for federal appeals court judge Timothy M. Tymkovich. He later built a law practice serving rural communities, focusing on rural electric cooperatives. A Republican, Hurd is a member of the Main Street Caucus, Problem Solvers Caucus, and Republican Governance Group. His platform centers on protecting Colorado's water, energy, and public lands; improving rural infrastructure; and advocating for Western energy independence and water rights. He originally entered the 2024 race to challenge incumbent Lauren Boebert; after she switched districts, he defeated state Representative Ron Hanks in the primary. Campaigning on being "as exciting as a bread sandwich," he defeated Democrat Adam Frisch in the general election. Notably, his Wetlands Conservation and Access Improvement Act was signed into law by President Trump — his first enacted legislation — strengthening habitat restoration funding and expanding hunting access. In April 2025, Hurd joined a dozen moderate Republicans urging House leadership to preserve Medicaid, stating they "cannot and will not support" cuts to coverage for vulnerable populations.
