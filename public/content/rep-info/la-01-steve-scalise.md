Born in New Orleans and a native of Jefferson Parish, Scalise pursued a career as a software engineer before becoming a marketing executive for a technology company. He graduated from Archbishop Rummel High School and Louisiana State University, where he earned a degree in Computer Science. A Republican, he has represented Louisiana's 1st congressional district since 2008 and serves as House Majority Leader, the second highest position in House Republican leadership. He advocates for fiscal discipline, lower taxes, a robust national defense, strong border security, and conservative values. In his campaigns, Scalise has stressed border security and the economy as top priorities. He champions American energy dominance, particularly important for Louisiana, and supports a national energy strategy to increase domestic production and reduce dependence on foreign oil. Notably, on June 14, 2017, Scalise was shot and seriously wounded during a congressional baseball practice by an anti-Trump gunman targeting Republicans, and underwent several months of treatment before returning to Congress. In 2014, it was confirmed he had spoken in 2002 at a white-supremacist organization's event; his adviser stated he was unaware of the group's extremist affiliations at the time.
