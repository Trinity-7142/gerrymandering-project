Born in Port Wentworth, Georgia, Earl LeRoy "Buddy" Carter is a Republican U.S. Representative for Georgia's 1st congressional district, serving since 2015. The district is based in Savannah and includes most of the state's coastal southern portion. Carter worked as a pharmacist, owning several pharmacies in Savannah, which shaped his policy views in Congress. Notably the only pharmacist in Congress, he has advocated for legislation that lowers prescription drug costs and improves transparency in the pharmaceutical industry. His platform emphasizes fiscal conservatism, health care reform, and coastal environmental issues. Carter sponsors the FairTax Act of 2023, which would abolish the current U.S. tax structure and replace it with a national sales tax. He holds an "A" grade from the NRA for his stances on gun rights. He called for the results of the 2020 presidential election in Georgia not to be certified and joined a group of Republican legislators who challenged electoral votes for Joe Biden, even though federal agencies and courts found no evidence of electoral fraud. Carter announced in May 2025 that he would not seek re-election to the House in 2026, instead seeking the Republican nomination for Senate to challenge Democratic incumbent Jon Ossoff.
