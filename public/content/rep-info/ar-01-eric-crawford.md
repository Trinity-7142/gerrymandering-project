Eric Alan "Rick" Crawford (born January 22, 1966) serves as the U.S. Representative for Arkansas's 1st congressional district. Born at Homestead Air Force Base in Florida, he grew up in a military family, with his father serving in the United States Air Force. Crawford enlisted in the U.S. Army and served as an explosive ordnance disposal technician. After his service, he attended Arkansas State University in Jonesboro, graduating in 1996 with a B.S. in agriculture business and economics. A Republican, he was first elected in 2010 as part of the Tea Party movement wave that helped Republicans regain the House majority. His victory made him the first Republican to represent the First District since Reconstruction. Crawford's platform has centered on addressing the national debt by enacting permanent spending controls. In January 2025, Crawford was appointed chair of the House Intelligence Committee, a notable leadership milestone. Controversially, Crawford was the only member of Arkansas's delegation to contest the electoral votes on January 6, 2021.
