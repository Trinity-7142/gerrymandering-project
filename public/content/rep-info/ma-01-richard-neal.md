Richard Edmund Neal (born February 14, 1949) is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Massachusetts's 1st congressional district since 1989. Born in Worcester and raised in Springfield, Neal grew up in a working-class household; he lost both parents at a young age and relied on Social Security survivors' benefits alongside his sisters. Before Congress, he was a high school teacher, Springfield City Council member, and Mayor of Springfield from 1984 to 1988. He has long advocated for expanding healthcare access, a simpler tax code, and retirement savings. Neal chaired the House Ways and Means Committee from 2019 to 2023, and spearheaded the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and CHIPS Act, and helped pass the bipartisan SECURE 2.0 retirement savings bill. Representing a relatively Catholic district, Neal holds a more conservative record on abortion than most Massachusetts colleagues. He leads the Friends of Ireland Caucus and played a prominent role in bringing about the Good Friday Agreement.
