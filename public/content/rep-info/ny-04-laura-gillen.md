Laura Anne Gillen is a Democratic attorney serving as the U.S. Representative for New York's 4th Congressional District since 2025, having previously served as Town Supervisor of Hempstead, New York. She was born in Rockville Centre and grew up in Baldwin. She earned a B.A. from Georgetown University and a J.D. from NYU School of Law. In 2024, she flipped one of the most competitive House seats in the country by defeating incumbent Republican Anthony D'Esposito. Her key priorities include addressing the cost-of-living crisis, border security, and reinstating the full SALT deduction. She is a member of the New Democrat Coalition, and has drawn notice for occasional bipartisan votes — she was among 46 House Democrats who joined Republicans to vote for the Laken Riley Act — as well as for leading congressional advocacy to extend Temporary Protected Status for Haitians.
