Robert Jacobsen Menendez Jr. is an American lawyer and politician serving as the U.S. representative for New Jersey's 8th congressional district since 2023. Raised in Union City as the grandson of Cuban immigrants and strong union members, he was born to a community leader and a public school educator. He holds a B.A. from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and a J.D. from Rutgers Law School. A progressive Democrat, he campaigned on addressing the needs of working- and middle-class families, rising costs of housing, healthcare, and education, as well as infrastructure investment, gun control, and reproductive choice. In his second term, Menendez won a seat on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce. His tenure has been shadowed by the high-profile federal conviction of his father, former Senator Bob Menendez, who was convicted on charges including bribery and acting as a foreign agent, and sentenced to 11 years in prison.
