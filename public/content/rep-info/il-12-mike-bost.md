Born and raised in Murphysboro, Illinois, Bost graduated from Murphysboro High School in 1979 and enlisted in the U.S. Marine Corps, serving from 1979 to 1982 as an electronics specialist and radar repairman, receiving an honorable discharge as a Corporal. He later spent two decades in the Illinois House of Representatives and served as a firefighter for the Murphysboro Fire Department. A conservative Republican, Bost campaigns on securing the border, growing the economy, protecting life, and defending the Second Amendment. In the 119th Congress, he serves on the Veterans' Affairs, Transportation & Infrastructure, and Agriculture committees, chairing Veterans' Affairs. Bost has been the primary sponsor of 15 bills that were enacted, many focused on veterans' benefits. Notably, in 2024 he voted against a $60 billion military aid package for Ukraine, with reporting noting some funding would have supported defense jobs in his district.
