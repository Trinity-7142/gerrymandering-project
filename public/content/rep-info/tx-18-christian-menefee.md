A Houston native raised by two U.S. Coast Guard veterans, Menefee was the first in his family to graduate from college. He attended Houston's public schools and, with the help of a Pell Grant, earned a degree from the University of Texas at San Antonio before completing law school at Washington University in St. Louis. A Democrat, he made history as the youngest person and first Black attorney ever elected Harris County Attorney, the chief civil law officer for the largest county in Texas. He won a special election runoff in January 2026, filling the seat left vacant by the death of Rep. Sylvester Turner. His campaign platform centers on overhauling the immigration system, providing a path to citizenship for Dreamers, expanding worker and student visas, and focusing enforcement on violent offenders. He also prioritizes housing and homelessness, calling for expanded mental health care and transitional housing programs. As County Attorney, he filed high-profile environmental lawsuits, including against Union Pacific over creosote-related contamination in Houston's Fifth Ward. He and Rep. Al Green advanced to a Democratic primary runoff for the redrawn TX-18 seat in May 2026.
