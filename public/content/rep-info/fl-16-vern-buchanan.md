Vernon Gale Buchanan (born May 8, 1951) is a Republican businessman, politician, and Air Force National Guard veteran who grew up with five siblings in a blue-collar household near Detroit, Michigan, served six years in the Air National Guard, and worked his way through college. A self-made businessman, Buchanan champions pro-growth, free-market policies. He serves as Vice Chair of the House Ways and Means Committee, which has jurisdiction over tax policy, international trade, health care, Social Security, and Medicare. His signature legislation is the Buchanan Balanced Budget Amendment Act; he also introduced a bill to stop automatic pay raises for Members of Congress until the budget is balanced. With 220,000 Medicare beneficiaries in FL-16, he has prioritized preserving Medicare's long-term solvency and lowering healthcare costs. He has been ranked one of the most bipartisan and effective members of Congress by the Lugar Center and the Center for Effective Lawmaking. Notably, a House Ethics Committee investigation concluded in 2016, finding "insufficient evidence" of wrongdoing on Buchanan's part following earlier campaign finance inquiries. On January 27, 2026, Buchanan announced he would not seek re-election.
