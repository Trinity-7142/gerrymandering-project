Born in Hackleburg, Alabama, Palmer grew up on a 40-acre family farm and was the first in his family to earn a college degree. He holds a Bachelor of Science in operations management from the University of Alabama. Before elected office, he co-founded and led the Alabama Policy Institute, a conservative think tank, and later chaired the House Republican Policy Committee from 2019 to 2025. His platform centers on reducing federal spending, reforming regulations, expanding American energy production, protecting life, and advancing patient-centered health care. He serves on the House Committees on Oversight and Government Reform and Energy and Commerce, chairing the Subcommittee on Environment. Notably, Palmer touted infrastructure funding he secured for the Birmingham Northern Beltline yet voted against the final bill, drawing bipartisan criticism. He also voted against certifying the 2020 presidential election results, objecting to Arizona's and Pennsylvania's electoral votes.
