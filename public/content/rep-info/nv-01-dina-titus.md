Dina Titus (D) has represented Nevada's 1st Congressional District since 2013. Born and raised in Tifton, Georgia, she earned advanced degrees from the University of Georgia and Florida State University, then taught American and Nevada government at UNLV for 34 years. She served in the Nevada Senate as its minority leader from 1993 to 2009. A progressive Democrat, she currently serves on the Transportation and Infrastructure Committee and the Foreign Affairs Committee. Representing Las Vegas, she prioritizes diplomacy and global partnerships, and is the original sponsor of the GLOBE Act to protect LGBTI rights worldwide. She is also a founder of the Congressional Wild Horse Caucus. She is the published author of *Bombs in the Backyard: Atomic Testing and American Politics*.
