Glenn "GT" Thompson (born July 27, 1959) is a Republican representing Pennsylvania's 15th congressional district. He was born in Bellefonte and grew up in Howard, Pennsylvania, the son of a Navy veteran. He earned a bachelor's degree in therapeutic rehabilitation from Penn State and a master's from Temple University, and previously worked as a rehabilitation therapist and volunteer firefighter. He chairs the House Agriculture Committee and serves as a senior member of the House Education & Workforce Committee. He authored the Strengthening Career and Technical Education for the 21st Century Act and was instrumental in crafting the 2018 and 2023 Farm Bills. In 2022, Thompson voted against the Respect for Marriage Act just days before attending his son's same-sex wedding, drawing widespread attention. He became the dean of Pennsylvania's congressional delegation in 2025.
