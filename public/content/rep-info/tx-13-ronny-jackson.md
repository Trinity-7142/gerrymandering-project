Ronny Lynn Jackson is a physician, politician, and former U.S. Navy officer serving as the U.S. representative for Texas's 13th congressional district since 2021. Born and raised in the small town of Levelland, Texas, Jackson worked from the age of 14, holding jobs ranging from grocery store clerk to oil field roustabout. He earned a bachelor's degree in marine biology from Texas A&M University in 1991 and a medical degree from the University of Texas Medical Branch in 1995. Jackson served as physician to the president from 2013 to 2018 under both Barack Obama and Donald Trump. A conservative Republican, he serves on the House Armed Services, Agriculture, Foreign Affairs, and Permanent Select Intelligence Committees, focusing on national security, agriculture, and economic prosperity. In 2018, Trump nominated Jackson to be Secretary of Veterans Affairs, but he withdrew amid allegations of misconduct, which he denied. In 2022, the Navy demoted Jackson from rear admiral to captain following a DoD inspector general investigation substantiating allegations of misconduct. In June 2025, the Secretary of the Navy reversed the demotion.
