Rep. Hillary Scholten (D) grew up in Hudsonville, a suburb of Grand Rapids, the daughter of a public school teacher and a sports journalist. She served as a DOJ attorney adviser for the Board of Immigration Appeals during the Obama administration before returning to Michigan. In 2022, she made history by flipping a 50-year Republican stronghold, becoming the first Democratic woman to represent West Michigan in the U.S. House. She is a member of the New Democrat Coalition and the Problem Solvers Caucus. Her platform centers on controlling healthcare and higher education costs, bringing jobs to West Michigan, combating climate change, protecting reproductive rights, and fixing the immigration system. Notably, she was the first in Michigan's congressional delegation to call for President Biden to step aside in 2024. More recently, she drew attention after announcing a separation from her husband, who filed for divorce, and a judge denied her motion to seal the divorce records.
