Born and raised in Merced, California, Gray grew up working in his family's dairy supply and feed store. He earned a bachelor's degree in political science from UC Santa Barbara. A Democrat, Gray serves as whip of the centrist Blue Dog Coalition. His platform centers on job creation, preserving agriculture, and ensuring safe and reliable water for Central Valley farmers and consumers. Appointed to the House Agriculture and Natural Resources Committees, he co-sponsored bipartisan bills supporting groundwater storage and introduced the Valley Water Protection Act to limit Endangered Species Act implementation causing significant regional economic harm. He won his seat in 2024 by just 187 votes — the closest House race of that election cycle. Notably, a 2024 report revealed Gray purchased real estate shortly before a $50 million state-funded redevelopment of the former Castle Air Force Base.
