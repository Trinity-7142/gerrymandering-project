Daniel Philip Meuser (born February 10, 1964) is a Republican businessman and politician representing Pennsylvania's 9th congressional district since 2019. He resides in Dallas, Pennsylvania. A Cornell University economics graduate, he served as president of Pride Mobility Products, a motorized wheelchair manufacturer in the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton area, and headed Pennsylvania's Department of Revenue from 2011 to 2015 under Gov. Tom Corbett. His legislative priorities include economic growth, border security, American energy dominance, and restoring peace through strength. He serves on the House Financial Services and Small Business committees. He won a fourth term in 2024 with 70% of the vote. Notable controversies include voting against certification of the 2020 presidential election results and a reported STOCK Act violation in 2021 for failing to timely disclose family stock trades worth up to $600,000.
