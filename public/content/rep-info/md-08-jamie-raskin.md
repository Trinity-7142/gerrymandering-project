Representative Jamie Raskin hails from Takoma Park, Maryland, and brings a distinctly academic background to Congress. Before his election, he was a constitutional law professor at American University Washington College of Law and earned his J.D. *magna cum laude* from Harvard Law School, where he was an editor of the *Harvard Law Review*. A progressive Democrat, he has represented Maryland's 8th congressional district since 2017. His platform centers on defending voting rights, reproductive freedom, and constitutional norms. As a state senator, he championed landmark legislation including marriage equality, abolition of the death penalty, the first Benefit Corporation law in America, and the first state National Popular Vote Interstate Compact law. In the 119th Congress, he serves as Ranking Member of the House Judiciary Committee. He was the lead impeachment manager for the second impeachment of President Donald Trump following the January 6th Capitol attack. He was also nominated for the 2025 Nobel Peace Prize for his "defense of freedom and democracy," along with other members of the January 6th Committee.
