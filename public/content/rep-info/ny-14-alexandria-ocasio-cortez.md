Born in the Parkchester neighborhood of the Bronx, Ocasio-Cortez grew up in a working-class family; her father owned a small architectural firm and her mother cleaned houses, and the family later moved to the Yorktown Heights suburbs in search of better schools. After college, she worked as a bartender and waitress to help her mother fight foreclosure of their home. She identifies as a democratic socialist and is known for her progressive politics and skillful use of social media. She advocates a platform that includes Medicare for All, tuition-free public colleges, a federal jobs guarantee, a Green New Deal, and abolishing ICE. First elected in 2018, she drew national attention after defeating Democratic Caucus chair and 10-term incumbent Joe Crowley — widely regarded as the biggest primary upset that year. Taking office at age 29, she became the youngest woman ever elected to Congress. She refuses to accept contributions from corporations. Notable legislative efforts include the Green New Deal resolution, envisioning a 10-year national mobilization to create union jobs, repair infrastructure, and address climate and economic crises. She has faced multiple ethics complaints, including allegations of misappropriating her taxpayer-funded member allowance.
