Born and raised in Austin, Texas, Doggett attended Austin High School and earned both his B.A. and J.D. from the University of Texas. He is considered one of the most liberal Democrats from a Southern district, with policies described as "muscular progressivism." He has been a strong advocate for Social Security, Medicare, healthcare, immigration reform, environmental protection, and public education. He serves as Ranking Member of the Health Subcommittee on the House Ways & Means Committee, and also sits on the Ways and Means Tax Subcommittee, the Joint Committee on Taxation, and the House Budget Committee. On Ways and Means, he has sought to close overseas tax shelters and authored legislation creating tax incentives for plug-in hybrid electric vehicles. He is the dean of Texas's congressional delegation. Notably, on July 2, 2024, Doggett became the first Democratic federal elected official to call on Biden to withdraw from the 2024 presidential race — a move that drew both praise and criticism. In August 2025, he announced he would not run for re-election if Texas's then-planned new congressional map was in effect for the 2026 elections.
