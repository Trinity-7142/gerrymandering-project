Bill Huizenga is a Republican U.S. representative from Michigan who has served since 2011. Born in Zeeland, Michigan, he graduated from Holland Christian High School and earned a B.A. in Political Science from Calvin College in 1991. From a Dutch-American family, he is co-owner of Huizenga Gravel Company and worked in real estate before becoming an aide to U.S. Representative Pete Hoekstra in 1996. A conservative, Huizenga has focused his platform on private-sector job creation, arguing it is "the government's responsibility to create a suitable environment for the private sector to succeed," and supports fiscal restraint and a balanced budget. Since 2017, he has co-chaired the bipartisan Great Lakes Task Force, fostering cooperation on environmental and economic policies for the Great Lakes region. Notably, in December 2020 he was among 126 House Republicans who signed an amicus brief contesting the 2020 presidential election results before the Supreme Court. He confirmed in December 2025 that he will seek reelection to Congress.
