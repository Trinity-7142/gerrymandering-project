Lateefah Simon was born in San Francisco, California. A young single mother at 18, she experienced firsthand how government systems failed marginalized communities, shaping a lifelong commitment to social justice. She has served as the U.S. representative for California's 12th congressional district since January 2025, and is notably the first member of Congress known to be born legally blind in both eyes and the first Muslim member from California. She aligns with the progressive wing of the Democratic Party, advocating for a federal jobs guarantee, universal health care, a $15 minimum wage, and strengthened safety-net programs. In Congress, she focuses on housing affordability, mental health, homelessness, lowering costs for working families, climate resilience, and immigrant communities. At age 26, she became the youngest woman to receive a MacArthur "Genius" Fellowship. A notable controversy arose when she was removed from the BART board after her residence was found to be outside her district; Simon stated she had moved due to threats stemming from her police reform advocacy and had been assured her new address remained within district boundaries.
