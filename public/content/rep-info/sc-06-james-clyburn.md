Rep. James E. Clyburn's humble beginnings in Sumter, South Carolina, as the son of an activist minister and a civic-minded beautician grounded him in public service. He attended South Carolina State College, a historically Black institution in Orangeburg. Clyburn is regarded as liberal and self-identifies as a progressive, though he believes liberalism and conservatism must be practically balanced. He has established liberal stances on health care, education, organized labor, and environmental conservation. His policy focus centers on combating rural poverty through his signature "10-20-30" plan, which directs federal spending to communities where 20% or more of the population has lived below the poverty line for 30 years. A civil rights movement veteran, he was the second African American and first South Carolinian to serve as House Majority Whip. His 2020 endorsement of Joe Biden is widely credited with boosting Biden to a decisive South Carolina primary victory and setting him on the path to the presidency. He received the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2024. In 2023, ProPublica reported that Clyburn secretly worked with South Carolina Republicans during the 2020 congressional redistricting process to dilute the state's Black vote — a claim his spokesperson denied.
