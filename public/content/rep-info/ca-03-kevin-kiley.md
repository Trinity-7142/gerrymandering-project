Kevin Kiley grew up in the Sacramento area, where his father was a physician and his mother was a special education teacher. He holds a bachelor's degree from Harvard, a law degree from Yale, and a master's in secondary education from Loyola Marymount. Upon graduation from Harvard, he joined Teach for America, teaching at Manual Arts High School in Los Angeles. First elected in 2022, Kiley has focused on fiscally conservative policies to reduce inflation, expand education choice, and manage public lands to prevent wildfires. He supports ending California High-Speed Rail and authored legislation to halt its federal funding. He introduced the PRESS Act, which the House passed with bipartisan support, to protect journalists' sources. Notably, in March 2026, Kiley formally changed his party affiliation from Republican to independent, while remaining a member of the House Republican Conference.
