Born in Seoul, South Korea to a Korean mother and an African-American serviceman, Strickland grew up in Tacoma's South End neighborhood after her family relocated when her father was stationed at Fort Lewis. She holds a B.A. from the University of Washington and an MBA from Clark Atlanta University. A Democrat, she prioritizes economic opportunity, affordable housing, and infrastructure development. Serving on the House Armed Services Committee, she secured a 14.5% pay increase for junior enlisted servicemembers. She has secured over $45 million in direct investments for the South Sound. She is the first African American to represent Washington state and one of the first Korean-American women elected to Congress. She was sanctioned by the Tacoma Board of Ethics in 2010 for accepting frequent flyer miles from a businessman for an official trip, later returning their value.
