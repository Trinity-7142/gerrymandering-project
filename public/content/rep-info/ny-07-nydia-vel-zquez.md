Rep. Nydia Velázquez has represented New York's 7th Congressional District since 2013 and is a member of the Democratic Party. Born in Yabucoa, Puerto Rico, she grew up as one of nine children, with her father a low-income worker in the sugarcane fields. She skipped several grades, became the first in her family to earn a college diploma, and graduated magna cum laude in political science. Velázquez is the first Puerto Rican woman to serve in Congress. A progressive advocate for the underrepresented, her platform centers on economic development, affordable housing, environmental protection, and access to quality education and healthcare. She became the first woman to chair the House Small Business Committee in 2007 and the first Hispanic woman to chair a full House standing committee. In 2024, she voted against military aid to Israel. In November 2025, Velázquez announced she would not seek re-election in 2026.
