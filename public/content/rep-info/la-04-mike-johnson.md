Born in Shreveport, Louisiana, Mike Johnson is the eldest son of a firefighter critically burned and permanently disabled in the line of duty, and was the first person in his family to attend college. A graduate of LSU's Paul M. Hebert Law Center, he worked as an attorney in private practice and for the Alliance Defending Freedom, a conservative Christian legal advocacy group, before entering politics. Johnson is a Republican member of Congress serving Louisiana's Fourth District and serves as the 56th Speaker of the U.S. House of Representatives — the first person from Louisiana ever elected to the position. A staunch conservative, Johnson has prioritized border security, energy policy, and fiscal responsibility. As Speaker, he secured passage of foreign aid packages for Ukraine, Israel, and Taiwan in April 2024, survived a motion to vacate in May, and was reelected Speaker in January 2025. He has drawn controversy for being among 147 Republicans who voted to overturn the 2020 presidential election results.
