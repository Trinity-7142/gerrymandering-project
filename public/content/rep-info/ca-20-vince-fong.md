Born in Bakersfield, Vince Fong is a Republican U.S. Representative for California's 20th congressional district, serving since 2024. He began his career in public policy with Congressman Bill Thomas, working on international trade, before spending nearly a decade as district director for Congressman Kevin McCarthy. He was the first Asian American to represent Bakersfield in the state legislature. In Congress, he serves on the Committees on Homeland Security, Science, Space, and Technology, and Transportation and Infrastructure. His priorities include strengthening Central Valley supply chains, advancing aeronautics and commercial spaceflight in East Kern, and supporting military communities at China Lake, Edwards, and Lemoore. Notably, California's Secretary of State initially deemed him ineligible to run for Congress due to his simultaneous Assembly re-election filing, but a Superior Court judge ruled in his favor.
