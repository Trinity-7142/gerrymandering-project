Born in Manchester, New Hampshire, Pappas graduated from Manchester Central High School before earning a B.A. in government from Harvard College in 2002. He returned to New Hampshire to help run his family's historic Puritan Backroom Restaurant. A centrist Democrat, Pappas was ranked the most bipartisan Democrat and 3rd in bipartisanship overall in the House in 2023 by the Lugar Center. In Congress, he has focused on improving access to health care, lowering prescription drug costs, combating the opioid epidemic, and protecting the environment and drinking water. He serves on the Veterans' Affairs and Transportation and Infrastructure Committees. Pappas is the first openly gay man to represent New Hampshire in Congress, and in April 2025, he announced his candidacy for the U.S. Senate seat vacated by Jeanne Shaheen.
