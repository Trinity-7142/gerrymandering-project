Rep. Jack Bergman (R) has represented Michigan's 1st congressional district since 2017. Born in Shakopee, Minnesota, he is officially listed as being "of" Watersmeet, Michigan. Bergman served in the U.S. Marine Corps from 1969 to 2009, retiring as a lieutenant general, and also worked as a commercial airline pilot and small-business owner; upon his election, he became the highest-ranking combat veteran ever elected to Congress. Identifying as a constitutional conservative, he asserts that government should safeguard individual rights and promote a free-market economy. His primary legislative focus has been veterans' issues, while also championing rural broadband expansion for northern Michigan and the Upper Peninsula. He serves on the House Armed Services, Budget, and Veterans' Affairs committees. Notably, following the 2020 presidential election, Bergman announced his intention to object to the certification of the Electoral College results. His dual residency in Michigan and Louisiana also drew scrutiny during multiple election cycles.
