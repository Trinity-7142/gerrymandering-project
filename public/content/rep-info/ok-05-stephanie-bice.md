A fourth-generation Oklahoman born and raised in Oklahoma's Fifth Congressional District, Bice graduated from Putnam City High School in Oklahoma City and earned a bachelor's degree in marketing from Oklahoma State University, later working in financial oversight, business strategy, and marketing for her family's technology company. She is the first American of Iranian and Pakistani descent elected to Congress. A conservative Republican, her platform emphasizes supporting Oklahoma's oil and gas industry, defending abortion restrictions, and securing the border. She previously represented the 22nd district in the Oklahoma Senate from 2014 to 2020, where the Senate Republican Caucus elected her Assistant Majority Floor Leader. She currently serves on the House Committee on Appropriations and the Committee on House Administration. Notably, on January 6, 2021, Bice voted to object to Arizona's and Pennsylvania's electoral votes in the 2020 presidential election.
