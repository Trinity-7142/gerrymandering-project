Rep. J. French Hill (R) was born on December 5, 1956, in Little Rock, Arkansas, where he grew up and attended Catholic High School for Boys. He earned a B.S. in economics from Vanderbilt University in 1979. He served as Deputy Assistant Secretary of the Treasury for Corporate Finance under President George H.W. Bush and later founded Delta Trust & Banking Corp., headquartered in Little Rock. Hill has represented Arkansas's Second Congressional District since January 2015. He has established himself as a reliable conservative vote, strongly supportive of pro-business measures. His platform emphasizes establishing federal frameworks for stablecoin regulation and expanding capital access for small businesses. He serves as Chairman of the House Financial Services Committee in the 119th Congress. Notably, Hill did not sign an amicus brief contesting the 2020 presidential election results and voted to certify both Arizona's and Pennsylvania's Electoral College results.
