Thomas Tiffany grew up on a dairy farm near Elmwood, Pierce County, Wisconsin. He earned a degree in agricultural economics from the University of Wisconsin–River Falls and later owned and operated a river cruise company, also working as a dam tender. A Republican, he is a member of the House Freedom Caucus, considered the most conservative bloc in the chamber. He previously served seven years in the Wisconsin Senate and two years in the State Assembly. His platform prioritizes immigration enforcement, fiscal restraint, and natural resource development. He has introduced legislation in every session to establish diplomatic relations with Taiwan, and in 2024 sponsored bills supporting Taiwan's participation in the World Health Organization. He was one of 126 House Republicans to sign an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania*, contesting the 2020 presidential election results. Tiffany has declared candidacy for the 2026 Republican gubernatorial primary in Wisconsin.
