Born to working-class Palestinian immigrants in Detroit in 1976 and the oldest of 14 children, Tlaib earned a B.A. in political science from Wayne State University and a J.D. from Thomas M. Cooley Law School. A member of the Democratic Socialists of America, she aligns with the left wing of the Democratic Party. Her platform champions raising the minimum wage, the Green Jobs Act, preventing insurance redlining, and protecting low-income housing. She is the first Palestinian American woman and one of the first two Muslim women elected to Congress. She has secured over $55 million in federal funding for more than 40 local community projects. In November 2023, Tlaib was censured by the House in response to her public statements following the October 7 attacks on Israel.
