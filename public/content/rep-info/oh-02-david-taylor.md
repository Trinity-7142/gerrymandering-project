David James Taylor, born November 16, 1969, is a lifelong Ohioan from Amelia who earned a B.A. from Miami University in 1992 and a J.D. from the University of Dayton School of Law in 1996. He worked as a part-time assistant prosecutor for Clermont County before becoming owner of his family's concrete business, Sardinia Ready Mix, in Brown County. A Republican, Taylor's platform centers on infrastructure and rural development, support for local farmers, and fiscal responsibility through reduced federal spending and regulation. He ran for the seat after incumbent Brad Wenstrup announced his retirement, winning the Republican nomination and defeating Democratic nominee Samantha Meadows. He serves on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and the House Agriculture Committee. He secured nearly $17 million in federal funding for 13 community projects across the district. A notable controversy arose in 2025 when a staffer appeared on a Zoom call from Taylor's office with an altered flag bearing a swastika; Taylor stated the flag was discovered and removed immediately, and that a full investigation found no employee knowingly displayed it.
