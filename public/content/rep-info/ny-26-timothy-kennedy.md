Born in Buffalo, New York, in 1976, Kennedy earned bachelor's and master's degrees in occupational therapy from D'Youville College and worked as a licensed occupational therapist. A Democrat, he served on the Erie County Legislature from 2004 to 2010 and in the New York State Senate from 2011 to 2024. He was elected in the April 2024 special election to finish the remainder of Brian Higgins' term. His platform focuses on infrastructure investment, affordability, public safety, and healthcare access. In Congress, Kennedy honored victims of the 2022 Tops grocery store shooting in Buffalo and introduced legislation with Rep. Grace Meng to ban the sale of enhanced body armor. He has missed zero of 785 roll call votes, outperforming the median among currently serving representatives.
