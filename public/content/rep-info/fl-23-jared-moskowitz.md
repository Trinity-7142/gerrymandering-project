Born in Coral Springs, Florida, Moskowitz was shaped from a young age by Democratic values, inspired by his father's work as a Democratic Party Committeeman. He earned a B.A. in political science from George Washington University and a J.D. from Nova Southeastern University. A Democrat, his congressional priorities include addressing gun violence, defending democracy, strengthening support for Israel, and protecting women's reproductive freedom. Notably, after 17 people were killed in the mass shooting at his alma mater, Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School, he helped enact landmark Florida legislation raising the gun purchase age and establishing red flag laws. A controversy arose in 2023 when an account belonging to his children sold bank shares the same day Silicon Valley Bank failed, just before a private congressional banking briefing, with shares falling roughly 20% shortly after.
