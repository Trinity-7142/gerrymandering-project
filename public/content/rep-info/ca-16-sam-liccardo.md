Samuel Theodore Liccardo is an American attorney and politician serving as the U.S. Representative from California's 16th Congressional District. He grew up in Saratoga, California, and is of Sicilian, Irish, and Mexican Californio ancestry. A centrist Democrat, he is a member of the New Democrat Coalition with roots in Silicon Valley's tech-aligned political culture. He previously served as the 65th Mayor of San Jose from 2015 to 2023, where his tenure was characterized by approaches to reducing street homelessness, expanding affordable housing, launching a 95% GHG-free public electric utility, and preventing gun violence. In Congress, he serves on the House Financial Services Committee and chairs the New Democratic Coalition's Innovation & Technology Working Group. His path to Congress included a contentious primary recount that left Liccardo and opponent Evan Low in a bitter dispute. He holds degrees from Harvard Law School, Harvard's Kennedy School of Government, and Georgetown University.
