David Scott (born June 27, 1945) is a Democratic politician and businessman who has served as the U.S. Representative for Georgia's 13th congressional district since 2003. Born in Aynor, South Carolina, he attended high school in Daytona Beach, Florida, where his father started a garbage collection business and Scott worked his way through school as a busboy, waiter, and dishwasher. He earned a bachelor's degree from Florida A&M University and an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. He prioritizes economic development, affordable healthcare, and strengthening agricultural communities. He secured $80 million in the 2018 Farm Bill for agriculture-focused scholarships at Historically Black Land-Grant Colleges. He was ranked among the most bipartisan House members during the 114th Congress by the Lugar Center's Bipartisan Index. In later years, concerns arose over his cognitive fitness, and an incident in late 2024 drew public attention.
