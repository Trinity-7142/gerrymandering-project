Born in Cleveland, Ohio, lifelong Clevelander Shontel Brown entered public service in 2012 as a Warrensville Heights city council member, later winning a seat on the Cuyahoga County Council in 2014. In 2017, she became the first Black woman to chair the Cuyahoga County Democratic Party. She was first elected to the House of Representatives in 2021. A Democrat, she focuses on strengthening public education, improving children's and family health, addressing hunger, protecting Ohio workers, and combating poverty. She has cosponsored legislation to protect workers against unfair trade practices, raise the minimum wage, and provide affordable healthcare. Her 2021 victory drew attention due to significant donations from AIPAC, and she has missed only 0.2% of roll call votes — better than the median among currently serving representatives.
