Al Green is an American lawyer and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Texas's 9th congressional district since 2005. A self-described "son of the segregated south," born in New Orleans in 1947, his mother worked as a maid and his father as a mechanic's helper. Before receiving an undergraduate degree, he enrolled at the Thurgood Marshall School of Law, earning his J.D. in 1973. A progressive Democrat, Green has committed to prioritizing legislation fighting all forms of invidious discrimination, improving affordability, and creating well-paying jobs. He has secured over $100 million in funding for community projects in the Ninth Congressional District. He was censured by the House 224–198 in March 2025 after repeatedly interrupting President Trump's address to a joint session of Congress. Green has also filed articles of impeachment against Trump multiple times, none of which have been successful.
