Born in Chicago to interracial parents, Rep. Sydney Kamlager-Dove is a progressive Democrat representing Los Angeles County's CA-37. She earned a bachelor's degree in political science from USC and a master's degree in arts management and public policy from Carnegie Mellon University. In Congress, she prioritizes Land, Economic Justice, Art, and Democracy. She serves on the House Foreign Affairs Committee as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on South and Central Asia, and on the House Judiciary Committee. She is Whip of the Congressional Black Caucus and Policy Co-Chair of the Democratic Women's Caucus. Notable accomplishments include helping secure the release of a constituent wrongfully detained in Venezuela and, earlier in her state legislative career, co-authoring the CROWN Act, which prohibits discrimination against natural or protective hairstyles. In April 2025, she drew controversy after accusing journalist Matt Taibbi of being a "serial sexual harasser" during a committee hearing, prompting a $10 million libel suit against her.
