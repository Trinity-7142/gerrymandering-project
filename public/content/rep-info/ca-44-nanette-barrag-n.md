Born in Harbor City, Los Angeles, Barragán is the youngest of 11 siblings raised by immigrants from Mexico. As the youngest of eleven children, her mother cleaned homes and worked in factories to make ends meet. A progressive Democrat, she obtained her undergraduate degree from UCLA and her Juris Doctor from USC Gould School of Law. Elected to the U.S. House in November 2016, she became the first Latina ever to represent California's 44th Congressional District. Her platform centers on environmental and health justice, immigration reform, strengthening the economy, and affordable education. She serves as Co-Chair of the House Democratic Steering & Policy Committee and on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce. She is also Chair of the Congressional Hispanic Caucus. Notably, she was the first woman in 10 years elected to the Hermosa Beach City Council, where she successfully blocked a proposal to drill 34 oil and water injection wells into the Santa Monica Bay. In 2024, Barragán drew attention when she criticized fellow Democrats for excluding the Congressional Hispanic Caucus from border-provision negotiations in a foreign aid package.
