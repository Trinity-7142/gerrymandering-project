Born in Missouri and raised in Pope County, Arkansas, Hern is a former aerospace engineer and successful McDonald's franchise owner who represents the Tulsa area. After moving to Oklahoma in 1999, Hern expanded his McDonald's franchises, eventually owning 18 in the Tulsa metropolitan area. A Republican, he has served in the U.S. House for Oklahoma's 1st congressional district since 2018. In Congress, Hern prioritizes fiscal responsibility, energy policy, and support for small businesses, and serves on the House Ways and Means Committee. He chaired the Republican Study Committee in 2023. In 2024, he defeated incumbent Republican Policy Committee Chairman Gary Palmer in Republican leadership elections. Hern drew scrutiny for violating the STOCK Act in 2021 by failing to properly disclose stock trades worth up to $2.7 million. He has since announced his candidacy for the 2026 U.S. Senate election in Oklahoma.
