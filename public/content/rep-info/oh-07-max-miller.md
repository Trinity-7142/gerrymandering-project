Max Miller (born November 13, 1988) has served as the U.S. Representative for Ohio's 7th Congressional District since 2023. He grew up in Northeast Ohio and graduated from Shaker Heights High School. He earned a bachelor's degree in history from Cleveland State University and served in the U.S. Marine Corps Reserve, later becoming director of presidential advance and a senior advisor in the first Trump administration. A conservative Republican, his platform centers on tax relief, workforce development, border security, supporting law enforcement, and American energy security. Miller and David Kustoff are the only Jewish members of the Republican Party in Congress. In 2023, he introduced and passed a resolution removing Rep. Ilhan Omar from the House Foreign Affairs Committee. Miller has faced personal controversies, including allegations of physical abuse from former girlfriend Stephanie Grisham, against whom he filed a defamation lawsuit.
