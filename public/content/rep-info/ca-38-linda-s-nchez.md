Born in Orange, California, the sixth of seven children to Mexican immigrant parents — her father an industrial machinist and her mother an elementary school teacher — Linda Teresa Sánchez is a former labor lawyer serving as the U.S. representative for California's 38th congressional district. A lifelong progressive, Sánchez has devoted her career to helping working people get ahead: advocating for families, improving America's education system, and bringing jobs to Southern California. She serves as a senior member on the House Committee on Ways and Means, where she works on tax, trade, retirement security, and healthcare issues, and is working to reform the tax code so that it is simple and fair. In 2009, Sánchez introduced the Gender Equity in Health Premiums Act, which became part of the Affordable Care Act signed into law in 2010. In 2016, her colleagues elected her vice chair of the House Democratic Caucus, making her the first woman of color elected to a leadership position in the history of the U.S. Congress. In 2024, she became the first Latina and woman of color to serve as a ranking member on a Ways and Means subcommittee.
