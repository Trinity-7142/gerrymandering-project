Thomas Massie is a Republican U.S. Representative who has served Kentucky's 4th Congressional District since 2012. Born in Huntington, West Virginia, he was raised in an Appalachian American culture in Vanceburg, Kentucky, where his father was a beer distributor. He earned degrees in electrical and mechanical engineering from MIT. Massie has been described as a libertarian Republican and a member of the Tea Party movement. He campaigns on transparency, constitutional rights, secure borders, an America-first foreign policy, and fiscal responsibility. Notably, Massie voted against the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, arguing it was not sufficiently fiscally conservative, prompting Trump to back a primary challenger against him. Massie also drew widespread attention as the key Republican who successfully pushed to force a vote requiring the DOJ to release its records on Jeffrey Epstein.
