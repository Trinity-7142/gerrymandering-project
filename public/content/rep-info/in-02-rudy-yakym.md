A fourth-generation Hoosier, Yakym was born in South Bend, Indiana, and pursues a conservative, pro-business ideology. He earned a B.S. in finance and business administration from Indiana University South Bend and an MBA from the University of Notre Dame. Before Congress, he served as Director of Growth Initiatives at Kem Krest, where he led efforts to streamline supply chains. After Rep. Jackie Walorski died in a car accident in August 2022, Yakym was selected as the Republican nominee to succeed her, winning the general election with over 64% of the vote. His top priorities include inflation and border security. He serves on the House Ways and Means Committee and has championed legislation including the bipartisan Improper Payments Transparency Act, aimed at improving federal efficiency. He was named the Indiana Life Sciences Association's 2024 Legislator of the Year for his work on patient access and life sciences innovation.
