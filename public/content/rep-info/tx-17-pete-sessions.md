Pete Sessions is a Republican U.S. Representative serving Texas's 17th congressional district. Born in Waco, Texas, his father William S. Sessions served as Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation. After graduating from Southwestern University, he worked for Southwestern Bell Telephone Company for 16 years, retiring as District Manager. A staunch conservative, his platform includes bolstering domestic energy production and placing restrictions on work-from-home for federal employees. He supports building a wall along the entire southern border. He chaired the National Republican Congressional Committee from 2009 to 2012, leading to a net 63-seat Republican victory in 2010. A key legislative accomplishment was sponsoring the Family Opportunity Act of 2005, enabling families of medically stressed children to receive comprehensive healthcare. Notably, Sessions was identified as "Congressman-1" in a 2019 federal indictment involving Lev Parnas and Igor Fruman, after he authored a letter calling for the removal of U.S. Ambassador to Ukraine Marie Yovanovitch.
