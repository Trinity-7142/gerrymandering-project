Born in Port Arthur, Texas, Republican Brian Babin represents Texas's 36th District and calls Woodville home. To fund his education, he worked as a janitor, merchant seaman, and postman before completing dental school at the University of Texas, earning his D.D.S. in 1976. He has served in Congress since January 2015. A staunch conservative, his priorities include strong border security, support for a border wall, deportation of criminal aliens, and cutting funding for sanctuary cities. As Chairman of the House Science, Space, and Technology Committee, Babin focuses on keeping the U.S. at the forefront of space exploration and technological innovation. In 2025, he introduced a bill to eliminate automatic birthright citizenship for children born to undocumented immigrant parents. On January 6, 2021, he voted to omit Arizona and Pennsylvania from the counting of presidential electors.
