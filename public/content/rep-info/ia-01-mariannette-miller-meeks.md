Mariannette Miller-Meeks is an American physician and Republican politician representing Iowa's 1st congressional district. The fourth of eight children from a military family without means to fund college, she left home at 16, enrolled in community college, and later enlisted in the U.S. Army at 18, eventually rising to Lieutenant Colonel over 24 years of service. She operated a private ophthalmology practice in Ottumwa and served as the first female president of the Iowa Medical Society. She is the only former State Director of Public Health currently serving in Congress. A member of the centrist Republican Main Street and Republican Governance Group caucuses, her platform centers on healthcare cost reduction, rural investment, and fiscal conservatism. In the 119th Congress, she secured over $24 million in Community Project Funding for southeast Iowa. She was among 47 Republicans who voted for the Respect for Marriage Act in 2022. Controversies include a residency dispute after redistricting drew her Ottumwa home out of the district, with subsequent questions about conflicting registration addresses and disclosure forms. She first won her seat in 2020 by a margin of just six votes, and won her third term in 2024 by 799 votes after a recount.
