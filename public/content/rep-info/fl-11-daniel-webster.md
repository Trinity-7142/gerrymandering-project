Daniel Webster is a Republican U.S. Representative serving Florida's 11th congressional district. Born in Charleston, West Virginia, he was raised in Orlando after his family relocated there when he was seven. He holds an engineering degree from the Georgia Institute of Technology and has worked in the family air conditioning and heating business. Before his congressional service, Webster spent 28 years in the Florida legislature and became the first Republican Speaker of the Florida House since Reconstruction. His platform emphasizes fiscal conservatism, opposing deficit spending, and he has returned part of his congressional salary and office budget, saving over $4 million in taxpayer dollars. He has also advocated for border security, including completing the border wall. In the 119th Congress, Webster chairs the Subcommittee on Railroads, Pipelines and Hazardous Materials. Notably, on January 6, 2021, Webster voted to omit Arizona and/or Pennsylvania from the counting of presidential electors.
