Born in Chicago, Illinois, Schakowsky earned her B.S. from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 1965 and went on to direct the Illinois State Council of Senior Citizens and serve as program director of Illinois Public Action. Regarded as a progressive member of the House, she was identified by GovTrack as among the most progressive members of the 111th Congress and has been a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus. Her platform has centered on equal rights for women, minorities, and LGBTQ+ individuals, protections for trade union workers, and national healthcare reform. She sponsors bills primarily in health, commerce, and international affairs. As co-chair of the Congressional Caucus for Women's Issues, she has been a consistent advocate for women's issues. She won her first general election with 75% of the vote and was subsequently reelected thirteen times. In 2025, her remarks at an Energy and Commerce hearing suggesting the word "manufacturing" could discourage women from the sector went viral and drew widespread criticism. On May 5, 2025, Schakowsky announced she would not seek re-election at the end of her current term.
