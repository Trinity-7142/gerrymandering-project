A third-generation Westchester native born in Mount Vernon, NY, Latimer earned a B.A. from Fordham University and a Master's in Public Administration from NYU's Wagner School. He then worked for over 20 years as a marketing executive at major corporate subsidiaries of Nestlé and ITT. A Democrat, he served in the NY State Assembly, NY State Senate, and as Westchester County Executive from 2017 to 2025. He campaigned on protecting reproductive rights, fighting climate change, improving infrastructure, and promoting affordable healthcare, housing, and education. Running as a centrist, pro-Israel Democrat, he defeated incumbent Jamaal Bowman in what was the most expensive congressional primary in American history, with $15 million in outside spending benefiting his campaign.
