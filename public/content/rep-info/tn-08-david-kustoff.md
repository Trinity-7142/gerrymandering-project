David Frank Kustoff is a Republican attorney and politician born and raised in Memphis, Tennessee, representing a district covering the bulk of West Tennessee, with most of its population in the eastern Memphis area. He earned both his undergraduate degree and his law degree from the University of Memphis. A conservative, Kustoff served as U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Tennessee from 2006 to 2008, during which time he oversaw the Tennessee Waltz trials that sent 12 defendants, including Senator John Ford, to federal prison. He serves on the House Committee on Ways and Means. Kustoff focuses on law enforcement, economic growth, and lower taxes, having signed the Taxpayer Protection Pledge with Americans for Tax Reform. He is one of four Jewish Republicans currently serving in Congress. He has also introduced bipartisan legislation, co-sponsoring the Save Struggling Hospitals Act to fix Medicare payment formulas for rural and lower-wage area hospitals.
