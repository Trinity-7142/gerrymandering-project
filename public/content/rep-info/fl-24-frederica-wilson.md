Born in Miami's Overtown community and raised in Liberty City, FL, Wilson learned the values of activism and community service from her parents, who were small business owners and civil rights activists. She earned degrees from Fisk University and the University of Miami in elementary education, later serving as a school principal before joining the Miami-Dade County School Board. She founded the 5000 Role Models of Excellence Project, a mentoring program aimed at supporting boys and young men of color. A progressive Democrat, her platform centers on creating jobs, improving education, lowering everyday costs, and safeguarding Medicare and Social Security. She passed into law the U.S. Commission on the Social Status of Black Men and Boys following the murder of George Floyd, and currently chairs the commission. Wilson gained national attention in 2012 for her comments on the death of Trayvon Martin. She is also known for her large, colorful hats and has worked to get Congress to lift its ban on head coverings during House sessions, which dates to 1837.
