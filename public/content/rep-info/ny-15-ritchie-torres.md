Ritchie Torres (born March 12, 1988) has served as the U.S. Representative for New York's 15th congressional district since 2021. Raised by a single mother in a South Bronx public housing project, Torres grew up in conditions of concentrated poverty. Upon his swearing-in, he became the first openly gay Afro-Latin American member of Congress. He represents the poorest congressional district in the United States. His platform centers on affordable housing, anti-poverty investment, and public health. In the City Council, he passed over forty pieces of legislation protecting affordable housing and, as chairman overseeing NYCHA, held the first-ever committee hearing in public housing. A self-described moderate Democrat, Torres left the Congressional Progressive Caucus in February 2024 due to disagreements over the Israeli–Palestinian conflict, making him a polarizing figure within his own party. In November 2025, a primary challenger emerged, citing Torres's support for Israel.
