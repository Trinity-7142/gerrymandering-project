Born and raised in Worcester, the son of small business owners, McGovern grew up working in his parents' package store. A Democrat, he has served in the U.S. House of Representatives since 1997, and is a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus. He has earned a national reputation as a champion for food security, human rights, campaign finance reform, social justice, and peace. He championed the expansion of SNAP benefits and played a leading role as chair of the House Rules Committee, and currently serves as its ranking member. Notable legislative accomplishments include the Tibet-China Dispute Act and the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act. China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs announced sanctions against McGovern in July 2024, alleging interference in its internal affairs.
