Ami Bera (born March 2, 1965) is a lifelong Californian and son of Indian immigrants who grew up believing America was a land of opportunity. He attended California's public schools through medical school, earning both his B.S. and M.D. from UC Irvine, putting himself through school with family help, scholarships, part-time work, and federal student loans. Prior to Congress, he worked as a physician and served as chief medical officer for Sacramento County. He first won election to the House in 2012. A centrist Democrat, he serves on the House Foreign Affairs Committee as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on East Asia and the Pacific and on the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence, and is a member of the pro-growth New Democrat Coalition. His priorities include healthcare, education, and economic recovery. Notably, his father was sentenced to a year in prison for federal campaign-finance violations related to Bera's 2010 and 2012 campaigns; Bera was investigated and no charges were brought against him.
