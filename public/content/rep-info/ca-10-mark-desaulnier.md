Born in Lowell, Massachusetts, DeSaulnier earned his B.A. in History from the College of the Holy Cross. As a young man he worked as a probation officer, truck driver, and hotel services employee before moving to California in the early 1970s, where he owned and managed several Bay Area restaurants. A member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, he was a Republican until 2000 before becoming a Democrat. His platform centers on growing the economy, lowering inflation, cutting health care costs, expanding affordable housing, and protecting democracy. Since coming to Congress, he has passed more than 60 legislative efforts. He was notably the first freshman in history to chair the California Assembly Transportation Committee. In the House, he sits on the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and the Committee on Education and Workforce, serving as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Health, Employment, Labor, and Pensions.
