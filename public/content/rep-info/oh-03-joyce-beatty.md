Born in Dayton, Ohio, and representing Columbus as her hometown, Joyce Beatty is a progressive Democrat who has represented Ohio's Third Congressional District since 2013. Before Congress, she served as executive director of Human Services for Montgomery County, a tenured professor at Sinclair Community College, and as a businesswoman. She was the first female Democratic House leader in Ohio history. Her priorities include voting rights, affordable housing, and financial inclusion. She served as the first-ever Chairwoman of the Financial Services Subcommittee on Diversity and Inclusion. She chaired the Congressional Black Caucus from 2021 to 2023. In 2021, she was arrested while demonstrating in the Hart Senate Office Building, pushing the Senate to support federal voting rights legislation. She faced scrutiny for accepting campaign contributions from financial services PACs while overseeing the House Financial Services Committee.
