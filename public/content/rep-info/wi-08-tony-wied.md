Rep. Tony Wied (R) has served as the U.S. representative for Wisconsin's 8th congressional district since November 2024. Raised in Ashwaubenon, Wisconsin, he attended St. Norbert College, where he played college football. A political outsider, he spent three decades starting, building, and operating businesses in Northeast Wisconsin. He owned a chain of gas and convenience stores called Dino Stop, which he sold in 2022. He was elected in a special election to succeed Rep. Mike Gallagher. His platform focused on securing the border, cutting everyday costs, and reining in government spending. On social issues, Wied opposes abortion but supports exceptions for rape, incest, and the life of the mother. In office, he secured $12.4 million for community funding projects in Northeast Wisconsin for FY26.
