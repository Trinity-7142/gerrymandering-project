Born in Moroleón, Mexico, in 1964, Carbajal immigrated to the U.S., eventually settling in Oxnard, California, where his father worked as a farmworker. He served eight years in the U.S. Marine Corps Reserve, including active duty during the Gulf War. A Democrat, he campaigns on fighting for middle-class families, lowering the cost of living, and protecting the environment and regional economy. Before Congress, he served as Santa Barbara County's First District Supervisor for twelve years. His first act in Congress was the California Clean Coast Act, banning future offshore oil and gas drilling along California's coast. As a member of the Transportation and Infrastructure Committee, he helped pass the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law in 2021. He currently sits on the Committees on Agriculture, Armed Services, and Transportation and Infrastructure.
