A native of Des Moines, Iowa, Hinson graduated from Valley High School in West Des Moines and the University of Southern California, where she studied broadcast journalism. Prior to public office, she was a reporter for KCRG-TV in Cedar Rapids, where she connected with Iowa's small business owners and community leaders for over a decade. A conservative Republican, Hinson focused her campaign on cutting taxes and building infrastructure. She serves on the House Appropriations Committee and the Select Committee on the Chinese Communist Party. Hinson and Mariannette Miller-Meeks are the first Republican women to represent Iowa in the House. In July 2020, *The New York Times* reported several instances of Hinson's campaign website plagiarizing portions of articles from media outlets. In 2022, she was among the Republican Representatives who voted for the Respect for Marriage Act. Hinson is also a candidate in the 2026 U.S. Senate election in Iowa.
