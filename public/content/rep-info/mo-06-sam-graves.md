Rep. Sam Graves was born in Tarkio, Missouri, where he operates a sixth-generation family farm raising corn, soybeans, hogs, and cattle. A farmer, pilot, and businessman, he earned a bachelor's degree in agronomy from the University of Missouri in 1986. A conservative Republican, first elected to Congress in 2000, Graves has become a leading voice on transportation and infrastructure, serving as Chair of the House Transportation and Infrastructure Committee. He has pointed to the 2024 FAA Reauthorization as one of his greatest accomplishments and has pushed the ALERT Act following a deadly midair collision at Reagan National Airport, which would require the FAA to implement aviation safety measures. The Center for Effective Lawmaking ranked him the most effective House Republican in the 118th Congress. A 2009 ethics inquiry arose over a conflict-of-interest appearance involving a committee witness tied to his wife's business, though the House Ethics Committee ultimately found no violations. In March 2026, Graves announced he would not seek re-election.
