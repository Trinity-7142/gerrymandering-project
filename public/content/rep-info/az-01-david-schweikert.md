Born to an unwed teenage mother in Los Angeles, Schweikert was placed for adoption and grew up in Scottsdale, Arizona. He earned a B.S. from Arizona State University and an M.B.A., and built a career as a business owner, realtor, and financial consultant. A Republican, he has represented AZ-01 since 2023 and is a staunch fiscal conservative. He made the U.S. national debt the centerpiece of his campaign, with a platform centered on economic prosperity through business growth, lower taxes, halting illegal immigration, and protecting constitutional freedoms. He sits on the Ways and Means Committee and chairs its Oversight Subcommittee. In 2020, Schweikert admitted to 11 ethics violation counts — including undisclosed loans, misuse of campaign funds, and improper office spending — receiving a formal House reprimand and a $50,000 fine.
