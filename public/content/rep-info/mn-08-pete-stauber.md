Pete Stauber is a retired law enforcement officer and former minor-league hockey player from Duluth, Minnesota. Representing a working-class and mostly rural district, he is only the second Republican to represent his district since 1947. Prior to Congress, Stauber served as a St. Louis County Commissioner, City Councilor in Hermantown, and Duluth police officer for over two decades. He currently serves on the House Transportation and Infrastructure, Small Business, and Natural Resources Committees, and chairs the Subcommittee on Energy and Mineral Resources. His platform centers on maintaining American energy independence and ending reliance on foreign minerals, as well as supporting law enforcement. His bipartisan American Water Stewardship Act passed the House unanimously, reauthorizing funding for the Great Lakes Restoration Initiative. In December 2020, Stauber was one of 126 Republican House members to sign an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania*, contesting the 2020 presidential election results.
