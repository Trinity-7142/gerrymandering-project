Rosa DeLauro is the Democratic U.S. Representative for Connecticut's 3rd congressional district, serving since 1991. Born and raised in New Haven to an Italian-American family, her parents were both active in New Haven civic politics. A founding member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus and the first executive director of EMILY's List, she has long championed causes such as paid family leave and raising the minimum wage. She currently serves as Ranking Member of the House Appropriations Committee and Ranking Member of the Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education Appropriations Subcommittee. In 2020, she became the second woman ever to chair the House Appropriations Committee. Her leadership was instrumental in passage of the expanded Child Tax Credit, which reduced child poverty to record lows. In late 2024, she drew national attention when she called Elon Musk "President Musk" during a House floor speech, prompting Musk to call for her expulsion from Congress.
