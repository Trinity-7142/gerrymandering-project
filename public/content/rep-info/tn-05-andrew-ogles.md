Andy Ogles is a Middle Tennessee native and entrepreneur who has owned and operated restaurants and a real estate investment business. He served as the first State Director of Americans for Prosperity Tennessee, leading grassroots coalitions for limited government and lower taxes, and later as Executive Director of the Laffer Center, which advises the White House and multiple governors on tax policy. Ogles has taken strongly conservative positions and been described by media as being on the far-right of the political spectrum. He has called for Christian nationalism in the United States and opposes abortion and same-sex marriage. His platform champions limited government, strict immigration enforcement, and a federal ban on chemical abortions. He won the general election in November 2022 with 56% of the vote, becoming the first Republican to represent the state's 5th district since the 19th century. Ogles is a member of the Freedom Caucus and was one of the original 19 members of Congress to vote against Kevin McCarthy for Speaker of the House. He has faced significant controversy: FBI agents executed a search warrant on Ogles in August 2024, and the House Ethics Committee opened an investigation into a $320,000 campaign contribution that he appears to have fabricated.
