Born in Fontana, California, and raised in a working-class family of Mexican descent in San Bernardino, Aguilar started his first job at age twelve and put himself through college with student loans and federal grants. He graduated from the University of Redlands, studying government and business administration. A mainstream Democrat, he is committed to policies that create jobs, support students, reform immigration, and safeguard programs for seniors and veterans. His congressional focus spans immigration, job creation, gun control, national security, and environmental protection. Re-elected in 2024, he serves on the House Appropriations Committee and holds the position of Chair of the House Democratic Caucus in the 119th Congress, making him the highest-ranking Latino in Congress.
