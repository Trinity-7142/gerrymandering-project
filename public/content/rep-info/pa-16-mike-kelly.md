George Joseph "Mike" Kelly Jr. (born May 10, 1948) is an American politician and businessman who has been a U.S. representative since 2011, currently representing Pennsylvania's 16th congressional district. A Butler, Pennsylvania hometown Republican, Kelly graduated from the University of Notre Dame in 1970 and built a career as a business owner before serving on the Butler school board and Butler County council. A former car dealership owner, he brings a business-minded perspective to Congress, prioritizing economic growth, healthcare reform, and strengthening manufacturing in western Pennsylvania. Kelly has served on the House Ways & Means Committee since 2013 and currently chairs its Subcommittee on Tax. He has authored legislation to expand Health Savings Accounts and influences tax and trade policy through Ways and Means. Notably, amid ballot counting in the 2020 election, Kelly filed a lawsuit to stop Pennsylvania from allowing voters to "cure" their ballots, then filed suit arguing that all mail-in ballots in the state should be discarded; the Pennsylvania Supreme Court unanimously rejected his suit. A congressional ethics watchdog also recommended subpoenaing Kelly in 2021 after his wife purchased stock in a steel company following his receipt of confidential information about it.
