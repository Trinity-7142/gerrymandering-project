Maggie Goodlander is a Democratic politician, lawyer, and former naval officer serving as the U.S. Representative for New Hampshire's 2nd congressional district since 2025. Born and raised in Nashua, she is a member of the prominent Tamposi family; her grandfather was a Republican real estate developer and her mother served as Assistant Secretary of State under President George H. W. Bush. A center-left Democrat and member of the New Democrat Coalition, her platform centers on healthcare affordability, environmental justice, and democratic accountability. Her career includes serving as a foreign policy advisor in the Senate, an intelligence officer in the Navy Reserve, a Supreme Court law clerk, a constitutional law professor, a deputy assistant attorney general at the DOJ, and a senior White House advisor. She served as counsel on the House Judiciary Committee during the first impeachment of Donald Trump. From January 2025 to April 2026, Goodlander missed 0 of 472 roll call votes.
