Born in Forest Hills, Queens, to a Jewish family, Wasserman Schultz now lives in Weston, near Fort Lauderdale. A Democrat first elected to Congress in 2004, she served as chair of the Democratic National Committee (DNC) from 2011 until her resignation in 2016. Throughout her career, she has supported pro-choice, gun control, and LGBTQ equality legislation. Key platform priorities include environmental protection of Florida's coastlines and strong support for Israel. A breast cancer survivor, she championed the EARLY Act to educate young women about breast cancer risks. She was the first Jewish congresswoman ever elected from Florida. She resigned from the DNC after WikiLeaks released emails revealing bias toward Hillary Clinton over Bernie Sanders in the 2016 primaries.
