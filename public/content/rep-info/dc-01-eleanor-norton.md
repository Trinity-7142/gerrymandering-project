Eleanor Holmes Norton (born June 13, 1937) is a Democratic politician, lawyer, and human rights activist who has represented the District of Columbia in the U.S. House since 1991. A third-generation Washingtonian, she attended Dunbar High School as a member of its last segregated class. She holds degrees from Antioch College and Yale University, including a law degree, and worked as a lawyer, law professor, and assistant legal director for the ACLU. President Jimmy Carter appointed her chair of the EEOC in 1977, making her the first woman to lead the agency. Her signature legislative focus is full congressional voting representation and statehood for D.C. Key constituent accomplishments include the D.C. Tuition Assistance Grant and a unique $5,000 D.C. homebuyer tax credit. In 2025, colleagues questioned her capacity to serve, citing declining public activity; she ultimately announced she would not seek re-election in 2026.
