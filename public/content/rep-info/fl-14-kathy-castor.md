Kathy Castor is a Democratic lawyer and politician representing Florida's 14th congressional district, serving since 2007. Born in Miami and raised in Tampa, she is the daughter of Betty Castor, a former Florida state senator, University of South Florida president, and state education commissioner. She earned a B.A. in political science from Emory University and a J.D. from Florida State University College of Law. Castor has served on the House Energy & Commerce Committee since the 111th Congress. She also chaired the Select Committee on the Climate Crisis during the 116th and 117th Congresses. Her platform focuses on healthcare access, climate and clean energy, and gun safety measures. She co-founded the bipartisan Children's Health Care Caucus with Representative Dave Reichert. She was the first woman to represent the Tampa Bay area in Congress. She won her 10th term in 2024 by more than 15 points.
