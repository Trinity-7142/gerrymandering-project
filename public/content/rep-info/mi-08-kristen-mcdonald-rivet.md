Born in Portland, Michigan, McDonald Rivet was raised by a construction-worker father and stay-at-home mother in a working-class, Catholic household. She and her sisters were the first generation in the family to go to college. A Democrat, she has served as U.S. Representative for Michigan's 8th congressional district since 2025. She campaigned on economic security and family-focused issues, with top priorities including cutting taxes for the working and middle class and lowering the cost of groceries, housing, prescription drugs, and childcare. She is the first woman to serve mid-Michigan in the U.S. House of Representatives. In Congress, she introduced the Susan Muffley Act of 2025 to restore pension benefits to 21,000 salaried Delphi auto-parts retirees. Notably, although Donald Trump carried the district by two points in the presidential race, McDonald Rivet won by nearly seven points. She was one of 46 House Democrats who joined all Republicans to vote for the Laken Riley Act.
