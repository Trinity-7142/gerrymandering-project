Bonnie Watson Coleman (born February 6, 1945) has represented New Jersey's 12th congressional district since 2015. Born in Camden, NJ, into a family with a long legacy of public service, her father John S. Watson was Mercer County's first African-American Freeholder and a six-term state assemblyman. Described as a progressive, she sits on the Appropriations and Budget Committees and chairs the Democratic Caucus's Task Force on Poverty. Her platform centers on healthcare access, civil rights, and criminal justice reform. She introduced the CROWN Act in 2021 to prohibit hair discrimination, which passed the House but stalled in the Senate. She is the first African-American woman to represent New Jersey in the U.S. House of Representatives. In November 2025, she announced she would not seek reelection.
