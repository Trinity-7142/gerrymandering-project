Raised in Dundalk, Maryland, a product of Maryland's public schools and a first-generation college graduate, Democrat Johnny Olszewski Jr. brings a working-class background to Congress. Beginning his career as a public-school teacher, he rose through Maryland politics, earning distinction as the youngest lawmaker ever to chair Baltimore County's House delegation. His state legislative legacy includes championing earned sick leave, spearheading a minimum wage increase, and casting a pivotal vote that secured marriage equality in Maryland. As Baltimore County Executive, he launched near-universal free community college and maintained fiscal responsibility affirmed by the highest AAA credit ratings. Sworn into Congress in January 2025, he secured a seat on the Democratic Steering and Policy Committee and serves on the Small Business and Foreign Affairs Committees. His platform centers on combating the cost-of-living crisis, expanding workforce development, enhancing education and housing access, and defending American democracy.
