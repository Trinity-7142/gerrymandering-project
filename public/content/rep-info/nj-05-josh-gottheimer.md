Josh Gottheimer was raised in North Caldwell, New Jersey, the son of a preschool teacher and a small business owner. A centrist Democrat, he graduated from the University of Pennsylvania, became a Thouron Fellow at Oxford, attended Harvard Law School, and later worked in the Clinton White House as one of the youngest presidential speechwriters in history. First sworn in on January 3, 2017, he is now serving his fifth term. His platform centers on bipartisanship, tax relief, law enforcement, and infrastructure. He helped launch the bipartisan Problem Solvers Caucus in 2017. He played a key role in passing the Bipartisan Infrastructure Bill, which for New Jersey includes funding for the Gateway Train Tunnel and NJ Transit improvements. He has faced criticism for stock trading practices, including failing to report trades within the mandated period and potential conflicts of interest.
