Rob Wittman grew up in Henrico County and has working-class roots, having held jobs as a short-order cook, a fishing-boat hand, and a cannery worker. He holds a Ph.D. in Public Policy and Administration from Virginia Commonwealth University, a Master of Public Health from the University of North Carolina, and a B.S. in Biology from Virginia Tech. A conservative Republican, Wittman was first elected to Congress in December 2007 and was elected to his ninth term in 2024. His platform centers on rebuilding the military, securing jobs, expanding broadband access, and improving education. He serves as Vice Chairman of the House Armed Services Committee and chairs its Tactical Air and Land Forces Subcommittee. His Chesapeake Bay Accountability Act, signed into law in 2014, increased transparency for how cleanup dollars are spent. Notably, in December 2020, Wittman was one of 126 House Republicans to sign an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania*, a Supreme Court challenge to the 2020 presidential election results; the Court declined to hear the case.
