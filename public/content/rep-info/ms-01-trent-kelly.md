John Trent Kelly is an American lawyer, politician, and retired major general from the Mississippi National Guard serving as the U.S. Representative for Mississippi's 1st congressional district since 2015. Born March 1, 1966, in Union, Mississippi, Kelly is a resident of Saltillo. A conservative Republican, he sits on the Committees on Agriculture, Armed Services, and the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. He deployed to Iraq in 2005 and again in 2009–2010, commanding over 670 troops. Kelly has received two Bronze Star medals, the Combat Action Badge, and numerous other military awards. In December 2020, Kelly was one of 126 Republican House members to sign an amicus brief supporting Texas v. Pennsylvania, contesting the 2020 presidential election results. In 2024, he voted against the $60 billion military aid package for Ukraine.
