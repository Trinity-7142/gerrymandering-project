Tom McClintock is a Republican U.S. Representative for California's 5th congressional district, serving since 2009 (renumbered from CA-04 after redistricting). Born in Westchester County, New York, he grew up in Thousand Oaks, California, and earned a degree in political science from UCLA in 1978. His district spans California's historic Sierra Nevada foothills and Central Valley, stretching from Placerville to Modesto, through Yosemite Valley and Kings Canyon. A staunch fiscal conservative, McClintock is a 13-time winner of the Club for Growth's Defender of Economic Freedom award and is widely regarded as a leader on fiscal policy. He serves on the House Judiciary Committee as Chairman of the Subcommittee on Immigration Integrity, Security, and Enforcement and is a senior member of the House Natural Resources Committee. Key legislative priorities include his Proven Forest Management Act, aimed at reducing the severity and frequency of catastrophic wildfires across the National Forest System, and the Shut Down Sanctuary Policies Act of 2026. McClintock has signed the Taxpayer Protection Pledge with Americans for Tax Reform.
