Nicholas Andrew Langworthy (born February 27, 1981) is a Republican serving as the U.S. Representative for New York's 23rd District since 2023. A native of South Dayton in the Southern Tier and the son of a small business owner, he developed a firsthand perspective on government's impact on the private sector. He previously chaired the New York State Republican Committee — a post he held after chairing the Erie County Republican Committee since 2010 — becoming the youngest state chair in party history. His campaign platform emphasized cutting spending, expanding domestic energy production, border security, and defending Second Amendment rights. In Congress, he chairs the Aviation Safety Caucus and was a key figure in the FAA Reauthorization Act of 2024. He serves on the House Rules, Energy and Commerce, and Oversight Committees. His Energy Choice Act, aimed at blocking state-level natural gas bans, passed out of the House Energy and Commerce Committee.
