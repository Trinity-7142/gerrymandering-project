Raised in SeaTac, Washington, where his father worked as a ramp serviceman at SeaTac Airport and was active in the local Machinists' Union, Democrat Adam Smith brings a working-class background to Congress. He worked his way through Fordham University loading trucks for UPS, earning a Political Science degree in 1987, before earning his law degree from the University of Washington in 1990. At 25, he became the nation's youngest state senator before winning his U.S. House seat in 1996. He serves as Ranking Member of the House Armed Services Committee, the highest-ranking Democrat on the panel since December 2010, focusing on national security, workforce development, and expanding access to health care. He introduced the End Hedge Fund Control of American Homes Act, which would require hedge funds to divest single-family homes and ultimately ban them from owning such properties. In September 2025, he led the first U.S. House delegation visit to China since 2019.
