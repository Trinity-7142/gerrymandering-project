Born in Riverdale, Georgia, Loudermilk enlisted in the U.S. Air Force after high school, serving from 1984 to 1992 as a communications operations specialist in Texas, Hawaii, and Alaska. A small business owner for over 20 years, he identifies as a Constitutional Conservative representing northwest Georgia's 11th Congressional District. His platform prioritizes a balanced budget amendment to curb deficit spending and restoring Congress's Article I authority. He also supports reforming Medicaid, Medicare, and Social Security, and repealing the Affordable Care Act. Notably, as of late 2024 he chaired the House Administration Subcommittee on Oversight, using the role to release a report targeting Liz Cheney over her role in the January 6th Committee. In February 2026, Loudermilk announced he would not seek re-election.
