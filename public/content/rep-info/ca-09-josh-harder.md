Josh Harder is a fifth-generation Central Valley resident born in Turlock, California. He earned a B.A. from Stanford and graduate degrees in business and public policy from Harvard, before working as a venture capital vice president and business consultant. A Democrat, he originally ran for Congress after his Republican representative voted to strip healthcare from people with pre-existing conditions, refusing corporate PAC money and relying on volunteers to flip the seat. His platform centers on economic growth, reducing healthcare costs, and securing sustainable water resources for agriculture. He ranked third most often among the California delegation for joining bipartisan bills, and has notably opposed the Delta Conveyance Project, citing risks to the San Joaquin Valley's ecosystem, fisheries, and agriculture.
