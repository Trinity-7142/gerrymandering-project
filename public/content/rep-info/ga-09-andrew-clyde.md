Andrew Clyde is a Republican politician and gun store owner serving as U.S. Representative for Georgia's 9th congressional district since 2021. Born in Walkerton, Ontario, Canada, he and his wife, Jennifer, live in unincorporated Jackson County. Clyde served in the U.S. Navy for 28 years and earned a master's degree in corporate finance and entrepreneurship from the University of Georgia. He is a member of the House Freedom Caucus and serves on the House Appropriations Committee and the House Budget Committee. A staunch conservative, his platform centers on Second Amendment rights, which he champions as a veteran and owner of Clyde Armory. A key legislative accomplishment came before his election: in 2019, Congress passed the Taxpayer First Act, which includes the Clyde-Hirsch-Sowers RESPECT Act, a law that limits what funds the government can seize — stemming from his own experience with IRS civil asset forfeiture. Controversially, Clyde voted against certifying Arizona's and Pennsylvania's 2020 presidential election results and described the January 6 Capitol attack as "no insurrection," likening it to a "normal tourist visit," though he had previously acknowledged helping to barricade the House chamber from the mob.
