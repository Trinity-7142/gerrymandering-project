A fourth-generation Idahoan, Russ Fulcher grew up on a dairy farm in Meridian, Idaho. He spent 24 years in the high-technology industry, working for Micron Technology and Preco Electronics, before entering public life. A member of the House Freedom Caucus, Fulcher platforms on border security, federal land reform, fiscal restraint, and free-market healthcare. Serving in the U.S. House since January 2019, he sits on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce and the House Committee on Natural Resources. His signature Idaho Senate legislation, the grocery tax credit, returned some $800 million to Idaho families. He was also the first to lead the effort to eliminate the federal income tax on overtime pay in the 118th Congress. Notably, in December 2020, Fulcher was one of 126 Republican House members to sign an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania*, contesting the results of the 2020 presidential election.
