Born in Brooklyn to Haitian immigrant parents and raised in Queens, Cherfilus-McCormick later moved to Hollywood, Florida, earning a B.A. from Howard University and a J.D. from St. Thomas University School of Law. She rose to CEO of Trinity Health Care Services, a Florida-based family home health care company. A Democrat, she won a special election in 2022 to fill the seat left vacant after the death of Alcee Hastings. She became the first Haitian Democratic member of Congress. She campaigned on progressive policies including the Green New Deal, Medicare for All, and a $1,000-a-month universal basic income. She serves on the Committee on Foreign Affairs and the Committee on Veterans' Affairs. In November 2025, she was indicted and charged with allegedly stealing $5 million in FEMA funds, money laundering, and making illegal campaign contributions; she has pleaded not guilty to those charges. A bipartisan House Ethics Committee subsequently found her guilty on 25 of 27 charges.
