A fifth-generation Kansan, Rep. Tracey Mann was born and raised on his family farm south of Quinter, growing up in a farmhouse his great-great-grandfather ordered from a Montgomery Ward catalog in the early 1900s. He earned a bachelor's degree in agricultural economics from Kansas State University in 2000. He now resides in Salina, Kansas, where he works as a commercial real estate broker. Prior to Congress, he served as the 50th Lieutenant Governor of Kansas. Mann has represented Kansas's "Big First" District in the U.S. House since January 2021 and is a conservative advocate for Kansas agriculture. He serves on the House Agriculture and Transportation and Infrastructure committees. His platform centers on farm policy, border security, Second Amendment rights, and lower taxes. Notably, on January 6, 2021, Mann voted to omit Arizona and/or Pennsylvania from the counting of presidential electors. He also voted against additional aid to Ukraine in 2024.
