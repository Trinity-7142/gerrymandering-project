Rep. Joe Neguse was born in Bakersfield, California, to Eritrean immigrant parents, and the family relocated to Colorado when he was six, eventually settling in the Denver suburbs. He is the first Eritrean-American elected to the United States Congress and Colorado's first Black member of Congress. A progressive Democrat, Neguse favors universal health care, robust funding for public education, and strong protections for public lands. He leads efforts on reducing gun violence, and lowering costs for working families through more affordable health care, childcare, prescription drugs, and housing. In March 2024, he was unanimously elected by colleagues to serve as House Assistant Minority Leader — the first Coloradan to serve in senior House Democratic leadership in nearly 90 years. Speaker Pelosi appointed him as a House impeachment manager for Trump's second impeachment trial, making him the youngest impeachment manager in U.S. history. The Center for Effective Lawmaking ranked him the second-most effective House Democrat in the 118th Congress.
