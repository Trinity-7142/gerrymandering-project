Born in Beaver Dam, Wisconsin, to a family with deep law enforcement roots — his father served as sheriff of Dodge County — Nehls spent nearly 30 years in law enforcement before his election to Congress, serving two terms as Fort Bend County Sheriff beginning in 2012. He deployed on combat tours to Iraq and Afghanistan, retiring from the U.S. Army Reserve as a Major with two Bronze Stars. A conservative Republican, his campaign platform centered on improving health care for veterans and protecting oil and gas jobs in Texas. In the 118th Congress, he chaired the House Transportation & Infrastructure Subcommittee on Railroads, Pipelines, and Hazardous Materials. The Office of Congressional Ethics found probable cause that Nehls converted campaign funds for personal use — allegations he has denied. In November 2025, Nehls announced he would not seek reelection in 2026 in order to focus on his family.
