James Robert Walkinshaw (born October 22, 1982) is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Virginia's 11th Congressional District since September 2025. He grew up in Prince William County and earned his bachelor's degree from New York University. His father was a plumber, reflecting a working-class upbringing. He served for more than a decade as Chief of Staff to the late Congressman Gerry Connolly before being elected to the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors in 2019. His campaign focused on supporting local businesses, healthcare, and protecting federal jobs. In Congress, he founded the first-ever Congressional Federal Workforce Caucus and has led efforts to block Schedule F and protect the nonpartisan civil service. He is the only Democrat serving on both the House Oversight Committee and the House Homeland Security Committee. His nomination drew scrutiny when rival candidates alleged the compressed primary timeline gave Walkinshaw an unfair advantage.
