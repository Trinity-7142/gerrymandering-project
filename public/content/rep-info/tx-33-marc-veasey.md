Marc Veasey is a Democrat serving Texas's 33rd congressional district since 2013. Born January 3, 1971, he grew up in rental homes in the Stop Six neighborhood of Fort Worth. His pre-congressional career included work as a legislative aide, health care consultant, and sportswriter; he served in the Texas House of Representatives from 2004 to 2012. He is the first African-American U.S. Representative elected from Tarrant County. Veasey has campaigned on expanding access to affordable health care, providing low-income communities with career opportunities, ensuring abortion access, and funding infrastructure. In the 118th Congress, he served on the Energy and Commerce and House Armed Services Committees. He has served as the named plaintiff in litigation against Texas's voter ID law. In July 2024, he made headlines as the first Congressional Black Caucus member to publicly urge President Biden to step aside from the presidential race.
