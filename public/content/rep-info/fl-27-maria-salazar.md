Born in Miami's Little Havana neighborhood as the daughter of Cuban exiles, Salazar holds a B.A. in Communications from the University of Miami and a Master of Public Administration from Harvard University. A five-time Emmy Award-winning journalist, she is notably the only U.S. Spanish-language journalist to have interviewed Fidel Castro one-on-one. She has served as U.S. Representative for FL-27 since 2021 and is a Republican assistant whip. She serves on the House Foreign Affairs Committee as Chair of the Western Hemisphere Subcommittee and on the House Financial Services Committee, focusing on individual rights, economic development, and immigration reform. Her signature DIGNIDAD Act proposes a pathway to legal status for up to 12 million undocumented immigrants and citizenship for DACA recipients. Notably, she was reported to have potentially violated the STOCK Act in 2022 over an undisclosed share exchange worth up to $500,000.
