Born in Philadelphia to Indian parents, whose family's roots in public service trace to his grandfather's participation in Gandhi's independence movement, Rohit "Ro" Khanna has served as the U.S. Representative for California's 17th Congressional District since 2017. He graduated Phi Beta Kappa in Economics from the University of Chicago and holds a law degree from Yale University. A self-described "progressive capitalist," Khanna has called for a "new economic patriotism" as a governing philosophy. His platform centers on Medicare for All, affordable childcare, and free public college, alongside anti-corruption reform. He authored the Endless Frontier Act, which formed the basis for the CHIPS and Science Act signed into law by President Biden. Khanna has faced backlash from some Silicon Valley tech leaders over his embrace of a wealth tax, and has built a national profile as one of the strongest advocates for banning stock trading by members of Congress.
