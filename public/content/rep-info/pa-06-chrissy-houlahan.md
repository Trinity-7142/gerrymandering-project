Rep. Christina "Chrissy" Houlahan is a Democrat serving Pennsylvania's 6th Congressional District since 2019. The district covers nearly all of Chester County, a suburban area west of Philadelphia, and the southern portion of Berks County including Reading. A third-generation veteran, she earned an engineering degree from Stanford on an AFROTC scholarship and a master's in Technology and Policy from MIT. She previously served as COO of AND1 Basketball, co-founded B-Lab, and taught through Teach for America. She is the first woman elected to represent the district. Her platform centers on healthcare, job creation, campaign finance reform, education, and veterans' issues. She serves on both the House Armed Services Committee and the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. In November 2025, Houlahan drew national attention after appearing in a video advising servicemembers they could refuse illegal orders; President Trump responded by calling the lawmakers involved traitors deserving sedition charges.
