Mary Gay Scanlon is an American attorney and Democrat who has represented Pennsylvania's 5th congressional district since 2019. She lives in Swarthmore and comes from a public-service family — her father served as a U.S. magistrate and her mother was a college English professor. Before Congress, she led class-action lawsuits at the Education Law Center to enforce the rights of students with disabilities. She serves on the House Judiciary Committee and the Committee on Rules, and is Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on the Constitution and Limited Government. Her legislative focus centers on children's welfare, healthcare, and civil liberties; she led House floor debate on the Equality Act and the reauthorization of the Violence Against Women Act. She was notably the victim of a carjacking at gunpoint in South Philadelphia in December 2021, though she was physically unharmed.
