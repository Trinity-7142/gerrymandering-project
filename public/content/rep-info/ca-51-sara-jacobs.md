Rep. Sara Jacobs is a third-generation San Diegan representing California's 51st Congressional District, which includes San Diego, El Cajon, La Mesa, and Lemon Grove. Her grandfather is billionaire Irwin M. Jacobs, co-founder of semiconductor giant Qualcomm, and with an estimated net worth of $76 million, she is one of the wealthiest members of Congress. A progressive Democrat, her platform centers on addressing climate change, ending gun violence, affordable housing, healthcare as a human right, and defending reproductive care. Before Congress, she worked at the UN, UNICEF, and the State Department, and advised Hillary Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign. She introduced the *My Body, My Data Act* to protect reproductive health data and became the first Member of Congress to discuss her period on the House Floor. She was also arrested at a Supreme Court abortion-rights demonstration in July 2022. She serves on the House Foreign Affairs and Armed Services Committees, and is the youngest member of California's congressional delegation and of Democratic House leadership.
