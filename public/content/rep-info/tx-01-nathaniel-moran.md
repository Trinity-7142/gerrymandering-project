Nathaniel Moran is an American politician and attorney who has served as the U.S. Representative for Texas's 1st Congressional District since 2023. Born in Whitehouse, Texas, he graduated from Whitehouse High School, earned a law degree from Texas Tech University, and worked as an attorney and partner at Ramey & Flock, P.C. He also attended West Point for two years and holds a BA in Russian and an MBA from Texas Tech. A conservative Republican, he has championed the values of faith, family, and freedom throughout his career. He began his elected service on the Tyler City Council, became Smith County Judge in 2016, and in Congress serves on the Ways and Means Committee. He also sits on the China Select Committee. Moran was among the 71 Republicans who voted against final passage of the Fiscal Responsibility Act of 2023. He was elected in 2022 with nearly 80% of the vote and ran unopposed in 2024.
