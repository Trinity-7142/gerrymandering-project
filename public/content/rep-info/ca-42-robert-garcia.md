Rep. Robert Garcia immigrated from Peru to the United States as a young child and was raised in Southern California, becoming a U.S. citizen in his 20s. A career educator and former Mayor of Long Beach, he is currently serving his second term representing Long Beach to Southeast Los Angeles in Congress. He is the first openly LGBTQ immigrant to serve in Congress and the first Peruvian American elected to Congress. A progressive Democrat, Garcia advocates for progressive education policy, addressing the climate crisis, supporting working families, and protecting rights for women, immigrants, and the LGBTQ+ community. In his first term, he championed pro-housing legislation and co-founded the bipartisan Congressional YIMBY Caucus to increase housing supply and address affordability. He currently serves as Ranking Member of the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee and was the first House Democratic second-term member elected to serve as Ranking Member of a committee in over 100 years. Garcia was also a leading figure in the expulsion of Representative George Santos.
