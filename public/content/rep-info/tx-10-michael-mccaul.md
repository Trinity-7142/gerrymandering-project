Michael McCaul (born January 14, 1962) is a Republican attorney and politician who calls Austin, Texas, home. He earned a B.A. in History from Trinity University in 1984 and a J.D. from St. Mary's University. Prior to Congress, McCaul served as Chief of Counter Terrorism and National Security in the U.S. Attorney's office and led the Joint Terrorism Task Force. He previously chaired the House Committee on Homeland Security and, during the 118th Congress, became the first Texan to chair the Foreign Affairs Committee. His platform centers on border security, including support for a border wall and the Remain in Mexico policy, as well as strong foreign policy — notably advocating for heavily arming Ukraine and sending fighter jets and missiles. China sanctioned McCaul in April 2023, alleging interference in its "internal affairs." In November 2024, McCaul was briefly detained at Dulles Airport after appearing intoxicated, attributing the incident to mixing Ambien with alcohol. In September 2025, he announced he would not seek re-election in 2026.
