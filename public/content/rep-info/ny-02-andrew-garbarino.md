Born and raised in Sayville, New York, Garbarino graduated from George Washington University and earned his J.D. from Hofstra University School of Law, after which he worked at his family's law firm in Sayville. He has served as the U.S. Representative for New York's 2nd Congressional District since 2021. A moderate Republican, Garbarino is a member of the Republican Governance Group and is known for frequently breaking with his party on high-profile issues. His platform centers on lowering taxes, protecting 9/11 health care, strengthening national security, and supporting local communities. A notable accomplishment is his leading role in securing full, lifetime funding for the World Trade Center Health Program, guaranteeing uninterrupted care for 9/11 responders and survivors. He has also crossed party lines to support the Bipartisan Background Checks Act, the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, and the Respect for Marriage Act. He currently chairs the House Homeland Security Committee.
