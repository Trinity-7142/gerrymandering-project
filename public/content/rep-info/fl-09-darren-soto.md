Darren Soto is a Democratic attorney and legislator from Kissimmee, Florida, born to a Puerto Rican father and an Italian-American mother. He earned a B.A. in economics from Rutgers University and a J.D. from George Washington University. He is the first congressman of Puerto Rican descent elected to represent Florida, serving in Congress since 2017. A progressive Democrat, his platform centers on defending the Affordable Care Act, protecting reproductive rights, reducing prescription drug costs, and addressing gun violence. In Congress, he serves on the Energy and Commerce Committee and the Natural Resources Committee. Notable accomplishments include legislation to increase hurricane readiness, protect Florida's coasts from foreign offshore oil drilling, designate the Pulse National Memorial, and grant Temporary Protective Status to Venezuelan refugees.
