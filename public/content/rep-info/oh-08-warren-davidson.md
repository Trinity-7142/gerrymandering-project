Warren Earl Davidson (born March 1, 1970) is a Republican U.S. Representative for Ohio's 8th congressional district, serving since 2016. Born and raised in Sidney, Ohio, he enlisted in the Army after high school, was stationed in Germany with the 3rd Infantry Division — where he witnessed the fall of the Berlin Wall — and later attended West Point, graduating in 1995. He subsequently earned an MBA from Notre Dame and returned to Ohio to help run the family manufacturing business. Upon election, Davidson joined the House Freedom Caucus and serves on the Committee on Financial Services and the Committee on Foreign Affairs. A fiscal conservative, he has signed the Taxpayer Protection Pledge with Americans for Tax Reform and founded the Sound Money Caucus in 2020, focused on financial stability and dollar hegemony. He has been a prominent voice on digital assets legislation and has consistently opposed large-scale aid packages for Ukraine. On January 7, 2021, Davidson objected to the certification of electors in the 2020 presidential election, alleging widespread voter fraud.
