Dave Min is a Democratic U.S. Representative serving California's 47th Congressional District since 2025. Born in Providence, Rhode Island, and raised in Palo Alto, California, he is the son of South Korean immigrants who came to the United States to pursue doctoral degrees. Before entering elected office, Min was a law professor at UC Irvine, where he was considered one of the country's leading experts on banking and housing policy. As a state senator, he shepherded 28 bills into law, including legislation enshrining abortion in the California Constitution, expanding protections for domestic violence survivors, and banning gun sales on state property. His platform centers on healthcare access, climate action, and gun safety reform, earning him 100% ratings from Planned Parenthood and the Sierra Club, and an 'F' rating from the NRA. During his 2024 campaign, Min was arrested and convicted for a DUI; he declined calls to withdraw from the race and went on to win the seat.
