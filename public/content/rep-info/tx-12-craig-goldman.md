Rep. Craig Goldman serves Texas' 12th Congressional District, covering parts of Tarrant and Parker Counties. A fifth-generation Texan and fourth-generation Fort Worth native, his career background includes running a family retail gourmet food and wine store, working on political campaigns, and serving as a partner in real estate investment companies. A conservative Republican, his platform focuses on securing the border, reducing taxes, and strengthening national defense. He previously served twelve years in the Texas House, chairing both the House Energy Resources Committee and the House Republican Caucus. His proudest state-level accomplishment is Molly Jane's Law, requiring police to log sexual assault reports into a national criminal database to help stop repeat offenders. Goldman is one of only a few Jewish Republicans ever elected to Congress and his 2024 primary was notable for pitting him against a Paxton-backed opponent after Goldman voted to impeach the Texas Attorney General.
