Born in Racine, Wisconsin in 1951 and raised in Milwaukee, Rep. Gwen Moore is the daughter of a factory worker and a public school teacher. She began college at Marquette University as a single, expectant mother on welfare, completing her degree with the help of the federal TRIO program. A progressive Democrat, she believes the federal government should play a significant role in the amelioration of poverty and the resolution of difficult local problems. First elected in 2004, she made history as the first African American elected to Congress from Wisconsin. She serves on the House Ways and Means Committee, which has jurisdiction over Social Security, Medicare, taxation, and Temporary Assistance for Needy Families. Her platform centers on anti-poverty policy, women's health, maternal and infant mortality, domestic violence, and leading efforts to reauthorize the Violence Against Women Act. She has been the primary sponsor of 11 enacted bills, including the *'Six Triple Eight' Congressional Gold Medal Act of 2021*.
