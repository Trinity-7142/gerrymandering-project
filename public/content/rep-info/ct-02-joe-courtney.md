Joseph David Courtney (born April 6, 1953) is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Connecticut's 2nd congressional district since 2007. He grew up in suburban Hartford, worked as a public defender after law school, and later became a law firm partner and Vernon town attorney. A center-left Democrat and member of the New Democrat Coalition, Courtney focuses on defense jobs, education, and healthcare. He is known for delivering submarine base funding and earned the nickname "Two-Sub Joe." He serves on the House Armed Services Committee and the Education and Workforce Committee, serving as Ranking Member of the Seapower and Projection Forces Subcommittee. In October 2024, he was appointed an Honorary Officer in the Order of Australia for his role advancing the AUKUS security agreement.
