Rep. Gabe Evans (born July 28, 1986) is a Republican politician and former law enforcement and U.S. Army officer serving as Colorado's 8th congressional district representative since 2025. The grandson of Mexican immigrants, the Fort Lupton Republican went to Congress after one term in the Colorado House of Representatives. Prior to public office, Evans served 12 years in the U.S. Army and Colorado Army National Guard as a UH-60 Black Hawk helicopter pilot and company commander, and 11 years as an Arvada police officer. His top priorities include securing the border, improving cost of living, and increasing public safety. He serves on the Homeland Security and Energy and Commerce committees. Evans defeated Democratic incumbent Yadira Caraveo in the 2024 election, and drew scrutiny during the campaign after firing his political director following reports of her promoting extremist content online. From January 2025 to March 2026, Evans missed zero of 470 roll call votes.
