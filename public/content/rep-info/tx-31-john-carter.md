Born in Houston, Carter has spent most of his life in Central Texas. He earned a history degree from Texas Tech University in 1964 and a J.D. from the University of Texas School of Law in 1969. In 1981, he was appointed judge of the 277th District Court of Williamson County, becoming the first Republican elected to a countywide position there. A conservative Republican, Carter retired from the bench in 2001 to run for Congress in the newly created 31st District. His platform emphasizes border security, fiscal restraint, and strong support for the military; he chairs the Military Construction and Veterans Affairs Appropriations Subcommittee. He passed the Veterans Transplant Coverage Act after a constituent was denied live-donor coverage from the VA. On January 6, 2021, Carter voted against certifying the results of the 2020 presidential election. He was reelected to his 13th term in 2024.
