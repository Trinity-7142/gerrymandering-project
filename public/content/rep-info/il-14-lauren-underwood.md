Lauren Underwood is a Democratic U.S. Representative and registered nurse serving Illinois's 14th Congressional District. She grew up in Naperville, Illinois, earned a nursing degree from the University of Michigan and two master's degrees from Johns Hopkins University, and began her career as a policy professional in the Obama administration, later serving as a senior advisor at the Department of Health and Human Services. Her platform has centered on strengthening the Affordable Care Act, expanding job opportunities, infrastructure improvements, and paid family leave. In 2018, she defeated Republican incumbent Randy Hultgren and, upon being sworn in, became the youngest Black woman to serve in Congress. In 2022, she was elected co-chair of the House Democratic Policy and Communications Committee, becoming the first Black woman to hold an elected Democratic leadership position since Shirley Chisholm in the 1970s. She has had eighteen pieces of legislation signed into law by both Democratic and Republican presidents. She is also co-founder and co-chair of the Black Maternal Health Caucus.
