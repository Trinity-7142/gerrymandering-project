Born in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, Michael Cloud is a Republican representative based in Victoria, Texas. Before entering politics, he was a small business owner and founder of a video production company, and served as media and communications director at Faith Family Church for over a decade. He is a member of the House Freedom Caucus and serves on its board of directors. His platform centers on promoting the south Texas economy, maintaining a strong national defense, and stricter border and immigration enforcement. He has also prioritized veterans' care and VA reform, and opposes taxpayer-funded abortions. Cloud is a member of the House Department of Government Efficiency Committee and serves as Vice Chairman of the House Appropriations Subcommittee on Energy and Water Development. In December 2020, he was one of 126 House Republicans to sign an amicus brief supporting Texas v. Pennsylvania, a Supreme Court challenge to the 2020 presidential election results.
