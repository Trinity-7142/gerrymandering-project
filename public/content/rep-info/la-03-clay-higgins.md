Born in New Orleans, the seventh of eight children, Higgins grew up in Covington, Louisiana. Raised on a horse ranch in South Louisiana, he attended LSU before serving in the U.S. Army and Louisiana National Guard, attaining the rank of Staff Sergeant. He gained viral fame through "Crime Stoppers" videos as a St. Landry Parish captain, earning the nickname "Cajun John Wayne," which propelled his 2016 congressional run. A Freedom Caucus member, Higgins champions smaller government, lower taxes, secure borders, and individual freedoms. The Center for Effective Lawmaking has ranked him among the most effective Republican legislators in the House. Controversies include floating an unfounded conspiracy theory that FBI agents infiltrated January 6 protests via "ghost buses," making offensive remarks about Haitian immigrants in 2024, which drew condemnation from the Congressional Black Caucus before he deleted the post, and being the sole member of Congress to vote against the Epstein Files Transparency Act in November 2025.
