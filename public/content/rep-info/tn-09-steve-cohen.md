Steve Cohen is an American attorney and politician serving as the U.S. Representative from Tennessee's 9th congressional district since 2007. A fourth-generation Memphian and grandson of Jewish immigrants, Cohen is Tennessee's first Jewish congressman. His voting record and legislative priorities place him on the left of the political spectrum; he is a member of the Progressive Caucus. Known as the "Father of the Tennessee Lottery," he championed the education initiative after nearly twenty years of effort, with more than $6.5 billion ultimately going to Tennessee students. He serves on the House Judiciary Committee, the Transportation and Infrastructure Committee, and the Committee on Ethics. Key policy priorities include police accountability, having introduced legislation requiring independent investigation of deadly-force incidents and greater use of body cameras. Speaker Pelosi called him the "conscience of the freshman class" in 2008; that year he was instrumental in passing the first House resolution apologizing for the enslavement and racial segregation of African Americans.
