Rep. Juan Vargas (D) was raised on a chicken ranch in National City and is one of ten children whose father immigrated from Mexico via the Bracero Program. As a young adult, he entered the Jesuits and worked with disadvantaged communities in El Salvador. He holds a J.D. from Harvard Law School. His district covers south San Diego County and California's entire U.S.–Mexico border; he champions comprehensive immigration reform, DREAMer protections, and preserving Medicare and Social Security. He serves on the House Committee on Financial Services. He secured $200 million in the Water Resources Development Act of 2024 to address sewage pollution in the Tijuana River Valley. A noted controversy arose in 2019 when Vargas spent over $124,000 in campaign funds on holiday cards to constituents.
