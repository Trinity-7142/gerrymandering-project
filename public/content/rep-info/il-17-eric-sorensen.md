Born in Rockford, Illinois, Eric Sorensen is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 17th Congressional District since 2023. He served communities across Central and Northwestern Illinois as a local TV meteorologist for over two decades before entering politics. Sorensen is a member of the New Democrat Coalition, and is the first openly gay member of Congress from Illinois. He is focused on lowering costs for working families, creating sustainable jobs, and supporting family farms and rural communities. In March 2024, Sorensen secured $123 million for western Illinois infrastructure projects, including water main repairs, road improvements, and river navigation upgrades. He was appointed to the House Armed Services Committee for the 119th Congress. Notably, in 2025, Sorensen was one of 46 House Democrats who joined all Republicans to vote for the Laken Riley Act.
