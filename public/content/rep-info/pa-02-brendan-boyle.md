Rep. Brendan F. Boyle was born and raised in Philadelphia, specifically in the Olney neighborhood. The son of an immigrant, his father was a janitor for SEPTA and his mother was a school crossing guard. The first in his family to attend college, he earned a degree from the University of Notre Dame and later a master's in Public Policy from Harvard's Kennedy School. A Democrat serving in Congress since 2015, he has represented PA-02 — entirely within Philadelphia — since 2019. He serves as Ranking Member of the House Budget Committee. His policy platforms include tackling climate change, creating high-paying jobs for Philadelphians, and student loan forgiveness and reform. He co-founded the Blue Collar Caucus to address wage stagnation, job insecurity, and offshoring. He also serves as Lead Democrat for the U.S. Congressional Delegation to the NATO Parliamentary Assembly.
