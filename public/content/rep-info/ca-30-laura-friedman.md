Laura Friedman is an American politician and former film producer from a Jewish family in New York, who earned a B.A. from the University of Rochester before relocating to Glendale, California, in 2000. A Democrat and member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, she previously represented California's 44th State Assembly District and served as mayor of Glendale. She became the first woman to hold the CA-30 seat, defeating physician Alex Balekian with 68.4% of the vote. Her platform centers on affordable housing, sustainable communities, and local investment. Notable accomplishments include authoring legislation making California the first state to ban fur sales and eliminating minimum parking requirements for housing near mass transit.
