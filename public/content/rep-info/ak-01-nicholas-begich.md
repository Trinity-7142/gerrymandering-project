Born in Anchorage and raised by his maternal grandparents, Nicholas Joseph Begich III is a Republican politician and businessman who has served as the U.S. representative for Alaska's at-large congressional district since 2025. He pursued higher education in the Lower 48, earning a BBA in Entrepreneurship from Baylor University and later an MBA from Indiana University. After graduating, he founded FarShore Partners, a software development company. A conservative aligned with the Freedom Caucus, Begich's platform centers on Alaska resource development: he secured mandated oil and gas lease sales in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, the National Petroleum Reserve-Alaska, and Cook Inlet through the One Big Beautiful Bill Act. He also sponsored legislation to make the Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend federally tax-free. He won his seat in November 2024, defeating incumbent Democrat Mary Peltola with 51.3% of the vote in the final ranked-choice round.
