Born on September 25, 1961, in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, to Cuban parents, Republican Rep. Mario Díaz-Balart is a conservative lawmaker who resides in Miami and represents a South Florida district covering Miami-Dade, Collier, and Monroe counties. His aunt, Mirta Díaz-Balart, was the first wife of Cuban president Fidel Castro — a family tie that has shaped his staunch anti-communist foreign policy views. Before joining the U.S. House, Díaz-Balart served in the Florida State Legislature in both the House and Senate, and was the youngest person ever elected to the Florida Senate. He has served in Congress from 2003 through the present and is the Dean of the Florida Congressional Delegation and Deputy Whip. His platform prioritizes eliminating government waste, supporting small businesses, securing the border, promoting energy independence, and countering adversaries such as Communist China, Cuba, Venezuela, and Iran. As Vice Chair of the House Appropriations Committee and Chairman of the National Security, Department of State, and Related Programs Subcommittee, his FY2024 funding bill achieved the largest year-to-year funding cut of all appropriations bills, at six percent.
