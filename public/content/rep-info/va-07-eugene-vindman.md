Eugene Vindman is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Virginia's 7th congressional district since 2025. He arrived in the U.S. at age four as a refugee fleeing Soviet Ukraine with less than $800. He grew up in New York, attended public schools and university, and received his ROTC commission as a 2nd Lieutenant. He served 25 years as a paratrooper, infantryman, and Judge Advocate General's Corps attorney, and was deployed to Iraq. A deputy legal advisor on the National Security Council, he came to national prominence after reporting a 2019 Trump–Ukraine phone call to senior White House lawyers. His platform priorities include protecting abortion rights and IVF, supporting the military community, delivering high-quality public education, and investing in local transportation infrastructure. In Congress, he serves on the House Armed Services Committee and has championed bipartisan whistleblower protection legislation. He and his wife Cindy reside in Dale City, Virginia.
