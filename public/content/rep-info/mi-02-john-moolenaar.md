Born in Midland, Michigan, Moolenaar worked at Dow Chemical Company as a chemist before entering public life. He holds a B.S. in chemistry from Hope College and a Master of Public Administration from Harvard University. A conservative Republican, his platform centers on border security, cutting federal spending, rebuilding the military, economic growth, and opposing abortion. He served in the Michigan House of Representatives from 2003–2008 and the Michigan State Senate from 2011–2014 before being elected to Congress in 2014. A notable accomplishment is his role as chair of the Select Committee on Strategic Competition between the U.S. and the Chinese Communist Party. In December 2020, Moolenaar was one of 126 House Republicans to sign an amicus brief contesting the results of the 2020 presidential election.
