The son of an ironworker and a postal clerk, Lynch was born and raised in the public housing projects of South Boston. Working as a structural ironworker for 18 years, he was eventually elected president of the Iron Workers Union — the youngest president in the history of the 2000-member union. He later received a law degree from Boston College Law School and continued his advocacy for working people as a labor and employment attorney. A Democrat serving in Congress since 2001, Lynch represents a district covering the southern portion of Boston and many of its southern suburbs. He is a center-left Democrat whose platform centers on fair wages, economic security, housing assistance, and preserving Social Security. He serves on the Financial Services Committee as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Digital Assets, Financial Technology and Inclusion, and is lead Democrat on the Bipartisan Working Group on Artificial Intelligence. He is also co-founder of the Congressional Labor and Working Families Caucus. Lynch ran for the Democratic Senate nomination in 2013, losing to Ed Markey.
