Betty McCollum is a Democratic politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Minnesota's 4th Congressional District since 2001. A Minnesota native who grew up in South St. Paul, she represents the residents of Saint Paul as well as Ramsey and most of Washington County. Prior to public office, McCollum worked as a high school social studies teacher and as a retail store manager. She made history in 2000 as only the second Minnesota woman elected to serve in Congress since statehood in 1858. Throughout her career, she has championed education, environmental protection, expanded healthcare access, and international engagement prioritizing diplomacy and human rights. In the 119th Congress, she serves as the top Democrat on the House Appropriations Defense Subcommittee. She has also introduced legislation addressing the human rights of Palestinian children under Israeli military occupation, a notable and sometimes controversial foreign policy stance. McCollum has been Dean of Minnesota's congressional delegation since 2021.
