Born in Southern California to working-class Mexican immigrant parents — a domestic worker and a farm laborer — Gomez worked fast-food and retail jobs after high school before earning a B.A. from UCLA and a Master's in Public Policy from Harvard's Kennedy School. A former labor organizer, he served as legislative and political director for the United Nurses Associations of California and as political representative for AFSCME. A Democrat, Gomez served in the California State Assembly from 2012 to 2017 before winning his congressional seat. His platform centers on housing affordability, reproductive rights, healthcare, and criminal justice reform. He is a founding member of the Medicare for All Caucus and founder and chair of the Congressional Dads Caucus and Congressional Renters Caucus. Gomez voted to support Israel following the October 7 attacks but called for a permanent ceasefire by November 2023; he later drew scrutiny for having received significant donations from pro-Israel lobby groups.
