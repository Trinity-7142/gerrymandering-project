Rep. Lloyd Smucker was born in Lancaster County, Pennsylvania, and was raised in a family that belonged to the Old Order Amish community until he was five years old. Prior to entering politics, he owned and operated the Smucker Company — a commercial construction firm — for 25 years, growing it to employ more than 150 people. A conservative Republican, Smucker serves as a senior member of the Ways and Means Committee and Vice Chair of the Budget Committee, championing increased economic opportunity, reduced federal spending, and regulatory relief for small businesses. His legislative priorities include workforce development through bills like the USA Workforce Investment Act and the Essential Workers for Economic Advancement Act. He supports repealing the Affordable Care Act and favors strict immigration enforcement. Notably, Smucker voted to overturn the Electoral College results following the 2020 presidential election. He won his fifth term in 2024 by a 26-point margin.
