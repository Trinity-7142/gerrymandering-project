Bryan Steil is a Republican attorney and businessman from Janesville, Wisconsin, holding degrees from Georgetown University and the University of Wisconsin School of Law. He worked as an aide to Rep. Paul Ryan in 2003 before spending a decade in the manufacturing industry in southeast Wisconsin. His top policy issues are workforce development, trade, and the student loan debt crisis. He has also advocated for border security and greater price transparency in the medical industry. Since 2023, he has chaired the House Administration Committee, a role in which he led hearings contributing to the ouster of Architect of the Capitol Brett Blanton. He voted against Republican-sponsored objections to Arizona's and Pennsylvania's electoral votes, helping certify Joe Biden's 2020 victory. In 2024, Steil won reelection by 19 percentage points.
