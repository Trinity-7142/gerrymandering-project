Lou Correa (D) has represented California's 46th Congressional District — covering majority-Hispanic areas of Orange County, including most of Anaheim, all of Santa Ana, and parts of Orange — since 2017. The son of working-class immigrants, his mother cleaned hotel rooms near Disneyland and his father worked at a paper mill. He earned a B.A. in Economics from Cal State Fullerton, and both an MBA and J.D. from UCLA. A centrist Democrat, Correa is committed to working across party lines to strengthen the middle class through investment in education, healthcare, and infrastructure. He has championed affordable higher education and introduced legislation to protect immigrant legal rights and care for veterans. Recent legislative priorities include the Student Loan Bankruptcy Improvement Act of 2025, which would loosen criteria for student loan discharge, and the Medical Debt Relief Act, which would ban medical debt from appearing on credit reports. He previously served in the California State Assembly, on the Orange County Board of Supervisors, and in the California State Senate.
