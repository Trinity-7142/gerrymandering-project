Born in Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico, and raised in Tucson, Arizona, Ciscomani immigrated to the United States as a young boy, where his father worked as a bus driver — a working-class upbringing that shaped his values of hard work and perseverance. He worked his way through school with maintenance and service jobs, attending Pima Community College and the University of Arizona, becoming the first member of his family to graduate from college. A Republican, he was elected in 2022, becoming the first naturalized American citizen from Mexico elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in Arizona history. He focuses on border security, economic growth, and water resources. As a member of the Appropriations and Veterans Affairs committees, he champions fiscal responsibility and advocates for the district's over 70,000 veterans. He has been named the most bipartisan member of Arizona's House delegation by both the Center for Effective Lawmaking and the Georgetown University Bipartisan Index. He was chosen to deliver the Republican response to the 2023 State of the Union Address in Spanish.
