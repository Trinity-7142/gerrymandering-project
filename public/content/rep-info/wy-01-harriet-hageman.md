Born in Douglas, Wyoming, and raised on a ranch outside Fort Laramie, Hageman comes from a family with deep political roots — her father served as a longtime member of the Wyoming House of Representatives. She attended Casper College on a livestock judging scholarship and earned both her bachelor's degree and law degree from the University of Wyoming. Hageman calls herself an "unyielding conservative" and is nationally known for challenging federal overreach, protecting water and property rights, and fighting federal land and wildlife mismanagement. She is a member of the Freedom Caucus. With the endorsement of President Donald Trump, Hageman defeated incumbent Liz Cheney by a landslide in the 2022 Republican primary. In the 119th Congress, she chairs the Subcommittee on Water, Wildlife, and Fisheries on the House Natural Resources Committee. A 2025 town hall drew controversy when Hageman faced a hostile audience over federal downsizing and ended the event early.
