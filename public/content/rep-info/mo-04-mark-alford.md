Mark Alford is a Republican politician and former television news anchor serving as the U.S. Representative for Missouri's 4th congressional district since 2023. Born in Baytown, Texas, he attended Sterling High School and the University of Texas at Austin, but left without graduating. In 1998, he joined WDAF-TV in Kansas City as an anchor for Fox 4 News, remaining there for 23 years. He and his wife, Leslie, live in Lake Winnebago, a suburb of Kansas City. A staunch conservative, his platform emphasizes protecting farmers from EPA overregulation, defending Second Amendment rights, and securing the border. He serves on the House Armed Services and Agriculture Committees, advocating for increased military resources and rural broadband expansion. From January 2023 to March 2026, Alford missed just 0.9% of roll call votes, better than the median among currently serving representatives. He drew attention for supporting federal workforce reductions, telling affected employees at a Missouri event that "God has a plan and purpose for your life."
