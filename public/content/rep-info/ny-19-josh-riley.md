Born in Endicott, New York, from a family that spent generations working in local factories, Josh Riley is a Democratic politician and lawyer serving as U.S. Representative for New York's 19th congressional district since 2025. He earned a bachelor's degree from the College of William & Mary and a J.D. from Harvard Law School. His career includes serving as a policy analyst at the U.S. Department of Labor and as general counsel to former Sen. Al Franken on the U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee. He pledges to accept no corporate PAC money and campaigns on fighting corruption, defending abortion rights, securing the border, and lowering costs. After losing a 2022 race to Republican Marc Molinaro, Riley defeated him in a 2024 rematch with 51.1% of the vote. He sits on the House Committees on Agriculture and Science, Space, and Technology. Notable legislative efforts include the bipartisan Fertilizer Transparency Act to mandate USDA reporting on fertilizer prices.
