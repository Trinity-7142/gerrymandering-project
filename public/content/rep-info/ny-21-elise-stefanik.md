Elise Stefanik (born July 2, 1984, Albany, New York) is a Republican whose parents owned Premium Plywood Products, a small family business where she worked from a young age. She has served as the U.S. Representative for New York's 21st congressional district since 2015. At the time of her first election, she was the youngest woman ever elected to Congress. Originally elected as a moderate Republican, she moved toward the right after aligning herself with Donald Trump. Her platform has centered on supporting small businesses, farmers, and veterans; lowering energy costs; and strengthening rural healthcare in the North Country. From 2021 to 2025, she served as Chair of the House Republican Conference. She gained wide national attention for her questioning of Ivy League university presidents on campus antisemitism, which led to multiple presidential resignations. Trump nominated her as UN Ambassador, but later withdrew her nomination citing concerns about losing her House seat given the GOP's slim majority. She announced in late 2025 she would not seek re-election to the House, briefly explored a gubernatorial run, and then suspended that campaign as well.
