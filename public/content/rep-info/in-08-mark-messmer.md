A Jasper, Indiana native, Rep. Mark Messmer earned a B.S. in engineering from Purdue University in 1985 and returned to Jasper to join his family's business, Messmer Mechanical, which performs commercial and industrial plumbing and HVAC work. A Republican, he previously served in the Indiana Senate from 2014 to 2024, including as majority leader from 2018 to 2022. His campaign describes him as a "commonsense conservative" focused on lowering taxes, eliminating regulation, and championing individual liberty. He defeated former U.S. Representative John Hostettler in the primary by roughly 20 points and won the seat following the retirement of longtime Rep. Larry Bucshon. In Congress, he serves on the Committees on Armed Services, Agriculture, and Education and Workforce. He has introduced legislation to repeal a Biden-era OSHA workplace heat-safety rule and has been an active sponsor of anti-abortion measures.
