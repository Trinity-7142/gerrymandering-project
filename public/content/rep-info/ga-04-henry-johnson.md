Henry Calvin "Hank" Johnson Jr. is an American lawyer and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Georgia's 4th congressional district since 2007. His hometown is Lithonia, Georgia, and he is a Democrat. He is a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus and the Congressional Black Caucus. Elected to his 10th term in 2024, Johnson serves as a senior member of the House Judiciary Committee and ranking member of its Subcommittee on Courts, Intellectual Property, Artificial Intelligence, and the Internet, where he has been a national leader on court reform. His legislative priorities include the Judiciary Act, the TERM Act for Supreme Court term limits, and the Supreme Court Ethics, Recusal and Transparency Act. He authored the Stop Militarizing Law Enforcement Act (SMLEA), which became the basis for Executive Orders under the Obama and Biden administrations and was adopted as part of the George Floyd Justice in Policing Act. Since 2007, Johnson has helped bring more than $5 billion in federal investments back to Georgia, including over $1 billion specifically for his district.
