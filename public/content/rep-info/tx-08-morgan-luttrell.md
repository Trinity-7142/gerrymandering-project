Born in Houston, Texas, Luttrell lives in Magnolia and was raised on a horse ranch as a 5th generation Texan. A Republican, he enlisted in the U.S. Navy to become a Navy SEAL and served as a SEAL for 7 years until being medically retired in 2014 for a severe traumatic brain injury and spinal cord injury sustained in a helicopter crash in 2009. He holds a bachelor's in psychology from Sam Houston State University and a master's in applied cognition neuroscience from the University of Texas at Dallas. Rick Perry appointed him Special Advisor at the Department of Energy, where he led the creation of the Artificial Intelligence and Technologies Office. His campaign platform centered on securing the border, gun rights, and restricting abortion. In Congress, he serves on the Armed Services, Homeland Security, and Veterans' Affairs Committees, and chairs the Disability Assistance and Memorial Affairs Subcommittee. He passed a House amendment allocating $15 million for clinical trials on psychedelic therapy for veterans with traumatic brain injuries. In September 2025, Luttrell announced he would not seek re-election.
