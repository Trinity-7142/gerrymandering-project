Julie Johnson (born May 2, 1966) is an attorney and Democratic U.S. Representative serving Texas's 32nd congressional district since 2025. She hails from Farmers Branch, Texas. Johnson earned a B.A. from the University of Texas at Austin and a J.D. from the University of Houston Law Center. She came to Washington from the Texas House of Representatives, where she served three terms since 2018. During her time in the Texas legislature, she passed 108 bills focused on patient protection and insurance reform, and authored the first bipartisan Medicaid expansion bill in Texas history. She was named the 2019 "Freshman of the Year" by *Texas Monthly* and "Champion of Medicine" by the Texas Medical Association. In Congress, she has focused on affordable health care, housing affordability legislation, reproductive freedom, and ICE accountability. She is the first openly gay member of Congress from the South and the first woman to ever represent District 32.
