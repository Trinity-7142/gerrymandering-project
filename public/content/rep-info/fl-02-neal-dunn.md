Rep. Neal Dunn is a surgeon and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Florida's 2nd congressional district since 2017. Dunn grew up in an Army family, stationed at over 20 places before college. A Republican, he served in the U.S. Army for eleven years, reaching the rank of major, before settling in Panama City, Florida, where he helped found the Panama City Urological Center and the Panama City Surgery Center. A self-described conservative, Dunn championed lower taxes, military and veterans' causes, healthcare innovation, and reducing bureaucracy. He authored legislation to streamline VA appointments and serves on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce. He also passed the FEMA Loan Interest Payment Relief Act, providing financial assistance to local governments for disaster recovery. In January 2026, Dunn announced he would not seek re-election, later revealed to be connected to a serious heart condition.
