Born in Havana, Cuba, in 1954 into a prosperous landowner family, Giménez arrived in the United States shortly after the Communist takeover of Cuba, settling in the Little Havana neighborhood of Miami. He is the only current Cuban-born member of Congress and the first career firefighter-paramedic to serve in Congress. Beginning his public service career in 1975 with the City of Miami Fire Department, he spent 25 years as a firefighter, the last nine as Chief — the first Cuban American to hold that role. A Republican, he later served as Miami-Dade County Commissioner and then County Mayor from 2011 to 2020. In Congress, he serves on the House Armed Services Committee, the Homeland Security Committee, and the House Select Committee on Strategic Competition between the U.S. and the Chinese Communist Party. His legislative focus spans transportation, international affairs, national security, and immigration. Notably, Giménez was one of 47 Republican representatives to vote for the Respect for Marriage Act in 2022, and was one of only eight Republicans to join the House majority in passing the Bipartisan Background Checks Act of 2021.
