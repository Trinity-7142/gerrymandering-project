Ann Wagner is a Republican politician and former diplomat serving as the U.S. representative for Missouri's 2nd congressional district since 2013. Born and raised in St. Louis, her parents owned carpet stores where she worked growing up, later earning a business degree from the University of Missouri and holding management positions at Hallmark Cards and Ralston-Purina. She is generally regarded as part of the moderate bloc of her party. Her platform centers on tax cuts, deregulation, border security, and pro-life legislation. A major legislative priority has been combating sex trafficking; she authored FOSTA, signed into law in 2018 and considered the most significant anti-trafficking law passed in nearly 20 years. She serves on the House Financial Services Committee, the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence, and chairs the Financial Services Subcommittee on Capital Markets. Before Congress, she chaired the Missouri Republican Party and served as U.S. Ambassador to Luxembourg from 2005 to 2009.
