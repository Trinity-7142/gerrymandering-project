Rep. Brad Sherman (D) was born and raised in southern California and represents the 32nd Congressional District, which includes parts of the San Fernando Valley in Los Angeles County and Simi Valley in Ventura County. He earned a B.A. from UCLA summa cum laude and a law degree from Harvard magna cum laude, later working as a CPA, attorney, and Certified Tax Law Specialist. Sherman is currently serving his fifteenth term in Congress. He serves on two major House committees — the House Foreign Affairs Committee and the House Financial Services Committee. His platform centers on consumer protection, gun safety, and U.S. alliances abroad; he was among the leaders behind the formation of the Consumer Financial Protection Agency. He worked to pass the 2024 National Security Supplemental providing Israel with $14.1 billion in emergency defense aid. Sherman also authored foundational Iran sanctions legislation, including provisions of the Comprehensive Iran Sanctions, Accountability, and Divestment Act.
