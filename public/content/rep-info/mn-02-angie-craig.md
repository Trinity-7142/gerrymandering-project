Rep. Angie Craig (D) grew up in a mobile home park, raised by a single mother, and worked two jobs to put herself through college. Born in Arkansas, she built a career in journalism and corporate communications before moving to Minnesota in 2005 for a position at St. Jude Medical. Her centrist politics have helped her win four consecutive elections in the competitive, purple 2nd District. Craig focuses on cutting taxes on small businesses and the middle class, lowering health care costs, securing infrastructure investments, and addressing the flow of illicit fentanyl. She is the first openly lesbian mother elected to Congress and the first openly gay person elected to Congress from Minnesota. In December 2024, she became the first openly LGBTQ+ person and first woman to serve as ranking member of the House Agriculture Committee. In April 2025, Craig announced a run for the U.S. Senate seat being vacated by Tina Smith.
