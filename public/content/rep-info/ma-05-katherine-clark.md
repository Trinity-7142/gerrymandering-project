Katherine Clark (D) is an American lawyer and politician currently in her seventh term as U.S. Representative for Massachusetts's 5th congressional district, having held the post since 2013. Born in New Haven, Connecticut, she attended St. Lawrence University, Cornell Law School, and Harvard's Kennedy School of Government. She worked as a lawyer in Chicago and Colorado before becoming general counsel for Massachusetts' Office of Child Care Services in 1995. A progressive Democrat, she is a leading proponent for ending wage discrimination, safeguarding access to abortion, ending gun violence, and improving access to affordable child care and paid leave. Elected Democratic Whip in 2022, she holds the distinction of being the highest-ranking woman in Congress. She was arrested at a protest in front of the U.S. Supreme Court building in July 2022.
