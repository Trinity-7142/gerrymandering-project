Adriano Espaillat grew up in Washington Heights, earning his B.S. in political science from Queens College, CUNY. He is the first Dominican American and first formerly undocumented immigrant to serve in Congress. A progressive Democrat, Espaillat is a staunch advocate of a fair living wage, affordable housing, criminal justice reform, infrastructure improvements, and better educational opportunities. He serves on the House Committee on Appropriations and is ranking member of its Legislative Branch subcommittee. In November 2024, he was unanimously elected Chairman of the Congressional Hispanic Caucus for the 119th Congress. During the 118th Congress, Espaillat introduced more than 50 bills aimed at protecting immigrant communities, supporting small businesses, and securing federal funding for NY-13.
