Rep. Nancy Mace was born at Fort Bragg, North Carolina, into a military family and grew up in Goose Creek, South Carolina. She often touts her humble beginnings as a high school dropout who worked at Waffle House before becoming the first woman to graduate from The Citadel's Corps of Cadets in 1999, earning a degree in business administration magna cum laude. She later became the first Republican woman elected to the U.S. House of Representatives from South Carolina, winning re-election in 2022 and 2024. A conservative Republican, she sits on the House Armed Services, Veterans' Affairs, and Oversight committees and chairs the Cybersecurity Subcommittee. Her platform has focused on banning offshore drilling off South Carolina's coast and immigration enforcement. Notable controversies include voting to remove Speaker Kevin McCarthy in 2023 and a House Ethics Committee investigation into alleged overcharging of a congressional housing reimbursement program. Mace officially filed to run for South Carolina governor in March 2026.
