Rep. Rivas grew up in Pacoima, raised by a single mother who worked multiple jobs to support her family. After attending San Fernando High School, she earned a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from MIT, worked as an electrical engineer for Motorola, and later pursued a Master of Education from Harvard. A progressive Democrat, she is the first Latina to represent the district and the only Latina in Congress with a STEM background. She founded DIY Girls, a nonprofit dedicated to helping girls become interested in technology and engineering. In Congress, her priorities include STEM education, immigration, and community investment; she has secured nearly $15 million in community project funding for CA-29 in FY2026. For the 119th Congress, she was elected by her peers to serve in House Democratic Leadership as the Freshman Leadership Representative.
