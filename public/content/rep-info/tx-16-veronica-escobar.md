A native of El Paso born in 1969, Escobar grew up near her family's dairy farm. She earned her bachelor's degree at the University of Texas at El Paso and a master's degree from New York University. Before entering electoral politics, she worked as an English teacher, nonprofit executive, and communications director for the mayor of El Paso. A Democrat, she served as El Paso County Commissioner from 2007 to 2011 and County Judge from 2011 to 2017. She took office in January 2019, making history as the first woman elected to the seat and one of the first two Latinas from Texas to serve in Congress. She serves on the House Judiciary Committee, House Armed Services Committee, and House Ethics Committee. Her platform centers on affordable healthcare, fair wages, immigration reform, and climate policy. She served as one of four co-chairs of the 2024 Democratic National Convention.
