Kat Cammack was born in Denver, Colorado, and raised on a 55-acre cattle ranch. A third-generation commercial sandblaster, she earned a B.A. in international relations from Metropolitan State University of Denver and a master's degree in national defense and strategic studies from the Naval War College. A Republican, Cammack previously served as deputy chief of staff to her predecessor, Representative Ted Yoho. With a record of supporting expanded gun rights, abortion restrictions, and border security, her platform has stressed tackling inflation and increasing affordability through tax reductions. She serves on the House Agriculture and Energy & Commerce Committees and chairs the Emerging Tech Task Force, leading House Republicans on issues like AI, quantum computing, and biotech. Cammack was one of 139 representatives who voted to overturn the results of the 2020 presidential election. Notably, in 2024 she experienced a life-threatening ectopic pregnancy and faced a delay in treatment amid uncertainty surrounding Florida's abortion ban.
