Rep. Marie Gluesenkamp Perez is a fifth-generation Washingtonian, former small business owner, and mom from Skamania County. Her father immigrated from Mexico and was a pastor at an evangelical church, giving her a working-class, cross-cultural background. She earned her degree in economics at Reed College and co-owned an auto repair and machine shop with her husband, Dean, before coming to Congress. She campaigned as a moderate Democrat, supporting both abortion rights and Second Amendment rights, and emphasized her focus on small businesses, job training, local industries such as timber, and opposition to political extremism. Her 2022 victory received widespread national attention, with *The Seattle Times* calling it "the most stunning political upset in the country this year." In 2024, she defeated Republican Joe Kent in a rematch, winning with 51.7 percent of the vote. Following her election, she took a role as co-chair of the Blue Dog Coalition and joined the bipartisan Problem Solvers Caucus. She has emphasized her role in securing $2.1 billion in federal funding to rebuild the Interstate Bridge carrying I-5 across the Columbia River. Her cross-party votes — including on immigration and the SAVE Act — have drawn both praise as independent and criticism from progressive constituents.
