Born in Chicago's Belmont Cragin community, Ramirez was raised in Humboldt Park after her family found subsidized housing they could afford. The daughter of working-class Guatemalan immigrants, she is an accomplished legislator, social service director, and community leader. She represents IL's 3rd Congressional District as the first Latina elected to Congress in Illinois and the entire Midwest. A progressive Democrat, she is the only member of Congress in a mixed-status family and leads the fight for comprehensive immigration reform and a pathway to citizenship for Dreamers. In the Illinois General Assembly, she passed legislation expanding Medicaid to seniors regardless of immigration status, secured over $450 million for affordable housing, and helped create an elected school board in Chicago. In 2025, remarks she made in Spanish at an international conference were disputed in translation by a conservative outlet, prompting some Republican members to call for her deportation.
