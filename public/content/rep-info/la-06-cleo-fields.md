Cleo Fields is an American attorney and politician serving in the U.S. House of Representatives, representing Louisiana's 6th Congressional District. Born in Baton Rouge in 1962, the seventh of ten children in a working-class family, he worked in a store and at McDonald's before earning both a bachelor's degree and a law degree from Southern University. A liberal Democrat, he received the highest liberal voting scores among Louisiana's delegation during his initial congressional term in the 1990s. Fields has emphasized education as a transformative force and a central campaign theme. He co-chairs the Congressional DEI Caucus, working to protect Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion. His return to Congress was enabled by a federal court ruling that Louisiana's post-2020 census map was an unlawful racial gerrymander, requiring a second majority-Black seat, finalized as the new 6th District in January 2024. The 2024 election was unexpectedly tight, with Fields earning just over 50% of the vote.
