A sixth-generation Minnesotan, Rep. Kelly Morrison resides in Deephaven, Minnesota. Born in Minneapolis in 1969, she earned a bachelor's degree in history from Yale University, an M.D. from Case Western Reserve University, and completed her residency in obstetrics and gynecology at Northwestern University. A Democrat, she practiced OB-GYN in Minnesota for over 20 years before entering politics. She served six years in the Minnesota legislature, passing over 90 bills — more than almost every other legislator. After the Supreme Court overturned *Roe v. Wade*, Morrison led the Minnesota Senate's passage of the Reproductive Freedom Defense Act, which Governor Tim Walz signed into law. Her platform centers on reproductive rights, lowering prescription drug costs, gun violence prevention, and climate change. She was described as the "first pro-choice OB-GYN elected to Congress," winning her 2024 general election 58% to 42%. In Congress, she introduced the bipartisan Warrior Infertility Act to expand IVF access for veterans.
