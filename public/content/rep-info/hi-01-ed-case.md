Ed Case (born September 27, 1952) is a Democratic lawyer and politician representing Hawaii's 1st congressional district, which covers the urban core of Honolulu, since 2019. Born in Hilo, he attended Williams College and earned his J.D. from the University of California. Case first came to prominence as majority leader of the Hawaii State Legislature and is identified as a Blue Dog Democrat. He is a member of the New Democrat Coalition and the Problem Solvers Caucus. His platform emphasizes reducing the high cost of living through responsible monetary policy, stronger supply chains, and boosted domestic manufacturing. He sits on the House Appropriations Committee. Notably, Case was one of only four Democrats who voted in favor of the SAVE Act, which places strict requirements to prove citizenship to vote in federal elections — a vote that drew criticism from fellow Democrats — underscoring his reputation as one of the most conservative Democrats in Congress.
