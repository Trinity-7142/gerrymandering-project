Born in Tucson, Arizona, Andy Biggs worked as a lawyer and later as a prosecutor before entering politics. He earned a B.A. in Asian Studies from Brigham Young University, a J.D. from the University of Arizona, and an M.A. in Political Science from Arizona State University. A staunch conservative, he has championed border security, lower taxes, and an "Arizona first" agenda since first being elected to AZ-05 in 2016. He sits on the House Judiciary and Oversight and Accountability Committees and has served as co-chair of the Border Security Caucus and co-founder of the bipartisan War Powers Caucus. Biggs served as chairman of the House Freedom Caucus from 2019 to 2022. He carries a lifetime rating of 100% with the Club for Growth and National Right to Life. Notably, Biggs spoke at rallies promoting the "Stop the Steal" election conspiracy movement and claimed antifa was behind the January 6, 2021 storming of the U.S. Capitol, allegations he denied involvement in. Biggs has announced he will retire from the House in 2026 to run for Governor of Arizona.
