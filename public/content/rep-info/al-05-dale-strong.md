Dale Whitney Strong is a Republican U.S. Representative for Alabama's 5th congressional district, serving since 2023. Born and raised in Monrovia, Alabama, he grew up working at his father's country store, with those efforts helping put him and his siblings through college. Strong earned a B.S. in business administration from Athens State University and an EMT license from the University of Alabama in Huntsville. He served in local government for 26 years, starting as the youngest Republican elected official in Alabama and becoming the commission's first Republican chairman in 2012. His platform focuses on border security and energy independence. In Congress, Strong serves on the House Appropriations Committee across three subcommittees, including as Vice Chair of Commerce, Justice, and Science. A notable controversy involved his decision to relocate a Confederate statue from the county courthouse, which prompted a state lawsuit and a $25,000 fine. In 2024, Strong voted against the $60 billion military aid package for Ukraine.
