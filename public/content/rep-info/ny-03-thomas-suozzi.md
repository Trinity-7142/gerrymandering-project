Born in Glen Cove, New York, to a family of Italian and Irish heritage — his father, an Italian immigrant, served as Glen Cove's mayor — Suozzi earned a B.S. in accounting from Boston College and a J.D. from Fordham University School of Law. A centrist Democrat, he pitches himself as a "common sense Democrat." In Congress, Suozzi has prioritized tax policy, authoring legislation to restore the SALT deduction capped at $10,000 in 2017. He also formed a bipartisan coalition to pursue border security legislation, fix the broken asylum system, and modernize immigration law. After Congress expelled George Santos, Suozzi won the February 2024 special election, reclaiming the seat for Democrats. In 2021, the Campaign Legal Center filed an ethics complaint alleging he failed to report nearly 300 stock transactions as required by the STOCK Act. In January 2025, Suozzi was elected Democratic co-chair of the Problem Solvers Caucus.
