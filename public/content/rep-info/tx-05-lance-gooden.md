Lance Gooden is a Republican U.S. Representative for Texas's 5th Congressional District, a seat he has held since 2019, representing parts of eastern Dallas and a large swath of exurban and rural territory to Dallas's east. A native of Terrell in Kaufman County, he earned both a B.A. in government and a B.B.A. in finance from the University of Texas at Austin. He worked as an insurance broker for energy companies before entering politics. Gooden previously served in the Texas House of Representatives, representing District 4 from 2011 to 2015 and again from 2017 to 2019. A staunch conservative, his legislative priorities include ending sanctuary cities and cutting U.S. funding to United Nations migration agencies. He sits on the House Armed Services and Judiciary Committees. Notably, Gooden was the only House Republican to vote for the George Floyd Justice in Policing Act in 2021, though he later tweeted the vote was accidental, claiming he pushed the wrong button.
