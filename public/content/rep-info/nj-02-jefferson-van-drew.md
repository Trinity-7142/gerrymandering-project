Jefferson H. Van Drew (born February 23, 1953) serves as the U.S. Representative for New Jersey's 2nd congressional district. Born in New York City, he earned a B.S. from Rutgers University and a D.M.D. from Fairleigh Dickinson University, operating a dental practice in South Jersey for 30 years before retiring. He resides in Cape May County. Originally elected in 2018 as a Democrat, he switched to the Republican Party after opposing the first impeachment of Donald Trump. He has maintained a hardline stance on immigration and has earned a reputation as a strong advocate for veterans, senior citizens, and consumers. He won re-election in 2024 with 58.1% of the vote. Van Drew voted against certifying the electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania following the 2020 presidential election, a notable controversy. In May 2025, Van Drew voted for the Republican "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" that significantly cut Medicaid, despite previously signing a letter opposing such reductions.
