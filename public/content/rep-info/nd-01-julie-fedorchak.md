Born in Williston and raised on a family farm near Belden, Fedorchak is a fourth-generation North Dakotan who grew up as the youngest of eight children, giving her a close connection to the state's agricultural communities. A Republican serving as North Dakota's at-large U.S. Representative since 2025, she is the first woman elected to the House from North Dakota. She previously served 12 years on the North Dakota Public Service Commission, during which she helped deliver some of the lowest utility costs in the nation and permitted over $15 billion in energy infrastructure projects. She also rose to president of the National Association of Regulatory Utility Commissioners (NARUC). In Congress, she serves on the House Energy and Commerce Committee. Her platform centers on grid reliability, affordable energy, and fiscal accountability. She has faced constituent criticism for holding only virtual town halls, and introduced legislation to remove Inflation Reduction Act tax credits for wind and solar energy.
