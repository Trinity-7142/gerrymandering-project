Mark Pocan (born August 14, 1964) is a Democratic U.S. Representative from Wisconsin's 2nd congressional district, serving since 2013. Born and raised in Kenosha, he earned a journalism degree from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 1986. He previously owned Budget Signs & Specialties, a printing business. A consistent progressive, Pocan is co-chair of the Congressional LGBT Equality Caucus and chair emeritus of the Congressional Progressive Caucus. He campaigns on fighting for middle-class families with a strong progressive voice. Recent legislative priorities include the Audit the Pentagon Act of 2026 and the ELON MUSK Act, seeking to ban special government employees from obtaining federal contracts. Pocan is openly gay and boycotted Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu's 2024 address to Congress in protest of the Gaza conflict.
