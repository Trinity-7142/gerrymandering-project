Born in Waterbury, Connecticut, Hayes grew up in public housing and became a teen mother before finishing high school. She earned degrees from Naugatuck Valley Community College, Southern Connecticut State University, and the University of Saint Joseph. Named the National Teacher of the Year in 2016, she went on to become the first Black woman elected to represent Connecticut in Congress. A progressive Democrat, Hayes supports public education and teachers' unions. Her platform centers on supporting working families, eradicating hunger, and addressing gun violence. She has had 11 pieces of legislation signed into law, including the Palomares Veterans Act. She drew scrutiny in 2024 for spending campaign funds on trips to Martha's Vineyard and the Democratic National Convention while constituents recovered from deadly floods.
