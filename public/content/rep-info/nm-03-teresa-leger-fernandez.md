Born in Las Vegas, New Mexico, Leger Fernández is a 17th-generation daughter of rural New Mexico. Her mother was a bilingual educator and her father served in the New Mexico Senate. She earned a B.A. from Yale University and a J.D. from Stanford Law School. Before Congress, she worked as an attorney and advocate, winning legal battles to advance voting rights, tribal sovereignty, and environmental protections. A political progressive, her platform has championed a "New Mexico Green New Deal," Medicare for All, and a transition away from fracking. She has perhaps been most prominent for her work passing legislation bringing billions in federal dollars to compensate victims of the catastrophic Calf Canyon/Hermit's Peak wildfire. She was elected chair of the Democratic Women's Caucus for the 119th Congress, having previously served as its vice chair. She sponsors bills primarily in the areas of Native American affairs and public lands.
