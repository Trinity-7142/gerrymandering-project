Born in Albany, New York, Van Duyne's family moved to Irving, Texas in 1986, which has remained her home base. She graduated magna cum laude from Cornell University with a B.A. in city and regional planning, government, and law. A conservative Republican, she serves on the Ways and Means Subcommittees on Taxation, Trade, Oversight, and Work and Welfare, focusing on tax relief, small business growth, and immigration enforcement. She was the first woman elected mayor of Irving, serving from 2011 to 2017, and was later appointed HUD Regional Administrator under President Trump. In the 118th Congress, she chaired the House Small Business Subcommittee on Oversight, focusing on workforce development and oversight of pandemic relief fund recovery. She was among 147 Republican lawmakers who voted to overturn the 2020 presidential election results by objecting to Pennsylvania's electors.
