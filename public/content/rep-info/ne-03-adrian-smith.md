Adrian Michael Smith is a Republican U.S. Representative for Nebraska's 3rd congressional district, serving since 2007. Born in Scottsbluff, Nebraska, he grew up in a rural neighborhood south of Gering. He graduated from the University of Nebraska–Lincoln in 1993. Prior to Congress, he worked as an educator and real estate agent, and served on his hometown city council. A consistent conservative, Smith prioritizes agricultural policy, rural healthcare access, and tax reform. A key platform priority is making the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act permanent, including doubling the standard deduction and lowering individual tax rates. Since 2010, he has served on the Ways and Means Committee, where he chairs the Trade Subcommittee. Smith is the dean of Nebraska's congressional delegation since 2022.
