Born and raised in Grand Rapids, Michigan, and now residing in Fort Pierce, Florida, Brian Mast is a Republican politician and U.S. military veteran who has served as the U.S. Representative for Florida's 21st congressional district since 2017. A veteran of Operation Enduring Freedom, Mast lost both his legs while serving as a U.S. Army explosive ordnance disposal technician in Afghanistan in 2010, and received the Bronze Star and Purple Heart for his actions. At the start of the 119th Congress in 2025, he became chair of the House Foreign Affairs Committee, with a legislative agenda emphasizing local environmental issues — particularly water quality tied to Lake Okeechobee — and veterans' health and services. In November 2023, while arguing for a bill to reduce humanitarian funding to Gaza, Mast drew controversy by comparing Palestinian civilians to Nazi German civilians during World War II. He also previously apologized for offensive Facebook comments made in 2009 and 2011.
