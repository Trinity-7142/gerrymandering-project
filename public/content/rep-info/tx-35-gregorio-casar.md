Gregorio Eduardo Casar (born May 4, 1989) is a U.S. Representative serving Texas's 35th congressional district since 2023. A former labor organizer and the proud son of Mexican immigrants, he champions an economy that works for working families over big corporations, and is among the most progressive members of the Texas delegation. His platform centers on raising wages, expanding immigrant rights, restoring abortion and voting rights, and tackling the climate crisis. Before Congress, he served seven years on the Austin City Council, where he passed renter protections, affordable housing programs, and paid sick leave ordinances. He serves as Chair of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, comprising more than 100 members. In his first term, he successfully pushed for the introduction of the first-ever federal heat rule, protecting over 30 million workers from extreme heat on the job. He also introduced the Connect the Grid Act to link Texas's electric grid to surrounding national grids.
