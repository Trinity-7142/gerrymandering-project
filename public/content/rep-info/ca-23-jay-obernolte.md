Born in Chicago and raised in Fresno, California, Obernolte earned a bachelor's degree in engineering from Caltech, a master's degree in artificial intelligence from UCLA, and a doctorate in public administration from California Baptist University. A conservative Republican, he served as mayor of Big Bear Lake before founding FarSight Studios, a video game development company. His platform centers on fiscal restraint, lower taxes, border security, fentanyl enforcement, and veterans' care. Notably, he co-chairs a bipartisan congressional task force on artificial intelligence alongside Rep. Ted Lieu. He drew controversy for voting not to certify Arizona's and Pennsylvania's electoral votes following the 2020 presidential election. He also notably voted for the Respect for Marriage Act, joining a minority of Republicans to codify same-sex marriage in federal law.
