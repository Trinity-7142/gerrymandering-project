Born in Story City and raised in Altoona, Iowa, Nunn is a sixth-generation Iowan from a family of public school teachers, nurses, and farmers. He attended Drake University before earning master's degrees from the Air Command and Staff College and the University of Cambridge. He served nearly two decades in the U.S. Air Force as an airborne intelligence officer, flying recon missions off the coasts of Russia and China and completing three combat deployments with over 700 combat hours. Axios has described Nunn as having "carved out a niche as a vocal moderate on key issues." On the 2024 campaign trail, he emphasized his bipartisan record while also pledging commitment to GOP priorities on border and national security. He sits on the House Financial Services and Agriculture Committees. In 2022, he defeated incumbent Democrat Cindy Axne and was re-elected in 2024. Abortion policy was a key controversy, with opponents citing his raised hand in a 2022 primary debate in support of a full abortion ban. Notably, he introduced bipartisan legislation to combat scams targeting older Americans, co-sponsored with members from both parties.
