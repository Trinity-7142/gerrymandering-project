A native East Tennessean, Harshbarger was born in Bloomingdale and was the first in her immediate family to graduate from high school and college. She attended East Tennessee State University and earned a Doctor of Pharmacy degree from Mercer University College of Pharmacy. A Republican and strong supporter of President Donald Trump, she was first elected to the House in 2020. A member of the Freedom Caucus and Republican Study Committee, she is the only woman in Tennessee's House delegation. Her campaign focused on addressing the opioid crisis, anti-abortion legislation, religious freedom, and reducing U.S. dependence on Chinese pharmaceutical imports. In 2021, she was reported to have violated the STOCK Act by failing to disclose over 700 stock trades. In 2025, she drew widespread condemnation after using racial and anti-LGBTQ slurs on a podcast.
