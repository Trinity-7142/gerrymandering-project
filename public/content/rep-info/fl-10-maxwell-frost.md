Maxwell Alejandro Frost is a Democratic U.S. Representative for Florida's 10th congressional district, serving since 2023. Born in Orlando to a Puerto Rican mother of Lebanese descent and a Haitian father, he was adopted at birth by Maritza Argibay-Frost, a Cuban immigrant and special education teacher, and Patrick Frost, a Kansas-born musician-producer. Elected at age 25, Frost is the youngest member of the United States Congress and was its first Generation Z member. Prior to Congress, Frost was an Uber driver and served as the National Organizing Director for March For Our Lives. His platform centers on housing affordability, healthcare, abortion rights, LGBTQ+ rights, gun violence prevention, and climate change. The first bill he introduced was the Office of Gun Violence Prevention Act, which became reality in September 2023 when President Biden announced the formation of the Office of Gun Violence Prevention. Frost is a member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, Congressional Black Caucus, and Congressional Hispanic Caucus.
