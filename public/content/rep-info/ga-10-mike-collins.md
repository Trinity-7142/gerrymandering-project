Mike Collins (born July 2, 1967) is a Republican politician and businessman serving as the U.S. Representative for Georgia's 10th Congressional District since 2023. Born, raised, and still residing in Jackson, Georgia, he and his wife Leigh Ann started a trucking company in the early 1990s that has grown to employ more than 100 Georgians. His father, Mac Collins, also served in the House of Representatives. Collins is a member of the Freedom Caucus and his top policy priorities include cutting wasteful spending and reducing regulations on small business owners. After the murder of Laken Riley, Collins introduced the Laken Riley Act, requiring ICE to detain undocumented immigrants charged with theft; it was signed into law by President Trump in January 2025. In November 2025, the House Ethics Committee announced it was reviewing a complaint regarding Collins and his chief of staff. Collins formally announced his candidacy for the U.S. Senate in July 2025, challenging Democratic incumbent Jon Ossoff.
