Darrell Issa is a Republican representing California's 48th congressional district. Born in Cleveland, Ohio, the son of a Lebanese Christian immigrant family on his father's side, Issa enlisted in the U.S. Army as a high school senior, receiving an ROTC scholarship and graduating with a business degree from Siena Heights University. He co-founded Directed Electronics in 1982, which became one of the largest makers of automobile aftermarket security products in the United States. As a congressman, Issa has championed limited government and legislation to balance the federal budget and promote transparency. He serves as a senior member of the House Judiciary and Foreign Affairs Committees and chaired the Oversight and Government Reform Committee from 2011 to 2015. In that role, he identified more than $10 billion in government waste, theft, and mismanagement. A holder of 37 patents, Issa has been a consistent advocate for intellectual property rights. Notably, Issa voted against certifying the electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania following the 2020 presidential election. In March 2026, Issa announced he would not seek re-election.
