Robert Frank Onder Jr. (born January 6, 1962) is a physician and attorney from Missouri. He received his MD from Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis and his JD from Saint Louis University Law School, and operated a private allergy, asthma, and clinical immunology practice for over 30 years. A conservative Republican residing in St. Charles County, his platform centers on fighting illegal immigration, pro-life causes, cutting taxes, Second Amendment rights, religious liberty, and education freedom. Onder announced his 2024 congressional bid after incumbent Blaine Luetkemeyer retired, defeated six primary opponents, and won the general election against Democrat Bethany Mann. He co-founded the Missouri Senate Conservative Caucus and received endorsements from Missouri Right to Life and the NRA. He serves on the House Judiciary, Transportation and Infrastructure, and Education and Workforce Committees.
