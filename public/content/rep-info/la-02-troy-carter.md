Born in New Orleans and raised in Algiers as the youngest of six children, Rep. Carter is a graduate of Xavier University of Louisiana, earning a B.A. in Political Science and Business Administration, and later graduated Summa Cum Laude with a Master of Science in Business from the University of Holy Cross. A liberal Democrat, he is a staunch advocate for criminal justice reform, women's health care, civil rights, environmental justice reform, and mental health. He has championed raising the minimum wage to $15 per hour, economic development, and student loan forgiveness. Carter has achieved a series of historic firsts, including being the first African American elected to the Louisiana State House from District 102, the first to represent New Orleans City Council District C, and the first to represent Louisiana State Senate District 7. Now in his third term, he serves on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce and the House Homeland Security Committee.
