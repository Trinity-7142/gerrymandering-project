Born on a military air base in New Mexico and raised on a cattle ranch in West Texas, Brandon Gill is a Republican politician and former investment banker who represents North Texas's 26th District. He graduated cum laude from Dartmouth College with degrees in Economics and History, then worked as an investment banker on Wall Street and later as an analyst at a global hedge fund. His principal policy objective is border security, and he supports mass deportations. He serves on the House Judiciary, Budget, and Oversight Committees, including the DOGE Subcommittee. Gill is a member of the House Freedom Caucus. He was endorsed by President Trump and Senator Ted Cruz. He has drawn controversy for remarks widely characterized as Islamophobic, including comments the *Washington Post* described as "blatant Islamophobia."
