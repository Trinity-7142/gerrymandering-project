Born in Torrance, California, to a working-class family — his mother worked in a cafeteria and his father was a Vietnam War veteran — Gilbert Ray Cisneros Jr. is a Democratic politician and former naval officer serving as the U.S. representative for California's 31st congressional district since 2025. After working as a shipping and manufacturing manager for Frito-Lay, Cisneros won a $266 million Mega Millions jackpot in 2010 and became a noted philanthropist. He previously served as Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness in the Biden administration and represented California's 39th congressional district from 2019 to 2021. He currently serves on the House Armed Services and House Committee on Small Business. His platform focuses on gun safety reform, educational opportunities, climate action, and women's rights. Notably, Cisneros co-launched the bipartisan Military Mental Health Caucus to support service members and their families on mental health issues.
