The daughter of Representative Thomas D'Alesandro Jr., Pelosi was born and raised in Baltimore. She later married Paul Pelosi, a native of San Francisco, where they settled. A lifelong Democrat, Pelosi was a founding member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus and is still widely considered a liberal. She served as the 52nd Speaker of the House, the first woman elected speaker and the first woman to lead a major political party in either chamber of Congress. Her platform has centered on affordable health care and clean energy and combating climate change. She contributed to the passage of the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021, the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, the CHIPS and Science Act, and the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022. Pelosi has been regarded as a polarizing figure, drawing criticism from both conservatives and progressives, and has faced scrutiny over issues such as the Trump impeachments and insider trading allegations. She announced in November 2025 that she would not run for re-election in 2026.
