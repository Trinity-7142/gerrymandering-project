Michael John Flood (born February 23, 1975) is an American attorney, businessman, and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Nebraska's 1st congressional district since July 2022. Born in Omaha, he was raised in Norfolk, Nebraska. In 1999, he founded Flood Communications, growing it from one Norfolk radio station into a statewide media network. A Republican, he previously served two stints in the Nebraska Legislature from the 19th district and was Speaker from 2007 to 2013. During his state legislative tenure, Flood introduced and passed the Pain-Capable Unborn Child Protection Act, the nation's first 20-week abortion ban. In Congress, his platform centers on economic growth, border security, tax cuts, and limiting federal intervention. He serves on the Financial Services Committee, including as Chair of the Housing and Insurance Subcommittee. Flood entered Congress by challenging incumbent Jeff Fortenberry, who resigned following a felony conviction, and won the resulting special election.
