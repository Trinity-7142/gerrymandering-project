Born in Allentown, Pennsylvania, Ryan Mackenzie is a ninth-generation resident of the Lehigh Valley, where his family has lived since colonial times. A Republican, he holds a degree in Finance and International Business from NYU and an MBA from Harvard Business School. Before Congress, he served in the Pennsylvania House of Representatives from 2012–2024, where he was Republican chair of the Labor & Industry Committee. In 2024, he defeated incumbent Democrat Susan Wild — by just 50.4%–49.4%, the smallest margin of any U.S. House race in Pennsylvania that year. His platform centers on economic growth, taxpayer protection, affordability, and stricter immigration enforcement. He serves on the Education and Workforce, Foreign Affairs, and Homeland Security committees, and chairs the Workforce Protections Subcommittee. Notably, his mother, Milou Mackenzie, is also a Pennsylvania state representative — making them the first mother-son pair to simultaneously serve in the Pennsylvania House.
