Raja Krishnamoorthi is a Democratic attorney and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 8th congressional district since 2017. Born into a Tamil Hindu family in New Delhi, his family relied on public housing and food assistance before settling in Peoria, Illinois, where his father became a university professor. He graduated summa cum laude from Princeton in mechanical engineering and earned his law degree with honors from Harvard. A moderate-to-progressive Democrat, his platform centers on healthcare affordability, middle-class economic support, clean energy, and national security. He is the first Indian American or person of South Asian descent to serve as a ranking member or chair of any full committee in Congress, notably on the House Select Committee on Strategic Competition with China. Some members of the Indian American community have criticized him for accepting contributions from figures aligned with India's Hindu nationalist movement. He ran for the Democratic nomination in the 2026 Illinois Senate election but lost to Juliana Stratton.
