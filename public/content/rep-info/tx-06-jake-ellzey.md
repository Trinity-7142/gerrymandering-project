Rep. Jake Ellzey (R) represents Texas's 6th Congressional District, with a listed hometown of Midlothian. He was born in Amarillo and raised in Perryton, Texas. Before Congress, Ellzey served 20 years in the U.S. Navy as a fighter pilot flying F-14s and F/A-18s, was deployed nine times including five combat tours, and concluded his military career as Air Boss aboard the USS Ronald Reagan. A conservative Republican, he champions veterans' issues, national defense, small businesses, and fiscal responsibility. In the 119th Congress, he serves on the House Appropriations Committee. Notably, Ellzey was one of 18 Republicans who voted against Jim Jordan's nomination for Speaker of the House all three times, drawing criticism from the right and highlighting his occasional independence within the GOP.
