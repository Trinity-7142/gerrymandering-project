Born on March 16, 1981, in Monroe, Louisiana, Julia Letlow is a mother and educator who dedicated her career to higher education, serving as executive director of external affairs at the University of Louisiana Monroe and as director of education and patient safety at Tulane University School of Medicine. A conservative Republican, she focuses on expanding rural broadband access, strengthening agricultural policy, and improving education opportunities for families in her district. Her husband, Luke Letlow, was elected to represent LA-05 in 2020 but died from COVID-19 before taking office, prompting Julia to run in the subsequent special election. She became the first Republican woman to represent Louisiana in the House. Key legislative accomplishments include authoring the Parents Bill of Rights Act, which passed the House in March 2023 and would require school districts to publicize curricula and budgets and mandate parental consent for medical exams at school. She serves on the House Appropriations Committee, including subcommittees on Agriculture and on Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education.
