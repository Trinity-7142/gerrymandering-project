Claudia Tenney (born February 4, 1961) is an attorney and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for New York's 24th congressional district since 2023. Born in New Hartford, New York, she earned a bachelor's degree in political science from Colgate University in 1983 and a J.D. from the University of Cincinnati in 1987. Her career includes co-owning Mid-York Press, Inc., and working as an attorney. She is a member of the Republican Party and has been ranked among the most politically conservative members of the House. Her platform centers on border security, upholding the rule of law, and supporting law enforcement. She serves as Chairwoman of the House Election Integrity Caucus and has championed election security legislation. She sits on the Committee on Ways and Means and the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. Tenney does not reside in NY-24, a fact that drew criticism from primary challengers; she nonetheless defeated Democratic challenger David Wagenhauser in the November 2024 general election.
