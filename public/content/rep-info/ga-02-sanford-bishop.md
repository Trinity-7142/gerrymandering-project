Sanford Dixon Bishop Jr. (born February 4, 1947) is a lawyer and politician who has represented Georgia's 2nd congressional district since 1993. Born in Mobile, Alabama, he is the son of the first president of Bishop State Community College. He earned his B.A. from Morehouse College in 1968 and his J.D. from Emory University School of Law in 1971. A member of the Blue Dog Coalition, he belongs to the moderate faction of the Democratic Party. He champions jobs, rural economic development, sustainable agriculture, affordable healthcare, and a strong national defense. He serves as Ranking Member of the Agriculture, Rural Development, FDA, and Related Agencies Appropriations Subcommittee. He became the dean of Georgia's congressional delegation after the death of John Lewis. A 2010 controversy involved reports that Bishop had directed Congressional Black Caucus scholarship funds to family members and associates between 2003 and 2005. In 2025, he was one of 46 House Democrats who joined Republicans to vote for the Laken Riley Act.
