Abraham "Abe" Hamadeh is a Republican politician, attorney, U.S. Army intelligence officer, and former prosecutor serving as the U.S. Representative for Arizona's 8th congressional district since 2025. Born into a Syrian immigrant family in Chicago and raised in Phoenix, he earned a bachelor's degree from Arizona State University and a J.D. from the University of Arizona. He is the first Arab American elected to Congress from Arizona. A U.S. Army Reserve intelligence officer since 2016 who attained the rank of Captain, he received a Meritorious Service Medal following a deployment to Saudi Arabia. His congressional platform centers on border security, which he named one of the biggest problems facing the district, opposition to Green New Deal energy policies, and treating abortion as a state-level issue. In 2022, Hamadeh was the Republican nominee for Arizona attorney general, losing to Democrat Kris Mayes by just 280 votes, after which he filed multiple unsuccessful legal challenges contesting the result. In Congress, he has sponsored legislation including the Improving Emerging Tech Opportunities for Veterans Act.
