Born in Pearland, Texas, Randy Weber is a proven conservative, former small business owner, and third-generation Texan who earned a Bachelor of Science in public affairs from the University of Houston–Clear Lake and founded an air-conditioning company in 1981. He succeeded Ron Paul in representing the 14th district, having previously served in the Texas House of Representatives. In 2024, GovTrack ranked him the most conservative member of the House during the 118th Congress. His platform prioritizes fiscal restraint, opposing unsustainable spending and waste in Washington, border security, and energy, liquefied natural gas, and infrastructure. He serves on the House Energy and Commerce Committee. Notably, Weber was one of 126 Republicans to sign an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania*, contesting the 2020 presidential election results.
