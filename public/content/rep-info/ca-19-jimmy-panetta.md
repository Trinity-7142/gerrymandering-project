Jimmy Panetta is an American lawyer, politician, and former Navy intelligence officer representing California's 19th Congressional District as a Democrat. Having grown up on the central coast of California, Panetta has deep roots in the region he serves. He is the son of Leon Panetta, former U.S. Secretary of Defense. He served as a Navy Reserve intelligence officer in Afghanistan in 2007, attached to Joint Special Operations Command, and was awarded the Bronze Star. Before Congress, he worked as a deputy district attorney in both Alameda and Monterey Counties. His platform centers on environmental protection, veterans' services, agriculture, and healthcare. He secured $1.4 billion in COVID-19 relief for his district and has passed legislation on clean energy, forest protection, military family hunger, and legal services for homeless veterans. Some local activists have criticized his support for U.S. military aid to Israel, arguing it reflects a disconnect with constituents calling for Palestinian human rights. He has missed only 0.2% of roll call votes, better than the median among serving representatives.
