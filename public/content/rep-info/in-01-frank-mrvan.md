Born and raised in Hammond, Indiana, and of Czechoslovak and Polish heritage, Frank Mrvan has served as the U.S. Representative for Indiana's 1st Congressional District since 2021. He previously worked as a licensed mortgage broker and pharmaceutical sales representative before entering public service. From 2005 to 2021, he served as township trustee for North Township, Indiana. A moderate Democrat and member of the New Democrat Coalition, Mrvan's platform centers on fighting for working men and women by lowering costs and bringing jobs to Northwest Indiana, while supporting border security through safe and humane policies. He serves as vice chairman of the Congressional Steel Caucus and highlights his work on the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and securing a federally designated Hydrogen Hub in Northwest Indiana. He supports abortion rights, including codifying Roe v. Wade, and is an original cosponsor of the Women's Health Protection Act. In the 119th Congress, Mrvan serves on the House Appropriations Committee.
