Born in St. Louis, Missouri, the fifth of 13 children raised by a single mother, Waters began working at age 13 in factories and segregated restaurants before moving to Los Angeles. She earned a Bachelor of Arts degree from California State University, Los Angeles, and began her public-service career as a Head Start teacher and volunteer coordinator. A progressive Democrat, she has represented California's 43rd congressional district since 1991. Her legislative priorities have included bills targeting homelessness, homeownership, economic regulation, and opportunity for underserved populations. She made history as the first woman and first African American to chair the House Financial Services Committee, a post she held from 2019 to 2023, serving as ranking member since. She faced a 2010 ethics investigation related to alleged intervention on behalf of a bank in which her husband held a financial interest; the House Ethics Committee ultimately cleared her of wrongdoing.
