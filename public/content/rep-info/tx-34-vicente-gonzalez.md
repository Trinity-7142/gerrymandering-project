Born in Corpus Christi, Texas, and raised in a military family, Gonzalez earned his GED in 1985 and later received a Juris Doctor from Texas Wesleyan University School of Law in 1996, subsequently opening his own law practice. Considered a conservative Democrat, he serves as co-chair of the Blue Dog Coalition. He represents the 34th District of Texas, encompassing eastern Hidalgo County and all of Cameron, Kenedy, Kleberg, and Willacy Counties. His platform emphasizes affordable health care, lower drug prices, and investments in South Texas's health care system, along with addressing the root causes of migration. Since coming to Congress, he has directed over $303 million in federal funds toward health care research and assistance. Notably, during the 2024 campaign, Gonzalez drew criticism for comparing Hispanic Trump supporters to "Jews for Hitler." The Lugar Center ranked him among the top 50 most bipartisan members of Congress during the 118th Congress.
