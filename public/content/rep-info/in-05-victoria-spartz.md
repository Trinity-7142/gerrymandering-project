Victoria Spartz is a Ukrainian-born American politician and Republican U.S. Representative for Indiana's 5th congressional district. Born in Nosivka, Ukraine, in 1978, she immigrated to the United States in 2000 after meeting her husband on a train in Europe, working her way up from bank teller to CPA, finance executive, and Indiana State Senator. Growing up in Ukraine shaped her conservative philosophy that limited government is preferable and that financial and healthcare decisions should be made by individuals, not bureaucrats. Her platform centers on constitutional rights, spending and debt reduction, immigration and border security, national defense, healthcare, and education. Notably, she has led a healthcare competition package targeting nonprofit hospital pricing and anti-competitive contracting practices. She faced a House Ethics Committee probe into allegations of staff mistreatment, and she announced she would not sit on committees or participate in the Republican caucus until she saw leadership governing responsibly.
