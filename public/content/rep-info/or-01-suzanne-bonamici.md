Born in a small-town Michigan household where her father worked at a local bank and her mother was a small business owner, Bonamici knows firsthand what it means to struggle financially, having worked her way through community college, university, and law school. After earning her J.D. from the University of Oregon, she worked as a consumer protection attorney at the Federal Trade Commission and later in private practice in Portland. A Democrat, she served in both chambers of Oregon's state legislature before winning her U.S. House seat in a 2012 special election. A member of the Congressional Progressive Caucus, her priorities include strengthening public education, protecting Social Security, Medicare, and Medicaid, and supporting paid family leave and workforce development. She serves as Ranking Member of the Early Childhood, Elementary, and Secondary Education Subcommittee and co-founded the bipartisan STEAM Caucus, integrating arts and design into STEM education.
