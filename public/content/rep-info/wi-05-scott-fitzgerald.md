Born in Chicago in 1963, Fitzgerald moved with his family to Hustisford, Wisconsin, at age 11. He ran the Dodge County Independent News in Juneau, Wisconsin, from 1990 to 1996 before entering politics. Prior to his election to Congress, Fitzgerald served in the Wisconsin State Senate representing the 13th District from 1995 to 2021, where he served as Majority Leader, Minority Leader, Co-Chairman of the Joint Committee on Finance, and Chairman of the Senate Corrections Committee. He joined the U.S. Army Reserve in 1981, completed the Army Command and General Staff College, and retired in 2009 at the rank of Lieutenant Colonel. A conservative Republican, Fitzgerald has focused on border security, reducing federal spending, and delivering relief to Wisconsin. He serves on the House Committee on Financial Services and the House Committee on the Judiciary. Notably, Fitzgerald voted against certifying the electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania following the 2020 presidential election.
