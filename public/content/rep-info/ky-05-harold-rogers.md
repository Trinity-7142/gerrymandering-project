Harold "Hal" Rogers is a Republican U.S. Representative for Kentucky's 5th congressional district, serving since 1981. Born in Barrier, Kentucky, he earned a Bachelor of Arts and a law degree from the University of Kentucky. He represents one of the poorest congressional districts in the nation. A conservative focused on fiscal responsibility, Rogers prioritizes economic development, job creation, fighting illegal drug use, and preserving the natural treasures of Appalachia. As House Appropriations Committee Chairman from 2011–2016, he reduced discretionary spending by a historic $126 billion. Upon Don Young's death in 2022, Rogers became the Dean of the House of Representatives. Critics have challenged his steering of homeland security funds to his district rather than major population centers.
