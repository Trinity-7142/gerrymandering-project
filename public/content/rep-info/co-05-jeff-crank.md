Born in Pueblo, Colorado in 1967, Jeff Crank earned a B.A. in political science from Colorado State University. His father was a World War II Navy veteran and later a manager for the Pershing II missile program at the Pueblo Army Depot. A conservative Republican, Crank served as Senior Vice President of the Colorado Springs Chamber of Commerce before working as Chief Operating Officer at Americans for Prosperity and president of his own real estate investment company. He focuses on results for district families, supporting military installations, veterans, and wildfire resilience. As a Capitol Hill staffer, he helped author the Military Family Housing Privatization Act, improving housing at over fifty military installations. Endorsed by Donald Trump and Speaker Mike Johnson, Crank signed the Taxpayer Protection Pledge and a Term Limits pledge. His 2024 winning margin was the narrowest ever posted by a GOP nominee in the district.
