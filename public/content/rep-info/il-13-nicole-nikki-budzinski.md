Born in Peoria, Illinois, Budzinski's grandparents were both union members — her grandfather a painter and her grandmother a teacher — roots that shaped her progressive, labor-oriented politics. She has served as the U.S. representative for Illinois's 13th congressional district since 2023 and is a member of the Democratic Party. Just before her election, she served as chief of staff to the director of the Office of Management and Budget in the Biden administration. She serves on the House Agriculture Committee, focusing on the farm safety net, nutrition programs, and conservation, and on the House Veterans' Affairs Committee, where she is Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Technology Modernization. As a state official, she led negotiations to raise Illinois' minimum wage to $15 an hour and chaired the Broadband Advisory Board. During her 2022 campaign, scrutiny arose over consulting fees she collected after leaving the Pritzker administration, including payments from organizations that don't disclose donors — contrasting with her platform pledge to reduce "dark money" in politics. In her 2024 re-election, she outperformed her opponent by 16 percent, increasing margins in every county.
