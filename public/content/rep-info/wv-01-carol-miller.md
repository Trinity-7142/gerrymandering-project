Carol Miller is a Republican politician, educator, and farmer serving as the U.S. Representative for West Virginia's 1st Congressional District. Born in Columbus, Ohio, she is the daughter of former Ohio congressman Samuel L. Devine. She lives in Huntington, where she owns and operates Swann Ridge Bison Farm and manages real estate. Her congressional focus includes job creation, economic diversification, trade, border security, and supporting West Virginia's coal, oil, and gas industries. Before Congress, she served in the West Virginia House of Delegates from 2006 to 2018, rising to become the first female Majority Whip. Upon her 2018 election, she became the first woman to represent West Virginia's 3rd congressional district. She currently serves on the Committee on Ways and Means. In December 2020, Miller was one of 126 House Republicans to sign an amicus brief contesting the 2020 presidential election results. Separately, auto dealerships owned by her husband received over $3 million in forgiven PPP loans during the COVID-19 pandemic.
