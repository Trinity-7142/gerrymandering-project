Born in Harris County and raised in San Angelo, Texas, Republican Representative August Pfluger is a decorated career Air Force officer who flew the F-15C Eagle and F-22A Raptor, accumulating over 300 combat hours before briefly serving on the National Security Council during Donald Trump's first presidency. His platform centers on energy dominance, border security, support for farmers and ranchers, and military communities. He is the first member representing the Permian Basin to serve on the House Energy and Commerce Committee. He was elected Republican Study Committee Chairman, leading the largest caucus of conservatives on Capitol Hill in the 119th Congress. Notably, Pfluger voted against certifying the electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania following the 2020 election, and was reported to have violated the STOCK Act by failing to properly disclose stock trades worth between $10,007 and $150,000.
