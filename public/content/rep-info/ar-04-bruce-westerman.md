Bruce Westerman is an American forester and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Arkansas's 4th congressional district. Raised in Hot Springs, Arkansas, he graduated as valedictorian of Fountain Lake High School. He holds a B.S. in engineering from the University of Arkansas and a Master of Science in forestry from Yale University. A conservative Republican, he has championed lower taxes, limited government, and strong family values. In Washington, Westerman serves as Chairman of the House Natural Resources Committee and sits on the House Transportation Committee. In January 2025, he introduced the bipartisan Fix Our Forests Act, aimed at improving forest management for wildfire risk reduction. In December 2020, Westerman was one of 126 House Republicans to sign an amicus brief supporting Texas v. Pennsylvania, contesting the 2020 presidential election results.
