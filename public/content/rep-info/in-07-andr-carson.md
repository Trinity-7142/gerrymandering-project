André Carson is a Democrat serving as U.S. Representative for Indiana's 7th Congressional District, which covers the northern four-fifths of Indianapolis. Carson grew up in a rough neighborhood, crediting that experience for shaping his policy views on education, public safety, and economic opportunity. Before taking office, he served on the Indianapolis City-County Council and worked in law enforcement, as well as in intelligence and counter-terrorism for the Indiana Department of Homeland Security. He was the second Muslim elected to Congress, after Keith Ellison of Minnesota. Carson is the grandson of his predecessor, Rep. Julia Carson, winning a special election after her death in office. He focuses on job growth, workforce training, middle-class tax relief, and combating unfair trade practices. As a senior member of the House Intelligence Committee, he led the first open hearing on Unidentified Aerial Phenomena in 50 years. His advocacy has secured over $1 billion in federal funding for Indianapolis, including mass rapid transit and infrastructure projects.
