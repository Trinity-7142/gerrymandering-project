Rick Larsen has served as the U.S. Representative for Washington's 2nd Congressional District since 2001. Born and raised in Arlington, Washington, he earned a master's degree in public affairs from the University of Minnesota and previously worked as director of public affairs for the Washington State Dental Association and as an economic development official with the Port of Everett. A member of the centrist New Democrat Coalition, Larsen has cultivated an image as a moderate. As Ranking Member on the House Transportation & Infrastructure Committee, he focuses on long-term infrastructure investment, job creation, and building a cleaner transportation network. His priorities include lowering costs for working families, protecting reproductive freedom, combating fentanyl, and supporting veterans. He has credited the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law with bringing $386 million to the 2nd District. He also serves on the NATO Parliamentary Assembly and co-chairs the U.S.-China Working Group.
