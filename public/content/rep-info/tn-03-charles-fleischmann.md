Charles Joseph "Chuck" Fleischmann has represented Tennessee's 3rd Congressional District — centered on Chattanooga and encompassing a large swath of East Tennessee, including Oak Ridge — since 2011. Born in Manhattan in 1962, he earned his B.A. in political science from the University of Illinois and his J.D. from the University of Tennessee–Knoxville. After law school he founded a private firm in Chattanooga and served as president of the Chattanooga Bar Association. A conservative Republican, his platform emphasizes lower taxes, reduced regulatory burdens, and U.S. energy independence. He chairs the House Energy and Water Appropriations Subcommittee, and in 2023 led Congress in self-directed earmark spending, securing $273.3 million for his district, the largest share going to the Chickamauga Dam lock replacement. Fleischmann was among 126 House Republicans who signed an amicus brief in *Texas v. Pennsylvania* contesting the 2020 presidential election results; the Supreme Court declined the case for lack of standing. He was recognized by *Roll Call* as one of only 13 House members with perfect voting attendance in 2024.
