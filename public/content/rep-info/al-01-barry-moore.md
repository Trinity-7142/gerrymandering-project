Felix Barry Moore (born September 26, 1966) is a Republican U.S. Representative for Alabama's 1st congressional district. Born in Coffee County and raised on his family's farm, he earned a B.S. in Agricultural Science from Auburn University in 1992. He served in the Alabama Army National Guard and later founded Hopper-Moore Inc., an industrial waste hauling company. Moore is a member of the House Freedom Caucus and is known for advocating limited government, fiscal responsibility, and support for veterans and the military. His platform emphasizes border security, election integrity, and pro-life policy. Court-ordered redistricting in 2024 forced an incumbent-vs.-incumbent Republican primary, which Moore narrowly won. He introduced legislation to designate the AR-15-style rifle as the National Gun of the United States. He has declared candidacy for Alabama's U.S. Senate seat in the 2026 Republican primary.
