Born in Bay Shore, New York, Republican Nick LaLota has represented New York's 1st congressional district since 2023. The son and grandson of law enforcement officers, LaLota graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy and served eight years in the Navy across three overseas deployments. He later earned an MBA and a Juris Doctor from Hofstra University. His platform centers on lowering the cost of living, supporting working families, securing the border, and maintaining a strong military. He serves on the House Appropriations and Homeland Security Committees. He is the Northeast's only Republican on the House Appropriations Committee and has secured over $15 million in community project funding for Suffolk County. Notably, LaLota was one of the first Republicans to call for a House Ethics Committee investigation into false claims made by representative-elect George Santos. He was also one of 18 Republicans who voted against Jim Jordan's nomination for Speaker all three times.
