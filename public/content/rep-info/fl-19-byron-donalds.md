Byron Donalds is a third-term U.S. Representative serving southwest Florida's 19th congressional district, born and raised in Crown Heights, Brooklyn, where he was raised by a single mother and described his childhood as difficult. Prior to entering politics, Donalds worked in the finance, insurance, and banking industries. Situated in the conservative wing of the Republican Party, he was a member of the Tea Party movement. During his 2020 campaign, Donalds described himself as a "Trump supporting, gun owning, liberty loving, pro-life" conservative, stating support for economic freedom, clean water, nuclear power, and decreased government involvement in healthcare, while opposing the Green New Deal. In the January 2023 Speaker election, Donalds was nominated for the speakership across multiple rounds of voting. In 2024, he faced a complaint alleging he violated the STOCK Act by failing to disclose over 100 stock trades valued at up to $1.6 million, including in companies overseen by the House Financial Services Committee. In February 2025, Donalds announced his candidacy for governor of Florida in the 2026 election. If elected, he would be the first elected Black Republican governor in U.S. history.
