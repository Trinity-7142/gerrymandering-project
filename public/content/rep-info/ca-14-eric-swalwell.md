Eric Michael Swalwell (born November 16, 1980, in Sac City, Iowa) grew up in Dublin, California, raised by Republican parents — a police officer father and a small-business-owner mother — before becoming a Democrat himself. After law school, he worked as a deputy district attorney in Alameda County, then served two years on the Dublin City Council. First elected to Congress in 2012, he unseated 40-year incumbent Pete Stark. He served eight years on the House Intelligence Committee, helped lead the Russia investigation, and served as an impeachment manager in both Trump impeachments. A progressive Democrat focused on gun violence, national security, and technology, Swalwell resigned from Congress in April 2026 after allegations that he had raped a staffer and sexually harassed three others — allegations he denies — prompted a DOJ investigation and bipartisan calls for his removal.
