Mark Eugene Amodei is a Republican representing Nevada's 2nd Congressional District since 2011. He grew up near Carson City in a middle-class family, majored in political science, and after earning a law degree entered private practice. He served in the U.S. Army Judge Advocate General Corps from 1983 to 1987. A self-described fiscal conservative, he has cited runaway federal spending as a core motivation for entering Congress. He sponsors legislation primarily in public lands, natural resources, and national security. He rose to chair the House Appropriations Subcommittee on Homeland Security in 2024, overseeing funding for Border Patrol and ICE. He has occasionally broken with his party on immigration, voting to grant legal status to undocumented agricultural workers and expressing skepticism about mass deportation logistics. In February 2026, Amodei announced he would not seek re-election.
