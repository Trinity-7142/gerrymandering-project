Tim Burchett, born in 1964, is a lifelong Knoxville, Tennessee native serving as the U.S. Representative for Tennessee's 2nd Congressional District since 2019. A former small businessman, he converted over 30,000 tons of grass waste into mulch and compost before entering public life. He served in the Tennessee House of Representatives from 1994 to 1998, the Tennessee Senate from 1998 to 2010, and as Knox County mayor from 2010 to 2018. A staunch conservative, his platform centers on cutting taxes, protecting American jobs, and defending individual liberties. He has pledged support for congressional term limits legislation. In the 119th Congress, he was selected as Chairman of the House Oversight and Government Reform Subcommittee on Delivering on Government Efficiency (DOGE). A noted controversy arose in 2018 when reports surfaced that he failed to disclose a $10,000 payment from a solar company on campaign finance forms, and that the FBI had questioned individuals about possible income tax evasion.
