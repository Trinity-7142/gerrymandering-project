Born in Topeka, Kansas, Ron Estes is a fifth-generation Kansan representing the state's Fourth District in the U.S. House of Representatives. He holds a B.S. in civil engineering and an M.B.A. from Tennessee Tech and worked in consulting and management roles across the aerospace, oil and gas, and automotive industries, including stints at Koch Industries and Bombardier Learjet. A conservative Republican, he champions tax reform, free and fair trade, retirement security, and lowering health care costs, serving on the influential House Committee on Ways and Means. His platform also calls for securing the border, stopping illegal immigration, and investing in the military and law enforcement. As Kansas State Treasurer, he oversaw more than $24 billion in public funds while enacting pension reforms and consistently coming in under budget. Notably, on January 6, 2021, Estes voted to omit Arizona and/or Pennsylvania from the counting of presidential electors.
