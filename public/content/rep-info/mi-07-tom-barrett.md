Thomas More Barrett (born April 30, 1981) is a Republican U.S. Representative for Michigan's 7th Congressional District, residing in Charlotte, Michigan, with his wife, Ashley, and their four children. A lifelong Michigan resident, Barrett grew up in a hardworking family and from an early age learned the importance of perseverance and the value of family. After high school, he joined the U.S. Army, serving 22 years in Iraq, Guantanamo Bay, Kuwait, and the Korean DMZ, and logging over 1,000 flight hours in advanced helicopters. Prior to running for public office, Barrett worked as an analyst for the Michigan Department of the Treasury. He then served four years in the Michigan House and four years in the Michigan Senate, becoming a strong advocate for veterans, tax relief for working families, and infrastructure investment. His congressional campaign centered on four key pillars: securing the border, reducing the cost of living, national security, and protecting communities from crime. He defeated Democratic nominee Curtis Hertel Jr. in 2024 after losing a prior 2022 bid to Democratic incumbent Elissa Slotkin. During the 2024 campaign, Black Michigan lawmakers called for a probe into a Barrett ad that listed Election Day as November 6 rather than November 5, alleging voter misdirection; his campaign denied the charge, calling it a proofreading error. In Congress, Barrett voted for the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, helping the budget and tax bill pass in a 215–214 House vote. Notably, his great-grandfather, Rep. Louis C. Rabaut, served Michigan in Congress, and Barrett successfully claimed the same Capitol Hill office Rabaut occupied in 1954.
