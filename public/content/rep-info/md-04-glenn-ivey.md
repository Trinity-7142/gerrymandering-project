Glenn Frederick Ivey is an attorney and Democratic politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Maryland's 4th congressional district since 2023. He grew up in Rocky Mount, North Carolina, and Dale City, Virginia, in a family shaped by public service — his mother was among the first Black teachers at several formerly all-white schools, and his father worked for a War on Poverty agency. He earned a B.A. in politics from Princeton University and a J.D. from Harvard University. He previously served as State's Attorney for Prince George's County from 2002 to 2011. His platform focuses on preserving the rule of law, safeguarding federal workers, policing reform, commonsense gun reform, and economic policies for working-class constituents. Ivey supports Medicare for All and the legalization of recreational marijuana. In the 119th Congress, he serves on the Ethics and Appropriations Committees. He was selected by Democratic Leader Hakeem Jeffries to join the bipartisan Task Force on the Attempted Assassination of Donald J. Trump, where he worked to identify security failures within the Secret Service.
