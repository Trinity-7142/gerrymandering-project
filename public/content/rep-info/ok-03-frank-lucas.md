Frank Dean Lucas is an American politician and farmer serving as the U.S. Representative for Oklahoma's 3rd congressional district. A fifth-generation Oklahoman, he was born in Cheyenne, Oklahoma, and earned a degree in Agricultural Economics from Oklahoma State University in 1982. A conservative Republican, his platform centers on cutting regulations on agriculture and energy production, lowering taxes, and pursuing fiscal responsibility. Lucas is the longest-serving member on the House Agriculture Committee and has contributed to every Farm Bill since 1996, including chairing the committee from 2011 to 2015 and crafting the landmark 2014 Farm Bill. In the 119th Congress, he chairs the Conservation, Research, and Biotechnology Subcommittee. He became the dean of Oklahoma's congressional delegation in 2023 following the retirement of Senator Jim Inhofe.
