Susie Lee is a Democrat who has represented Nevada's 3rd Congressional District — covering southern Las Vegas and much of unincorporated Clark County — since 2019. Born to a working-class family of ten in Canton, Ohio, her father was a military veteran who worked at a steel plant. She worked her way through Carnegie Mellon University and later moved to Las Vegas in 1993, where she led education nonprofits, founding After School All Stars and serving as President of Communities in Schools of Nevada. In Congress, Lee has championed bipartisan leadership on affordable healthcare, public education funding, and veterans' support. She helped negotiate the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and the Inflation Reduction Act. She also created the Battleground Leadership Representative position in 2022 to give members from competitive districts a seat at the Democratic Caucus leadership table. Notably, Lee was one of 46 House Democrats who joined Republicans in voting for the Laken Riley Act in 2025. She was reelected in 2024, defeating Republican Drew Johnson with 51% of the vote.
