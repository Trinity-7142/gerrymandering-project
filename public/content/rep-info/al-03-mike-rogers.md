Michael Dennis Rogers (born July 16, 1958) is a Republican U.S. Representative for Alabama's 3rd congressional district, serving since 2003. A conservative Republican, he is a sixth-generation East Alabamian and the son of a retired textile worker and fireman, having earned his B.A. in political science and Master of Public Administration from Jacksonville State University, and his J.D. from the Birmingham School of Law. Rogers chairs the House Armed Services Committee, having previously served as its Ranking Member and as Ranking Member of the House Homeland Security Committee. He holds conservative positions, opposing abortion and the ACA, while supporting increased military spending, stronger immigration enforcement, and cuts to regulatory agencies. He co-authored an early proposal to create a Space Corps, which passed the House but failed in the Senate; similar legislation was later signed into law establishing the U.S. Space Force. In January 2023, Rogers made national news for an on-floor altercation with Florida congressman Matt Gaetz during the contentious House Speaker vote.
