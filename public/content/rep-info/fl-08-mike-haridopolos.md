Michael John Haridopolos (born March 15, 1970) is a Republican politician, consultant, and former educator who lives in Indian Harbour Beach, Florida. Born in Huntington, New York, his father was a Greek immigrant who served as an FBI agent. He began his career teaching U.S. History and Political Science at Brevard Community College and later taught at the University of Florida. A Republican, he served in the Florida Senate from 2003 to 2012 and was Senate President from 2010 to 2012, presiding over the largest Republican majority (28–12) since Reconstruction. He ran for the open FL-08 seat after Bill Posey's retirement and was elected with 62% of the vote in November 2024. His campaign platform centered on stopping illegal immigration, fixing the economy, reducing the national debt, protecting the Indian River Lagoon, and supporting space exploration. In Congress, he serves on the House Financial Services Committee and chairs the Space and Aeronautics subcommittee of the Science, Space, and Technology Committee. A notable first-year accomplishment includes securing more than $25 million in federal funding for local projects, including Indian River Lagoon restoration and upgrades at Melbourne International Airport.
