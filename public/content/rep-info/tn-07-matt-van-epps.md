Matthew Robert Van Epps (born March 29, 1983) is a politician and former Army officer representing Tennessee's 7th congressional district, based in Nashville, Tennessee. A West Point graduate (2005), he commissioned into the Army as an aviation officer, serving on active duty for ten years. He is a decorated helicopter pilot and Lieutenant Colonel in the Tennessee Army National Guard, continuing to serve as a reservist. A conservative Republican, Van Epps is a self-described "fiscal hawk" whose platform aligns with the "America First" agenda. He supported the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, a reconciliation package extending the 2017 tax cuts, implementing welfare reforms, and increasing border security funding. Citing his military background, he advocates a "Peace Through Strength" foreign policy, a robust defense budget, completion of the U.S.–Mexico border wall, and deportation of undocumented immigrants with criminal records. He also supports school choice policies allowing education funding to follow students to private or charter schools. He resigned as Tennessee's Commissioner of General Services in June 2025 to run in the special election triggered by Rep. Mark Green's resignation. Van Epps won the Republican primary with 51.6% and defeated Democratic nominee Aftyn Behn in the December 2 special election with 53.9% of the vote. Analysts noted that Behn significantly narrowed the margin in a district Donald Trump had carried by more than 22 points in 2024. He serves on the Homeland Security Subcommittee on Counterterrorism and Intelligence and introduced the Major Non-NATO Ally Terror Threat Assessment Act.
