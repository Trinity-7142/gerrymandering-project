Valerie Anne Hoyle is a Democrat serving as the U.S. Representative for Oregon's 4th congressional district since 2023. Born on Travis Air Force Base in Fairfield, California, she grew up in Nashua, New Hampshire, where her father was a firefighter and union official. The first in her family to earn a college degree, she attended Bunker Hill Community College before graduating from Emmanuel College with a degree in political science. Before Congress, she served as Oregon's Labor Commissioner and as a state legislator representing West Eugene. Her platform centers on lowering costs for working families, developing a container port in Coos Bay for job creation, and transitioning to clean energy. In her first term, she passed five bipartisan bills into law, spanning tribal rights, worker protections, and government transparency. While generally aligned with House Democrats, she notably joined a small group of Democrats to vote to lift COVID-19 vaccine mandates for healthcare workers.
