Born and raised in Glenside, Pennsylvania, Dean completed her undergraduate studies at La Salle University and earned her law degree at Delaware Law School of Widener University. While raising three sons, she shifted careers and taught writing and ethics in the English Department at La Salle University from 2001 to 2011. She later served as Abington Township commissioner before winning a special election to the Pennsylvania State House in 2012. In 2018, when Pennsylvania held 18 Congressional seats and zero were held by women, Dean ran for the Fourth Congressional District seat and won. A progressive Democrat, she champions education, equal access to healthcare, gun violence prevention, clean water, combating climate change, and ethical governance. Now in her fourth term, she serves on the House Appropriations and House Foreign Affairs Committees. Following the Sandy Hook shooting, Dean co-founded the PA-SAFE Caucus, a coalition of legislators and advocates dedicated to ending gun violence. In 2024, she was named one of six Democratic members of a bipartisan task force investigating the attempted assassination of Donald Trump in Pennsylvania.
