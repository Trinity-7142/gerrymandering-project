Born and raised in Chicago, Illinois, Walberg attended Moody Bible College and earned a bachelor's degree from Fort Wayne Bible College before working as a minister. He now lives in Tipton, Michigan. He holds the designation as Michigan's most conservative Member of Congress based on the American Conservative Union's lifetime score. His platform centers on reducing taxes, stopping wasteful spending, making health care affordable, and pursuing a responsible energy policy. In the 119th Congress, Walberg serves as Chair of the House Education and Workforce Committee. He was ranked by the nonpartisan Center for Effective Lawmaking as the most effective member of Michigan's congressional delegation in the 115th Congress. In 2024, Walberg drew widespread controversy when he remarked that the U.S. "shouldn't be spending a dime on humanitarian aid" for Gaza and suggested it "should be like Nagasaki and Hiroshima," prompting CAIR to condemn the comments as a "clear call to genocide." Walberg denied advocating nuclear weapons, saying he "used a metaphor."
