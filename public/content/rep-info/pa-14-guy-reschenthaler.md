Guy Reschenthaler was born in Pittsburgh and raised in the city's South Hills. The son of former teachers, he is an attorney, judge, and U.S. Navy veteran. A Republican, he has represented Pennsylvania's 14th congressional district since 2019 and is a conservative aligned closely with the national GOP. His priorities include limiting government and lowering taxes, reforming the criminal justice system, supporting veterans, countering China, and helping senior citizens. He served as a district judge and in the U.S. Navy JAG Corps during the Iraq War. He currently serves as the Republican Chief Deputy Whip in the 119th Congress and sits on the House Appropriations Committee. Notably, Reschenthaler was one of 147 Republican representatives and senators to vote against certifying the 2020 presidential election results. In the 2024 general election, he defeated Democratic nominee Chris Dziados with 66.6% of the vote.
