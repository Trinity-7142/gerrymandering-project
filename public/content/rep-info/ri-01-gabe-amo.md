Born and raised in Pawtucket, Rhode Island, to immigrant parents — his father from Ghana and his mother from Liberia — his mother is a nurse and his father owns a liquor store. Amo earned a B.A. from Wheaton College and was a Marshall Scholar at Oxford University. A progressive Democrat, he served in the Biden administration as deputy director of the White House Office of Intergovernmental Affairs, and previously worked in the Obama administration and for Governor Gina Raimondo. His campaign focused on protecting Social Security, Medicare, and abortion rights, while tackling gun violence and climate change. He became the first person of color elected to represent Rhode Island in Congress. He drew criticism for accepting over $20,000 from federal lobbyists representing major corporations. He serves on the House Committee on Foreign Affairs and the Committee on Science, Space, and Technology.
