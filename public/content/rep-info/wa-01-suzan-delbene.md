Suzan DelBene is a Democratic politician and businesswoman who has represented Washington's 1st Congressional District since 2012. Born in Selma, Alabama, she grew up in a family that struggled financially, with parents constantly moving in search of work. She built a career in the tech industry, spending nearly a decade at Microsoft before co-founding drugstore.com. She chairs the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee (DCCC) and is a former chair of the New Democrat Coalition. She sits on the House Budget and Ways and Means Committees. As a Ways and Means member, she has championed making the expanded Child Tax Credit permanent and has backed a bipartisan proposal to build 2 million more units of affordable housing. She has also been a prominent advocate for digital privacy reform and reining in government surveillance programs.
