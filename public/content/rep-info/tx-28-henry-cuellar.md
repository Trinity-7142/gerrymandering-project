Born one of eight children to migrant farm workers in Laredo, Texas, Rep. Henry Cuellar has represented Texas's 28th congressional district since 2005. Regarded as the most degreed member of Congress, he holds a Master's in International Trade, a J.D., and a Ph.D. in Government from the University of Texas at Austin. Known as a leading centrist Democrat, he is one of the most conservative House Democrats. He serves as the only Texas Democrat on the House Appropriations Committee and is Ranking Member of its Homeland Security Subcommittee. His platform centers on border security, appropriations for South Texas, and bipartisan infrastructure. He was instrumental in shepherding passage of the bipartisan Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act in 2021. In May 2024, Cuellar and his wife were indicted on money laundering, conspiracy, and bribery charges, with prosecutors alleging nearly $600,000 in bribes from Azerbaijan and a Mexican bank. On December 3, 2025, President Trump pardoned Cuellar and his wife, ending their federal legal battle.
