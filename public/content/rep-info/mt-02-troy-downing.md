Born in Indio, California, Downing built a vacation home in Big Sky, Montana, in 2000 and moved there full-time in 2009. He began his career as an educator at New York University's Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences before founding a technology startup that was later acquired by Yahoo!. He then joined the U.S. Air Force and Air National Guard, serving eight years with a Combat Search and Rescue squadron, including two tours of duty to Afghanistan. Most recently, he served as Montana State Auditor, overseeing the state's insurance and securities industries. A conservative Republican, Downing advocates for lower taxes, reducing government regulation, and policies promoting economic growth, energy independence, and national security. He serves on the House Financial Services Committee and House Committee on Small Business. Notably, Downing pleaded guilty in 2018 to two misdemeanor charges of obtaining resident hunting licenses while not officially a Montana resident, arguing the matter stemmed from his accountants listing him as a California resident; he was fined $2,110 and lost hunting and fishing privileges for 18 months.
