Roger Williams was born in Evanston, Illinois, in 1949, and raised in Fort Worth, Texas. He attended Texas Christian University, earning All-Southwest Conference honors in baseball, and was later drafted by the Atlanta Braves, playing in their farm system before becoming a small business owner. Williams served as Texas Secretary of State from 2005 to 2007 before being elected as a Republican to represent TX-25, serving since January 2013. His platform centers on seven pillars: lower taxes, less governance, cutting spending, defending borders, listening to generals, understanding the 10th Amendment, and standing with Israel. He serves as Chairman of the House Committee on Small Business and on the House Financial Services Committee. A notable controversy arose when the House Ethics Committee investigated Williams after he inserted a provision into a major transportation bill that would have exempted dealerships like his own from rules preventing rental companies from renting recalled vehicles.
