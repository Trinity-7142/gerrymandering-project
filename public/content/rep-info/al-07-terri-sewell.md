Born in Huntsville and raised in Selma, Sewell is the daughter of a high school basketball coach and a high school librarian. A proud product of Alabama's rural Black Belt, she was the first Black valedictorian of Selma High School. Before entering politics, she worked as a securities lawyer and as a public finance lawyer, where she was the first Black woman to make partner at her Birmingham firm. She is the first African-American woman elected to Congress from Alabama. A liberal with a focus on job creation, she arguably holds the most left-wing voting record of any person to represent Alabama in Congress. She sits on the House Ways and Means Committee and in the 119th Congress was selected as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Oversight — the first Black woman to hold a leadership role on that committee since its establishment in 1789. She is an outspoken advocate for job creation, workforce development, skills training, and economic opportunities for her constituents. In both impeachments of Donald Trump, Sewell voted in favor of articles of impeachment — the only representative from Alabama to do so.
