Rep. Maxine Dexter (D) has served Oregon's 3rd Congressional District since January 2025. She grew up in the Pacific Northwest in a working-class family and, at 16, took a union job at Albertsons, which helped her become the first in her family to graduate from college. She subsequently practiced for over 15 years as a lung and critical care physician at Kaiser Permanente, becoming the first woman to chair its Northwest Permanente Board of Directors. Her congressional priorities include expanding healthcare access, improving air quality, transitioning to a single-payer health system, reducing greenhouse gas emissions, increasing public housing funding, and stricter gun control. In Congress, she serves on the House Veterans' Affairs Committee and the House Natural Resources Committee, where she is Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations. Notably, as a state legislator she authored a bill expanding access to the opioid antidote naloxone and decriminalizing fentanyl test strips, which was signed into law in August 2023. In April 2025, she traveled to El Salvador in an effort to assist Kilmar Abrego Garcia, who had been deported by the Trump administration under disputed legal circumstances.
