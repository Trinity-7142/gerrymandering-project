Derek Schmidt is a Republican lawyer and politician serving as the U.S. Representative for Kansas's 2nd Congressional District since 2025. A native of Independence, he learned the value of hard work from a family of teachers, farmers, laborers, and small business owners, and holds degrees from the University of Kansas, the University of Leicester, and Georgetown University Law Center. A fifth-generation Kansan, Schmidt previously served 12 years as the 44th Attorney General of Kansas, focusing on prosecuting crimes against children and protecting senior citizens. He served as president of the National Association of Attorneys General and was named the nation's Outstanding Attorney General by his colleagues in 2019. Schmidt was the Republican nominee for governor of Kansas in 2022 but narrowly lost to incumbent Democrat Laura Kelly. In Congress, he serves on the House Armed Services, Judiciary, and Small Business Committees, focusing on border security, reducing federal spending, and limiting federal bureaucracy. His key legislative priorities include border security, tax relief for Kansas families and farmers, and curbing federal spending.
