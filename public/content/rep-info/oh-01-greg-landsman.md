Born and raised in Cincinnati to a Jewish family, Landsman is a former teacher who spent much of his career working on behalf of children and families. He holds a B.A. in economics and political science from Ohio University and an M.A. in theological studies from Harvard Divinity School. A Democrat, he is a member of the New Democrat Coalition and the Problem Solvers Caucus. His platform centers on protecting freedoms, lowering costs, and making it easier to raise a family. Before Congress, he led a ballot measure making Cincinnati the first city in America to provide two years of quality preschool. He was elected in 2022, defeating 13-term incumbent Steve Chabot, and won re-election in 2024, becoming only the third Democrat to represent a significant portion of Cincinnati for more than one term since the Civil War. In his first term, he pushed for a House vote on the Social Security Fairness Act.
