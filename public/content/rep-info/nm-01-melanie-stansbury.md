Stansbury grew up in a working-class family in Albuquerque, New Mexico, and holds a Master of Science in development sociology with a minor in American Indian studies from Cornell University. She previously worked as a STEM educator and researcher on land and water issues, and as a staffer for the U.S. Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources and the White House Office of Management and Budget. A progressive Democrat, her priorities include addressing climate change through renewable energy, economic opportunity via fair wages, expanded Medicaid access, and investing in public education. She has pledged support for Medicare for All, a federal assault weapons ban, student loan debt cancellation, and federal marijuana legalization. She won her seat in a 2021 special election after President Biden nominated Deb Haaland as Interior Secretary. During the 117th Congress, she voted with President Biden's stated position 100% of the time. She drew attention at the 2025 Joint Session of Congress by holding a protest sign opposing mass federal worker firings.
