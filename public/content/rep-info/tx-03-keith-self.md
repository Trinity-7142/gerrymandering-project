Keith Self is a Republican politician, military veteran, and former judge representing Texas's 3rd congressional district since 2023. Born in 1953 and raised in Amarillo, Texas, he earned a B.S. in engineering from the U.S. Military Academy. He served in the U.S. Army from 1975 to 1999 as a member of Army Special Forces and Army Rangers. He subsequently served three terms as Collin County Judge from 2007 to 2018. A hard-right conservative and member of the Freedom Caucus, Self's platform centers on strengthening the military, border security, veterans' support, and Second Amendment protection. He earned a 100% score from Heritage Action for his votes and co-sponsorships in the 118th Congress. Controversies include his response to the 2023 Allen, Texas mall shooting, in which eight were killed, drawing criticism for his remarks about prayer and gun violence, and his outspoken criticism of Islam, including co-introducing a bill to ban sharia and delivering an anti-Islam speech on the House floor in November 2025.
