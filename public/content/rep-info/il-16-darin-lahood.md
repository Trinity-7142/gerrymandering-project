Rep. Darin LaHood, born and raised in Peoria, Illinois, serves the 16th Congressional District. He is the son of Ray LaHood, the 16th U.S. Secretary of Transportation and a seven-term congressman for the same district. A Republican attorney by background, LaHood worked as an Assistant U.S. Attorney and was selected as Chief Terrorism Prosecutor in Las Vegas before entering politics. He serves on the House Ways and Means Committee and chairs its Subcommittee on Work and Welfare. His priorities include fiscal responsibility, market access for Illinois farmers, lower taxes for working families, and rural healthcare. He also serves on the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. Notably, LaHood was one of 126 Republicans to sign an amicus brief supporting *Texas v. Pennsylvania*, contesting the 2020 presidential election results, though he ultimately voted to certify Biden as President-elect on January 6, 2021.
