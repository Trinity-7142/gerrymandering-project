Brittany Pettersen was born in Colorado and grew up in Jefferson County, the district she now represents. Facing challenges as an at-risk youth, she became the first in her family to graduate high school and college, earning her degree at Metropolitan State University of Denver by waiting tables to put herself through school. A Democrat, she served in the Colorado state legislature for a decade before being elected in 2022, and is the first woman to represent this district. Her platform centers on mental health and substance use disorder treatment — shaped by her mother's struggle with addiction — as well as consumer protections, housing affordability, and cracking down on illicit finance. Her bipartisan legislation to prevent the financing of illegal synthetic drugs passed the House in May 2023. She is also working with Rep. Anna Paulina Luna to allow proxy voting for members of Congress who have recently given birth.
