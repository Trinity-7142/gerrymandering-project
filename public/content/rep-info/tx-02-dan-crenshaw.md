Dan Crenshaw was born in Aberdeen, Scotland, and grew up in Katy, Texas. A sixth-generation Texan, his father's career in the oil and gas industry moved the family internationally, including Colombia, where Crenshaw attended high school. A conservative Republican, Crenshaw lost his right eye in 2012 during his third deployment when he was hit by an IED explosion in Afghanistan's Helmand Province. He was medically retired as a Lieutenant Commander after ten years in the SEAL Teams, earning two Bronze Stars — one with Valor — the Purple Heart, and the Navy Commendation Medal with Valor. In November 2018, he was elected to represent Texas's 2nd Congressional District. He focuses on national security, border security, and fiscal conservatism. He serves on the House Energy and Commerce Committee and the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence, where he chairs the Subcommittee on Defense Intelligence and Overhead Architecture. Crenshaw lost the 2026 Republican primary to State Representative Steve Toth, running without a Trump endorsement.
