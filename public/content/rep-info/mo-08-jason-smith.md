Jason Thomas Smith (born June 16, 1980) is a Republican politician who has served as the U.S. Representative for Missouri's 8th congressional district since 2013. He comes from humble roots, having grown up in Salem, Missouri. A seventh-generation Missourian and fourth-generation owner of his family's farm, Smith earned two degrees from the University of Missouri in agricultural economics and business administration and later obtained a Juris Doctor from Oklahoma City University School of Law in 2004. A staunch conservative, his platform centers on tax relief, fiscal restraint, and advocacy for rural communities. He chairs the Ways and Means Committee and became the youngest Ways and Means Chairman since before the Civil War. He has earned 100% ratings from the National Right to Life, the Christian Coalition of America, and the National Federation of Independent Business.
