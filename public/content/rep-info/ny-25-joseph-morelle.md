Joseph D. Morelle is a Democrat serving as U.S. Representative for New York's 25th Congressional District since 2018, formerly a member of the New York State Assembly representing eastern Rochester and Monroe County suburbs. Of Italian American heritage, he was born in Utica, New York. The son of a Korean War veteran and union member, he is a former small business owner who also served as New York State Assembly Majority Leader from 2013–2018. In Congress, he serves as Ranking Member on the House Administration Committee and Vice Ranking Member on the Appropriations Committee. His platform focuses on working-class economic investment, healthcare access, and democracy reform. He launched an Anti-Corruption and Democracy Reform Task Force to address government ethics. An early controversy arose in 1990 when a court ruled he fraudulently obtained ballot petition signatures; he remained on the ballot and won, later admitting family members had illegally signed petitions on others' behalf.
