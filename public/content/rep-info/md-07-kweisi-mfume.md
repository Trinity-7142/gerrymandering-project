Born Frizzell Gerard Tate on October 24, 1948, Rep. Kweisi Mfume is a Democrat from Baltimore whose biography reflects a dramatic personal journey. After his mother's death when he was 16, he dropped out of high school to work and support his sisters, but later earned a B.S. from Morgan State University and a master's degree from Johns Hopkins University. He is known as a Democrat who balances progressive ideology with a capacity for practical compromise, focusing on federal aid to inner cities, civil rights, and health equity. In the 119th Congress, he has championed legislation such as the Affordable Inhalers and Nebulizers Act, aimed at reducing prices for asthma and COPD medications. He previously left his seat to serve as president and CEO of the NAACP from 1996 to 2004, and stepped down amid an internal investigation into allegations of sexual harassment, acknowledging a relationship with an NAACP employee. He returned to Congress in 2020 after the seat was vacated by the death of Elijah Cummings.
