Born in Omaha, Nebraska, in 1952, Rutherford grew up in Jacksonville, Florida, where he earned a B.S. in criminology from Florida State University and spent 41 years at the Jacksonville Sheriff's Office. Elected Sheriff of Duval County in 2003, 2007, and 2011, he ran a 3,200-employee office and brought homicide and overall violent crime to a 40-year low. A conservative Republican, his platform centers on law and order, border security, and America First values. In Congress, he sits on the House Appropriations Committee, serving on subcommittees covering Homeland Security, Military Construction and Veterans Affairs, and Transportation and Housing and Urban Development. He faced scrutiny when the Office of Congressional Ethics found substantial reason to believe he had not filed timely financial disclosure reports, and the House Ethics Committee opened an investigation into repeated STOCK Act reporting violations. He also voted against certifying electoral votes from Arizona and Pennsylvania following the 2020 presidential election.
