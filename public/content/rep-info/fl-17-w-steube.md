W. Gregory Steube is a U.S. Representative for Florida's 17th congressional district, serving since 2019. Born on May 19, 1978, in Bradenton, he earned a degree in Animal Science and a Juris Doctor from the University of Florida. After law school, Steube entered the U.S. Army JAG Corps, serving as a Captain and deploying to Iraq in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom. A conservative Republican and member of the House Freedom Caucus, he has prioritized veterans' issues, Second Amendment rights, and regulatory reform. He currently sits on the Committee on Ways and Means and the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. In May 2024, Steube accomplished the rare feat of shepherding a complete discharge petition, securing enough votes to bring a disaster relief bill to the House floor. Notably, he was among 126 House Republicans who signed an amicus brief in *Texas v. Pennsylvania* contesting the 2020 presidential election results, a case the Supreme Court declined to hear.
