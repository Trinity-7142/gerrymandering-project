Emily Randall is a Democratic politician and healthcare advocate serving as the U.S. Representative for Washington's 6th congressional district since 2025. Born in Port Orchard, she grew up on the Kitsap Peninsula, where her father worked at Puget Sound Naval Shipyard and her mother became a paraeducator. The first in her family to attend a four-year college, she earned bachelor's degrees in Spanish and women's studies from Wellesley College in 2008. A progressive Democrat, her platform centers on defending reproductive rights, expanding access to quality healthcare, and ensuring broad educational opportunities, including technical training and apprenticeships. She previously served in the Washington State Senate from 2019 to 2024, where she rose to leadership and helped pass landmark legislation protecting abortion access, expanding Medicaid, and improving apprenticeship programs. In Congress, she serves as co-chair of the Congressional Equality Caucus and has re-introduced the Equality Act to establish explicit nondiscrimination protections for LGBTQI+ Americans. Randall is the first LGBTQ+ Latina elected to the U.S. House of Representatives.
