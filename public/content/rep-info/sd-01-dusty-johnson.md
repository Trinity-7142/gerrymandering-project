Dusty Johnson (R) has served as U.S. Representative for South Dakota's at-large congressional district since 2019. He grew up in a large working-class family in central South Dakota and was named a Truman Scholar in 1998, working for the U.S. Department of Agriculture in Washington, D.C. As a member of the centrist Problem Solvers Caucus, Johnson is generally considered to be a moderate Republican. His policy focus centers on agriculture, rural broadband, transportation infrastructure, and national security. His leadership prohibiting purchases of American agricultural land by the Chinese Communist Party and banning TikTok on government devices led to his appointment on the Select Committee on China. He also served as Chairman of the Republican Main Street Caucus from 2023 to July 2025, leading a group of 70+ pragmatic conservatives. In June 2025, Johnson announced that he would run for Governor of South Dakota in 2026.
