Born in New York City in 1948 to a Jewish family, Frankel earned a bachelor's degree from Boston University and a J.D. from Georgetown University Law Center, settling in West Palm Beach in 1974. A liberal Democrat, she served 14 years in the Florida state legislature and became the first woman to serve as Democratic minority leader. She was then elected mayor of West Palm Beach in 2003, serving two terms until leaving office in 2011 due to term limits. Now in her seventh term in Congress, her platform prioritizes lowering costs for families, protecting Social Security and Medicare, expanding affordable healthcare, and defending reproductive freedom. She founded and chaired the Democratic Women's Caucus, now serving as Chair Emerita. She has also sponsored resolutions recognizing equal pay and the wage disparity between men and women.
