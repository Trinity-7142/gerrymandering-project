Born in Harlem, New York, the daughter of a small business owner and postal worker, Rep. Robin Kelly moved to Illinois to attend Bradley University in Peoria, where she later earned a Ph.D. in political science from Northern Illinois University. A progressive Democrat, she has championed expanding economic opportunity, community wellness, and public safety since first being elected to represent IL-02 in 2013, with a focus on reducing health disparities and ending gun violence. A staunch advocate for gun reform, Kelly co-chairs the Congressional Gun Violence Prevention Task Force and authored the 2014 Kelly Report on Gun Violence in America. In 2021, she became the first woman and first African American to chair the Democratic Party of Illinois. During her time in Congress, she secured more than $245 million in community project funding for the 2nd District. In May 2025, Kelly announced she would retire from the House to run for the U.S. Senate seat being vacated by the retiring Dick Durbin.
