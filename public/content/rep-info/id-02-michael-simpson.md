An Idaho native, Mike Simpson was born in Burley and raised in Blackfoot. After earning his dental degree, he joined his father and uncle at the Simpson Family Dental Practice in Blackfoot. Generally regarded as a moderate Republican, Simpson supports DACA and work visas for undocumented agricultural workers, while championing border security, a balanced budget, and reduced federal spending. He served as Speaker of the Idaho House of Representatives from 1992 to 1998 before entering Congress. He serves on the House Appropriations Committee as Ranking Member for the Subcommittee on Energy and Water Development, and on the Interior and Environment Subcommittee. Simpson is one of the House's leading advocates for nuclear energy research and development. In December 2020, Simpson was one of 126 Republicans to sign an amicus brief contesting the 2020 presidential election results, though he voted to certify both Arizona's and Pennsylvania's Electoral College results in January 2021.
